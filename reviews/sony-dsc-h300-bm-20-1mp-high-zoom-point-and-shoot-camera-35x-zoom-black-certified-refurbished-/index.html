I really like this camera but it does have a few drawbacks …

I really like this camera but it does have a few drawbacks that keep it from being a 5.Plus - if you’re familiar with cameras, then this is one you can take out of the box and start using. There are more things it can do than you will initially intuit, but it’s usable without knowing all the features. It isn’t too heavy or awkward; as someone with arthritis in all my joints, that makes a difference on my hands, elbows, and shoulders. It zooms and pulls back fairly well/quickly and I don’t find it loud or too slow, albeit I’m not trying to take action or sporting shots. The quality of picture is excellent. I make my own prints and greeting cards and have received many compliments on the photos I’m able to get with this camera (keep in mind, these are hobbies, not a livelihood - this isn’t professional quality).Minus - as others have mentioned, this camera struggles to focus ‘in the middle’ of it’s zoom capacity. I also find it doesn’t always do well at it’s full zoom and will readjust itself before getting the shot, which can be frustrating and time consuming. My biggest complaint though would have to be it’s poor performance with night/low light photography. In fact, it is for this final reason that I am now looking for a low end SLR that will allow me to take night and macro shots.For ordinary use, particularly for family and home pictures, I am very happy with this camera. It does well with landscape and portrait shots in normal lighting conditions and I’ve taken some really lovely photos with this camera; the biggest drawback for me is not being able to take pictures of a night sky or in low light conditions (minus flash).
