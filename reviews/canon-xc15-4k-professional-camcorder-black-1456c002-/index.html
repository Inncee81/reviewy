Took a Chance & Fell in Love

I just bought this camera after trying out a few Sony cameras, Panasonic etc.. to venture into 4k, to go along with my C100’s (it has log) and to see if it would work on the Zhiyun Crane. I am BLOWN away by it’s quality. You have to learn how to work around a few things (the lens is only 2.8 at about 24mm and goes to 5.6 at the long end) but I just shot a wedding with it on a handheld gimbal and I’m floored by the footage I got from it. It matches my C100 Mark II’s perfectly. I also grabbed an ND filter (58mm) because it only goes down to ISO500 in C-Log and there’s only one ND filter internally so by throwing on an additional ND, it was a dream outdoors. With good lighting inside, it was fine too. I was careful not to go above ISO3200 because I read that it has automatic noise reduction (although I haven’t noticed it) but didn’t want to take any chances. I bought this camera with a very specific use in mind though so it may not be for everybody but I personally love it. Being able to get IBIS, Canon colors, C-log & Wide DR, up to 60fps in 1080p and having 24p 4k was more than I could have hoped for. People dog this camera but I strongly disagree. It’s a little beast. Oh, just make sure to use the new LP-E6N (same as the 80D batteries). They last about an hour and 40 minutes each (give or take) and they’ll charge in the camera if you need them too. The older LP-E6 batteries will work but they don’t last very long and can cause issues. All in all, great camera.
