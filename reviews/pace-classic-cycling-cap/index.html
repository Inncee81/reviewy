Better Than Other Hats

Wearing this hat prompted a snapchat with the tagline “First day of kindergarten” courtesy of my totally-still-friend Jean. Regardless of our ever waning friendship, I love the hat. It works great under my helmet for protection from rain and sun. I like that it flips up for when not in use, and that when I look in the mirror I know that I am better than other people. I told my boyfriend that it would be great for keeping other men away while biking and he said, “That includes me.” If you have a strong sense of your biker self, buy this hat. See you on the road.

Yes, it does fit oddly but… (update)

I generally wear a 7 1⁄4 size fitted hat and this cap does fit pretty snugly. Not at all uncomfortable, but I probably wouldn’t want to wear it on a head that was much larger than maybe 7 1⁄2. But my beef with it so far is that the 3 panel design leaves 2 “humps” flanking the red stripe down the middle. So there are these two weird peaks that follow the seams between panels. I do like the look of the hat in general, and maybe it will eventually form better if I wear a helmet over it or steam it. I might try to get it wet and mold it to my head, but with the peaks, it looks pretty odd. I’ll report back about my fitting methods!Edit: Okay, I’m still not thrilled that the hat needs some modification to fit well– however, like others, I found the bumps on either side of the colored band down the middle to be annoying. I couldn’t decide if it looked like a butt or part of the accoutrement of some non-existent ecclesiastical office. Anyway, I tried a couple of remedies:Soaked it in hot water and let it dry on my head. Results: wet head.Soaked it again and used an iron on the hottest setting on the side panels and seams until the water had mostly evaporated. Results: Much better! I also used it along the seams with the hopes of shrinking the fabric a little because, clearly, the hat is misshapen because the material that makes the stripe is different from the cotton material that makes up most of the hat. The strip is either too small or shrank after it was attached making a bizarre looking dip down the middle of your head. If you can shrink the cotton a little, you can fix the shape of the hat. You might have similar luck if you run it through the wash with hot water/rinse, but it seemed easier to spot-target with the iron.The stripe seems to be synthetic and you might want to keep the iron away from the stripe. It’s a lot to go through for a cycling cap, but I really like the color of it! If you already have the hat, it’s a good solution.
