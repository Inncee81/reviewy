Good alternative to a window fan

This sits very comfortably on the 3⅛” (8cm) window sill in our bathroom. It’s eliminated olfactory nightmares in our small apartment almost completely (the cat has yet to learn to operate it). The best $20 I’ve ever spent.

Surprisingly loud with a poorly located power cord.

I was looking for a pair of slim profile fans to sit on windowsills, and these seemed to be the slimmest. However, the power cord design adds and inch to the depth by virtue of sticking out the back in a fixed location (as opposed to on the side). For my purposes, this means the fans don’t fully fit on the windowsill and the windows catch the cord when sliding them open or closed behind the fans. But the mains reason for the 2-star rating is the noise these fans make: at both the low and high speeds, the produce a steady, vibrating hum that is surprisingly loud - much louder than the pair of similar Holmes fans I replaced. A minor complaint is that you have to switch through the high position to get to low speed - a common convention on fans, but annoying, nonetheless. I’ve made my peace with these drawbacks, given the difficulty of finding fans that will fit my situation, but if you’re looking for something more general-purpose, you can find a much quieter option.

You might get one that doesn’t have the clicking noise?

I reduced the rating because the fan makes a very loud and very annoying “clicking” noise. Hard to describe noises, but it changes with the speed of the fan. It is less irritating at high speed as the fan is much noisier at that speed which masks the clicking noise somewhat. It may be a bearing noise, if the motor even has a bearing, but it’s definitely coming from the motor. I kept the fan for use in the garage, where the noise doesn’t bother as much.
