It was a Wal-mart special and I straight up hated that thing

I managed to survive without a bike helmet for years. But then we had kids and my wife wanted them to wear helmets, so that meant that suddenly I was wearing a helmet too. It was a Wal-mart special and I straight up hated that thing. Hot, uncomfortable, goofy looking, and I never could get the straps adjusted so that it would stay where I wanted it.My kids are almost grown now, so I hardly ever (never) wear it anymore. A while back, a guy was riding near our neighborhood, was clipped by a car, and took a header into a street sign post. He was scuffed up, but basically ok, mostly because of the helmet. so when I bought a nice shiny new bike I thought that maybe I should get a helmet that I would actually wear.This helmet weighs almost nothing and has an adjusting knob on the headband so that I can get a proper fit, and after playing with the straps for a few minutes it stays where I want it.The only negative that I have is that it comes with a pad around the strap where it goes under the chin. Maybe it’s just the shape of my head or something, but because of where the buckle ends up, the pad actually sits on the side of my chin, rather than centered. I’m still playing with the adjustment, but will probably just end up taking the pad off and tossing it.I have no idea how long a bicycle helmet should last if you actually wear it, but if I get a year out of this then I’ll consider it money well spent and probably buy another one.
