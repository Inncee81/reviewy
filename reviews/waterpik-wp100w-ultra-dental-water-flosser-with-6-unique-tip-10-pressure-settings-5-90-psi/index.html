I made the right choice in going back to the WP100 model.

I bought this model because the newer, more sleek and even a bit quieter model continued to leak all over my bathroom counter due to the Inadequate (gravity-dependent) mating of the reservoir with the pump unit, resulting in the vibration of the WaterPik was sufficient to dislodge the reservoir just enough for water to leak out. This ‘older’ model of WaterPik, on the other hand, has a wonderfully simple and effectively designed ‘bowl’ into which the reservoir fits quite snugly; any water that might escape the reservoir after it is pushed into position (which has never happened, so far), would be contained within this bowl instead of diverted outside the unit.

A decent product that does what it is supposed to.

I have had two waterpiks and one other brand oral irrigator (water flosser) over the past fifteen years or so. That I’m only on my third unit should say something about the longevity of these products. At any rate, I use them every few days or so, and the current one (the model I’m reviewing) has lasted about six years and counting. (In fact, I just bought a second one for my kids’ bathroom.)The biggest issue with the unit is that the hose to the wand gets brittle and either splits or breaks off after (for me) two or three years. The replacement tube and wand (a single unit) are available here on Amazon and are easy-peasy to install (two screws on the bottom, the old one unplugs, and the new one plugs in, rescrew and you’re done in two minutes).That the cut-off button on the wand can get sticky never bothered me – I can get it to unstick by tapping either end of the button (no need to dial up the pressure).The appearance is nice, and it’s easy to keep clean – though the plastic /will/ yellow with age. The wand tips interchange easily, and are readily available. My favorite is the rubber-tipped periodontal wand.Overall, I’m satisfied with this product. It’s not made to last a lifetime, but for me, six years and still going is pretty good.
