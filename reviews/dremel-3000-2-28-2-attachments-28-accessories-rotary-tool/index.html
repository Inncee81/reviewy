Dremel 3000 makes it easy

As an amateur carpenter most of my adult life, I have begun doing a lot more work in wood toys for grandchildren since being semi-retired. I bought the Dremel 3000 in January and have since bought the Dremel Plunge Router Attachment and extra sanding drums. My band saw, drill press, scroll saw, etc. are all essential, but this tool has added a dimension sorely needed. I recently used it to cut a 3⁄8” wide, 1⁄4 inch deep dado in cherry. I do not like my full size router because it is so big and unruly. The Dremel 3000 is very easy to control. I use it regularly to sand (and grind) between the gross cut and the hand sanding. Formerly I used various files for the intermediate work. The Dremel makes it easy.

I’m a beginner, but it handles well.

I am new to the rotary tools, so I am still learning how to use this Dremel. After researching, I ordered the 3000 because it seems easier to handle than the larger models, and I work with smaller items. It does handle pretty well. Those buffers, with the included compound, work wonders on bullet shells! There is not much else I have done, so I’ll have to come back later for an update. I am a woman with average-sized hands.

Very Useful

A capable little tool with lots of accessories. So far I’ve only had the opportunity to use it for two projects:1) Cutting staples out of wooden boxes. The staples were stuck, so I was cutting off the exposed ends of them. It wasn’t as fast as an angle grinder, but got the job done quickly and with no hassle.2) Routing a 1⁄4” groove in some sort of soft-ish wood. I had to buy a separate bit for this, but did NOT buy an actual router guide - I just used the freehand tool that came in the package. It was easy to control but difficult to cut in a straight line, especially around knots. It was also less powerful than I would have liked, but that’s sort of what you get with a high-speed / low-torque tool like this.Conclusion: it did two jobs that would have typically required two different tools whose prices would have added up to or surpassed the cost of this one. The Dremel 3000 may not be the most powerful tool out there, but it more than makes up for it in versatility and price.
