Great scope for a reasonable price

I purchased this for Science class for a 7th grade home schooler. I was very pleased to see the quality of the microscope.It arrived with all advertised contents, as well as a few extras. It even has a plastic cover to protect the eye piece. The focus buttons are just the right size and easy to use. It magnifies to the advertised 1200x. It even has a light, which you turn off by flipping the light over so that the mirror is showing. The only thing I didn’t like was the “knife” which doesn’t even scratch paper, but that’s to be expected with so many different ages using the scope. I recommend it for basic science microscope use.

Shame on the Smithsonian

While I realize this is an inexpensive microscope, I still expected a product Smithsonian would put their name on would be a little better quality, While the frame could, understandably, be cheaply made, I would think at least the lens and lighting quality could have been better. I am really disappointed and would not recommend.

A window on the world

The microscope was bought as a gift for a seven year old boy. Its purpose was to support his curiosity about the world around him with an introduction to the world of microscopy. While is was novel it was a fascination, but then the novelty wore off. Then it became an investigative tool into the world of wee little things. He would take it out to answer his questions about the structure of butterfly wings and feathers. He got to take a close look at dust, and at the stuff the accumulates on windows, necessitating their cleaning. Of course, having parents who would support his quest to find his own answers and help him with the process also helped.
