Very pleased with the screen but paltry mounting hardware

This screen was fairly easy to mount by hanging from the ceiling but the way they explain on how to hang it leaves it at a bit of an awkward angle. I dropped a star for the paltry installation hardware it came with. You basically get the screen and you pretty much need to use your creativity from there if you aren’t mounting it to a couple studs. It does come with a small level that could be handy if you don’t have one already. I found it easiest to mount using large ceiling anchors hanging it by S-hooks on the back holes as they explain, correcting for the awkward way it hangs by screwing small 3⁄4-inch blocks against the rear mounting bracket so it could rest against the square crown moulding it was butt up against. Without doing that it would swing back and the screen wouldn’t come out directly from the slit but an an angle. It probably doesn’t matter but it didn’t seem right to stress the motor and screen unnecessarily. Anyway, besides a complete lack of mounting hardware except some wall anchors and screws, the screen itself is excellent. After hanging for several days it has straightened out a bit from when I first extended the screen. I know some people have complained about it curling at the corners but mine (albeit one of the largest so it’s probably less susceptible) is almost perfect, with just an ever-so-slight curl at the extreme bottom corners that has since just about worked itself straight.The motor is smooth albeit slow, but really, who cares as long as it doesn’t take ten minutes. The remote works well as does the wall mounted switch. I didn’t bother connecting the trigger to the projector.I had no issues with this screen and I’m really happy with it, with the exception of the terrible mounting instructions and lack of any mounting hardware. Given this screen is so huge it REALLY needs better mounting instructions. In a dark room, it presents a sharp bright image with a BenQ HT2050 projector with no added grain or blurriness, and given it’s not a tensioned screen, it’s surprisingly straight and seems to work itself out if you let it hang for a few days. The black borders do a good job of absorbing any stray light keeping the image nice and sharp at the edges with no weird bleed or reflection.
