Learning to Use It

This seems to be doing a good job, but I’m still learning how to get the most from it. I used to use a “hummingbird” that used floss and had hoped that this would be similar. This is “different” but seems to be able to do the job of cleaning between my teeth. The “learning” part is that if I touch my lip with any part of the devise (which is almost impossible to avoid) it tickles beyond my ability to cope. I’d rather not have to use both hands and perhaps a mirror, but it may be necessary.

The flosser itself is fine, but the tips that come with it are …

The flosser itself is fine, but the tips that come with it are incredibly thin & too flexible. Too hard to get it between your teeth. They even recommend using 2 or 3 tips PER floss because they are so flimsy. I read they have stiffer flossing tips, so I maybe that will help. I used a Hummingbird by Oral B for years and loved it. Unfortunately it was discontinued.

LOVE IT until you have to replace the battery…

very practical

I find it far less tedious than flossing with regular floss. Also, if you have a bridge or dental work this would certainly make it a lot easier to care for that area. I have no complaints, though I think I will still floss the regular way at least a few times a week because while the Power Flosser easily fits into the area at the gum line, it is too thick to slide all the way up and down between some of my teeth.
