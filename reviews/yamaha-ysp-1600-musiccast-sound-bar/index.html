The Music Cast sound bar enhances any TV watching experience and can also provide wireless multi room audio playback using your existing Wi-Fi network by adding other Music Cast speakers, sound bars or AV receivers in other rooms.

Product Reviews

Decent Sound but Not Up to Yahama Standards

Reviews in Cruthcfield and Amazon held promise, but after suffering through 6+ months of connectivity and sync issues I almost gave up. Latest issue was a loss of sync between speech and audio. Once again I had to go into the AV Sync set-up by changing to the HDMI 3 input in the middle of a program, found and selected the “lip-sync” setting and returned to the program on HDMI 1. The sound quality is “good,” but nowhere near what I expected from Yahama, especially at this price. There has never been real clarity in the treble range. And while this can be somewhat compensated by the “Clear Voice” setting, there is still a lack of clarity…we’ve resorted to using our LG flat screen’s speakers when watching TV programs. When we want to watch something with the sound cranked up the YSP1600 performs its best. But, earsplitting is not the mode desired most of the time.Connectivity has been at issue, as well. Control (I-Tunes) from my computer works well, but whenever we opt to use MusicCast iPhone app for control we suffer from drop-off and, yes, I’ve had the wireless checked and even switched the YSP to the same wireless network and still had issues. It seems that Yahama made this unit for tech-savvy customers with a lot of patience and time to make adjustments for every different mode of operation when simple would have been preferred. We use the YSP in link with a WX-010 speaker which always works well, syncs easily and does not suffer from all the quirks of the YSP1600. A friend has a SONOS soundbar that seems to work flawlessly in a similar network environment. But, I’ve invested far too much to go back now.
