It even included spare extension arms to fit multiple projector designs. It includes spaces and washers and even large mounting screws for the mounting plate. It is a BenQ HT3050.• The mounting unit is made of steel and it is ruggedly designed• It comes in white so it matched my ceiling and it matched my projector• It has four ways to adjust the mounting angles for the projector to make sure that it is aligned the way you need it to be.• I loved that it had a drilling templet in the box and I used it to design and build a mounting system that extended down from the basement ceiling joists to the level of the drop ceiling.• The mounting bracket is fairly priced, especially if you consider its quality and all of the supplied hardware that is supplied.Overall I am very pleased with the unit as it worked perfectly and the installation is solid. Seems to work on Epson 2040

My Epson 2040 only has three mounting holes, but I believe I was able to get a reasonable configuration using this bracket. I added photos if anyone has a 2040 and wanted to see my setup. I used two long and one short arms, and Q and R spacers under each due to the length of the screws (L). For the small arm in the back, it is completely under the bracket and the mounting screw is also through a hole on the bracket. I haven’t mounted it to the ceiling yet, but I believe this will work out fine.

Works well

Works very well for Epson 5030UB and Epson 5040UB. Includes lots of “adapters” to fit a variety of projectors. It took me a little while to figure out the right combination of adapters to use but once figured out, worked well. My only other issue is that I was expecting to be able to lock in tightly all settings (tilt, yaw, etc.) but I cannot. The projector does not move by itself but I can easily make rough adjustments even after I made everything as tight as possible. Maybe this is by design? It’s not a big deal, though.

Used with Epson Powerlite Home Cinema 1040

Of course, this mount already has a ton of great reviews which lead me to it. In the box you will find everything you need to put it together; everything comes neatly packaged in its own sealed bag, is listed on the instructions manual, and is included in a numbered diagram. It doesn’t get more straight forward than that. I am using this particular mount (QualGear PRB-717-WHT Universal Mount) with an Epson Powerlite Home Cinema 1040 which should have near identical mounting holes to the Epson Powerlite Home Cinema 2040. The metal used is stamped sheet metal averaging 16 ga. and 13 (maybe 12) ga. on the main pieces; it is sturdy and will not flex.Note: If I were to make an improvement, the (up to) four bolts used to secure the mount to the ceiling (stud) do not come with washers. The manual makes it clear that washers will not be used here, but I still prefer to use washer (they prevent the bolt head from scratching off the paint etc.). With that said, I will also note that none of the paint flaked or scratched off during the installation, kudos.Note 2: My particular package came with a “Free Gift” voucher inside. According to this, if I leave an Amazon review and follow the (included) link to input some information, they will send me a free HDMI cable. I will update this review if/when that arrives.
