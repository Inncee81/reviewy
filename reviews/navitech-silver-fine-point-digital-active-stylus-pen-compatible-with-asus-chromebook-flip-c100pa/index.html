This is a great pen for accurate writing and it’s well balanced too!

Bought a new tablet and wanted to shift to all digital notes for business. I bought this pen and I love it. I have big hands for a women and I find it’s like writing with a big crayon. The control/outcome of your writing is phenomenally accurate. It’s balanced really well and only takes 1 battery. It works really well. It is a little noisy when you bring the “nib” or tip to the screen - I’ve heard this is common, but it’s the only digital stylus I’ve tried! I will be ordering another soon.

This pen works partially and it is not the pens …

This pen works partially and it is not the pens fault. Most of the touch screens that are designed for finger touch do not have sufficient contact points to allow for such a small pen tip. Drawing lines just does not work but using it to click on small boxes and radio buttons make this very usable. Do not think that an inexpensive pen will turn your low-rez (regarding touch points)screen into a high-rez screen; it is not going to happen.

Reasoably good pen for Synaptics interfaces

This pen does work on the Synaptics interfaces used on the Asus T100CHI and some other tablets and touch-screen notebooks. Its main problem is that it does not have mouse buttons although some people think that the buttons get in the way. Most interfaces will treat a sudden change in pressure as a tap and generate a left click automatically but there is no way to get a right-click unless the software does some kluge such as a long-touch. It does not turn itself on automatically, you have to press the button, but it does turn off after a while if you are not using it. It is powered by an AAA battery which makes it somewhat fat and heavy but I rather like the heft. The battery is easy to find and lasts a lot longer than the AAAA type used in the skinnier pens.(Edit)This is actually a generic active touchscreen stylus like the M.Pen. It should work on most touchscreen devices but it probably does not use the pen interface on the T100CHI and its accuracy is limited to what the touch screen supports. The Dell 750-AAGN, used for the “Venue” tablets, is the best choice for the T100CHI and others using the Synaptics pen interface. This is available new and used on Amazon.
