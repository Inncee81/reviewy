Disappointed Expectations

After using this computer for a while I can officially say that I regret buying it. It is faster than the last computer I had (which was why I was looking for a new computer) but other than that I’m not super happy with it. I didn’t think that the lack of back lit keyboard would be a problem but I find it difficult to tell the difference between the computer being off and just asleep. The entire computer feels very cheap to me (I guess you get what you pay for). The keys don’t push down very smoothly and the space bar is especially frustrating since you have to hit it really far in the middle in order for it to work properly. The touch-pad also took some getting used to. It is very plastic feeling and sometimes squeaked when i first used it. Also there isn’t nearly as much space on this computer as I might have hoped and the webcam is awful. I appreciate that it has a cd drive which I actually haven’t used yet and that it is fairly light. The battery life, although at first super great, it’s now down to just a few hours less than a month later. The charging cable again is very thin and cheap and feels like it will break if you accidentally roll over it with a chair. I guess for the price it’s an ok computer but not what I was hoping it would be.

Lots of Features for the Price

Overall, I have to say that this is a pretty sweet laptop for the price. You pay a little extra for the SSD storage, but I believe it’s worth the premium. The system boots very quickly and I’ve found that it opens up some heavy apps like Photoshop without much effort. It’s only 256GB, so that is a small trade-off. But with cloud storage, etc. there are plenty of ways around that limitation.On the downside, I wouldn’t say this is the sharpest screen I’ve ever seen. But that’s to be expected for a budget laptop. The only problem I’ve run into so far is that a Windows 10 update left the touchpad non-functioning. On the bright side, ASUS tech support got back to me right away and helped me find a solution (basically resetting the system to factory specs - not a big deal since I had just opened up the box).The keyboard is nice and roomy. I have carpal tunnel syndrome and use a Microsoft Ergonomic keyboard on my desktop. I have to say, for a laptop keyboard, this ASUS model hasn’t bothered my wrists so far.In this price range, finding a system that has a good bit of RAM, an SSD and a 7th gen i5 processor is tough. This model has all 3 and I’m very happy with it so far.
