Product Specifications

  • Head Size: 97 sq. in. / 625.81 sq. cm.
  • Length: 27in / 68.58cm
  • Strung Weight: 11.7oz / 331.69g
  • Balance: 12.6in / 31cm / 10 pts HL

  • String Pattern: 16 Mains / 19 Crosses

    Product Description

A lighter more accessible version of the Pro Staff RF 97 Autograph gets a familiar cosmetic update but retains many of the same characteristics that made this a best seller at RacquetGuys. The new matt black paint is highlighted by glossy red at 9 and 3 o’clock on the head in similar fashion to the popular Pro Staff 97S. Lighter and faster than the RF 97 Autograph, the Pro Staff 97 is still a head light racquet to give you even more whip and access to spin. Strung this racquet weighs 11.7 ounces and can withstand punishing blows from heavy hitting opponents. At net the 7 points head light balance makes the racquet extremely maneuverable. Like Pro Staff’s in the past, this racquet features a thin beam and provides a lot of feel and control in the 16 x 19 string pattern.

I was so glad to hear that Wilson had chosen to create a …

Using a comparison tool online, my son and I researched many, many racquets similar in weight, balance, swing style, etc. I was so glad to hear that Wilson had chosen to create a slightly lighter version of the 97S. But I still had my reservations because he had recently been struggling alot with tennis elbow, so our “on-paper” research only meant so much to me.Then I was fortunate enough to read another reader’s comments, discussing how despite making the transition to this racquet was actually a move up in the overall racquet weight from the one he’d previously used, that the increase to this racquet’s “points headlight” actually resulted in a lower overall swingweight for him! (less effort to swing the same or even a greater weight) I made a quick comparison of the swingweight of the 2017’s 97 Pro Staff to the swingweight of my son’s then racquet’s swingweight and BINGO!!! The racquet had my undivided attention!I was still aware we wouldn’t truly know if it was right for him until he used it, so I decided to take the plunge. Well, he’s VERY happy with it, so-much-so I just purchased a second one so he will have a backup for when he’s competing in tournaments and when he goes away to tennis camp. (for those unfamiliar, strings do pop at the worst times!)Happy kid, happy daddy.
