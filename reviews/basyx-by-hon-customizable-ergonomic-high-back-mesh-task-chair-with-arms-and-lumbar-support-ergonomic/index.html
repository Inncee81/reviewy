Helps with my seating posture

The HON Basyx chair has the features and the trendy look of a high-end office/home office chair. I have been through several construction/building expansion projects in the last few years and had the privilege of working with many office furniture vendors, including HON. This chair offers the same great features you would expect from a modern furniture piece. There are several factors that typically affect the price of an office chair: warranty, number of features, and upholstery durability. Unfortunately, I could not find any information of this chair’s number of rubs. Here’s why I find it important.When choosing a chair, you would look for a fabric that will hold up to the intended use which is determined by how it rates on the double rub test. The test is a back and forth motion on the fabric that approximates the wear and tear that happens when furniture is sat upon. 3,000 double rubs equal one yearâ€™s worth of use. In the ideal situation, you would look at 30,000 or more double rubs. In a commercial setting 100,000; 250,000 and even 500,000 rubs are considered for places like call centers, nurse stations, etc. The chairs often come with a 12 year warranty.To my surprise, HON Basyx comes with a 5-year warranty, which should be ideal for a typical home office, 2-3 hours of computer use per day. We tend to retire a chair after 2 or three years of moderate use in our household due to only two reasons: the pneumatic mechanism dies or the cushioning becomes too flat for the chair to be used comfortably. Considering the offered 5 year warranty, and its reasonable cost, this chair is already a winner in my book.The HON Basyx was extremely easy to install and took me no more than 5 minutes. Right out of the box, I noticed the heavy duty features of the main seat adjustment mechanism. It offers height adjustment, tilt adjustment of both the seat and the back, back height adjustment, arm height adjustment, as well as 360 degree swivel.Here’s where I personally find HON Basyx a lot less comfortable that a chair I use at work. Neither the seat, nor the back recline as far back as I would typically prefer. However, the positive aspect of such limitation is that the chair controls my posture superbly. It keeps my back straight, there’s a decent lumbar support (without a true lumbar support piece found in some chairs) and prevents me from reclining all the way back. The mesh back keeps provides good support and allows for good air circulation. The seat is cushioned well too.Overall, considering its very reasonable price point and a 5 year warranty, this may be a good option for you. Be aware of its limited reclining angle.READ MORE
