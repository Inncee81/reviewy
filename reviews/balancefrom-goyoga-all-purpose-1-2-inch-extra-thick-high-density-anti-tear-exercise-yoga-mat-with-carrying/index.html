Tradeoff between comfort and balance

I bought this yoga mat when I was a newbie. The class that I was attending provided mats, but oh my goodness did they smell awful. Probably never cleaned, and part of the class had us lying face-down on these atrocities. So I bought my own. However, I quickly learned that you can buy a mat which is too thick. Now, this product is advertised as “Extra Thick,” so this is not a fault of the product. Rather, this is advice to newbies such as myself regarding buying a thick mat:Pro:+ The cushioning is really nice, especially if you’re in positions involving your knees on the floor, etc. Much less pain.+ The carrying strap, while simple, works really well.Cons:- The cushioning makes it surprisingly challenging to balance. I would say my one-legged balance is better than average, but I eventually decided to just step off the mat for those moves.- The mat is loud. Strange thing to say, right? Imagine dragging styrofoam across cling wrap. It’s sort of like that, except in my case it was a yoga mat on a hardwood floor. The material that makes this mat so thick and squishy also makes it stretchy. So if you do a warrior pose, for example, the force from your splayed legs will stretch the mat across the floor and create very un-yoga-like sounds.Conclusion:Even if you really want that cushioning for added comfort, compromise and get something thinner. You will find that you balance better, and you will develop tolerance to any added knee pain anyway.
