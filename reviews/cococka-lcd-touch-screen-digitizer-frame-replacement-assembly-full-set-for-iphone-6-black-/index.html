Not Easy, but Doable

I purchased this product to replace a failing iPhone 6 screen which was displaying fuzzy lines and almost completely stopped responding to all touch commands. My gf and I watched several guides on YouTube, namely one by JerryRigEverything and Repairs Universe and thought we were confident enough to go ahead with the repair. We had to watch both of them side by side over and over again as we were doing the repair since each guide quickly summarizes the crucial reassembly steps without going into too much detail.Important pointer using this kit: Make sure you remove the plastic brackets holding your front facing camera and ear piece connectors from your old screen since the kit does not come with it! Another crucial tool missing in this kit is a micro tweezer. Regular tweezers are terrible at picking up the screws and lining them up. The provided philips screw driver seems to strip the screws more then actually screw them in, so I recommend having a replacement for that as well. Lastly make sure you order some spare screws just to replace the ones you stripped or lost.The repair took us closer to 5 hours since we had some issues removing a few stripped screws, and just being confident on making sure we didn’t miss any steps on reassembly. If I were to do it again, I might be able to cut that time down in half or just buy the $40 kit that has everything. I recommend doing this with help on the first try, and set aside at least half a day for it. If money is not an issue, save yourself the time and grief and go to a repair shop with warranty. At the end of the day we got we wanted, which was a working phone looking almost brand new for a little over $20.
