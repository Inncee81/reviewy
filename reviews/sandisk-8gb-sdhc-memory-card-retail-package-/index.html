Small Affordable High Capacity Storage

When I purchased the NIX 10.4” Digital Photo Frame I knew I would need to purchase storage for the unit because it lacked internal memory. I know, but the unit had everything I wanted. And beside the price-point on all forms of portable storage has dropped to next to nothing so letting go $6.99 for a new “SanDisk 8GB Class 4 SD High Capacity (SDHC) Card” was doable.The ProductThe “SanDisk 8GB Class 4 SD High Capacity (SDHC) Card” features a high transfer rate up to a 2.5MB/s burst transfer rate. The unit is backed by a 5-year limited warranty and is fully compatible with all SD-compliant devices.First Impressions:I love this card. What’s not to love; it is easy to use, high capacity; it’s completely plug-n-play. The SanDisk 8GB Class 4 SD High Capacity (SDHC) Card is easy to use, and as I pointed out above, the drive is designed for the Windows Vista, Windows XP and Mac X Operating Systems (OS). All the Operating Systems have the ability to automatically recognize new hardware via plug-n-play technologies. There is no software to install.Once inserted into an available SD media card slot, the SanDisk 8GB Class 4 SD High Capacity (SDHC) Card is automatically set up as a removable drive, and as such is accessible just as any hard drive or a CD/ROM drive. They can even be formatted as boot devices. File transfers are quick and painless via the SD Media card slot, and I have experienced no file corruption to date, nor do I expect any.Be advised, that if you have a preponderance of mapped drives already on your computer, as I do, Windows XP/7 may not assign your removable media the next open drive letter; this happen to me. If you insert the SanDisk 8GB Class 4 SD High Capacity (SDHC) Card and get the message that new hardware has been found and is now available for use, but the device is not assigned a drive letter, you must go to Control Panel /Administrative Tools/ Computer Manager/ Disk Manager, to manually assign the drive letter.But as I stated above I bought this unit to store picture for use with the NIX 10.4” Digital Photo Frame. After I copied said photos to the card I removed it from my Windows 7 computer I inserted it into the NIX and it was immediately recognized it. Nothing could be easier.ConclusionI couldn’t ask for a better product then the SanDisk 8GB Class 4 SD High Capacity (SDHC) Card. If you need a small, versatile, hi-speed, large capacity replacement for those pesky floppy drives, or smaller capacity flash drives look no further than the SanDisk 8GB Class 4 SD High Capacity (SDHC) Card.

Works Great and best for Point and Shoot Photography !

First up a word of warning : certain cameras cannot read memory above a certain capacity so make sure that your camera is capable of handling an 8GB SDHC card.Now..I have had this card for more than two years now and it is in great working condition. It is ideal for a point and shoot camera from any manufacturer. Plugging it in and out of my camera and inserting it into the slot on my laptop hasn’t damaged its integrity one bit(pun intended). I have been able to take thousands of pictures and just like good music needs to be listened on high quality surround equipment for maximum enthrallment, my camera and this card have come together to give us some great memories.Coming to the cons it does have issues recording HD videos for a sustained period. I would recommend moving to a higher category card or just buy a camcorder if you are more of a video guy. The packaging wasn’t what I was expecting turning up in a poor but pithy bubble wrap. However, since this was for personal use, it did not matter.In the end, I am satisfied with my purchase. It loses half a point for recording less than stated speed and half for the lack of a case. However for the price and more than adequate performance, it can’t be beat. I will commend Sandisk for manufacturing high quality SD cards over the years and would advocate this card for every amateur photographer out there.
