Product Reviews

Efficient and time-saving burner

What a time saver! I’ve been using the burner from a turkey fryer for decades, though I’ve used other home brewer’s set-ups as well. This is a huge jump in price, but it’s quiet and efficient. The design has a whole bunch of individual jets so you don’t get hot spots that burn your malt and this thing came to temperature like nothing I’ve experienced. I’ve used it for two batches now and I’d say it cuts about 45 minutes our of my brew time.

Works great but…

Only problem I had was with the hose unit. A small needle valve and very small brass connector is on the stove end. The connector broke. I did a google and discovered that many others have had the same problem. I bought a replacement (with a much better connector) at a box store. This is why I dropped a star. I also went ahead and ordered the extension legs which makes a very big difference. The unit works fine.

The perfect addition to begin upgrading your brewing equipment

This thing really kicks butt. For several years, I’ve been using Bayou Classic burners for my brewing, which produce just over 55,000 BTUs. The problem started when I went from 5 gallon batches to 10+ gallon batches with my much larger MegaPot 1.2 Stainless Steel Brew Kettle Pot - 15 Gallon with Ball Valve and Thermometer - 60 Quart. It took nearly an hour to get the kettle to boil, although the Bayou Classic is fine for mashing and heating the sparge tank. The Bru Burner, with its 72,000 BTUs, and much larger burner, makes short work of a tank filled with 10 gallons of wort. It feels very sturdy and I’ve experience no problems whatsoever with more than 80+ pounds sitting on it (I should note that I also bought the extension legs for this). If I could change one thing, it would be to add some sort of pads to the bottoms of the extension legs so that it would be a bit easier to slide around the brewing cave when necessary. Other than that minor issue, I can’t think of a single bad thing to say about this product. It’s rock-solid, totally functional, and looks like it should cost far more than it really did.
