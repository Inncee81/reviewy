Product Reviews

Awesome little MP3 player

So my iPod Classic 160GB finally bit the dust and I’m looking for a player to hold a least 90 GB and thinking that there would be a plethora of them available and cheep! Wrong. Well I decided to get this APGtEk for $21.99. Can’t go wrong right? I have a scan disk 64 GB card I thought I’d try it out, going against the manufacturers warnings about compatibility issues. Well it worked fine. I’ve transferred just over 4000 songs onto the card and I’m super happy about that. I actually got rid of some stuff I didn’t need or want anyway. The volume is a little lower than I would like. Car and gym playback is great but when mowing grass I’d like a little extra bump in volume. The navigation is a little weird but I’ve only had it for a day. I’ll get used to it. I will never figure out why there is an eBook folder. Silly with such a small screen. But I do love the size of this. Much lighter than the iPod. I have the armband made for this and I barely know its there. Sound quality is good. I really can’t tell any difference between the iPod and this one. All the album art didn’t transfer but I finally figured out to use Media Monkey. Still working on that but having fun with it.

May give it one more try, needs to be further improvement

I purchased the 2015 version back in July of 2015 and it lasted for 8 or so months, but the battery slowly deteriorated over this time. I kinda neglected the on and off switch most of the time, but the company doesn’t seem to mention much that simply powering it off with the center button won’t stop battery drain.I ordered my new 2016 model in late April, and unfortunately it was never correctly delivered to me by the USPS. This is no fault of Amazon or the seller. USPS has a reputation for being lackluster in package delivery.This leads me to my now THIRD AGPteK player. The previous one was fully refunded by Amazon and they were awesome enough to give me free one day shipping on my new A02.The player lasted two weeks roughly before the screen went white. I could hear my music but couldn’t navigate the menus. Further, the MP3 player wouldn’t power on unless plugged into USB. 2 or so weeks and the thing bricks on me. Come on AGPtek!Here’s my list of needed improvements for this to be a solid player, which it falls short of, sadly:1. Better build quality: This is a paper light MP3 device. As such, it seems as the technology is crammed into a small and flimsy place and because of this, it is prone to far more wear. I think this is part of the reason mine white screened after two weeks. MP3’s should be something you can slide in a pocket and know it will be safe. That is not the case exactly with this player. Also, the rubber coating can wear off quickly on these players2. Organized files by ID3 tag: As others mention, this player organizes everything alphabetically. This means that under “From Ashes To New” you will get “Shadows” as the first track under the “Day One” album. The correct first track is “Land of Make Believe”. The only way to slightly adjust for this is to name the actual track “01 Land of Make Believe”, If you’re like me and have a collection, you will have several songs under “All artists” competing for the number one spot on your personal billboard. Really annoying! AGPteK says to go into folder mode, but this is not convenient because I like to shuffle all my songs while I’m driving. You cannot shuffle all songs in folder mode. This mistake has been pointed out and there’s no reason the company cannot fix this.3. True “Expandable” Memory: I never have had this problem, because I’ve never filled the player up fully, but apparently there is a 4,000 song limit. IDK if this is total between MicroSD and player or overall, but if you’re gonna sell a device that is capable of up to 32 or more GB of music, at least make it function correctly!This is probably your best bet for an MP3 for extended battery life and storage capacity, but it falls short of greatness because of it’s vulnerable hardware and few software issues. If AGPtek takes reviews like this into serious consideration, they can bring the dying MP3 market back to life with a device that costs a fraction of an Ipod with plenty of features. I am not a video-on-the-go type of person, so those things dont matter to me, but don’t look here for videos, photos, etc.If one more MP3 player fails on me, I will upgrade to a smartphone and leave the MP3 market behind. I really don’t want to, as I like having separate devices, but my luck with Amazon MP3 players is running out. Budget MP3 players, might I add.Take my review with a grain of salt and remember. You get what you pay for!
