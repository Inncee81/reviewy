Heavy Duty Materials (Glass and Metal), But Poorly Designed and Machined

Heavy duty materials (glass and metal). However, the four holes drilled through the vertical support bar/post (two on top and two on bottom) are too large for the bolts. This creates too much play in the attachment of the two L-shaped brackets at the top and bottom of the vertical support bar/post. Thus, leveling TV the on the stand once it is entirely assembled is difficult to impossible without disassembling the entire frame, including adjusting the two vertical brackets (in the picture, the brackets with holes) attached directly to the back of the TV. Also, the vertical cylindrical bars that hold the two horizontal brackets together (if not machined properly, as mine weren’t) cause the entire frame (attached to the vertical support bar/post) to torque, throwing the frame out of square, causing additional leveling problems. If that wasn’t enough, once I managed to get TV stand to nearly level (based upon the bottom edge of the TV), I noticed that the entire TV was tilted slightly forward top to bottom). In other words, the TV leans about 10 degrees from the top. Was this by design? I dunno. Fortunately, the glass base seems heavy enough, and the vertical support bar/post far enough back on the base, to offset the obvious lean. Finally, the small thumb-screws used to secure the TV to the stand’s horizontal bottom bracket seem wholly inadequate to hold the TV onto the heavy stand. While it should be easy to remove the TV from the stand in the future because these set screws are so flimsy, the weight of the TV plus the stand creates the very real problem of the stand sliding off the TV bracket when moving the TV and stand as a unit, even if moving entails cleaning the TV and/or the stand in the future. My TV is an LG 37LK450 with the following specs, W 35.9” x D 10.4” x H 25.2” and W 28.66 lbs., but this TV should not be straining this stand if it the stand was engineered better, or if the stand’s parts were machined properly. Perhaps I will figure out a way to tweak this stand in a way that will make it more level – right to left and top to bottom – and easier to service, move and clean. Until then, I will warn my family and guests of the inconveniences and dangers this stand poses. I do not recommend this TV stand for 37” TVs.
