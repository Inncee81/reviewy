Run out on the set I received was horrid and by so much that it’s noticeable when in the spindle and turning by hand. Drilled a few holes in some FP4 just to test how bad the run out was and holes measured roughly 75% oversize as a result and immediately went back to my name brand bits. If you’re frequently breaking bits, poor run out may be the culprit.Additional notes:These bits are intended for extremely high rpm’s which is also very dependent on material being drilled. For example, layered circuit board type material would be in the range of 60-70k rpm for the .3mm down to 30-35k for the 1.2mm. Too low of rpm will result in poor cutting, excess heating of the bit and premature breakage.If you can’t spin these fast enough, an alternative would be high speed steel bits (shorter life but not nearly as brittle).
