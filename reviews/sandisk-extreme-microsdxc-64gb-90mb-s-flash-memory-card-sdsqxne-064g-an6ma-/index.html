Product Reviews

The only SD card that Amazon sells that GoPro ‘officially’ supports.

As the only MicroSD card that Amazon sells that is on GoPro’s officially supported SD card list, it’s obvious why most people buy this for use with their GoPro. Other then that, it’s a standard MicroSD card. It does it’s job, doesn’t complain, and is reliable as I would expect a SanDisk product to be. What makes me mad is GoPro told me that the first card I bought (an Ultra class card) was not fast enough for my GoPro Hero 4 Session. I was having issues where the camera would shut off after 5-8 mins of recording. They said the write speed was too slow. Well, I used a USB 3.0 card reader on my USB 3.0 port and achieved the same read/write speeds on both cards (give or take 5MB/s), so I think GoPro really just tries to up sell you to get the one direct from them which is also a SanDisk Extreme, but more expensive. The customer service advisor even went so far as to say that the Ultra card would cause the camera to not respond, and brick itself. While I was out filming I thought I was going to run out of space so I went to Best Buy and picked up another Ultra class card, which worked just fine. GoPro support neglected to mention that even if the camera was plugged in while recording, if the battery was below a certain threshold, it will stop recording after a couple minutes. As long as my GoPro is fully charged, as long as I have it plugged into the USB cable I can record until the card is full.So, if you want to play it safe, this is the card to get for use with your GoPro.
