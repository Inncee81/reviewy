WARNING: This is not an Ultra-HD Blu-ray Player

Since I have so many friends misunderstanding what this Blu-ray player is I thought I’d post a review here. This is not an Ultra-HD Blu-ray player, and there are no Ultra-HD Blu-ray players on the market yet (also referred to as 4K or UHD). Nor are there any Ultra-HD Blu-ray discs available yet. Don’t get me wrong, this is an awesome Blu-ray player, and if you don’t have one it’s an excellent buy. But the Ultra-HD player standard was just finalized and players/discs won’t be available until sometime in 2016. And when they are released they are expected to be in the $400 to $500 price range. The discs will also be significantly more expensive, though of course it will depend upon the studio and movie.What this player does do however is an awesome job of taking conventional DVD/Blu-ray discs and streaming media and converting them, called upscaling, to a higher resolution so they look better on UHD/4K TVs. And this players upscaling is indeed top of the line. Of course all UHD TVs have built in upscaling, but depending on the model and brand this player may do a better job. So if you get your brand new TV and conventional media looks grainy or pixelated this player will probably fix the problem. However if the picture already looks good I’d save my money for an Ultra-HD player, which should be available mid-2016.
