143.4 MB/s reads and 33.7 MB/s writes

If you’re looking for a compact USB-C drive to store small files on then this little Samsung drive is the one to get. The drive consistently averages 143.4 MB/s reads and 33.7 MB/s writes, which isn’t bad at all for it being a slash drive. However, If you’re looking for something to move and read larger files like ISO images or virtual machine backups, then I suggest you look at Samsung’s T3/T5 SSD line of drives. I’ve decided to use this drive for stuff like application backup and configuration files and my Samsung T3 for everything else. Hope this helps!

Small and fast at a price not much more than Type-A USB flash drives

When it comes to flash memory products, Samsung is king. I’ve repeatedly been impressed with their products for their performance and value in the past several years. I’ve recently begun buying more of their USB flash drives as well and this USB Type-C drive is my very first USB Type-C flash drive. Starting with performance, I got sequential read/write rates of 146MB/s read and 101MB/s write. I’ve uploaded the CrystalDiskMark screenshot as reference.Physically, the drive is tiny. The plastic body is a little bit slick, but not too bad. The cap slides on tight, but is easy to remove. The plug is USB Type-C, not the more common Type-A. One of the great benefits of Type-C is that you can insert it in either direction. No more trying to plug it in, then having to flip it around to the other side.The Samsung USB Type-C flash drive is a great drive and doesn’t cost a lot more than the Type-A drives.
