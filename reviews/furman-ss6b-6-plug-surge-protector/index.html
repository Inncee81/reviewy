It’s alright, but not as good as it should be.

I bought this power strip to gig with, and since I had already owned rack mounted Furman power products that worked well this one seemed like the logical choice. Well the first one arrived with the lighted rocker switch smashed in so it was rattling around inside the case. No big deal, I got in touch with Amazon and they shipped a replacement with no headache. The replacement arrived and seemed fine. Not more than a few gigs in I noticed that one of the power outlets on the unit was cracked. Not a show stopper, as the outlet still works and there are 5 others to choose from even if it didn’t, but the moral of the story here is that anything on this unit not made of steel seems overly fragile. This is not what I would expect of a Furman product. My gear doesn’t get drop kicked or punished, but for the price I would at least like something that is a little more rugged. As someone else pointed out, this isn’t a power conditioner or anything like that, it’s just a power strip so with things cracking and breaking I don’t see how this is any better than something I can get from another manufacturer for half the price. Side note: The advertised “EMI/RFI Noise attenuation” doesn’t seem to be doing much of anything either.PROS: Long cord & steel body.CONS: Anything on this unit not made of steel (rocker switch, receptacles, etc.) seems a whole lot more fragile than it ought to be.BOTTOM LINE: It’s still working and I’m still using it (thus 3 stars), but if it got stolen I wouldn’t bother buying another one.
