Good Entry Level 4k TV

I purchased this through another retailer simply since it was in stock and easier to handle then hoping to wait for a delivery.This is my first 4k TV that I picked up solely because I had recently also picked up an Xbox One S, so I figured, why not see what 4k was all about.On its own, I will say its interface takes getting used too. There is no manual to quickly orient you with the functions, so for some this may be a sticking point, but overall it is not that hard to find everything once you start navigating the menus. It quickly and easily hooked up to my wifi and both my Xbox and PS4. The Xbox did all the hard work for me in recognizing the TV as 4k and adjusting as such. You will need to tweak picture settings, like all Tvs to your liking. Other than that it works great, even the apps on it load fast and are even more responsive than on my Xbox which I use heavily for streaming as well as gaming. While gaming I do use the Game Mode on the TV, which in my opinion makes the picture darker than it needs to be (this can be tweaked), but I didn’t find this to be a bad thing while playing Overwatch to test it. If anything it makes the lighting seem more realistic.Do note, TURN OFF ENERGY SAVING. This is on automatically even in Game Mode and it darkens the screen terribly and washes everything out. Also, with at least my Xbox as a 4k player, you will need to turn off the TruMotion that is automatically on, unless you like the “Soap Opera Effect”. Which sometimes I like, but in movies with heavy CGI, it looks quite terrible.Overall a good entry level 4k TV with decent sound (speakers are underneath the TV) and good picture. It could use a better remote and stand, but I wont knock any points off for that, since that too is also personal preference.
