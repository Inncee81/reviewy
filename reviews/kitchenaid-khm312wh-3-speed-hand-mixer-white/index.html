THE BEST HAND MIXER I’VE EVER OWNED - OR EVEN SEEN

I’ve been through many brands of hand mixers over the years. Some are terrible. Some are OK.Some brands:1. are a pain to clean2. don’t mix worth a darn3. splatter4. jam5. stink6. burn out7. make enough noise to wake the dead.I won’t name names, but if you’re shopping for a hand mixer, you may know at least one of those I’m talking about.For the above reasons, I was without a hand mixer for quite a while after my last one died. Working with my wire whisk and wooden spoons, I wasn’t actually shopping, just cruising in case maybe I saw one that looked like a better idea. I figured I couldn’t afford a Kitchen Aid, but I did look at them, wistfully, from time to time.Then I saw this one. I couldn’t believe it sold for under $20. It’s lightweight and easy to hold. It’s balanced that so long mixing doesn’t strain my arm muscles or make my fingers go numb. The beaters are uniquely shaped, easy to clean, and work extremely well, even on law speed.Remember the list of bad mixer attributes above? Well, this mixer doesn’t have ANY of those traits.To wit:1. It’s easy to clean because the beaters are shaped to avoid splatters. The cabinet is enclosed so no food gets up past the beaters, which are a breeze to clean with no finger pain and cramping.2. The mixing action of the uniquely shaped betters makes mixing easy and fast.3. There is no splatter to speak of, again because of the shape of the beaters, wider on the bottom, tending to pull the food down from the sides of the bowl.4. I haven’t had any trouble with jamming, even in cookie dough. Obviously, the gears and other internal parts are good quality, especially the motor.5. This unit has no strong plastic odors. The lubrication odor was noticeable on Day One, but dissipated.6. I can’t imagine a Kitchen Aid burning out.7. Noise? What noise. This unit has a moderate buzz-hum that isn’t annoying.One other thing, it has an adjustable cord holder that can be used left or right handed.On top of all that, it’s pretty.What else can I say? If you want an all purpose, good quality hand mixer that should last a long time, this is the one.

Perfect - don’t pay a penny more!

KitchenAid has, I think, about four different variants of this mixer, each at different price points, and with different features (speed, accessories, etc.).I guess the not-so-secret secret is that they are all the same model - the same motor, and a similar mould - but with some hardware caps put in place. As a consequence, this cheapest model will perform just as great as the more expensive one.So if you are looking for a reliable, small, convenient mixer, search no more. If you need to whisk eggs, the speed of this mixer is completely appropriate (unlike what is implied to make you want to buy the more expensive models), and you simply need to graph the KitchenAid KHMPW Whisking Accessory.Bought this a year ago, and it is still working fine, and never have I thought that I needed to trade up.
