Good replacement brushes

These are good replacement brushes for the e Series Philips Sonicare. They come in factory original packaging and the price - for six brushes - is good, better than I can get at local stores. We have four in the family using these brushes, so getting them by six is not a problem. Overall we are big fans of the Sonicare and have been using them for years after recommendation by our dentist. Weak point of the system are the rechargeable handles. We have had several replaced over the years (some under warranty after only a few months of use). I recently found a seller on Amazon (Genuine Sonicare Parts) who sells the handles as replacement parts, a good and cheaper solution if you do not need a new charger and/or brush.

Nothing Phony about These Brushes

Well, my brushes arrived this week, mid September 2016. Guess what? Like the set I ordered a few years back, they are REAL Philips Sonicare brushes in REAL factory sealed packaging. And, just for the fun of it, I screwed one into the head. No extra noise, no extra vibration. There is something REALLY weird to me about all those earlier comments that made claims about these heads not being the real thing, not to mention being defective. Wear out way too quickly ~ 1 month

Each brush is advertised to last at least 3 month. In my experience they last about a month before becoming soft and loosing color ( an indication of replacement time). I bought a 6-pack in October and have used of all 6 brushes in less then 9 months! The replacement heads I used to buy at Target or other retailer ( usually a 2-pack or a 3-pack) used to maintain its firmness and color for at least 3 months.

Perfect fit and dishwasher safe

These are the standard replacement heads for the older Sonicare toothbrushes and the e-series (the ones they sell at Costco and most major grocery stores). They are dishwasher safe (I wash ours once a month) and last about 3-4 months with daily use. They come in a pack of six: two light gray, two light blue/aqua, and two dark gray so you can color code the family’s toothbrushes. They have blue bristles. When the blue disappears, it’s time to replace your toothbrush head. Sonicare is meant to work through sonic vibration. You press the brush edges against your tooth and gumline and it cleans by holding it there. It beeps after two minutes which is how long you’re supposed to brush your teeth. Sonicare also makes a line of toothpastes including one with extra fluoride that is only available through your dentist (they cost about $12). None of the other “sonic” toothbrushes work nearly as well as Sonicare and they haven’t raised their prices in several years. This makes it much easier for kids to brush their teeth and be effective when getting the space between their teeth and behind their front teeth which is where most plaque starts.
