Good keyboard but bulky overall

Cooper Cases™ Touchpad Executive Lenovo A10-70 A7600 / Tab 2 A10-70 Tablet Bluetooth Keyboard Folio in Purple (Removable QWERTY Keyboard; Built-in Stand; Rechargeable Battery)The keyboard has all the keys including function keys and they are well spaced with a good feel- rugged enough considering the form factor. Once paired with blue tooth, I was able to switch the KB on and off and it works without intervention although it may take a few seconds after turning on to be recognized. The mouse action can sometimes be sluggish and the touch screen still works to supplement the touch pad.The case with the tablet feels heavier and bulkier(thicker) than I would like. I ordered the blue color and it is a good shade of blue, rather neutral which is exactly what I was looking for. The tablet fits well with the elastic clips and the finish is good.I wish that the keyboard was also attached with clips (or push buttons on bottom) so it wouldn’t move relative to the case. The advantage of magnets is you can carry a couple of pages of documents between the keyboard and the case ! The stand is not as adjustable as it may seem because it only supports one height/angle. When the case is closed, the tablet and keyboard meet haphazardly such that some of the keys gets pressed; this is because the case folds like a book but the binding in not rigid. Moreover the keyboard itself shifts.My suggestion would be to use an acrylic hinge to make the case follow a more predictable folding path. Also make the hinges like a notebook’s hinges so tablet converts to a notebook- then you can place it in your lap which the current design does not allow.It took about 2 weeks from order to delivery (came from Hong Kong, standard shipping).
