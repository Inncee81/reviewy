Finicky compatibility

I got this for reading camera SD cards. It works great on my Samsung Galaxy Tab S2 (Android 5.1.1), but didn’t work at all on my Moto Droid Turbo (Android 5.1) and Moto G 3rd gen (Android 6.0). I know it’s possible to make a gadget like this that does work on both; I have an earlier model of the LDesign 4-in-1 USB OTG reader and it works fine on those devices.Other than the compatibility issues, it’s will made, small, and the short cable means you can plug it in even if your device is wearing a case.P.S. Compatibility glitches seem to be common with these kinds of gadgets. I also purchased a similar MXtechnic OTG card reader and it has exactly the opposite problem - it works on the Moto devices but it doesn’t work on the Galaxy Tab S2.

This works for tab pro 10.1

I completely forgot that this device is only using micro usb 2.0. I thought I could use this for my Note 3.All I can say is that this works well for my tab pro 10.1. I just quickly used it to check if it reads. I plugged in the sdhc card and I was able to read it. The same goes with the USB flash drive. I already connected them before I connected the device into the tablet’s charging port. I noticed that the connector is a bit snug. A little pressure needs to be exerted to put it into the charging port.I haven’t tried plugging in the USB and the sdhc at the same time. My guess is it won’t work. I’m not sure about it.To disconnect, make sure you unmount this device as if it’s a memory card inserted directly into the device to prevent any damage to the card/USB itself.

Great for Dell Venu 8 Pro, Samsung Galaxy Note 3

Works perfectly with my Dell Venue Pro 8. I use it to look at 15-20 SD cards of pictures from game cameras, several thousand pictures every week or two. I though the cable would be floppy, but it’s not. It has enough “body” that the reader sits above the edge of the tablet like a little antenna. It’s firm enough that I can get the cards into and out of the reader without it wobbling all over the place, but flexible enough that I’m not worried about it damaging the USB port on the tablet if I do move it around a bit.
