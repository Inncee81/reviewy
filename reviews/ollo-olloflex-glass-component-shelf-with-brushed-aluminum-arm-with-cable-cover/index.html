Great Value, Nice Looking, Functional AV Shelf

Very elegant, fairly priced, well designed shelf for your AV components. I needed something to get my LCD projector on the wall. Moved to a room with cathedral ceilings to no way to use its ceiling mount, and found that wall mounts specifically designed for projectors can cost a great deal. Stumbled across this by accident and glad I did. It’s the perfect size and capacity to hold a projector, cable box, game console, VCR, DVD player, etc. It comes with a two piece “sleeve” for hiding the cables but you only need one to hang it. In my case the second piece was discarded. Materially, this looks nice so it’s not an eyesore and with a little creative use of wire-molding you could completely hide any and all cabling. Very nice product, perfect for my needs and budget.

Beautiful AV shelf

This is a very well designed product that does it’s job perfectly. A solid glass shelf looks great for holding the set-top box underneath a wall-mounted TV. The wall mount is easy to install on a wooden wall stud - I used a 5mm drill bit and the hex bolts are very solid.The only tricky part is the flush screws that are used to mount the shelf onto it’s support arm - they are very shallow and take a lot of fiddling to screw in. The cable routing and cover are plastic but easy to use and look good on the wall. Will definitely buy more of these.

Average Electronic Shelf

The shelf itself is easy to put up. Except when installing it you have a hard time tightening the bottom bolt because of the arm that sticks out. I had to use plires near the end to finish tightening the bottom bolt down. With the shelf weighted down I noticed it does sag a little bit which is noticeable.

Only a nice looking shelf AFTER making modifications. Not good engineering or design.

This unit defiantly needs some reengineering. The mounting is extremely poorly designed and the lower part of the cover, which is a small snap on piece will not stay in place. After completely recutting and redrilling the back plastic part of the cover, totally recutting the large piece of the top cover, and trashing the two other pieces of the top cover, we were able to make a good looking mount for the shelf. The shelf still does lean slightly forward when loaded, another design flaw, which could be a problem depending what the shelf is used for. Again, some extremely poor engineering. If you are able to rethink the proper mounting to suit your particular requirements and have the tools to accomplish it, can accept the slight forward tilt, then you might have a good shelf. If you do not desire to take the time and effort to remake the mounting or accept the tilt I would consider other units. It is my belief that the designer of this shelf should find a new occupation.

Perfect little shelf

Wire-hider on this shelf is only a few inches and does the job nicely. Good buy.
