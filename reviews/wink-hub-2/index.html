Downgrading my review from 5 to 2 until this issue is addressed. I will update when it is.ORIGINAL REVIEW:Picked up at the local home depot last week. I have had this version plugged in for almost 2 weeks now and it has not lost connection once!If you have the old hub, this hub will detect and do mostly a good job at transferring your devices and settings over to the new hub. In my case it worked almost for everything. The only thing that did not transfer over was my schlage smart door lock, so I had to re-add it manually.The Wink app to control this hub is still the same app you use to control the old hub. I don’t think there is much to improve on this app. The interface is very intuitive and offers several ways to automate tasks ( Robots, Shortcuts and Schedules). Below is a quick summary of each:- Robots: Performs one or more actions when a specified trigger happens (e.g. Turn on the lights in the living room when the front door is opened between 7pm and 11pm)- Shortcuts: A way to group actions together for convenience ( e.g. Create a shortcut named “Movie time” which turns off or dims some lights )- Schedules: A way to setup schedules for things to be on or off (e.g1: Schedule Thermostat upstairs to turn heat on and set the temperature to 72 Mon-Friday at 7pm, e.g2: Setup outside light to turn on Sunset and turn off at Sunrise). Sunset and Sunrise are actually options when choosing times!I noticed that the response time when communicating with my devices seems significantly faster with this hub. Turning lights on and off works almost instantly for me ( I have 15 smart lights ), which is a huge improvement compared to the old hub.I would love to see the new Wink hub integrate with the new Google Home. Much, much better than the original! Seamless

The original Wink hub is great but it seems only tinkerers and DIY’ers like myself feel that way. It’s understandable because the original Wink takes patience, patience and some more patience. It’s outstanding if you’re willing to reconnect it, re-pair lights and be willing to forgive some times it disconnects. All that said, the price is outstanding and it’s worth it if you like going that route. I didn’t have a lot of issues but some growing pains that were easy to handle. I’ve not experienced (knock on wood) these same issues with the Wink Hub 2 and it’s been intelligent in adding items (it automatically added a Wink Relay I just got whereas I’d have had to add it manually on my old hub). There have not been connection issues and it is a TON faster than the original. More on that later…Migrating my hub to the new one took 10 minutes and it was so simple and intuitive. Hooking it up via Ethernet cable is a MUST to me at least (something the first iteration could not do). This version is ready for the masses (if only all the bulbs and such by other companies were as well).Pros:Hub migration from the first is easy and fast. I didn’t have to re-pair anythingSleek designHooks to router via Ethernet (hardline) to avoid spotty connectionsAutomatically pairs to new itemsSo simpleA good deal for the priceCons:Not much so far…Philips Hue lights are faster but they still have to go to the cloud I am pretty sure and take longer than Z-Wave items…by orders of magnitudeIsn’t hooked up to Google Home (yet?)Give us Bluetooth!! I would love for this to pair to Bluetooth light switches already in my possession!This hub is great because unlike the original for me, robots work flawlessly and so does IFTTT.If you want to have a great smart home experience without the growing pains, go with this version of the hub and I don’t think it will disappoint you. Plus the customer service is very helpful and local to the USA. I had an issue with the Wink Relay I just got and them coupled with Amazon did an Amazing job getting me taken care of!Highly recommended!!!READ MORE
