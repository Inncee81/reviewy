    Product Description

The BLACK+DECKER BDCS20C 4v Max Lithium Cordless Rechargeable Screwdriver features a compact handle and a powerful 180 RPM motor for easy driving and removing of screws in tight spaces. Its inline trigger grip provides comfort and control when installing mini blinds, hanging curtain rods, assembling furniture and a variety of other household tasks. 180 RPMs and 35 in-lbs of torque provide plenty of power for a variety of applications. This unit is also attachment capable, allowing for compatibility with all of Black & Decker’s future attachments. Includes: (1) BDCS20C 4-volt max Li-ion cordless rechargeable screwdriver, (1) charger, and (2) screwdriving bits.

Product Reviews

It’s just ok, sort of.

I rated this three stars, so far. What kept me from rating it any higher is due to the following: The forward/lock/reverse switch is in an awkward position for switching, I use this for unscrewing/screwing parts for my lathe chuck. Also, my deceased unit had the switch located so you didn’t have to shift your hand to change direction. I like being able to see where the bit is going, there is no light, handy to have when working in close spaces. The biggest fail is that there is no charging light, it is nice to see that the unit is charging, and when it is at a full charge, that little light also indicates that you have it plugged into a working outlet but the adapter, for some reason, has worked it’s way lose. I will give it credit for the torque, it was able to drive deck screws into fence posts, that really surprised me.Would I recommend it? Not really, spend a few more bucks and get one that at least has a charging light, and perhaps a better located forward/reverse switch.

Very nice auto screwdriver

Very nice auto screwdriver. I’m originally using this as a poor man’s drill and it does the job splendidly. Although the RPM isn’t very high, it’s fast enough and strong enough to get the job done. This can handle anything but the tightest of screws, in which case just use a manual screwdriver. It has 3 speeds: clockwise, counterclockwise and neutral (or off) for safety. I haven’t had it for very long, so I can’t say anything about the battery life yet, but just treat it like regular rechargeable batteries and dont overcharge

False start but happy with replacement.

After a replacement for a faulty unit we used this heavily in renovating our fifth wheel. It performs very well. It has quite a bit of torque and the battery seems to last quite a while. It’s ergonomic and easy to hold. As others stated though, the switch to change it from forward to reverse is pretty stiff and it lacks a light. A magnetic bit holder would also be nice. All in all we are happy with the unit. It does require the 19 hour initial charge.
