Just fine… Except it is terrible

First the pros:Sufficient size to hold a variety of equipmentA variety of partitions allow for many configurationsLooks pretty decentSeems fine right? It would be except for one fatal flaw, the zipper does not stay shut!The zipper is designed such that when fully closed, the bottom edge can very easily become disconnected and then ENTIRE zipper will unravel. I very nearly lost over a $1000 worth of camera equipment because I trusted the zipper. And the backpack is designed such that the compartment will open fully, so if the zipper does fail, it is very likely that all your equipment will come flying out. What is the point of a backpack if you can’t trust the zipper? This backpack is literary worth nothing if you can’t trust it to stay shut.If you haven’t bought it yet: Don’t buy itIf you have bought it: Return itI have added a video to show what the issue is. It only takes a relatively little amount of force to pop the zipper, and could easily happen during a walk if you have some weight in the bag (as is what happened to me).

Best camera backpack ever!

In general it’s a wonderful bag, because the straps are wide and comfortable, the waist strap the same, the chest strap is adjustable, The inside is configurable, and it fits perfectly my Nikon D 800, two lenses, spare batteries and charger, a remote release, etc. There are plenty of outside pockets and straps to accommodate things like a tripod, cell phone, a small blanket or an umbrella etc. Really well thought out, good quality and wonderful. Good inexpensive bag for basic use

I picked up this bag along with my T6i kit and accessories, for something to carry everything in the interim before making a decision on a higher-quality solution.It isn’t QUITE as small as it’s made out to be in some reviews, but if you’re expecting something that’s going to be a full-size backpack, you’ll be disappointed. However, I can carry my body with a 50mm prime on it, as well as the standard kit lens and a 75-250mm telephoto, along with filters, flash, shotgun mic, and more. The tripod straps work well for low-end and “thin” tripods but a heavy duty tripod will likely be too wide for the straps, and might be heavy enough to throw the balance off. The chest straps are pretty meager, but they do sort of work depending on how big you are. I haven’t had any issues with the zippers or construction yet. And a nice touch, there are protective leather flaps that cover the zippers on the inside pouches to protect your gear from accidental scratches from that. I’m not sure that I would count on it for a long term piece. But for a basic pack for the everyday user, it should suffice.

A great go-to bag for daily use.

I also have my cleaning supplies, various other supplies and filters, and the tripod securely strapped to the side.I really think of this as a 5 star bag, but set it at 4 for 2 reasons: First, although I’m new to DSLR photography, I think the picture showing the lenses upright is a little misleading.
