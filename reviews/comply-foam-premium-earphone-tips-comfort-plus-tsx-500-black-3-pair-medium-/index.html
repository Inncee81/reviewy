Overpriced and fragile but the only tips that work for my Jay Birds

Overpriced and they wear out after a few weeks of use. But they are the only earbuds I can use with my Jay Bird’s Bluebud X headset and hear my podcasts while cycling, with wind roar completely blocked.Installation of these tips onto the headset posts is a very tight fit but it can be achieved. Roll the tip into an oval and “screw” it onto the Bluebud’s post, making sure to get it all the way to the base of the post.These “Comfort PLUS” units cost even more than the “regular” ones but are easier to live with.Mediums work for me. They’re tight to get into your ear. I pinch and moisten each tip just a little using my lips, then “screw” it into my ear.Because they’re basically foam rubber, these tips self-destruct in normal use. Inserting them and wearing them breaks down the foam over time – and not much time. A set will last me a few weeks, maybe 20 wearings. That’s more than $1 every time I use them.I never thought I’d willingly pay for tips this expensive, but the ones provided with my headset do not seal out wind noise and pop out withing minutes of insertion, no matter what size I use. So I’m stuck with these Complys.
