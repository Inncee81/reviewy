Sloooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooow

Well it did store about 120G formatted. But this thing was slower than molasses on a February morning. It’s supposed to be C10 but I was getting an average of about 4-6Mps while transferring my 70G of MP3s to this thing. This more like C6 or C4 performance. Other microSDs I have transfer in the 30-40Mps range easily. So where other microSDs transferred this collection in less than a hour, this one took over 3 hours. Playback off the card seems to be OK for MP3s, so I won’t send it back. But I am guessing if you try playing back video on any decent resolution you are going to have problems. I’d rate this 2 1⁄2 stars for MEH, if I could. But it did hold my entire collection and I have lots of room to expand, so I’ll be generous and round up here.

Overall a great SD card

I bought this microSD card for use in my GalaxyS7 phone. The storage is about as much as anyone can ask for. I’ve got around 100 albums worth of songs saved to it, a plethora of pictures, and a good number of apps. I do notice some slow load times when I’m trying to play music. Sometimes it’s as long as 30 seconds. Some of that time is probably due to the app I’m using (AmazonMusic) or my phone’s reading speed, but I think most of it is because my music is saved on the SD card. Overall, I can’t be upset with the price I paid for an extra 128 GB of reliable storage on my phone.
