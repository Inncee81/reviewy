Product Reviews

tough day on the mountain

we have used Lucky Bum Ski Wedge in the past - with quite a lot of success. However, this time, the conditions were wet, and raininy - and not really a ski weekened… but we went anyway. we had a really hard time tightening this product enough to overcome the set slippery conditions. They kept sliding off the ski tips. in those conditions, I was really wishing that it had a larger handle for tightening the screw. I don’t know that it’s a defect in the product, or just that we were using it in undesirable conditions… either way, at the end of the day we had given up trying to put these on.

My friend got the velcro kind and they were much better.

Looking at the pictures, it’s hard to tell but the black plastic ‘C’ part is not big enough to put the screw through the holes in the front of the skis. Therefore, installing this product on the skis requires you to really tighten the screws on the sides of the skis and damage them. They actually came off a few times for my son. My friend got the velcro kind and they were much better.

Does not work well with lucky bum skis.

I purchased the lucky bums easy wedge as well as the lucky bum skis and harness, I was disappointed that the easy wedge did not fit easily onto the lucky bum ski, it would pop off easily and broke when I ran over it with my ski. The lucky bum ski comes with holes in the tips but the easy wedge was not able to attach to it.

Great for little ones

I used this with my 4 year old son. It was great. The ski instructors at whitefish were using these on the little kids as well. He still was able to “walk” around with his skis to get around on flat areas by the magic carpet. My 8 year old was strong enough she didn’t need these and they would have gotten in the way. I strongly suggest the co-pilot ski trainer as well.

Best Wedge Helper

This was the first wedge tip holder I bought and it’s definitely the best of the three that I’ve tried. I bought the Edgie Wedgie for a friend, and a velco one came with a harness I bought.I liked the Lucky Bums the best because it seemed to be the sturdiest; I liked that it was made of elasticized cord rather than just tubing. While it might not be the case, the Edgie’s tubing seemed like it would come off whereas the Lucky Bums’ band seems firmly attached to the clamps. The clamps on the Lucky Bums Easy Wedge are also slightly wider and fit a wider variety of skis. We actually had a pair of skis that the Edgie Wedgie’s clamp was too small for. Overall, I’m very pleased with the Easy Wedge. It does exactly what it’s supposed to do and rarely comes off the skis; when it does come off, it’s very easy to reattach. I feel bad that I purchased another brand for my friend as I thought they were pretty much the same but they’re definitely not!
