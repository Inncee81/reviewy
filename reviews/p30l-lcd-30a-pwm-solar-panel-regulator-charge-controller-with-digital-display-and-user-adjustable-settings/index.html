Product Specifications

  • WindyNation P30L PWM 30 Amp solar charge controller prevents battery from being overcharged and damaged by the solar panel. Ideal for 12 and 24 volt batteries. Controller can be configured for AGM, lead acid, gel and other battery types.
  • Advanced electronic protection: overload, short circuit, and reverse polarity protected
  • LCD digital display provides system information (amperage, voltage, temperature, load control etc.)
  • Battery temperature sensor (BTS) is included. See photo gallery.

  • System settings are user adjustable by using the MODE and UP and DOWN buttons

    Product Description

Size:30A Charge Controller WindyNation’s P30L solar charge controller is a premium grade PWM solar controller. The digital display provides all necessary solar system information and also serves as a user interface for adjusting the charge controller’s settings. It is the perfect solar charge controller for an RV, boat, small cabin or any other off-grid installation, where user configurability and system information is important.

Poor output, poor warranty, poor product

I have used many charge controllers from different manufacturers, and after one of the more expensive ones failed (after 6 years) I decided to give a cheaper one a try. Cheaper is the word for this. although it ‘worked’ the output from this controller is far less then a decent unit and far less then I expected. I kept it in service on a relatively unimportant system (powering a well pump), and after about 16 months, it failed… period…just stopped working. The warranty is a mere 1 year which is very low for the industry and likely due to the expectation of failure.I recommend that you invest a bit more money and get a better made controller with a stronger warranty. In the end it will save you money. Buying 3 of these in the same period as 1 of the others actually turns out to be more expensive

This is part of my first solar power system and so far so good..

Works well and I would purchase it again

I chose this particular low cost controller unit based on the high number of positive reviews it has received on amazon. I installed it about two weeks ago, and it seems to be functioning well. The manual is clear and well written. I am using one 12 volt 100 watt panel to charge two 6 volt golf cart batteries wired in series, which are hooked up to a larger older 2000 watt Heart Inverter in my remote shop. I have not tried all the functions yet, as I’m satisfied with the factory settings so far. It does seem simple to operate and program. The display is much more informative than just simple lights like some other controllers have. I have not used the 12v load/timer section, but could see where that is a nice option which I may use in the future. The terminal block seems somewhat flimsy for attaching 8 or 10gauge wire to it, and it takes a little care to get the screw terminals tight without breaking the terminal strip. I would also recommend securing all incoming wires to some sort of restraint to keep the tension off the terminal block To be fair, you must expect some weak points in a controller that costs only around 30 dollars I’m very happy with this unit!
