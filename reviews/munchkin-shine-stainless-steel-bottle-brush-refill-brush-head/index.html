Loved it so much, I bought three

Since having my son two years go, we’ve been through many bottle brushes. There was the Dr. Brown’s Deluxe Bottle Brush, Blue, which had bristles that were too firm, and the Dr. Brown’s Bottle Brush, Blue, which had bristles that were too soft and died fairly quickly. My husband was a fan of the OXO Tot Bottle Brush with Nipple Cleaner and Stand, Pink so much so that we had one at each of our 3 sinks but I hated the stand, which would constantly fall over into the sink and have to be rescued and the bristles got very dingy, very quickly.Cut to receiving the Munchkin Shine Stainless Steel Bottle Brush & Refill Brush Head for review. So far, I LOVE it. I really like the suction stand. I also prefer the stainless steel sleek look, which matches the metallic finish of all my sinks. Because of this the bottle brushes blend into the background in a way the others we had previous tried did not. I like the weight on the bottle brush as well. It feels heavier than the ones I’ve used before but that just makes it feel more substantial not heavy. The bristles are much more like the Dr. Brown’s Deluxe and I foresee them lasting longer than the Oxo. We haven’t used the nipple brush as yet as we’re mainly using the bottle brush on sippy cups and regular dishes.But my all-time favorite thing about this bottle brush is that it comes with a second replacement head and that I can buy more replacement packs for pretty cheaply, Munchkin 2 Piece Shine Stainless Steel Bottle Brush Refills!
