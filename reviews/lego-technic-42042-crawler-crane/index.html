LEGO Technic 42042 Crawler Crane

by people ...

Product Specifications

  • Features Power Functions motorized boom, winch, superstructure and caterpillar tracks
  • Also features an opening claw, tilting cab, extendable boom, extensive cabling and a realistic exhaust pipe and ventilation grid
  • 2-in-1 model: rebuilds into a Mobile Tower Crane
  • Crawler Crane measures over 24” (61cm) high, 23” (60cm) long and 7” (20cm) wide

  • Mobile Tower Crane measures over 23” (60cm) high, 22” (58cm) long and 7” (20cm) wide

    Product Description

Make way for the powerful LEGO Technic Crawler Crane! This huge, robust 2-in-1 model comes with an array of realistic details and exciting features. Activate the included Power Functions motor and use the joysticks to operate the boom, raise and lower the claw, rotate the superstructure and drive the huge caterpillar tracks that maneuver this mighty machine! Includes an authentic double winch and pulley system with extensive cabling, working claw, detailed cab with tilt function, exhaust pipe and a ventilation grill. You can also extend the boom for even greater height! A cool blue and white color scheme adds the finishing touch to this awesome model. Rebuilds into a huge Mobile Tower Crane!.

Rediculoooooooous!!!

Just picked this up at a competitor’s store for $149 + tax. I been looking at it for a couple weeks and finally sprung for it. Being an adult I feel, for lack of a better word, stupid, for buying it but hey, you only live once and this thing looks awesome. I have always loved heavy equipment stuff, and I am actually a heavy equipment mechanic.So, I went through the instruction book and prebuilt it in my mind before opening the bags. It looks very complex and attention to detail seems paramount. But, the instructions appear very comprehensive and upon starting the build it should be obvious where things fit.So, time to pop some bags open and start building this beast. I’ll rehash this review as it gets built.OK, so I spent about 5 hours putting this together, and, wow is about all I can say. The scale and dimensions are amazing. Having worked on dozers, and excavators, and the tracks are about as spot on as you can get. The grousers, drive, idler and rollers look so real.The gear train to make everything operate is a work of art. It has what I would call synchronizers like in a syncronized manual transmission that are operated by the levers on the deck. Even the battery box is used like a counterweight, and even attaches like a real crane counterweight does.To make a fair review, the only thing I can see that needed fixing is tying the strings to the winch drums and to the end points. I figure there could be something a little better to accomplish that, and, really that’s not that big of a deal but that’s the only thing I thought was inadequate.So, in conclusion, Lego did an amazing job engineering this thing, and I am now deciding between the AROCS truck and the loader..
