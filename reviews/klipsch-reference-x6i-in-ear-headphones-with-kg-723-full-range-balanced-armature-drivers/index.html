    Product Description

Color:Black Taking the next step from our legendary, award-winning R6i in-ear headphones, the Klipsch Reference X6i in-ear headphones are packed with a single high-clarity, full range balanced armature, offering flat frequency response with an incredible fit. The Klipsch Reference X6i sheds size in the most important area—overall thickness. The thin design follows the oval contour of your ear and sits naturally inside the ear canal. Klipsch’s custom, single full-range KG-723 balanced armature delivers cleaner, more natural sounding vocals and defined upper frequencies only achievable through balanced armature technology. With the introduction of X6i comes the most comfortable ear tip we have ever designed. Pre-installed on the product, we have redesigned our most popular ear tip into a super slim form factor. After extensive research, our surveys lead to the conclusion that ear canals are even narrower than we had ever expected. The super slim ear tip addresses that by using an even narrower and more tapered design, enabling people who have never found in-ear monitors to be comfortable to wear them without fatigue. However, all ears are unique, so included with the product are five additional sizes to choose from.

Very good quality build and sound

I bought these because my R6i’s stopped working, and I have to say that the X6i is definitely a step up. The clarity on these are very good even compared to the already good R6i. My only complaint (and this is not a major complaint at all) is that Klipsch has still not totally solved the issue of cord noise, despite changing the cord to a completely new design. There is a bit of space where the buds meet the cord, and when they bump against each other it makes a bit of a bump sound. I found it odd that it was designed this way; but not that big of a deal for me so far. The second gripe that I have is that it is a bit soft on the bass response, which is weaker than the R6i; but again not really a problem for me for most of they type of music that I listen to, regularly. Overall, the clarity and balance of the sound more than makes up, so I’m definitely very satisfied with these.

Durable, Clear Sounding Earbuds

Very, very happy with these earbuds. From 2011 until 2016, I purchased 9 pairs of either Klipsch, S4’s, S4i’s and S4ii’s. I loved the sound but though I was very careful with them and kept them in a case, they would only last a few months, which was quite frustrating at their price point. Great earbud for the dollar

Listening to my new X6i as I type this review. Compared to my set of R6 and the older set of Image S4 these are intended to replace, they are definitely flatter in frequency response. Clarity is better too. They really pull out nuances in well mixed/mastered recordings that are more buried with the R6/S4. Other major differences I can tell in the first 15 or so minutes of listening is they don’t have the pronounced low end of the others I mention. They still reproduce low notes no Bose I’ve tried can touch. As a live IEM, I’d still take my Shure 315s any day, but these are listening earbuds, and pair well with an iPhone. Maybe a stretch to call them “reference” earbuds, but the sound coloration is definitely less pronounced than other Klipsch I have owned, but still have all the qualities I love about Klipsch earbuds. I’m a fan.
