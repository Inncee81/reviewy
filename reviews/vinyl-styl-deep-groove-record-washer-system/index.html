Whoa - this works far better than I imagined!

I buy a lot of older, used records that have issues with static and skipping from debris. To clean them I’ve been going to a local shop that uses an ultrasonic cleaning machine. The machine removed most of the static and cleaned up some but not all of debris-related skips. But at $2.50 a pop and a drive across town I thought I’d give a home remedy a try. After pondering between the Vinyl Styl and the Spin Clean, I decided to go with Vinyl Styl because of the way everything stores together. I cleaned 20 LPs from the 70’s & 80’s and when I dropped the needle on the first record I was stunned at how silent it is - no static whatsoever, it sounds brand new! The kit cleverly stores everything together in one storage case which is really convenient and well thought out. The spindle keeps fluid off the label, however I did dry off the gasket between records. That’s another thing - keep a microfiber towel handy to manually wipe down each record before you remove the spindle as there is a lot of fluid residue left on the record. I used a microfiber cloth that came with another record cleaning solution. I also used a back-and-forth spinning motion to clean the records making sure to clean them both clockwise and counter-clockwise. The brushes inside the unit have a tendency to creep up out of their slots but I just pushed them back down - more of an inconvenience than anything else, they don’t damage the record. And you can see the cleaning fluid doing it’s job, it changes color and debris collects in the tray. Recycling the fluid is difficult, it’s hard to transfer from the washer back into the bottle so plan to buy extra fluid to supplement what you lose (it’s now available at Amazon).READ MORE
