Logitech Pop Home Switch Starter Pack for One-Touch Control of Smart Home Devices In Any Room

by people ...

Product Specifications

  • Simple smart home control for the whole family: control multiple smart home devices wirelessly with one press of a switch
  • Controls smart lighting: turns smart lights on/off to preset brightness, warmth and Color levels. (Works with philips hue, lifx, insteon, lutron lighting)
  • Controls SONOS music systems: quickly access preset favorite stations, playlists and volume when your mobile device is out of reach.
  • Versatile customizable control: assign three “recipes” per switch for different combinations of smart lighting, music and more

  • Starter pack contains 1 bridge and 2 switches

    Product Description

Color:White  |  Style:Starter Pack Logitech pop home switch gives everyone in the home easy access to basic control of multiple smart home devices. Set the scene with lighting presets, SONOS playlists, and more. Use three different commands to control smart home devices-individually or in groups-or turn harmony activities on or off.

Excellent addition for your smart home

This product is an exceptional addition to the smart home.I was so excited to get the POP based on what I read it could do. We have a full array of Sonos devices in our house as well as August locks and Lutron Caseta lights. We also use the excellent Logitech Hubs for our remote controls.I purchased a starter kit and 2 additional pops. Here is how I am using them.Kitchen - POP button 1:1. Play/Pause Sonos with Apple Music2. Play/Pause Sonos with Spotify Top Hits3. Play/Pause Sonos with NPRKitchen - POP button 2:1. Turn off Sonos, and Turn on TV2. Unused3. Turn off TVBedroom - POP button:1. Play/Pause Sonos with NPR2. Play/Pause Sonos with Spotify Top Hits3. Turn off Sonos and Bedroom LightsSetupSuper easy. Plug in hub, set up app, and add POP devices.In the app you discover all your local devices (Sonos) and then cloud authorize Harmony, Lutron and August.Each POP comes with that cool double sticky stuff that is removable and re-usable.ProgrammingYou just drags and drop, chose options for each device (such as toggle or on/off) and you can also add multiple devices per commandEach button has 3 commands (single click, double click, long press)ShortcomingsSonos - updated on 10⁄21 - fixedYou cannot group speakers together. No sonos interface can do this except Sonos for some reason. Also, if you make changes to your Sonos playlists (which is how you select items to play) it doesn’t seem to refresh without re-discovering.You can’t easily control volume.You can’t skip songs.Harmony - updated on 10⁄21 - fixedIf you have two Harmony hubs in your house (for 2 TVs) you have to pick one to use. You cannot seemingly pair two different buttons to different Hubs. It’s one hub per account.Update: the latest POP update supports Sonos Grouping and multiple harmony hubs. Great job Logitech!

Great design, great integration with Harmony Hubs

I debated between these vs the Philips Hue Dimmer Switches, but settled on these for the increase compatibility with other smart devices (namely the Harmony hub). The only downside I see is the lack of a dimmer built in, but after living with Hue lights for a few years now- I’ve found I rarely am wanting to manually dim any lights.Setup: We have Hue lights in each nightstand lamp and throughout the house. I have a Pop switch on each nightstand and one at the door.Each nightstand switch is configured as such:1 tap: toggle the nightstand light on and offdouble tap: toggle the TV on and off (via a harmony hub)long press: toggles the entire house to a dim red color for an entire-house nighlightBeing able to do all of this from a button rather than messing around with a phone at night is a huge bonus- especially with a newborn baby that needs to be fed often (being able to long press for the red nightlights is awesome). The double tap to turn the TV on/off is a bonus for my wife who often comes to bed later than me and doesn’t have to then search for the remote to turn the TV off if I had fallen asleep.It’s been rock solid so far- no networking issues at all. Also very impressed with the look and feel of the buttons- feels very high quality and has a great minimalist look. I was so pleased with these that I just ordered a third switch to place near the entry door to easily turn on and off all the lights as we leave/enter.Great product.

Coverage issues. Works well with smartthings hub.

I use it with smartthings and zwave switches and it works well.Main problem is coverage area of the bridge. If your button is close to bridge it registers every click immediately. If button is farther away (e.g. lower level) you need multiple clicks till one registers. The experience is inconsistent so that is disappointing.According to Logitech web site specs the bridge range is 50ft, and you can add more bridges for larger coverage but as of now there us no way to buy these.Edit 2/28/2017I have been using this for new months now. Logitech updated the app and added toggle options, however the bridge became unstable and need power cycle every few days. What I did to workaround it is to connect the bridge to power through a 25$ wifi plugin outlet adapter that I programmed to power cycle every night. With this and using buttons only near the bridge I have stable setup, ymmv and I still view this as 3 stars considering the limitations and workarounds.On the positive side the button works reliably for months in my steam shower, which is very nice.
