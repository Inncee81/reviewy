Great smaller bit set for general usage…these are well-made, affordable, and hold up very well considering the price.

For general-purpose usage, these are excellent bits. I initially purchased this set a few years ago when it had a different packaging and case for my own old Dremel. Having had good experiences with them, I purchased another set of these for my girlfriend to go along with the Dremel 4000 I got for her.These bits are made from high-speed steel, and a good choice for general use. High-speed steel is a highly wear-resistant steel with good strength and reasonably good heat resistance. With good performance for a reasonable price, it’s a great material for higher-speed applications such as usage with a rotary tool. For general usage with materials such as wood, plastics, composites, aluminum, and mild/thin steel, these are great. They have a fast start and they do a good job resisting slipping. They hold up well but aren’t terribly expensive if you happen to break them (or would prefer to replace them instead of sharpening.) They work well with the Dremel, but also work well with other drills with a chuck design that allows smaller bits. I agree with others that when using these with a standard drill, prolonging the life of these bits means using the fastest RPM setting and to let the speed do the work.My only complaint is that these are more on the rust-prone side than bits of other materials. However, that’s expected of most HS steel and an occasional coating of any good rust preventive (I use Corrosion X) and this is a non-issue. I just wanted to note it as a heads-up given it is very easy to prevent rust, but much more of a pain to remove once the rust has already occurred.Beyond occasional usage on mild steel that is thin, I would not advise using these to drill steel because this will accelerate bit wear and may generate so much heat that the bits themselves become damaged. For this usage, I recommend using drill bits made of cobalt steel, as they are designed specifically for drilling hard metals.So overall, this is a great little set. It’s versatile, it’s affordable, and it’s well-made.

Great Dremel Bits from Tiniest to Largest

Absolutely the highest quality drill bits specifically for the Dremel Moto-Tool. They are durable, sharp as all get-out as they should be, and work in almost all materials; I’ve never tried them out on anything such as concrete or anything that hard, and don’t plan to do so. These are great for woods of all types, acrylic plexiglass and other materials of that hardness. I also like the fact that they come in their own little case so that I don’t have to worry about losing them. The sizes are fantastic from the tiniest one that is great for miniature sized screws, to the largest one which is great for a small regular screw. It’s also great for use if you use a center punch to start and mark where your hole is going to be drawn, to start the hole itself so that the screw (or nail) doesn’t get set off center. Just be certain that you hold your Dremel straight up and down, and you’ll get a terrific alignment.
