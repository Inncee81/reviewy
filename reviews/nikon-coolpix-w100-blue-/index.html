    Product Description

Color:Blue Capture family trips to the beach or out on the slopes with the tough blue COOLPIX W100 Digital Camera from Nikon which features a rugged body rated to be waterproof to 33’, dustproof, freezproof to 14°F, and shockproof to 6’. The camera is designed to be used by all members of the family, including younger kids, and features special cartoon effects and a welcome screen that adjusts based on usage in order to provide an easy-to-use and fun operating experience. This model also features SnapBridge with Wi-Fi, NFC, and Bluetooth support that combine to enable a variety of functions, including instant image transfer, geolocation tagging, and more.For imaging, the W100 sports a 13.1MP 1⁄3.1” CMOS sensor with a sensitivity of up to ISO 1600. This combines with the NIKKOR 3x optical zoom lens, equivalent to 30-90mm, to produce sharp images in a range of situations and environments. The camera features a contrast-detect AF system and Electronic VR to help create crisp imagery. Also, it offers Full HD 1080p video recording at 30 fps and users will be able to compose shots using a 2.7” 230k-dot rear LCD. Additionally, for working in bright light, the W100 is equipped with a 2-stop ND filter. Not Waterproof in my experience

This camera is NOT waterproof. Well, maybe if you drop it in a puddle. I took it snorkeling and within the first 20 minutes, it clouded up, got condensation, and was essentially useless. So very disappointed. I tested it out on land first, and the pictures were good as a regular camera, and I liked the wifi feature where the photos automatically sent to your phone. But I bought it for snorkeling. I stay on the surface and don’t venture down from the surface ever, so extremely disappointed that I basically now have a ruined camera after only a couple of uses.

Water damage not covered under warranty!

We purchased this Nikon camera, used it one time at a local water park and there was condensation behind the viewing screen. The camera went under the water 5 times for a picture and then back out, so I wouldn’t say this had excessive exposure to the water. We turned it into Nikon to see if we could get it repaired. We received the item back with notification that it could not be repaired as Nikon does not cover water damage under its warranty for this camera. This is a water camera and they wouldn’t cover water damage??? We were able to return it to the store where we had purchased the camera from but I will not be purchasing another water camera from Nikon. The photos that we did get, were just okay. Not typical for a Nikon product in my experience. I have purchased 3 other Nikon cameras and have loved every one of them except this one.

Died first time under water.

Very disappointed in this camera. I purchased this for its underwater properties, as we were headed for a trip to the Great Barrier Reef, and had a limited budget for a camera. I tried it out on land to get used to how the camera works and was quite satisfied for what it is. However, even though I followed all underwater instructions to a T, it died after 30 minutes in the water (near the surface while snorkeling) on it’s maiden voyage. It was working fine, then suddenly stopped working and wouldn’t turn on again. Eventually there was some condensation noted on the inside of the lens, and after following all instructions, I opened it up to dry. The only noticeable water inside was two drops on the battery, and condensation on the lens and viewer. I have not been able to resurrect it. Thankfully, there does not appear to be any damage to my SD card, so at least that has lived to capture another day (in another camera).
