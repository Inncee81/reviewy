Works perfect for my D600 + 24-70mm. Only con is poor design on rain cover.

It took so long to find a travel/hiking case that I actually like.This is a perfect fit for my D600 w/ 24-70 with a long plate for video. I also keep (3) 77mm filters in the front pouch.There isn’t a lot of extra room for batteries, and it definitely wouldn’t fit a grip on my D600, so keep that in mind- you might need a larger model if you want the case to hold more gear.Rain cover is also easy to access and stuffed in a small pocket at the bottom of the bag. And that’s the only weakness of the bag: if you use a chest harness, the rain cover pulls out from the side of the bag that’s facing your chest, and is extremely awkward to fish it out. I’m going to snip it and purchase a dry bag instead: consumer and prosumer cameras aren’t meant for operating in downpours, so might as well put it in my pack in a dry bag for full protection.Altogether awesome bag though. For me it was about having easy access to my camera in the backcountry. I was taking it on trips and hardly using it because it was a pain to take my pack off, but with the Toploader and a chest strap, it’s always easy get to, and has 4 lash points, so it’s a snug fit and doesn’t wobble around.
