Product Reviews

Working great for me so far, exactly what I was looking for

These brackets served the exact purpose of what I needed. Unlike other reviewers, I had no problem using all the provided hardware. The drywall anchors worked fine for me. Ive attached two pictures to show my install

Easy to install and very sturdy

I have a 120” projector that these L-brackets support and i feel very safe that it isnt going anywhere. These are very sturdy and very easy to adjust how far you want your projector to hang. I have a tv on my wall above the fireplace so i had to clear the 9” that the tv sticks out and these worked perfectly.

Skip the 6” brackets, get the 12” brackets for true quality

** Review for the 6” brackets: The metal brackets I received, came as pictured, all hardware was included. The biggest problem is they were definitely not square. It had a slight offset from the end where the hooks to the wall. I kept thinking something was unleveled but it was this offset that threw me off. The wall anchors left a lot to want, I ended up using nicer 120lb wall anchors. All in all, I decided to go ahead and order the 12” ones to give me a little more clearance for my TV I have behind the screen. I would purchase again, just mind the offset and hardware.** Review for the 12” brackets: I am much more pleased with the 12” brackets. They were way more sturdy and didnt have any offset. I wish the quality of these were in the 6” ones. I would buy these over the other even if you only needed the 6” extension. It will last longer and serve you better.Upping the rating to 4 star due to the quality of the 12” brackets

Works as intended

Does the job, included mounting gear allows for adjusting after install, I paired it with an elite screen and it fit well I can see these working with other screen brands too. Would buy again.I guess I should note that I was mounting on brick and I didn’t use the included anchor or screws.

Good gear

These worked out well. The mounts have holes for three screws which let me relax amidst visions of my hundred foot screen ripping the wall out. I bought carabiners as well as various bolts with washers and hooks from Hardware store to attach it to the screen. Just so I had options. I think it might have come with screws. I used regular screws on the studs rather then drywall screws in the perfect aesthetic place. Again didn’t want to worry about my 28lb screen pulling the drywall open like a monster. However, this is pure paranoia as the drywall screws individually could probably sustain the weight. I go over the top with these projects, hence a hundred foot screen in an apartment.

Missing the mounting hardware

I ordered these wall mounts and really it is just thin metal for around $25 at the time of writing this. When they arrived they were missing the mounting hardware so I had to return them.I went to the hardware store and bought shelf brackets that cost $5 each, plus 4 lag bolts at $0.30 each and was actually able to get a stronger mount than what these offered, plus the shelf brackets come in larger sizes. I really would stay away from these. I would have used them if the hardware was included in my box, but it wasn’t so I found an alternative and I am much happier with what I am using.

I bought this for a new projector screen I had …

I bought this for a new projector screen I had ordered. These brackets were huge. Way bigger than I had expected and therefore they wouldn’t fit in the area I needed it for. My tip would be to look at measurements and make sure it would fit correctly. It obviously wouldn’t work for our needs so I can’t comment on the quality of the product.
