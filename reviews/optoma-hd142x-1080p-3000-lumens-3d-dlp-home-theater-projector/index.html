It has the best quality picture and feature set for the price range …

I purchased the Optoma HD142X after a ton of research and time spent looking at projectors online. It has the best quality picture and feature set for the price range I was looking for. It really does have an amazing picture. I am currently using it with a 120” motorized screen. One of the reasons I went with this model was because of the higher brightness rating. I have it in my living room which has blinds over the windows, but theres still a decent amount of ambient light in the room in the middle of the afternoon. This projector is bright enough that it looks just as good as my LCD during the day.Another nice feature of this projector is the 12v trigger port. Alot of projectors dont include this often overlooked feature. It allows you to automate your electric screen. By wiring the 12v trigger to the control box of my screen, I now have my screen automatically lowered when I turn on the projector. When I turn it off, the screen automatically goes back up.Overall, Im very satisfied with the overall quality of this projector. It was very easy to tune and set up. The vast array of options and features make this one of the best, if not, the best budget home theatre projector with a full 1080p native resolution.UPDATE: So Ive had this projector for a few months now and can say Im still just as happy as the day I got it. I use it daily for watching TV and occasional video games. Everyone who comes over cant believe how great it looks! Ive found that I really appreciate the backlit remote at night as it helps to find the keys. I also expected that it might start to get louder over time as dust built up inside, but it still runs quiet as ever. I sit right in front of it and dont even notice the sound even after watching tv nearly all day sometimes.

Bare-bones projector but a really good value

So after days of bouncing between Epson, BenQ, and Optoma projectors I went with this entry level Optoma.PROS:- Low entry price for first time home projector- HDMI- Picture quality is greatCONS:- If you have multiple devices running into the projector via an Audio receiver (say, a Blu ray player and a Xbox), if you switch devices (say, stop watching a Blu Ray and turn on Xbox), the Projector starts displaying weird Red and green lines. I have to switch from HDMI Input 1 to HDMI Input 2. Then I switch back to Input 1 and the lines are gone. Seems the Projector doesnt like when you switch devices that are on a shared input.- Turns out I am sensitive to the Rainbow Effect (some people seem Red/Green/Blue colors when white text is on a black background if a DLP projector is used). Not bad enough to return it since I would have to get a LCD projector as all DLPs have this issue.I like this projector - its bare-bones but for my first projector I like it. I will get about 5 years out of it before I look into projectors above $1000.If your budget is around $500 this is the one to get
