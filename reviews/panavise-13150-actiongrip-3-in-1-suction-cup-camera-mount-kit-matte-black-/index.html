A good value suction mount with lots of flexibly

I have to start out by saying I’m a fan of Panavise gear - it’s one of my goto brands right there with Manfrotto, but it’s a bit less polished and bit more industrial. I have a couple of their ball heads (that are similar in design to the ballheads on this, but all metal which is much nicer but also much heavier) and they’re rock solid - plus I can buy a couple for the price of one 482 (which is probably the closest analogy).I have a couple of suction cup mounts, but in general I try to avoid using them as much as possible because they can be tricky to work with (getting good contact, not damaging the surface, being rigid, etc). My favorite suction mount is basically 4x the size of this one, pump actuated and about 4x the cost (no ball heads) - but could hold a DSLR if it needed to. So how does this compare to it? Very well - I’m not going to put a DSLR on it, but it holds a GoPro like a champ, and their ball head system gives you a ton of options. Keep in mind that in general you want to use as few adapters between the suction cup and the camera as possible - that gives it the most rigid configuration.It’s worth point out that while these ball heads look nearly the same as their other ones, they are definitely not as heavy duty and use more plastic components. This is probably more due to weight than cost reduction, but it’s worth pointing out - I definitely prefer their metal ball heads over these, except for the camera locking nut, which I like better on this (I think it’s easier to lock in place than the metal one).For the money, I think you can’t go wrong with this mount, it’s got a ton of flexibility in camera positioning, the suction mechanism itself is nice with an easy release, and it’s perfect for pairing with a light weight camera like the GoPro. I’m pretty picky when it comes to mounts, especially suction ones because the last thing you want is to save a couple bucks buying a cheaper one and watch your camera fly off because of it. Oh, and never use a suction mount without having a tether on it - just because you buy a good mount, don’t tempt fate. ;)

Extremely configurable for a variety of needs

I looked at several other similar products for mounting my smartphone, action camera, etc. in various locations. When adding to my kit, I want items that can be used in many different scenarios; you never really know what situation you might be in on the road. This kit has a great deal of utility as it can be configured with zero, one, or two ball joints and either a GoPro style mount or a traditional 1⁄4” tripod mount. This means it can hold nearly any small device (I don’t think I would trust the suction cup with a heavy DSLR) in just the right angle needed to get your shot. The suction cup lock works smoothly and has no issue grabbing a smooth, clean surface solidly. Overall, I highly recommend this kit for anyone who wants a flexible suction mounting solution for small to medium sized devices.

OK, I was looking for an action camera suction …

OK, I was looking for an action camera suction mount to use to film helicopter students during flight instruction. I mostly use it to film students from the inside of the aircraft so that we can review the lessons after the flight. I have also used it to take action videos with the mount stuck on the outside of the aircraft. I have use the suction cup on glass, on painted sheet metal, and on platic (Lexan I think) windshield. This thing holds solid with a GoPro4 silver mounted to it with all the vibrations of an aircraft. It has stayed solid at windspeeds of up to 120kts. That’s about 138 miles an hour. (Max cruising speed of a Robinson R44). If you have any doubts, a drop or two of water will make the seal practically unbreakable. Most of the parts are made of metal including the arm and ball joints. The quality of the plastic bits that are used in the construction this mount are top notch.Basically, if you’re looking for a mount with which you can trust your expensive action camera too… this is it. This is a serious mount!
