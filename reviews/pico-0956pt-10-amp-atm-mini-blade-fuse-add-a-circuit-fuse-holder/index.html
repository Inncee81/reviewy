ATM Mini Add A Circuit Fuse Holder Tap, Plug Into An Existing Fuse Holder Protects Original Circuit Use Wire Lead For New Circuit Add Additional Fuse For New Circuit 16 Gauge 10 AMPSingle PackThis Add A Circuit allows you to get a professional installation of any accessory up to 10 amps without cutting or splicing into the current wiring. Fast Easy- Add additional circuits for electrical accessories such as car phones, CD players, fax machines, etc. Turns One Fuse Slot into Two- Plugs into any fuse slot, even if the slot has a fuse installed. Provides protection for both circuits. Maintains New Car Warranty- Does not void new car warranty since existing circuits are not altered.Installation Instructions:1. Disconnect power source2. Select and remove a fuse in your vehicles fuse box( not to exceed 10 amps) with constant or switchable power capabilities that matches the power requirements of the accessory you are adding.3. Insert new accessory fuse (not to exceed 10 amps) into added slot. 4. Install Add-A-Circuit with the newly added fuse installed, back into the fuse block. Reconnect power source. Turn on ignition to test new accessory for proper operation. If the accessory does not operate, turn the Add-A-Circuit 180 degrees and reinsert into the fuse block. Retest new accessory.5. Replace the original fuse into the remaining slot on the Add-A-Circuit. Test both accessories for proper operation.

Product Reviews

Did the trick!

In conjunction with the SpyTec wire harness, this allowed me to do a professional-looking install of a DashCam to my car’s fuse box. As other reviewers have commented previously, would have been nice to include the wiring diagram. I also had some personal confusion/education about fuse sizes: standard vs. mini vs. micro, and normal-profile vs. low-profile. With this particular “Add-a-Circuit Kit”, it uses Mini sized fuses. I tried low-profile first, which SEEMED to fit, but did not make a reliable circuit connection. When I changed to normal-profile Mini fuses, I could not push them in using only hand-strength alone. But very carefully using pliers I was able to get the fuses to insert all the way (without breaking them) and now the circuit works great (but I doubt I’ll be able to extract those fuses again easily if one of them burns out).

Works as expected. PSA for individuals with new cars. Be sure to check what type of ‘mini’ blade your car uses. This may not fit some newer cars, including Hondas which require the even newer mini low profile blades.

I wasn’t sure how this would work at first, …

I wasn’t sure how this would work at first, I thought it was adding a fuse in series to an existing line, but once I received the product I realized it actually just adds another line in parallel using the fuse boxes power block. The original fuse is removed, then placed in this fuse holder and another fuse is added for the additional line. This allows two separate circuits to be run from a single socket on the power block.The product does stick out a bit, maybe 3⁄4” but the cover on my fuse panel didn’t have a problem closing after installation, though I could see this being an issue for some. The fuse holder is simple, but well built and insulated. I would purchase this product again if the need arises.

I’ve got the power…!

These worked perfectly for my 2002 Chevy Silverado. Quality is great. Intend to buy more to have around just in case. If you’re running smaller electronics I would recommend these as opposed to wiring directly to the battery. Wiring is easier and you don’t need to go through the firewall (in my application).
