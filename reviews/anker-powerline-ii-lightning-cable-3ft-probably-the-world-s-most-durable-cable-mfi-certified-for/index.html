I needed a second charging cord to add to an earlier version of the Anker lightening cable. The PowerLine II lightening cable is thicker and seemingly more sturdy than the other Anker cable (but I have had no problems with the first charging cable). Also the connectors have a larger protective shield for the two USB port connectors. The two improvements should extend the useful life of the newer power cord.The PowerLine II Dura Lightening Cable, attached to an Anker 2-port 24W USB wall charger, charges my iPhone at 6% every ten minutes. At that rate a completely discharged iPhone battery should recharge in less than 3 hours.With a lifetime replacement warranty and responsive service department you can not go wrong purchasing this cable.

A lightning cable that lasts even when stressed.

This is a winner. I use lightning cables in the field where a phone is connected to a battery in my back pocket by the lightning cable. The phone is in and out of my front pocket each time needed. Other high-quality cables only held up to such stress for six or eight weeks. After that, the phone lost power for a moment here and there— just enough to close running apps and cause other weird behaviour from the phone. The cable was disconnecting more and more as the cable grew older.I finally tried these Anker cables. Month after month they hold up to my daily abuse. Although they are a little more costly, they will be around much longer than the ones I had to discard.If you are looking for a cable that’s going to hang in there a long time, this is the one I believe will do it for you!

Finally, a lifetime warranty

Anker cables are my favorite full spec cables, and while I understand why they would avoid reversible plugs, it always bothered me that they didn’t offer lifetime warranties on such sturdy cables. My previous lightning cable did eventually get a hole right in the middle, but it’s been so long that I can’t find any record of the purchase to even try and get it replaced under warranty. The serial number has worn off the cable, and I couldn’t find the right box in my pile of Anker boxes.It was my favorite cable, however, and when I saw that they had released a version even stronger, it was the clear choice. It’s barely heavier or thicker, but feels tougher somehow. Although that could be because I had obviously worn enough of my old cable off to see the wires inside.I’m considering getting another one, although I’ve recently been more drawn to reversible cables, and even micro USB cables with lightning adapters. If I catch Anker making one of those, I’m jumping on it immediately
