Great quality, low price, VERY small!

These are some of the best constructed bicycling gloves I’ve seen. While some others are more substantial, the extra material increases the weight and reduces the ability of your hands to stay cool. The glove holds together very well. Unfortunately, the glove is made so that the bottom of the glove doesn’t sit on the wrist, but rather on the bottom part of the meat of the palm. If you look closely, this can be seen in the example picture. This is uncomfortable for me, and even more so because the glove runs VERY small. I wear a US Size Large in gloves; I ordered XL and they are still a little too small. For reference, from wrist to tip of my middle finger is 8”, and my palm is 4” across just above the thumb.

They have lasted and still have decent padding - Almost 2 years later.

I will have these gloves two years in July, I have worn them for approximately 5,000 miles. I originally thought for the price they looked pretty well padded in the right place for my hands. I was a little wary of them coming over from China. But they have been worth it. They sort of look cheap to me, and they got beat up looking relatively quick, but they have held up with out falling apart and they still have pretty good padding after all these miles. I have seen new gloves that are far less padded than these are in their present state. I use them for cycling with my road bike. I am seriously considering buying them again (in July). I figure 2 years would be a good time to get a new pair.Update: 6/03/17 - Just ordered my third pair of these, just to have handy at this low price, though I don’t need them for awhile as I have been getting 1 1⁄2 - 2 years out of a pair. For me these are the best cycling gloves (I have a road bike) I have had. I’ve tried others (more expensive) and nothing pads my hands in the right spots like these gloves.

Best of all

I’m not a biker. I’ve been on crutches for a month and will be for a least another month, with more crutch-time to come in the future. Within days my hands were so bruised, swollen, and sore I could barely use them. A co-worker who bikes suggested biking gloves and I tried these out of desperation. Wow! What a difference a little extra padding makes on the heels of my palms and thumbs! And since the palms of the gloves are made to grip, I don’t even have to hang onto the crutches as tightly as I used to. Best of all, they don’t add a lot of bulk, like when I tried wrapping the handles with washcloths. I’ve shown these to a lot of people in the medical community and they all say the same thing, “Why didn’t I think of that??? Definitely going to start recommending those to all my patients!” As an added bonus since the finger tips are free, I don’t have to keep taking them on and off as I move around the office working. My hands are much happier now!
