Speculative product reviews

Some information to combat the sudden influx of negative reviews:0. This mixer, the KL, is identical to the KP, except for the bowl lift rod. (KL: W10598835, KP: 9706787) The W10598835 is a replacement part for the 9706787.1. I called and asked to confirm a comment from /r/cooking. Yep, rep says it’s just an alternate model number.2. The model number is likely different here so that a Cyber Monday sale doesn’t devalue the product, so that KitchenAid can track units that were used in Amazon’s sale, or because the accessories included differ slightly from other models (look around on Amazon, you’ll see this last is quite common.)3. It does seem rather unlikely KitchenAid changed their entire manufacturing line to help Amazon scam people during their most highly-publicized sale of the year; it would tank their reviews and brand-name credibility.4. Professional Series vs Artisan Series

I have had the KitchenAid KSM150PSOB Artisan Series 5-Qt. Stand Mixer with Pouring Shield - Onyx Black for 9 years. I purchased it here on Amazon for a good price and it still works great after using it on average about once a week. So my review is more a comparison review for anyone looking at either purchasing their first one or considering upgrading to a larger mixer.KitchenAid Artisan-*Current Sold by Amazon Price - $279*Included Attachments - Flat beater, dough hook, 6-wire whip and 1-piece pouring shield*Color choice - 30+*Bowl Capacity (Quarts) - 5*Bread Yield (Loaves) - 4 1⁄2*Cookie Yield (Dozens) - 9*Mash Potato Yield (Pounds) - 7*Wattage - 325*Mixing Speeds - 10*Bowl Finish - Polished Stainless Steel*Bowl Handle - Comfort HandleKitchenAid Professional:*Current Sold by Amazon Price for Empire Red - $549*Included Attachments - Powerknead spiral dough hook, flat beater, and stainless steel wire whip*Color choice - 3*Bowl Capacity (Quarts) - 6*Bread Yield (Loaves) - 8 1⁄4*Cookie Yield (Dozens) - 13*Mash Potato Yield (Pounds) - 8*Wattage - 575*Mixing Speeds - 10*Bowl Finish - Polished Stainless Steel*Bowl Handle - Comfort HandleAs you can see its size and power that are the main difference between these two. For 9 years I can say that as an average home baker the Artisan has been enough; I can only remember a few times that I had to split up a batch of cookies or felt concerned that there wasn’t enough power to mix a stiff dough. Beyond that it has been great and I still think it was one of my best purchases here.I think what it comes down to for anyone looking at buying is: Do you really need the extra capacity and power and are you okay with limited colors. For me although I am happy to have the Professional Series and will gift my Artisan to a family member, if I were looking to buy now I’d still stick with the Artisan as several things in my opinion make it better for the average home. First being the size; its much easier to store the Artisan because of the height. Second the colors: I originally chose black because it went with our kitchen decor, red not so much, but with the Artisan you have dozens of colors to pick from. Third is the price and the included attachments; yes you may be able to find the Professional at a lower price than what is currently listed, but it doesn’t include the pouring shield which if you want will be an extra $20 (KitchenAid KN256PS 1-Piece Pouring Shield -Only Fits Bowl-Lift models KV25G and KP26M1X).Overall I do think the Professional Series is great mixer and easily give it 5 Stars based on its use and if when compared to the Artisan you feel the additional power and capacity are something you need I don’t think you be disappointed!
