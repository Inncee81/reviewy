Little sub that could

I took my time to look around, I was looking at many other brands. Some 100watt, 150 and upwards to 500. I looked at 8”, 10” and 12” from all brands.Many bass bins are loaded but often over-power and I did not want something like that. I wanted something that could offer low thundering lows, while maintaining good frequency and not over-powering the Lows and Mids.I took a gamble on this as I thought this would be perfect for what i was wanting. My thoughts where right. It’s perfect. Anything bigger would have been too much.I call this the little sub that could. If I crank it all the way up its pretty powerful! I have the gain and frequency about halfway. I have this placed in the corner of the room. Keep in mind the speaker only sounds as good as to where it is placed. The sound waves need something to bounce off of… putting this in middle of the room will not do any good and placing it along the wall also won’t do much good. I found the corner works best… Just try multiple places until you find your sweet spot… The sub will be heard no matter where its location but if you want the true buety of thundering lows it needs to be placed just right. The gains and frequency also need to be rapidly adjusted until you find the sweet spot!

Excellent match for Audioengine A2+

Purchased this sub to use with Audioengine A2+ speakers on my computer. This sub is the perfect pairing for these speakers. Set the crossover on the sub to around 110, turn gain to roughly 2⁄3, and enjoy the sound. Admittedly this is not the sub for room shaking home theater bass. It shakes my office and does an excellent job matching the output of the A2+ (which is also better suited to a home office than a home theater setup). It is important to remember that it is an 8” sub with 100 watts of output. It will pair great with small lower output systems and fits in small spaces. Sound is tight and clean. This sub delivers solid bass sound without excessive boom.If you have reasonable expectations, this subwoofer will not disappoint you. It is actually quite good for the price. You just need to keep in mind that this is not a larger, higher powered subwoofer. It certainly does not put out as much as the Klipsch R-12SW in my home theater setup, but it should not be expected to either. If you are considering this subwoofer for home theater use, I could see it working better if you used a pair of them instead of one. For the price two of these may be a reasonable substitute for more expensive larger single speakers.

nice addition to the sound system

I am using the SW-8MK2 with a Sony STRDH550 AV Receiver, currently in 2.1 mode, along with the Pioneer SP-BS22-LR bookshelf pair. What I have is a smart TV with HDMI with ARC going to the receiver, so when TV is on, it turns on the receiver automatically. The sound is good, although I only have the TV’s built-in speakers to compare to, so it’s not saying much. The auto standby does work; when there is no sound signal, it turns off the blue LED on the front, but I have yet to measure the power consumption when it is off vs. on.
