Get REAL SanDisk MicroSD Cards from Amazon

Wish they were faster. Very reliable.

It’s amazing that so much data can fit in such a tiny form factor, but … I wish it was faster. I use two 128GB SanDisk Ultra cards, each just over half full (and filling more over time), in a Fiio X5 music player. It might be the OS or it might be the SanDisk cards, or both, but it takes much longer to organize itself than it would on an iMac or a MacBook with a flash drive. If I continue playing where I left off when I last shut it down, or if it continues the shuffle it’s already determined, it’s fine. No slowness. But if I leave the shuffle and go to “All Songs” or “Genres” or “Artists”, the new sort takes a painfully long time. On a plane, I’ve got plenty of time and I might just nod off while I’m waiting. Anywhere else on the go, it’s a pain. I know we have ridiculously high expectations. (“You’re listening to perfect recordings, from a box, in your hand, with earphones better than the best h-fi speakers available when the iPod came out.” I know.) But the iPhones, iPads, iMacs and Macbooks and high-end PCs have set a high bar. It takes a long time to copy files to these the SD cards, whether the card is in the player and connected by USB cord, or the card is plugged into the card slot in the back of the imac. Lots longer than it takes to copy to USB hard drives or thunderbolt or firewire hard drives. Same when the X5 is connected to a Windows 7 laptop and you wait for the files on the SD cards to show up in “My Computer.” The cards are formatted with FAT and I have run chkdsk /f from the Widnows laptop. The file structures are fine. The speed is about the same on both cards. They never crash.
