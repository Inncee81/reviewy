Product Reviews

Just as fast as the Extreme Pro 95MB/s UHS-I microSD

Until recently, SanDisk Extreme Plus MicroSD UHS-I U3 cards were rated 80MB/s read and 50MB/s write. This new Extreme Plus microSDHC card (as of October 2015) caught my attention because it is advertised at 95MB/s read and 90MB/s write, the same as the current Extreme Pro microSD card!This card is UHS-I compatible and rated UHS Speed Class 3 (U3). The speed class means it can sustain at least 30MB/s continuous write speed so it is suitable for recording 4K video that requires high bitrate.Both old and new Extreme Plus microSDHC cards look exactly the same, the only difference is the packaging and the batch numbers on the back of the cards. I ran some benchmarks of the cards as well as an Extreme Pro microSD to compare read and write speeds.New Extreme Plus (95⁄90) UHS-I U3 microSDHC 32GB <- this itemSequential Read: 97.578 MB/sSequential Write: 91.331 MB/sOlder Extreme Plus (80⁄50) UHS-I U3 microSDHC 32GBSequential Read: 97.806 MB/sSequential Write: 65.360 MB/sExtreme Pro 95MB/s (95⁄90) UHS-I U3 microSD 32GBSequential Read: 97.587 MB/sSequential Write: 91.316 MB/sAmazing! I don’t understand SanDisk marketing. Maybe is a faster Extreme Pro microSD UHS-II card coming soon? The prices of SD cards change frequently, so if considering this or the Extreme Pro, just buy whichever is cheaper.

Nothing But SanDisk

The real deal

I was skeptical at first because the description didn’t specify 128gb and this same product is selling in stores for a much higher price, but I was pleased to receive exactly what I purchased. Same packaging as the over priced store microsd and true to the size. Actual storage is slightly less than 128, but that’s to be expected. I now have a fast, large, microsd in my new Samsung s7 and couldn’t be happier!

Not sure what the difference is?

I mean, yes I get the name is different and the speeds are different. But how different are they? I quickly tested both of them on a Sony A7RII in 4k at 100mbps and I couldn’t tell the difference. Maybe I am not running a proper test, so take my ‘quick’ test with a grain of salt. Anyhow, they both seem to be working without a problem. I read that if the card is not fast enough, the camera may lose some frames when recording to the card.
