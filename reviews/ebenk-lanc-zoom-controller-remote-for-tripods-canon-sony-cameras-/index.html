eBenk LANC Zoom Controller Remote for Tripods (Canon & Sony Cameras)

Product Specifications

Product Description

This eBenk LANC remote control works camcorders and cameras with LANC jack’s. It puts full camera control within your reach. This precision remote gives you the ability to zoom, record, and control shutter release functions from several feet away. Works with most Canon, Sony and JVC cameras with a LANC input. Does not work with Panasonic cameras.

Waste of money!

This is my second one, and just as I expected, the zoom rocker broke within the first couple minutes of use. The first one is not so bad; it just feels weird. I guess the spring broke, or slipped out. On the second one, the zoom rocker gets stuck each time you zoom in or out. So if you try to zoom just a little bit, it will keep zooming after you take your finger off the rocker. You have to press the opposite end, or lift up the end you were pressing. All the other buttons work just fine though. I have a Sony FX7, and a Canon Vixia HF-G20, and the focus works great. Even when either camera is set to auto focus, pressing the “N” or “F” buttons on the this remote will over-ride the auto focus.

For the price it’s ok

For the price it’s ok, not the best. So, I’ve seen a lot of comments here about it not working and making the camera turn on and off. It’ll do that unless you have both the camera and the remote turned ON BEFORE you plug the remote into the camera. And you have to do the same to turn it off, don’t turn the camera or the remote off, unplug the remote BEFORE you turn off the camera. If you do that it works great, I have it on my Vixia HFG10 and it works fine as long as you follow those instructions. The one thing I don’t like about it is that the zoom control is really loud, you can hear it click on your recordings, so be careful when you use the zoom during a shot that requires good audio. Also, the photo button doesn’t do anything, I wish it did because I hate that on the Vixia HFG10 if you want to take a picture you have to use the touch screen, which bumps the camera, so it would have been cool if the photo button on this remote worked, but it doesn’t, oh well.
