Not great for motorcycle use

This Halo Skull Cap has a heavy neoprene band running around the rim that is both too tight and too tall. The band covers my ears and presses quite hard on my ear plugs, resulting in a rather loud ride. Seams are on the outside instead of being flat, and while the neoprene band is rigid the white top portion isn’t. Putting on my helmet either pulls the Halo down too far or tries crunching the tips of my ears between helmet and seam. Neither one being terribly comfortable. The yellow rubber sweat stripe leaves a lovely divot in my forehead, so I tend to wear this thing backwards. For reference, I wear a large Shoei full-face helmet and a 7.5 hat.Locating a motorcycle helmet liner is rather a Goldilocks problem. I’ve come close a few times, but haven’t located that “just right” item yet. SweatVac makes (or used to?) a nice ventilated cap that worked perfectly under a full-face helmet. Ear tips weren’t squished or pulled hard, and it doesn’t hit ear plugs resulting in a deafening experience. Finding them is getting to be tougher, though. Zan had a little Coolmax cap that worked okay, but stretched easily and needed frequent replacement. Ah well, I’ll keep trying different caps and seeing what works.

Socks for the (gear) head…

I really like this skull cap and actually use it year round more to keep my helmet clean than to keep my head warm (the way I see it, this is like socks for the head!). The band on the edge does a good job of keeping the sweat out of my eyes… however, do be aware that after the ride, you’ll be walking around with the “indents” of the band right across your forehead which definitely looks a little goofy - it does go away after a while, but not as quickly as I would like. I’ve washed mine multiple times and have had it for more than a year now - washes extremely well and I’m impressed it hasn’t worn out yet given the amount of biking that I do.
