A god product at a good price

This is an evaluation of the Pruveeo P3 Dashboard traffic camera. This is not my first traffic camera and I don’t intend to get into a protracted discussion on the deficiencies of the previous cameras. Suffice it to say I had to find one that actually performed; and this one does.The P3 camera is very compact and fits just in front of the rear view mirror and does not block my forward view at all. It does not come with a suction cup, but rather, it uses a double back adhesive tape to attach a mounting adapter to the inside of the windshield. The parcel had two mounting adapters and adhesive stickers, for easy transfer of the camera between two vehicles. You should be careful to select where to mount the adapter, because once it is stuck, it would be really difficult to pull it loose and re position it. If your vehicle power plug (formally called cigarette lighter) is activated with the ignition switch, the camera will automatically start and stop recording with the position of the ignition switch. Conversely, if the power plug remains active at all times, you would have to connect the camera directly to a switchable circuit while being very careful to maintain correct battery polarity to avoid severe damage to the camera. Or just plug it in when start driving and unplug it when you stop. Just don’t forget to unplug it or you might end up with a dead battery. Unlike most products produced in China, this instruction pamphlet was clearly written by a native speaker of English; it is concise and easily followed. The camera does not come with an SD card and will not work without one. The camera uses (up to) a 32 GB micro SD card, which is available here on Amazon for around $10. Then again, if you already have a micro SD card smaller than 32gb it would work because the 10-minute loop recording requires just 1.3 gb. You will have to lift the camera off the mount to insert or extract the SD card, because the slot is not accessible while the camera is mounted. Also, you will need a SD card reader/writer adapter to format and or download the video files to the USB port on the computer. You should format the SD card with FAT32. My personal preference for an SD reader is Tragus high-speed SD/SDHC/ T-F, which will accept anything up to a 64 gb SD card. I set the camera resolution at 1080 and the daylight video is remarkably clear and a license plate digit is legible within about 25 feet.In conclusion, this is the best dash camera I have seen at any price and I have seen quite a few. I can recommend this camera and the seller. You should connect the camera to a power outlet that is active only when the ignition switch is turned on. Use only the included voltage regulated power adapter, because the camera operates on 5 volts, not 12volts. The camera will automatically start recording when you start the engine and stop when the ignition switch is off.If your rearview mirror has wiring connected to it, you could tap into that power and connect the dashboard camera an eliminate the unsightly wire hanging down from the camera.There are two questions regarding that wiring arraignment and some soldering of wires would be required.Test the voltage on the wires on the mirror and determine (1) that it is 12vdc and (2) that the power is there only when the ignition switch is on.This modification will work only if these two tests are affirmative. The camera operates on 5 volts DC and 12volts would immediately destroy the camera. The provided power plug adaptor has a small circuit board inside it and you must open the plastic housing of that and cut he wires and remove the printed circuit board. You must pay attention to polarity (+ –) of all four wires and which two are inputs (12volt) and which two are (5 volt) leading to the camera. Clip the wires off the circuit board while leaving as much length as possible attached to the board. Strip a short section of the insulation from the ends of all four wires. Cut the camera wire about 6 inches away from the special plug that fits into the camera and strip a short section of the insulation from the two colored wires. Twist the camera plug wires to the circuit board wires, while observing common color match of the insulation and solder it and use shrink tubing to cover the unions. Pull the power plug from the back of the rearview mirror and using a volt meter to identify the power (12volt) wire and the ground wire. Carefully cut about a quarter inch of the insulation from both wires, while not actually cutting the wire. The removed insulation from the power and ground wires should not be directly adjacent to each other. Use a section of the previously discarded camera wire and while observing the previously established polarity attach and solder to the two wires to the mirror harness. You can’t use shrink tubing here, so wrap the solder joints individually with electrical tape. Cut the repurposed camera wire off about 6 inches from the solder joints. Split the insulation sheath and separate the two internal wires, slide a short section of shrink tubing onto each and solder the wires to the circuit board wires. Slide the shrink tubing over the solder joints and lightly drag hot the tip of the solder iron across the shrink tubing. Install the camera and reconnect the vehicle plug to the rearview mirror, plug the special connector into the camera and the camera will start recording each time the ignition is turned on.
