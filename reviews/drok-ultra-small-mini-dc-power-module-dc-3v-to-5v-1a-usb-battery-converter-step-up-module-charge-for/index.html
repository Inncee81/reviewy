2 AMP 5 Volt step up goodness

This module is so handy and useful I had to order another one. I ordered it for an altoid solar usb charger at first. It stepped up the current to 5 volts, and was strong enough to draw 2 AMPs and power charge my flagship phone quickly. I then used it with two of my window solar panels in parallel rated 6 volts 583 mA and it also stepped up the voltage when the sun wasn’t at its highest (5v 1A) and didn’t fail either when exposed to 6.15V. Then I used it with a couple of 18650s to make a power bank. The greatest thing is the indicator led, and the amount of current it can put out(great for 2A fast charge devices!). Note this module does not step up current, so directly connecting it to a small solar panel will only step up your voltage. My next plan is to add 3 more solar panels in parallel to create a 2A output on my window, and this module will surely be key for it!

Not enough current draw for fast charging but otherwise works fine

I initially had some trouble with this converter. The first time I tried to plug my phone into it, my circuit kept browning out. Eventually I figured out that my phone was drawing too much current for the converter to handle. On my phone, I was able to fix this by going to the battery settings and turning off fast charging, which causes the phone to only draw 1 amp from the charger, albeit slower.That being said, this charging circuit is otherwise fairly well built. I tested its output at 5.17V, which is perfectly safe for charging your phone. It gets a little hot when in use but not enough to worry me. In all, it’s fairly good.

Provides decent voltage, as stated

As long as you stay above 3V, it boosts to ~5V (usually hovers around 5.0xV for anything in the 3.5-4.5V range. The output current, from my testing with 4xAA NiMH 2800/2100mAh batteries, tended to only get to 200-400mA. My circuit is simply 4 AAs in series to the + input of this and the - output to the voltage source. The usb is connected to an iphone where it gives a reliable 5V/200mA until the input voltage gets below 4V. Anyone have better experience with NiMH batteries and higher output current with this converter? The inner pins are shorted(?) or powered, so an iPhone detects it as charging.
