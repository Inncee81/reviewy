Product Reviews

Great starter kit for a budding scientist

I love these! We purchased them as a birthday gift, to be given with a pocket/portable microscope Carson MicroBrite Plus 60x-120x Power LED Lighted Pocket Microscope (MM-300) ($10 from amazon, and great quality).My six year old thought they were neat, and even my three year old found a few moments of fun looking at the “tiny dead ant” included!There are six slides, under the following categories: 1. Insects 2. Tiny Creature 3. Pollens & Spores 4. Textile Fibers 5. Plants 6. Animals. Each slide includes 4 specimans for a total of 24 from the set. The following are the specific specimens provided:1. Insects: (Locust leg, Worker bee wing, Bee antenna, Bee abdomen)2. Tiny Creatures: (Common red sponge, Angora rabbit hair, Ant, Mouse fur)3. Pollens & Spores: (Tulip pollen, Leaf spore, Pine tree pollen, Orange pollen)4. Textile Fibers: (Panya, Nylon, Wool, Handmade paper)5. Plants: (Stem of corn, Leaf of nerium, Onion rind, Silver berry scaly hair)6. Animals: (Cat hair, Canary feather, Dog hair, Sheep hair)Someone might find the inclusion of sheep hair and wool to be redundant, but I found it to be clever. There are subtle differences, and that can create an educational conversation to promote further learning. It will also push your young explorer to pay more attention to the “tiny” details.

Sufficient quality for kid or adult

Bought this set together with an Amscope for kids as a birthday present for a 6 yr old. The slides are plastic so that children will not easily break them. They are very good for a child quality experience. If you are expecting laboratory quality mounts, then this is not what you are looking for. I had no trouble viewing at low magnifications and up to 1200x. You can see detail in all the samples. These are not micro-tom sliced samples. Some better than others, but all of sufficient quality to use for the average kid or adult.The box they come in is paper/cardboard and can be reused to hold the slides. The top opens and can be folded back easily.I had a great time with them. I recommend and am sorry I didn’t get the full 2 box set.

Nice variety but could use a few more insects…

I bought this set for my 7 year old niece and we viewed it together. The specimens seem very professionally done – well-dyed, clearly labelled, and constructed to stand up to the type of rough handling you’d expect from little hands. There are 5 specimens per slide so there really is a lot here to look at. There’s a broad variety of subjects and enough “odd” items to inspire lots of questions. Of everything we viewed, it was (surprisingly) the pollen samples that drew the most oohs and aahs from my niece. We did both find ourselves wishing for more insects, and the textile samples were rather “ho-hum”, but overall this is a nice set and, I think, worth the purchase.
