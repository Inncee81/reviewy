    Product Description

RAM stands for Round-A-Mount and was introduced in 1995. Far and away the most innovative product line of its kind, RAM continues to evolve into one of the most sought after accessories for electronics. RAM products have quickly become an essential mounting component for a wide variety of applications including rugged vehicle, industrial, military and defense, material handling as well as any application requiring a robust mounting solution.

Great for quick mount location changes.

I’ve use this to mount a GoPro, suspended on up to three 4” arms, on my motorcycles. It holds tight and secure. I have no worries about it loosening or failing in any way. I love that I can quickly change the camera position, from handlebar to engine guard to cargo rack, etc, in seconds.I do recommend checking the screw that holds the ball onto the clamp before your first use. It’s a Phillips screw threaded into a captured locknut. During first use I noticed the ball flexing slightly and causing camera shake. I found the screw needed to be tightened up about a turn to make everything solid. After that everything was great.I also recommend another style mount if you’re mounting something semi-permanent, such as a GPS or cellphone holder. There are RAM mounts that are more secure, especially from theft, if you do not plan on moving your mount location often. I use a RAM u-bolt mount for my handlebar mounted GPS.

I have purchased RAM mounts for many years and I have not found anything better. They are bulky, heavy, ugly and decidedly very low-tech, which makes them reliable and pretty well bulletproof. Overall good mount

Many reviews said the jaws would have to be lined with a strip of inner tube, or similar, to keep in from “twisting” and that is true if mounting a GPS on the horizontal portion of motorcycle handle bars. It stayed in place on a 200 mile trip with that alteration. The only thing I disliked about the setup is that on my 2004 Harley FXDWG and because of the inner tube lined clamp there is quite a bit of “wiggle” that is transferred to the GPS. That didn’t seem to affect anything though, just slightly annoying. I think that if were practical to mount it on the vertical portion of the handle bars there would be no “twisting” motion, so no issue. That’s the only reason I gave it 3 stars, otherwise I’d have given it 5.
