I did my research…found out that all microwave drawers are made by Sharp with other labels installing their own fronts….and prices. Sharp is the best value & best fit the opening for our below-counter installation. Sharp does make a less expensive model but we chose this one because of the clean lines and tilt-out control panel. Buyer Beware.

Giving up on Sharp after the second microwave they sent us had the same exact problem as the first: Horrible grating noise (like a garbage disposal) with smoke & a terrible electrical burning smell coming from unit. The first microwave and service call was covered under warranty, but to have someone come look at the second unit to verify what was wrong would have been $100+. Even then Sharp said they would only cover the magnetron if that was found to be the problem.Way too much trouble for what was supposed to be a top of the line product. Going with a basic countertop microwave instead.

So far so good. We love it

So far so good. We love it. This microwave looks fantastic in our kitchen island and it functions perfectly well. I do have some concerns with how it will hold up to regular use. The drawer open/close feature is motor actuated. Im not sure if it will last as long as the rest of the electronics especially around little kids. Mine are a tad impatient while waiting for the drawer to shut and tend to give it little nudges to help it along. I can totally see the bearings and assembly wearing out prematurely with this kind of abuse. Not sure if the drawer will be able to shut with some manual help in case the motor / actuator dies. Longevity concerns aside, we love this sharp microwave.
