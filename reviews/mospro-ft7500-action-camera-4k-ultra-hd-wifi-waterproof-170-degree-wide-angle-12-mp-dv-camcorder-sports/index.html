I am going to be brutally honest here…my expectations for this camera were fairly LOW. I have only used it a couple of times, so I can’t comment on longevity but I can comment on what I know about this camera, so here goes…Build Quality:Camera quality is actually really good! It doesn’t feel super cheap; it feels sturdy. The buttons are tactile and snappy, and the finish is very nice.Image/Video Quality:No big surprise here…the images are good–but not excellent. I wouldn’t use this to print images, but for fun videos and great action shots, this fits the bill nicely. Minimum focus distance is around 8-10 inches.For my rudimentary video quality test, I simply walked along my porch looking at the flowers in the garden. No action, just slow panning to see how the camera would react:4K 25 FPS: Video quality is shaky/jittery even when using the latest/fastest SD card. This is a major bummer…video not usable2K 30 FPS: Video quality is shaky/jittery…not usable.1080P 60FPS: Wow, video looks really good. This is probably what I will use!1080P 30FPS: Video still looks good…this will use less storage but quality isn’t as nice as 60FPS780P 120FPS: Did not look good/video was jittery and shaky even though the recording was smooth(I had uploaded a video, but the compression by Amazon made it look horrible)Package Deal:Wow, tons of usable mounts! I plan to keep this camera in the waterproof case at all times, unless I find a reason otherwise. I like to keep it protected. I was able to drill a couple of holes into the tripod mount and mount it below my GARMIN out-in-front mount on my bicycle. With a little piece of rubber from a blown tube as a shock absorber, the camera shake isn’t horrible.Points to note:-Video is recorded in 10 Minute chunks-Wifi doesn’t connect to your network at home, it connects to your phone and allows you to control/view the phone with an app-Phone app is not bad, but it crashed several times while I was using it-Recording time will likely be closer to an hour, not a full 90 minutes-Record with the camera in your RIGHT hand to help avoid stray fingers ending up in the shotI really like the camera and have purchased a GoPro Session 5 to compare; but for the price, this tiny camera is great!
