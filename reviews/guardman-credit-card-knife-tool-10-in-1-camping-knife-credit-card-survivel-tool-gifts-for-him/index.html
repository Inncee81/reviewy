In My Opinion……

I paid full price for this “tool”, so my comments are not jaded by free or a much discounted price. The compass is about 1⁄4” in diameter - you need the magnifying glass to read it - except that the magnifying glass is about 3⁄8” in diameter-(placed right next to the compass) - okay for reading one letter at a time, if you have all day to read your OTC instructions. I had to get my pliers out in order to remove the tiny can opener. You probably will have some difficulty getting any leverage going to actually open any cans - I can see opening the cap on a beer bottle. You have half of a Phillips screw driver - will take twice as long to use, but it will get the job done eventually. Don’t count on the “cute little tweezers” hanging onto anything for very long - they slide right off most anything you are trying to pick up. Ah, the toothpick - now there is something that might really work! The knife, which is why I purchased this little item, could have been designed a little better. It comes completely separate from the case, making it kind of difficult to cut anything much larger than bait for fishing. Better leverage could have been achieved by designing the knife so that it swings out of the case, remaining attached so that the case acts as a handle. Then again, just maybe the 10 in 1 that I received is not the GuardmanCard Knife that I ordered. It has no “Guardman” name or emblem on it. The box is written in (I assume) Japanese and I have to wonder if I was sent a “substitute”? Cannot recommend it for the money paid.

Multi-function at a Great Price.

One of the toughest pocket cards I’ve had! Where others have had the plastic casing bent, broken and damaged, I’ve had this one longer with nary a scratch! Everything clips into place and holds quite snug, even the bits you’d expect to wear out after use such as the toothpick and tweasers.Its a decent size, but it is a bit fat and adds considerable bulk to my wallet, good things my wallet is expandable!The serrated blade is handy and pretty comfortable to hold, but needs sharpening straight out of the box for any tough cuts, and you may want to consider the same treatment for the can opener.It has many tool features in as small a space as you can manage, slots for small nuts and bolts on the blade of the knife, even a small screwdriver on the can opener!I use all of the hardware pieces quite often, and have even used the ruler edge in a pinch.The compass seems to be of a fair accuracy when lined up with a higher end compass, and the magnifying glass can definitely aid in starting a fire, though I’ve mostly used it for scrawling designs into wood when I’m bored.If I had one critique about this tool, aside from its bulk, its got to be the tweezers. Made from cheap plastic, I’ve had a few occasions to try to make them work. The tips aren’t small enough to grab at splinters, and there’s no spring in the material at all, they just lay there like a dead, floppy fish. I’d consider using some sort of tin replacements, you may even be able to fashion some yourself.All in all, I don’t have enough good things to say about this multi tool, its handy in a pinch, would make an awesome gift for someone who craves utility in everything they own, and I’ll be one person to carry this moderately priced thing around everywhere I go.
