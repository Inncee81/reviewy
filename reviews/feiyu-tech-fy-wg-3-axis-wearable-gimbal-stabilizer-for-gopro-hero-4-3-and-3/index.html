Little help for those who has a ‘dead’ unit

Mine actually arrived and it worked right away. Well, at least AFTER the camera has mounted on it, and some troubles that I almost going to send it back.!!!WARNING!!!DO NOT start the unit WITHOUT the camera on it!DO NOT play around the panning axis by making it to turn multiple round!!!The above warning has no where mention in the user manual as extreme caution, and I believe that happened to quite some users especially couple units that I saw posted on YouTube seeking for help to resolve their problem.Doing so for what I warned for, actually pull the connector wire which is inside the panning axis which render your unit - dead. Whenever you turn on the unit, it will try calibrating, making a spin on the turn axis, and then stop - like a near dead fish making its very last stroke then, dead. You turn off and turn on again it does the same time - like you tried reviving it with your deepest soul’s prayer. Nah.I was packing and about to return and saw the L-key that matches the screws of the unit. I thought about it and open up - sure enough, there the connector pulled out with the wife curled up inside the tiny chamber. You will need very very, fine, skillful hands to put it back. I was kidding. It was just not that hard.I attached some pictures here so you could see what I mean.1. Before removing the cover make sure batteries are removed.2. Unscrew the panning axis screws (3) on the side, and slowly pull it out by holding the GoPro bracket.3. See the 2nd picture for the closeup. When you pull it out if the white connector is already disconnected, you found the problem. The male connector should reconnect to the female. You will need 2 tiny tools to help push the connector back in as the chamber is small.3. Match up the 3 pin (black) when closing the unit.4. Put back the screws and batteries.5. IMPORTANT: mount the GoPro!!!!6. Start the unit (press and hold for 2 seconds) (did I hear some HOORAY already?)I hope it helps to some who are broken hearted. Help is on the way!Performance: the unit works pretty well except that I do not like the horizontal turn axis (turning left and right), not sure what this is called. When I was expecting the unit to make a smoother turn it would randomly make a sharp one. I have updated to the latest version but it seems no improvement. Perhaps I am using it with a selfie stick/GoPro stick so turning movement is a lot more than using it with a chest mount/helmet mount/bike mount, which you only need to face it to the same directions without making sharp turn of the angle.All in all a nice gimbal for a first time user like me! I wish I will get some time and would like to take some footage to show you guys. Enjoy!

Nice gimbal

Nice gimbal for my gopro hero4. Compensates for high frequency vibrations very well. Makes videos a lot smoother than without it. As for neg side, Just like any other gimbal, when you do fast turns and corners, the pan motion will be delayed and not look natural. Doesn’t have a lot of features like osmo, karma grip etc, but it just does what a gimbal is for so is good enough for me. Keep a lookout, if it runs out of battery… all the arms/axis goes loose and free motion so don’t leave it hanging around after it runs out of juice, store it in your bag else it will slam around. But anyway, battery seems to last 3-4hrs as advertised so good enough for a usual moutain bike ride and i’d be carrying spare bat anyway. Other than that, it is a nice gimbal, does its job.

Fantastic product if a little immature on the software side.

This miniaturized 3 axis gimble from Feiyu Tech is a marvelous piece of kit but it’s not perfect. Mine arrived and was leaning quite heavily to the left, with a quick search I realized I needed to update the Firmware and this is where my problems began. Upgrading the firmware on this is not for the faint hearted, you must read the instructions numerous times before embarking and even then the software to do so is immature and certainly not intuitive. I bricked my Gimble after accidentally clicking the wrong button. Feiyu customer services are very friendly and tried their best to help me resolve the problem before giving up and asking me to send the unit back to China. The cost to return is quite high but Feiyu didn’t charge me anything to fix the problem or to return the unit. Now my Gimble is working fabulously and I am very happy. It’s a fragile unit and I certainly won’t be attempting to update the firmware anytime soon but as long as you are somewhat tech savvy and patient I would recommend this product.
