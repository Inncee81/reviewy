This TV is amazing. The colors and brightness are superb

This TV is amazing. The colors and brightness are superb. I use it with my Ps4 Pro and my Pc at full 4K HDR. The text are sharp and colors are perfect. The sounds is… well I don’t expect much from stock speakers on a TV this thin. It does have optical out but not analogue out, so keep that in mind to make sure it’s compatible with your sound bar.This is pricey but for what you get really your only option in the TV space right now is the Sony 800, which is around the same price depending on where you get it. However, if you see them side by like the local store had them on display, there really is no comparison. The Sony is nice on it’s own but cannot match the brightness and style of this Samsung.As a monitor it is really nice, it is only 60Hz, so you professional gamers may not be satisfied, but for us mere mortals this is great. I really couldn’t tell the input lag difference from my monitor.

Thumbs Up with Some Tips

I’m really enjoying this set. I got the 65” size, which fits our home theater nicely. The room is not a lot wider than the screen, so off-angle viewing isn’t a problem.When it was first set up the installers tweaked the “Standard” video setting a bit, and I found the picture oversaturated to the point of garishness. I tested it with my Oppo play using Blu-Ray DVDs of Planet Earth I and Comcast DVR-recorded Planet Earth II. After resetting the video to “Movie” everything looked much better. I’ve looked at a wide variety of sources and been generally quite pleased—a big step up from my old Sony HD TV, particularly in dynamic range (range of lights to darks). Because of this and its larger size, it puts out enough extra light that my wife can see to study her knitting while we watch in the darkened viewing room.I haven’t been able to try the ultra HD features because like most people I don’t have access yet to true Ultra sources. I got a Roku Ultra box that can stream 4K material, but because there is a long cable run between my equipment rack and the TV I will need a booster balun. The baluns now available, like the AV Access HDMI Extender, deal with 4K resolution, but not with the widened color gamut and dynamic range of full Ultra HD.My installer says there is one in the works that should be released later this year, and I’ll have that installed when it’s available which I’ll be using to boost the signal fed through my current Cat 5 cable.Meanwhile my Oppo DVD player can’t handle 4K, so there’s no point in purchasing 4K discs until I get a new player. Netflix doesn’t rent out Ultra 4K discs. Sooner or later Ultra 4K sources will be more widely available, but right now your best bet is streaming via the Internet. But for that you need a fast connection, or you may experience glitches.So the moral is, you’re investing in the future when you buy this set. You won’t necessarily be able to turn it on and view it at its full capability. But even as a standard HDTV, it’s a beauty, and reasonably priced from Amazon, which didn’t charge anything to me as a Prime customer to ship it and have it taken into the viewing room and unboxed. I didn’t pay Amazon for installation because it had to be integrated with my other equipment and my universal remote, so that cost me another $430. There was also no sales tax charged, which may depend on which state you’re in (I’m in Washington State).
