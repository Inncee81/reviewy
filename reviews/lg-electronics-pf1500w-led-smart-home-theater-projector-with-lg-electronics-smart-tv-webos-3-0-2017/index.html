Nice coffee table projector for family movie night but has severely limited zoom and no lens shift

I own several projectors from the low to high end and would rank this model a decent starter model but would probably persuade folks towards a similarly price Epson LCD model with more flexibility and lumens. This LG is really compact with nice color and long bulb life, but if you really enjoy a projector you will probably start itching for an upgrade soon and end up with something with more flexbility.Limited Zoom:This projector offers VERY limited zoom and no lens shift. By limited, I mean almost non-existent. At 6 feet, it gets about a 60” display.. you can adjust the zoom and get that down to 55” but that’s almost useless for adjusting. At 8 feet, you’ll get about 80” diagnol display, and at 10 feet it’s about 100” (about 10” per foot seems to be a good figure for this). The further away the lower the lumens that are hitting the wall. I would say 100” is really the limit for this model with 1500 lumens. I’d guess most people already own a 50-60” TV in their family rooms so you will need to be able to place this projector about 8-10 feet away from you wall to get a bigger image than your TV. Ideally for a true movie experience you want 100+ inches. Don’t expect that you can place this anywhere in your room and adjust the picture size to whatever you want. You really need to invest in the higher end projectors to get into lens shift and flexible zoom options however there are some projectors in this price range that offer these features and those features are worth it in my opinion. It does offer automtic pin cushion adjustment when you tilt the projector up, which is accurate and useful when your using the projector from a coffee table.Picture quality:Picture and colors are actually great in a dark room. It’s not as nice as my Epson due to the Lumen difference but I was actually impressed with the color brightness from some such a small projector. My Epson full-time home theater projector is easily 10x the size of this LG. If you have a light colored wall, you’ll be able to using this for displaying right on the wall, however a dedicate screen will get you a much better experience. I’d recommend a slight grey screen to get better blacks.Lumens:1500 lumens will get you a nice bright picture from the projector at night, but you will have little chance of watching this (enjoyably) during the day or in a well-lit room. If you want a projector that is usable during the day you need to get 2500+ lumens and need to be looking at bright LED models from Epson to accomplish this.WebOS:I also own an LG 4K TV with WebOS and I think the interface is great, however, I have noticed that the experience on this projector is not as good as the one on my TV. My LG TV has several gigabytes of internal storage for “Apps” from LG’s store and I’m able to use Amazon, NetFlix, and Plex with a very nice experience. On my TV, I actually removed my Roku because the 4K support from the WebOS apps was superior to that of Roku’s 4K apps (Roku doesn’t support Dolby Vision’s HDR while LG’s apps do). However, the WebOS on this projector does not allow you install Amazon Prime app or the Sling TV app like are available on my LG TV. It’s just absent when you search for it on the projector’s App store but present on the TV. That’s disappointing if you are a cord cutter and streaming user. I also found using the apps over Wifi to be very sluggish on the projector. I plugged in my Roku Stick and found the experience to be much better. The built-in apps are tolerable, but if you are used to using these apps on other platforms you will likely find them sluggish and slow. In general, the WebOS is an improvement, the built-in TV tuner is good, and you can get by with no other boxes or add-ons assuming you don’t need more than Netflix and Plex.Other things:Wifi works well but seems slow, luckily it has ethernet too if you’ve got a cord nearby. Bluetooth sound to a speaker or headphones is nice because the built in speaker is tolerable for my kids but nothing I would want to listen to long-term.In summary, if you want a small (almost portable) projector to throw up on the wall on Saturday night if your table or such is 10-12’ from the wall. If you need to mount your projector further or closer than that then you should look into either short throw or more expensive models that offer lens shift and more useable zoom. There are much better options for those scenarios in this price range from Epson, BenQ, and others.
