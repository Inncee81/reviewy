Product Reviews

Great little case for my point-and-shoot

I use this case to hold my Canon Powershot S110 camera. Fits the camera like it was made for it. It’s a soft case; I don’t think it would protect too well from drops. But it’s perfect if you don’t want your camera rattling around loose in your bag or backpack.The fabric, clip, and zipper all seem sturdy, especially so given the price. There’s a separate external pocket that I use to hold a spare battery and memory card.

Nikon S6300 Case + 2 Batteries

“Amazon Verified Purchase”: I purchased a Nikon CoolPix S6300 and wanted a case to carry both the S6300 plus two (2) extra batteries. Case Logic makes excellent products; therefore, I went to the “CaseLogic” website ([…]) to use their “CaseFinder” to find a case. The “CaseFinder” is located in the upper right corner of the website and is entitled “CaseFinder Electronics Fit Guide”. After clicking on the “CaseFinder” I chose the “Point and Shoot” category then entered the width (3.7”), height (2.3”), and depth (1.1”) of the Nikon S6300; “CaseFinder” gave me five (5) recommended cases that would fit my camera; all appeared to have more room in the case than I wanted; I wanted a case that would fit in the pocket of my slacks plus be able to clip onto my belt. I changed the camera depth to 1” (vs. 1.1”)and “CaseFinder” gave me sixteen (16) recommended cases; I chose the TBC-402 to meet my needs.The Nikon S6300 fits perfectly into the case’s camera pocket which will accommodate a camera with 3.875” width, 2.375” height, and 1.125” depth. The accessory pocket comfortably holds my two (2) extra EN-EL 12 rechargeable batteries. The case’s 2” long Carabineer is excellent quality and the 2.5” wide belt loop is double stitched into the nylon case. The camera pocket zipper opens in a manner that will assure the camera will not fall out when you open it to retrieve your camera if the case is on your belt or hanging from the Carabineer.

Perfect for Sony Cyber-shot

Case Logic TBC-402 Camera Pouch

The 402 is a little larger than the 302. It would also work great for the Canon A1400. I am currently using several to hold USB/Charging cables and wall outlet adapters for Olympus point and shoot cameras – SZ12 and VR-340. (Side note - I use a Case Logic DCB-302 for the camera, extra battery, extra memory card, and cleaning cloth for each of these two cameras. The DCB-302 is an entirely different case than the TBC-302). If you are looking for a camera pouch for a Canon SX160 or SX170, or a Nikon L620, definitely take a close look at the TBC-403. Does not fit Sony DSC-RX100M2

I bought this b/c another review said that it fit the Sony RX100 perfectly. However, I didn’t realize that the RX100M2 is slightly thicker than the first version (RX100). My camera doesn’t fit so I had to send it back. It looks like a great case and plenty durable, but don’t get it if you have the Sony RX100M2.

Just the right size case

The pocket on the front is large enough to hold an additional memory card and a second battery, so I always have everything I need to take photos. And the clip allows me to attach it to my purse - I always need to have a camera with me.

Decent case for the money

I bought this case for my PowerShot D30 and it is a tight fit, but likely to keep my camera well protected. I like the clip and belt loop, and with the camera strap hanging outside the case (it won’t fit inside) I have 3 ways to carry it. It seems well made and durable. The zippered pocket is big enough for a battery and a couple of memory cards, but depending on how big your camera is it may not be too useful.

Great Point & Shoot Carrier

I don’t like reviewing things immediately to allow time for their operating characteristics in different environments to be observed. I’ve had this one for about a month or two now and after lots of use, it’s still going good. It’s a perfect little case for carrying the average modern point & shoot camera and its accessories. Not a hard shell case, but there is enough padding to keep a fall from waist height from being catastrophic to the contents. Pocket on the front is spacious enough to fit a battery or two and an SD card case. Zipper is easy to activate quickly to catch (reasonably) quick shots. The loop and carabiner are also strong enough for the job, something that I’ve found lacking from other cases in the past.
