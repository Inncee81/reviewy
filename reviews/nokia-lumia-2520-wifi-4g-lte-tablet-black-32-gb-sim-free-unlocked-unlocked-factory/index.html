Nokia Lumia 2520

As most people have commented, this is an excellent tablet, and the only caveat is the Win RT. If you can live with MS app store, then this is a real deal. Since there are enough details about typical aspects of this tablet, I’ll write few words that some of you may fine helpful (I had to search very hard, but did not find this information anywhere).1. This is essentially an unlocked AT & T/T tablet in US. On T-Mobile network, only 3G will work (missing the 1700⁄2100 MHz bands). But in Europe and most other countries it will cover a pretty good portion of LTE bands (going by the specs here). So, if you’re looking to travel with this, check the LTE frequency bands used in that country before heading out.2. I activated this for StraightTalk (Walmart), using AT & T option (the starter kit comes with both AT & T and T-Mobile sims). The sim size you need is “micro”, but don’t need to buy it separately unless you don’t plan to use an activation kit like the one mentioned above. The tricky part of the final step of connecting the tablet to the broadband network; when you select the broadband icon on your Networks panel, and select “Connect” it asks for an APN name (go figure, I guess). After searching the web for a long time, I finally hit a support article on the StraightTalk website. The name to use is “RESELLER”. This worked fine for me. If you use a different service plan/carrier, this could be different.3. This tablet has Micro USB - A/B ports (first I could not figure out what it really is). This means you can use an external USB3.0/2.0 HD with this tablet (I tried it and it works). Also, you can use this tablet as an external HD with any PC (tried this as well). It’ll work as USB 2.0 as well (plenty of speed, if you ask me)Finally, the graphics are awesome. No lag what so ever, beautiful colors. No heat at all (at least I did not feel any). Overall, this is a terrific device for the $$.Update: This actually works on T-Mobile network (4G LTE). So, my earlier comment was inaccurate (sorry). I don’t get a strong T-Mobile signal where I live, so it just takes longer to find the LTE network.
