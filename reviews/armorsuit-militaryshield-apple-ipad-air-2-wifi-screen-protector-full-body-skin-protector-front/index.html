Great protection but be patient

I’ve used Zagg’s Invisible Shield for a long time and this product is almost exactly the same. If you haven’t applied this type of skin on a device before, prepare for some headaches. It really takes patience and attention to detail to get the fitment right, especially around the speaker grills on the iPad Air 2.The trick is to use the included spray liberally. This will prevent fingerprints and will allow you to position the shield before final application with the squeegee. For the edges of the skin, use the warmth of your hands to work out the spray until the adhesive begins working.It took me over 30 minutes to apply just the back, which is quite a long time but the fitment was just perfect. There were some micro bubbles under the shield but they disappear after a day or two. Just be sure to work out the big bubbles when the skin is still wet, otherwise you’ll have a nightmare on your hands.I didn’t apply the screen protector because I don’t like how it feels to the touch. A glass shield would feel better but would cost many times the price of this full body shield. Overall, it’s a great product but be patient when applying it.

ArmorSuit Military Shield ipad Air 2 Screen Protector.

No this is a screen protector. I have this on my Gear watch. This is so much easier to apply then those ballistic glass ones. With this, you just clean your hands and screen and then spray the screen and the back of the film to make it easier to maneuver around. It might take a few tries but once it is in place for a minute or so, you squeegee the bubbles out. Then let it sit for 24 hours to completely dry. My only concern is the film for the back of the tablet. It is much bigger and you would have to fold it over and trim it. I left mine off since I use a cover for the tablet. Highly recommend this over the other ones unless you are an expert of not getting air bubbles or dust specs.
