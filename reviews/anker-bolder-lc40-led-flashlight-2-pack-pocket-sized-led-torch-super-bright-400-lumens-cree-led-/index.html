Solid. Seems well made

Nice simple flashlight. This Anker Bolder LC40 is a no frills, solid flashlight for a decent price. It is nice and heavy and has a lanyard that can serve as a wrist strap. The beam is fixed and does not zoom. This is not a bad thing because the LC40 produces a bright inner circle, surrounded by a larger less focused outer circle. It looks to me like a nice compromise between zooming in and out.The 2 things I noticed that was not so good were both related to the end. The cap has to be tightened all the way down for the flashlight to operate. If I loosen the end cap about 15 degrees (like going halfway between 12:00 and 1:00 o’clock on a clock), the light turns off. That could just be my 18650 battery, though it works fine in another flashlight. A lot of flashlights have special edges on the light end on barrel. This is supposed to be for breaking a window in case of an emergency. With the LC40, the sharp edges are on the tail end. After screwing the end cap on a off about 4-5 times for testing, the web of my hand between the thumb and forefinger is sore from where the edges have contacted my skin.Overall, I am happy with the light. It seems well made.

And the good thing about this flashlight is that you don’t have to turn it off and then back on again, just tap the button and it goes to the next mode. Very cool. You can buy a nicer #18650 rechargable battery (Olight ORB-186P26 works great) and get a much bright beam, or use 3 AAA batteries.
