Product Reviews

my Sumo has been a great workhorse. Why workhorse

As with many of the reviewers here, my Jumping Sumo stopped jumping soon after I received my Sumo. However, other than the lack of ability to go airborne, my Sumo has been a great workhorse. Why workhorse? Because I used my Sumo Bot for teaching purposes. One of my 4th grade classes spent all year studying drones. Now, technically, Sumo isn’t really a drone, since it doesn’t go airborne (and even when it was jumping, it’s technically not an airborne robot). However, my students decided that the most useful robot around school would be one that could deliver papers from classroom to classroom. So Sumo was used extensively to transport papers on a variety of student-built contraptions. Being able to control Sumo from a tablet was very convenient, as we’re an iPad classroom. In addition, some of the contraptions were quite heavy or had a great deal of drag on them, but Sumo still prevailed, so the rolling motor is quite a workhorse and I was very impressed. My students were also able to start programming Sumo using the Tickle app. Overall, other than the non-jumping issue, I was quite happy with Sumo and am considering purchasing a second Sumo for my classroom for next year. I’m hoping to be able to get an educational discount or something similar, as I’m working on a teacher’s salary, and I purchased my first Sumo (and Rolling Spider) for my class using my own funds.
