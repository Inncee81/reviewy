Like many new TVs

I bought this cable to connect a computer to a 1080p HDTV. Like many new TVs, mine has a VGA (aka “pc”) input, but I wanted to to keep everything digital so I needed to connect to one of the TV’s HDMI ports (VGA is an analog connection). This cable works great and going form my computer’s DVI port to HDMI means there is no analog to digital conversion going on.The cable itself is thick and sturdy and has solid connectors. My TV is mounted on an articulating wall mount and is pulled away from the wall on a regular basis (out for TV watching and against the wall for computer use). Have had no problems with the cable connectors coming loose. Quality product, excellent value. Don’t spend tons of money on name brand cables, this cable works great. I also got a 2’ HDMI cable for around $3 to connect my cable box to the TV and it is working out just fine.If you are looking to use an HDTV as a computer monitor, I would highly recommend getting a 1080p TV. I was told that I would not be able to tell the difference between 720p and 1080p on a 32” screen, but as it turns out that depends on how close you are sitting. For viewing distances >5 feet you may not be able to tell the difference, but I assure you there is a BIG difference when sitting 4-5 feet away looking at a screen full of text. I returned my 720p and the 1080p I replaced it with is nice and sharp. Look great!

Works great and can’t beat the price!

I needed a cable for a video card that has DVI-I output to pump the signal to a TV for maintenance such as loading ESXI for VMWare and changing BIOS settings. This cable did the trick without any issues. The cable is very well built and has very good strain relief. The length was more than adequate for my use. For the price this cannot be beat. Just be sure that you need a DVI-D (digital) cable, and not a DVI-A (analog). I think people that typically rate this cable and others like it with 1 or 2 stars for not working do not know what they need. You have to have a DVI-D (digital) or DVI-I (digital + analog) to be able to use this. HDMI is a digital format so your video card has to be able to output digital, otherwise you would need a analog to digital converter. I considered buying a adapter instead of the cable but for a few cents more I got the cable. I doubt I will ever need this cable for anything else though so it was purchased for a very specific work need.
