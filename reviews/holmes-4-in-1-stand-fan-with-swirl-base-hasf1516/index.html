Product Reviews

Used to be a quiet fan in the past, but no longer

I have one of these fans that I purchased maybe six years ago and love it due to the durability and how quiet it was. It’s still working, but I decided to order a new one before my old one eventually dies just so I’ll have it on hand. (That’s how much I really liked this fan!) I have read about the cap problem on the newer versions of this fan, but I read a great solution someone provided on here, which was to replace the cap with an 8 mm reverse thread nut. So, before I even bought the new fan, I ordered the replacement nut online. (Cost me about $11 for five of them, from the UK.) And, lo and behold, the nut fit when I got the fan, so I was psyched. However…this new fan is too loud for me. (I will admit I like it really quiet, else I can’t sleep with it.) As I stated, I still have my old fan, so it’s easy to compare the two. They look exactly alike (except for color), but the motor is really amped up on the newer version. In a side by side test, the low setting on this new fan is at least as loud/fast and the second setting on my old fan. Now, maybe this won’t bother most of you, but I can’t sleep with it. The second setting on the new fan is WAY loud, and forget about the third setting. Because I bought this specific fan because of its quietness…and because I went out of my way to buy a special nut to replace the cap which is now known to break…I am very disappointed. It would be okay to use on the first setting in the daytime, but as I said, the motor on this one is different that it used to be. Admittedly, maybe some of you would like the extra power. But, no longer considered a quiet fan.

Very quiet, but poorly constructed

This fan is very quite however, the nut holding the fan blade onto the motor shaft is horribly made. after 6 hours of use it had broken causing the fan blade to come loose inside the housing. I had Amazon send a replacement fan thinking it was just 1 bad part. The replacement one made it 2 days before the fan blade nut broke. The problem is the fan blade nut is made entirely of plastic (previous Holmes fans have used a metal nut surrounded by plastic for aesthetic appeal.) the fan motor shaft gets very hot during use and I guess that is what causes the fan blade nut to break. I really did like the fan and even tried going to a large hardware store to find a nut that would fit I could get nothing to work it is larger than 8mm and smaller than 9mm it also does not fit using any American sizes. I went with a Lasko fan and so far am enjoying that fan

I would have gave it a five star, if …

I would have gave it a five star, if not for the plastic capnut that holds on the fan blade. It broke apart within a couple of hours of use. I bought three units. I had to buy special hardware. I bought M8-1.25mm pitch reverse thread nuts plus ordinary 1⁄4 inch washers. I could not find the nuts at my local hardware store. I had to order them online. Holmes should use a metal nut and washer instead. It would eliminate the capnut breaking up, rendering the unit useless.After reassembling the fan blade assemblies, I have had no problems with these fans. They have decent airflow. The swirl base makes them stabile, and easy to move around.
