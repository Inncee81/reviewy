A dramatic, eye catching photo can mean dramatic profits. Getting a well exposed photo is extremely important. Photographers often turn to a flash to help light the scene. The problem is that flash can cause harsh reflections or glare spots or create deep shadows that distract viewers. This little, portable universal external flash diffuser will prevent those problems when using a flash. Simply put the unit over the camera’s flash, point and shoot. The light will be softened and your photos will be well exposed, without the harsh glare spots.

Inexpensive But Effective Miniature Soft Box

If you’ve made the decision to use an external flash to replace the useless pop-up flash, you’re already well on your way to improving the lighting in your photographs. The Opteka Soft Box takes it even a step further to diffuse the light softly to reduce any harsh glare and sharp shadows. It’s made of a stiff fabric that can conform to the flash head and is cinched with a Velcro strap to keep the soft box from falling off of the flash head. The fabric can be crammed into a corner of your camera bag without being damaged and pulled out and re-applied when you need it. A worthwhile investment, particularly for portrait photographers on the go.

Quick and simple diffuser

When some friends of mine asked me to shoot their wedding, I purchased this very affordable and portable diffuser. It did a great job, and I’m very happy with the results I got. It’s still a bit harsh in close-up shooting, but not near as much as a bare flash and it works great for wider or distant shots. I’m debating doing some white balance tests to adjust some skin tones a little, but I’m very happy with the lighting and exposure. The material is very thin, particularly on the side panels, and as a result it feels a little flimsy and sags a bit while mounted on the flash head. However, it folds flat for easy storage and portability in a camera bag. I’d like to see a future version with more rigid sides that close off gaps and make it sit a little straighter.

Sloppy fit and difficult to store

A Velcro strap is required to be threaded through a pair of loops on each of the four tabs and a rubber band must be slipped over the head of the flash. The length and width of the tabs are slightly too big and prevent snugging the Velcro strap around the head of a Sunpak Digiflash 3000. The diffuser has a tendency to slip and drop out of position despite the rubber band. Although it folds down nicely you must unthread the Velcro strap in order to do so. This makes it a little cumbersome to setup and store back in your camera bag.
