Product Reviews

Plain, simple and works great!

My wife and I like to freedive as well as snorkel and we’ve read in a few places that snorkel flexibility is a nice feature. Neither of us could really figure out why, but at under $15 this seemed to be a good way to test it. It’s hard to describe. Perhaps it creates less drag as the dive happens or perhaps it’s because this model doesn’t have extra purge valves and splash guards that create turbulence. Either way, the Cressi Gringo was a nice change from what we had been using.As the other reviews state, the lack of those extra features also means that there’s less to break or fail. This snorkel just works.If I had to comment on the negatives it would be that it could have been 1 inch longer and the loop that attaches it to a mask could have been easier to set. It wasn’t so much a problem for my wife because she just took it off and replaced it with one made of neoprene that she got for her long hair, but it was quite a pain for me to get back on one I had threaded the band to my mask through.Despite those two things, you just can’t go wrong for something like this that works and is available at a great price.

Durable Snorkel

Although this was a decent snorkel for a beginner, it was a little too short for my needs. I purchased it for spearfishing, but ended up borrowing a friend’s snorkel after a few hours because I kept getting water in mine. It is made of good quality materials and I would recommend it for basic snorkeling, but if you are doing anything more than that, consider getting one with the attachment on top to prevent water from leaking in.

Great with one caveat…

This is a nice, light snorkel that has a smaller sized mouthpiece than other adult models I’ve looked at. It is comfortable to use. It does not, however, come with a sliding elastic clip to attach to your mask as the description states. What you see is what you get. You have to either tether it to your mask or goggles with the headband on your mask or goggles or provide your own tether to hook them together and keep the snorkel upright. After experimenting a bit I found that I could simply run the headband (which is very thin) from my prescription goggles through the elongated loop on the snorkel. Wider bands could be problematic. Otherwise this is a good product and a good value.
