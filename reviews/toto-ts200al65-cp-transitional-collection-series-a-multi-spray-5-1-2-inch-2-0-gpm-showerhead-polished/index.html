Best shower head in the history of shower heads.

Kinda expensive, but can’t say enough good. Heavy, smooth action–no hint of plasticky cheapness. No faddish wiggle-waggles or glittery bumpy-skunk settings you use once and shiver away from after. With factory flow reducer, this head provides an amazing compromise of water-stinginess and acceptable wetting. I mean, that is what we shower for, right? Getting wet. There’s a soft, wide-flow rain setting, a nice narrower massage, and a economy wrap-you-entirely-in-stinging-mist-while-using-four-drops-of-water setting. For full economy, you also get a quick navy-style pause. But here’s my advice: get a pair of needle-nose pliers and remove the flow control. Then, spring for an adjustable flow-control like the KES Brass Shower Flow Control Valve, K1140B3, for under $10. Then you have the killer combo. Guilt-free, environmentally-responsible 2-5GpH* showers for every-day use, and on special occasions, flip the valve open a skosh and treat yourself to the full 7GpH* thumping massage, painful needly mist, or wet, wet, WET rain setting. Aaah… baby. Keep an eye open for refurb’s to save another $10 or so.

I’ve been doing a lot of research on shower heads as I’ve been trying to replace one that formed a crack. I started out with the Delta H2OKinetic, which by the way is REALLY good. And not just really good for $20, but it’s by far one of the best shower heads I’ve ever used. At 1.85 gpm, it had the most awesome water pressure and it was a wonderful shower. I actually looked forward to my shower every day. Unfortunately, my girlfriend told me that it was horrible for her and it was too much pressure - like “bullets hitting my head” she would say. Anyhow, this led me to this shower after reading all the different reviews online.This shower head sucks. I live in a fairly newly constructed house with good water pressure, and this shower head had absolutely no pressure. On the setting with all the jets open, the water couldn’t even shoot out straight and just drooped coming out. I had to use the setting where the middle jets weren’t on and then it gave an okay shower. It was very light pressure, but it was good coverage. The massage mode was more pressure, but not really that great (when compared to the pressure of the Delta), and the mist was cool, but also made the water rather cold. The water restrictor that came with it was slightly bent, maybe that was some of the issues with the pressure, but if you do the math at 2 gpm, and the number of nozzles and no special technology like the Moen’s immersion, or Kohler’s Katalyst, the pressure just can’t be that great with this shower head.I ended up getting the Kohler Forte Katalyst 2gpm, and it is fantastic (will be writing that review next)
