Works for the Triton

I recently bought a Triton (2000012033) Stove and ultimately decided that I needed a carrying case to go with it. I scoured all around various web sites to see if this bag would fit since my model number is not listed in this product’s description. The best I could find was some other reviews that said that yes it would fit. So I took a chance and was pleasantly surprised when the bag arrived and the stove did fit in it. Although there was not much extra room to spare on top. So yes this bag does work for the Triton stove.

Good addition to our tent camping setup

I used to keep my stove in a plastic bin with a bunch of other camping stuff, but as that got more and more filled over the years, I bought this to make some room in the other bag.My stove (Perflect flow, older model - not sure of the number) just fits in the bag and it’s a little snug puling the zipper around the corners. Not a bad thing, but the first time I put the stove in, I was worried about the zipper seam pulling. But the case has a stretched a little over time and now it fits perfect.I also like that you can keep two propane tanks with the stove, instead of trying to keep track of them.

Sturdy

Plenty of room for the coleman stove - and even stacks of newspaper for the fire on our camping trip. This is made of synthetic leather - appears to be well constructed of naugahyde with a sturdy zipper which glides smoothly. There is an inner divider of fabric so the top of your grill does not get scratched.Easily holds two propane tanks on each side.

It fits juuuuust right.

Haven’t used it in the “wild” yet, but it fits my Triton 2 burner perfectly. Maybe too perfectly, as there is zero room for any “extras” like an oven mitt or matches, etc. - things that would make sense to pack it with. I do like the pouches for propane cylinders, that’s a nice touch. Will check back in after a season of camping to inform of sewing quality, etc.

Great Case

Everything about this case is heavy-duty; the thick vinyl fabric is finished in a faux-leather grain and has a mesh fabric lining that makes it impervious to a puncture or tear except with the sharpest of instruments; while the zipper is plastic, it too is hefty and operates smoothly across the entire face of the case and my only (very minor) comment here is that I wish that the pull was larger.The handles appear to be adequate for the weight of the stove with a couple of propane bottles thrown in and the handles are finished nicely inside the seam which demonstrates that some care and craftsmanship went into the design and construction of the entire case. However, keep in mind that this case is not padded so while it will protect the stove against scrapes and bumps, avoid big crashes if you can.Though I use the propane tank off of my BBQ with my stove, I tested the pockets with the propane bottles that I carry for my lantern and had no problem with the fit. There is one point that I want raise about fit: out of the box the material is going to be stiff and it may be a bit clumsy fitting your stove for the first time so you have to give the case a chance to relax and break-in before you pass judgment. Another point about compatibly; Amazon’s technical info is often incomplete or outdated, so I suggest that you visit the manufacture’s web site before you order anything that is not universal.Now my pet peeve…when you review a product like this, please identify the model of the item that you are trying to fit/use with the product that you are using. It doesn’t do anybody any good when someone only writes “doesn’t fit” and leaves out what it is that doesn’t fit. And I’m amazed that some people rate a review like that “Helpful”! For the record, our stove is a Coleman 5469 series and it fits perfectly.After toting our stove around in its original box for a couple years, buying this case was long overdue. If your stove is compatible, you won’t regret getting this case.
