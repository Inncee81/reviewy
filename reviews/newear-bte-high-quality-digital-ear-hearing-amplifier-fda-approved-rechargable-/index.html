Let there Be Sound!

I had a professional hearing exam and was told I needed hearing aids to boost high frequency response in both ears. I declined to buy a set of $4,000 hearing aids.Instead I went on Amazon.com and ordered a single NewEar BTE Digital Hearing Amplifier for about $50. The results were so dramatic I immediately ordered a second one. I wear both with the volume turned down to the first level, they work every bit as good as the audiologist’s in-office demonstration of what I should expect from his $4,000 product.It will take a little getting used to for wearing them to be completely comfortable, but now the refrigerator is making all sorts of noises instead of just an indistinct dull whirring noise. I can actually hear the refrigerator fan when it is running.For about a $100 a whole world of new sound has opened up to me. Splashing of water in the sink produces almost musical tones although the simple tearing of a piece of paper is now a sound of almost indescribable violence. The clinking of dishes is sharp and pronounced, something I’m going to have to get used to.Thanks to Amazon.com I saved myself $3900 and can hear things I have been missing for years. The NewEar BTE Digital Hearing Amplifier is a definitely a great buy.

Good product for the right situations

I have a diagnosed hearing loss and my issue is specific to the low end range of voices. Put 2 people both 10 feet away from me in a meeting room and I can hear one person just fine, but if the other person speaks softly or in a lower tone, I will miss about half of what they say. I really need either hearing aids or a stapedectomy, but both are very expensive.I got this to wear in meetings a work. It is basically an amplifier. It is not great in noisy places, but in a quiet conference room where voices are the only sound, it does its job. I only got one, and I realized my other ear seems to compensate and hear less, like my brain is trying to process all the sound from the amplified ear. It would probably be more balanced with two of these.Some other reviews mentioned a hissing noise. I heard it when I cranked up the volume, but I found the lowest volume setting to be fairly loud and did not need to turn it up beyond that level. If you needed the loudest setting, I could see how that could be annoying.This device is conspicuous. People will notice you are wearing it, unlike more expensive hearing aids. So if you have an issue with that, you may want to look elsewhere.Bottom line, for the purpose, it is a good product that does what it says. It is not really a long term substitute for hearing aids or surgery, but can bridge the gap in some situations.

Exceeds expectations

We were looking for something to aid with my husband’s hearing loss since he had lost 50% hearing in both ears due to nerve damage from chemotherapy. Of course, ENT specialists wanted to sell him hearing aids for up to $5000! Since we are unsure of his prognosis he refused to pay that much money for something he might only be using for a short period of time. I was looking for something that would be easy for him to handle, not too small but with good amplification & not too cheap…because you get what you pay for, but not too expensive either because he would need one for each ear, & so we could have the TV at a normal level & we wouldn’t have to shout for him to hear what we were saying. The reviews for this amplifier were very helpful & mostly positive. I wasn’t sure how long the batteries would last, but he has had this for almost a month & we have only changed batteries once & he wears it all day. Of course, you do have to remember to turn it off when you are not wearing it. The amplification is very good & many times he has to turn it down if there are a lot of people talking along w/TV, etc. And he has to turn them off when going to a movie theatre! Comfort level is excellent; he tends to forget he is even wearing them. I would definitely recommend this product to anyone to try before spending thousands buying conventional hearing aids.Paula K.
