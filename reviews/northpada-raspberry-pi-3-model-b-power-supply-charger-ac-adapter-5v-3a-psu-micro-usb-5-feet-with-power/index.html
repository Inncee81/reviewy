Good, accurate power ratings with a toggle switch, but I wish it had a power light

I own two of these. One I purchased to power a Raspberry Pi 3b, and the other I purchased to power a home-built electromagnet (my wife is a science teacher). For the Pi, the adapter just works. Not much to say there. But for the electromagnet, I was pleased to find that my calculations for expected current were only off by about 2%, meaning the voltage provided by this supply is very close to 5V true. The electromagnet only draws 1.5A, so I can’t speak to the maximum amperage. Overall it seems to be a reliable power supply. Add the clicky power switch, and you’ve got a winner.My only complaint is that it’s a little difficult to tell what state the clicky switch is in. It would be nice if the switch had a built in LED. This isn’t really necessary for the Pi, though, since the Pi has its own power LED. Just kind of stunk that I had to put one on the electromagnet, particularly since the electromagnet doesn’t expose a nice place to put one. :(
