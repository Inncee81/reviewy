    Product Description

The Brinno TLC200 is a new class of camera designed to make HD time lapse photography simple, yet it has the flexibility to change lenses and custom program time intervals for the serious time lapse movie maker. The TLC200 has advanced power management, providing months of battery life for extended duration capture periods. It even has an optional external shutter release for creating stop action movies (model ATS100 Shutter Release Cable, sold separately). The 1.44 inch LCD preview screen allows for accurate targeting of subjects and precise framing of scenes. The interest in time lapse photography has been growing steadily in recent years, as shown by the vast numbers of time lapse movies being posted to video sharing sites. In the past the user had to use an expensive digital camera making the camera unavailable for normal use while making the movie; but not any longer. To use the TLC200, simply turn it on, set a time interval, aim the camera and wait for the video to finish. The TLC200 can be mounted on a tripod or simply set on a flat surface. An optional wide angle lens is also available (model ATL045, sold separately). Output format: AVI (video), JPEG (still image mode using optional shutter line). Resolution: 1280 x 720, 640 x 480.Field of view: 59 degrees. Focal length: 36mm (35mm equivalent). Focus distance: 29.5 inches (75cm). Minimum time interval: 1 second to 24 hours. Memory storage: 2GB SD card, included (supports up to 32GB). LCD screen: 1.44 inch TFT LCD. Power source: batteries, 4xAA (included) or DC in 5 volts (micro USB connector, not included).

Very Nice, Simple Camera

I have used the TLC200 for a couple of week so far and really like it. I have wanted a simple way to take time lapse videos since seeing some in early Disney programs. Since my time as a USAF weather forecaster, I have been interested in cloud development and this camera is great for that. Setting up the camera takes a few button pushes because of the very limited number of buttons, but the menu is easy to learn in about 5 minutes. Simple multiplication and division is enough to set the image capture rate and display frame rate. You will develop a feel for the best settings in just a few short trial runs. Storing digital video sequences on a computer is very simple, especially if the memory IC fits directly in your computer, as mine does. One of these days, I’ll try the other two functions supported by the camera: stop motion photography and motion-cued video. I was able to order batteries for it directly from SAFT on the internet at low cost.It is a great buy and a lot of fun..

Love It - Time Lapse Made Simple

I really love this camera. It makes time lapse simple and it is almost impossible to make mistakes with it. Just load batteries and an SD card and press record. Pictures come off of the SD card stitched together in movie format, ready to play. I can’t believe how easy it is to use.You can change settings on quality and how often it takes pictures. Batteries last for 6 weeks at a time when I have it set to take pictures every 30 seconds. A 32 GB SD card lasts at least 6 weeks as well. I am sure that the batteries and SD run down faster when taking pictures more frequently and at different resolutions, though, so don’t use my experience as a guide unless you have the same settings.
