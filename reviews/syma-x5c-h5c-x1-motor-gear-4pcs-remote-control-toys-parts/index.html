Buy this now if you own a X5C, whether you need them now or not

Almost all of my smaller quads get flown in the house and I’ve never had any problems. The one and only time I decided to take my X5C outside and send it way up in the air for fun, I had it about 60’-80’ in the air and all of the sudden it just tumbled straight down out of the sky and smashed on the street. I don’t expect anyone to believe this but other than a few scuffs there was nothing broken, not even a prop. It turns out one of these little gears (the stock ones, not these) had slipped off of its motor, basically shutting down that whole arm. I ordered these and now I’m up and flying again. If you own a X5C I suggest you just go ahead and buy these now whether you need them or not. They’re cheap, and the first time you lose one like I did you’ll be glad you already have one on hand instead of having to wait to get them shipped in.

Perfect fit and fast delivery!

If you own a quadcopter at some point you are going to need a set of these extra gears. for whatever reason my Syma X5C Quadcopter was designed so that the gears are exposed? These gears are super tiny and fit snugly on the end of the motors shaft and transfer the motors power to the larger gear that rotates the blades. If you crash it, which you will especially when first learning to fly and control one as I did when a gust of wind decided to blow 30 ft against the side of a house which knocked one of these little gears right off, they are so small that the chances of finding it are slim. So do yourself a favor and buy a set of these. It fit perfectly on my quad and I had it up in the air good as new in seconds of getting them in the mail! I would also like to point out that I tried purchasing these gears from another seller first who sells them cheaper but what I failed to realize is that they are shipped from china and I am still waiting for them to arrive, so it was worth paying a little more for them from this seller becuase they showed up in a couple days and not a couple weeks!

Works some what for the DRONIUM ONE.

I got these for my dronium one. I realized they were for the syma quads but I knew these would work for my quad. Although the hole in these were just a tad too big for my dronium one, it still matched the propeller gear. I just used a little super glue. Be sure the glue doesn’t reach the teeth on the sprocket and doesn’t run down into the motor. So far the glue hasn’t broke off but I’m expecting it too. Haven’t really thought of a permanent solution yet. But works good so far. These get 5 Sara because they were made for a different product and yet they still did the trick. And great price.

Also works for the Lishitoys L6052

Exact replacement for a 9T pinion used on the Syma X5C and JJRC H5C and will work also on the Lishitoys L6052 quadcopter. Please note, while this pinion gear will work perfectly on the L6052, the X5C motors are not compatible with the L6052. I realize these are just pinion gears being sold, I wouldn’t want anyone to assume that the motors with these 9T pinions were also interchangeable, which they are not for the L6052 and X5C.
