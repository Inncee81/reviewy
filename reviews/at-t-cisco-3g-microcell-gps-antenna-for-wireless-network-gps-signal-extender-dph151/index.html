AT&T and Cisco have worked together to develop a robust femtocell solution for the home and home office: the AT&T 3G MicroCell. The AT&T 3G MicroCell acts like a mini cellular tower right in the home. It connects to AT&T’s network using existing broadband Internet service. With AT&T 3G MicroCell, customers receive improved cellular signal performance for both voice calls and cellular data applications. It can provide up to 5 bars of AT&T cell signal to your 3G and 4G mobile phones (including iPhone, Android, and Windows mobile).

Extremely cheap construction, unreliable connector.

I purchased this antenna to improve my ATT MicroCell installation. Simply getting it up onto a window was not effective, so I drilled a hole through the house wall in preparation of mounting it outside on a small shelf I fabricated out of sheet metal. When I tried to remove the antenna connector from the MicroCell, the connector pulled apart, leaving the outboard-most piece of the connector firmly seated in the MicroCell and the center conductor protruding from the cable. I am an old amateur radio op and know to never pull/tug a connector off of a rig by the cable, and did not do so in removing this connector. I simply grabbed the fat molded-rubber end piece and pulled straight out (it is a MCX-type push/pull fit, not threaded). I examined the broken connector and found no compression ring, marks, or sleeve.I was looking for a bargain when I bought this antenna and got bit in the behind in doing so. You get what you pay for, right? Anyway, avoid this device if you ever plan on having to unplug the antenna, or else fabricate something to aid removal.Also, while the antenna does have an MCX connector, the product photo shows a ribbed metal collar at the distal end of the cable … which is what seduced me into buying this unit … but that’s not what I received … I got the rubber collar version which delightedly does not let you see what’s under the covers. So I’m off to look for an alternative.
