What’s not to like?

This is a well-made novelty keyboard that has the flair of a classic but also has a white LED under each key and several nice moving lighting modes to really jazz it up. It has key switches that make an audible “click” past a certain point of key travel and feel very uniform and work very reliably. This keyboard has a woven cloth-covered USB cable reminiscent of the old power cords that appliances and machinery had many decades ago, and I think that is also a real plus. As others have said, it is heavy, with a metal base, and seems like it will last for many years. I suspect you will grow tired of it before you actually wear anything out. The key tops themselves are plastic and the chrome rings at their edges are plastic too, but this all seems completely fine.Everything here is great quality and I see no significant problems. Do be aware that the LEDs are for dramatic effect and do not actually light through the key tops; the key tops are completely opaque, so the LEDs create a light show and plenty of spillover lighting (and light the ceiling of the room too, to a degree) but not a gentle lit symbol through each key.If you want to be typing quietly at the highest possible speed, forget this keyboard. This is one of those items that keyboard fetishists and those who want their computer desks to pop with style might want. It’s fun and a perfectly good keyboard, but not for high-production work in a quiet work environment where coworkers will get irked at the old-time newsroom clatter you’ll be pumping out. Enjoy it, but you might need an alternative board at certain times.

It reminded you every time you pushed down on a key that you were writing in a real newsroom. The noise was intoxicating. This keyboard has the old-fashioned keys like a Royal that was older than the one I learned to type on, so it is even cooler. It also has a clicky sound that reminds me of what being a journalist used to sound like. So far, so good. I have only had it about a week.
