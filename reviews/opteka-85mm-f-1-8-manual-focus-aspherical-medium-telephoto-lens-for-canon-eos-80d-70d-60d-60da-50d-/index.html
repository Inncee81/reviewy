How can you not love this lens?

After doing a lot of research, I decided to give this lens a try. I have a Canon 7d and I wanted a nice portrait lens on the cheap. This is a manual lens. After attatching it to my Canon 7d, I set my dial to manual and started shooting. I was amazed at the quality of this lens! At 1.8, it gives very nice depth of field. As far as sharpness goes, it is very sharp. However, I think modern photographers have gotten use to image stabilization in their lenses and maybe their technique is not as strong when you using a manual lens. If you are shooting in low light and your hands are not steady, you will not get sharp images. Yes the focus ring is stiff, but it did not hamper my shooting in any way. I could not be more pleased with this lens and its performance. I have 3 Canon L lenses and I love them dearly, but this little gem has some unique magic to it. It will be in my bag for a while. Also, this lens is not the fastest, but for portrait work, it is awesome. If you need a lens for portrait work and you cannot afford a top branded lens, this is an excellent choice.Thank you for reading my review and happy shooting. :)

Updated to 5 stars

Updated Review:I had bought one of these awhile ago. Recently I purchased another one. For some reason construction seemed to be better or different. Originally the front element was very loose and seemed to wiggle in the case. Now it is all straight with no movement. The image quality seems to benefit a little by this.This biggest change is when I installed the ‘dandelion’ AF confirm chip from ebay (version 4). For about 20$ it adds the ability to use the focus confirmation when manually turning the focus ring (since this is a manual lens). This has made the usability infinity easier since I dont need to use live view. This combination makes it a great lens for portraits at a great cost.Original review:At 1.8 the lens is soft, and being 85mm the depth of field is shallow, making it hard to get a sharp image. At 5.6 the lens is sharp across the image. I uploaded two example photos cropped to 100%. The 12 sign is showing the lens at f5.6 on the right, against the canon 28-135 USM at 90mm f5.6. The eye is comparing the 50mm f1.8 on the left, against the 85mm on the right at f1.8. It also has some purple at 1.8 in strong light. For the price, its a good prime lens to have stopped down at 2.0 or 2.5.

You won’t find a better deal

If you are looking for a 85mm prime lens, this is the best deal you can find. It is completely manual, so be aware. You can purchase an AF confirmation chip and install in separetly. I have not done this yet, but I am considering it. I haven’t used it too much yet, but after first use, the images appear very sharp and produces quality photos.The focus ring is fairly stiff, but I see this as a positive. It is much easier to get a finely tuned focus this way rather than a very loose focus ring. It is consistently stiff throughout the focus range.The lens itself fit perfectly on my 70d, no issues there at all. It is compact, yet feels very sturdy and solid.Update: More than a year later, and I am still loving this lens. This is my go to lens for any portraits. The images are clear and sharp and provide beautiful bokeh. Highly recommend this lens. If you are still having any doubts….don’t, just buy it already!Again, for this price, you won’t find anything better. I am sure there are better 85mm lenses, but you will pay 3-10 times as much also.

Minimum necessary for minimum price

The lens performs what it needs to, and has quality where it needs to, but little else. The images are sharp and bright. However, you get what you pay for. The focus mechanism is VERY stiff at around 4-8 feet. Almost stuck stiff. Manual aperture ring is a nice touch for me personally. The minimum focus is claimed at 2.78 feet and I measured at about 4 feet. This longer minimum focus occurs across the entire range so the numbers printed on the lens are useless for measuring. That said, if you need an 85mm and have no money to spare, this will get the job done and the final product will be satisfactory - you’ll just get frustrated along the way.
