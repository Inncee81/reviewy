A Thorough Comparison Demonstrating APEMAN’s Great Capabilities

APEMAN 3D Glasses DLP Series Rechargeable Glasses Hi-Brightness/Hi-Contrast Compatible with All DLP 3D Projectors - 2PACKThese APEMAN 3D DLP Glasses were used for and are compatible with my ViewSonic PJD5255 3D projector (http://amzn.to/2rWp2SB).So, a while back I purchased two pairs of the ELEPHAS DLP link glasses (http://amzn.to/2sUnxcH) and I was truly impressed with their capabilities. Recently, I purchased two pairs of these APEMAN DLP glasses to compare performances of the competing brands. And, I don’t know, but I think I’m leaning toward APEMAN’s product as my preference, and I’ll share why.First, the ELEPHAS glasses are very lightweight (barely noticeable) and are flexible enough to fit over glasses. The lenses will dim the image while in operation, but really only minor dimming. Like, there’s hardly a difference between images with and without the glasses. They came fully charged with appropriate charging equipment and other accessories. I used them moderately for about four months (and, unknowingly, letting them rest in Stand-By mode instead of properly shutting them down) before the charge quit. After only 2 hours, each pair was fully prepared and operational. (It should be stated the accessories and charging capabilities are similarly featured with the APEMAN glasses.)In contrast, I will say the APEMAN glasses are bulkier and a little heavier than the ELEPHAS. Although the weight difference is barely noticeable, the thickness is of the APEMAN pair is quite obvious, which really wouldn’t seem like much unless someone requires an additional pair of prescription glasses. Yes, the APEMAN glasses DO fit over prescription glasses comfortably, but really only as comfortable as having two thick pairs of glasses a nose can allow. The lenses also dim the image even more than the ELEPHAS.So, why do I prefer APEMAN to ELEPHAS? Honestly, the bulk of the APEMAN works in favor for a better viewing experience. The ELEPHAS are wonderfully light and slim, but until I had tried the APEMAN, I hadn’t noticed just how much peripheral light the ELEPHAS pair had let in. Unlike the ELEPHAS, the APEMAN has a thick cover on each side of the glasses which almost completely blocks out any side light from entering my eyes.A benefit to the light reduction with the APEMAN glasses is an allowance for the eyes to focus only on the projected three-dimensional image, instead of receiving sources of light both through and outside the lenses. So, even though ELEPHAS has lesser dimming lenses than the APEMAN, the leaking light almost offsets the whole experience. In this way, APEMAN wins out by providing a more controlled viewing area which offers incredible clarity.Other bonuses to the APEMAN, however minor, come in a more conveniently placed Invert/Power button located on the side of the glasses as opposed to ELEPHAS’ placement of the same feature on the bridge of the glasses; and there’s also just an overall sturdier feel.Hey, you made it!
