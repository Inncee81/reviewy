Common defects, poorly bonded, and may damage your motherboard

I work in computer/cellphone repair and have fixed 200+ iPhone 5C devices. I bought this to fix a friend’s phone and it came in very nice packaging with a free screen protector, tools, and a nice note. I had my own tools and don’t really recommend using the provided ones. All of these side bonuses would be great if the actual product was. These screens are obviously not tested as well as screens from other brands which means the number of defective parts is probably 3x more than a higher quality one. Also, the adhesive they use to bond the glass to the plastic assembly is terrible and will separate even without you dropping your phone. The screen I used worked fine for 2 days and then the digitizer (touch screen) stopped working. I later found out that it wasn’t the digitizer on the part, but that the part shorted out the digitizer controller on the motherboard. This means that the phone was now useless and would never have touch functionality again. Don’t buy these screens unless you want to end up buying a different part or new phone down the road.
