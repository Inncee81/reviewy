Solid Rotary Tool Performance (Beware Of The Gradually Decreasing Warranty Period)

I’ve been using this Black & Decker rotary tool - which is the equivalent of the more familiar Dremel tool, for more than ten years now. The three speeds and ergonomics of this tool have always been wonderful, and fine home/residential use. I’ll concede that I have had three of these rotary tools fail, over the last ten years - most of those failures associated with the induction motor. Luckily, on each occasion Black & Decker was true to its warranty, and after determining that a repair was not possible - they quickly forwarded a replacement tool.You should be aware that my rotary tool was originally covered by a five year warranty. I noted on my last replacement tool, that warranty has subsequently been “narrowed” down to two years. Fortunately, I kept my original documentation, and Black & Decker has been willing to honor the warranty associated with the original tool purchase date (5-year). This tool works well, but has product durability limitations, and should work well enough for non-commercial use, but be aware of the now 2-year warranty (which will almost certainly slip to a 1-year warranty at some point in the future).

Solid tool but some quality control issues (which customer service remedied).

Works well but there were some quality control issues. The collet included with the “kit” was too small for the included attachments and was also jammed inside the collet nut. I was able to pop the jammed collet out of the nut by inserting the screw end of the mandrel attachment into the top of the collet and nut then resting the other end of the mandrel on my counter and VERY gently tapping the nut with a hammer. Good Product, Bad Ad

Two clicks past lowest setting it gets very very loud, just something to note but not an issue. They hover in the shades of grey in how they describe what you are going to get with this product. Not really 3 speed. It’s a variable speed dial with little white H, M, L letters painted on as their respective points. Which really is better, but I’m very curious as to why they advertize 3 speed. Also about this 30 accessory deal… Don’t think they have a right to call it 30 accessories. More like 4 accessories with 20 extra saw disks and 6 extra sandpaper rings. The two clamps are good though.My first use was to deal with my dog’s nails. She has never had them trimmed in her life and she weighs over 100 pounds so you can imagine. Anyways I sawed off the nail a couple cm in front of the quick with a disk cutting attachment and then ground it smooth with the grindstone attachment. For the purpose of cutting a dogs nails this would be difficult to use on dogs scared of noise. On it’s lowest setting it is very very quiet but its hardly enough umph to grind the nail down at least on a dog as large as mine. As I said before, after about two clicks of the variable speed dial up in speed, it gets very loud.For any other use this is a great dremel especially for the price. Plenty of power and in my opinion more reliable because of the coord. I only knock one star because I was majorly let down by the “30 accessories” though the value of the clamps plus the 4 accessories is more than the difference between this product and the one that is the dremel only so still a good deal.
