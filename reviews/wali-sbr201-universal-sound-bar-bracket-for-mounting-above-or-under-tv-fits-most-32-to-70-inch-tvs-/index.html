These worked to hang the VIZIO 38” Sound Bar under my older 43” Samsung LED TV. I originally purchased the EchoGear Sound Bar Mount, but it wouldn’t work with the Kanto Full motion Mount and the ridge on the Samsung TV. So I got the Wali Universal Brackets.The VESA hole pattern on the TV is 8” apart, while the mounting holes on the Sound Bar are 31” apart.I had to use spacers to lift the mounting brackets for the sound bar and the Kanto wall mount so they would clear the ridge on the base of the TV.I placed the Wali brackets under the Kanto brackets on the lower holes on the TV (and put an extra washer to compensate on the top holes) and angled the brackets outwards in an inverted ‘V’ to reach the sound bar holes. They matched up fine and I could leave the recommended 1” gap between the TV and speaker. I was able to thread the bolts through the brackets’ end holes and didn’t have to use the long slots in the brackets. I did have to go to the hardware store and get four M8-35mm bolts to work correctly with the brackets, spacers and TV.Wali Brackets work fine to hang the bar speaker under the TV. With two caveats:1. The speaker will swing from side to side like a pendulum if pushed on either end or I move the wall mount tot he side. Easy to put back in place, but mildly annoying.2. The brackets are flexible because they are relatively thin and have no stiffener. So, the speaker will move backwards if pushed from the front. It returns when released. If you turn the TV on its mount, the speaker bounces a little back to front because the brackets act like a spring.This is totally because they are attached on top of spacers at only one screw point on the TV. (If you have the wider spaced VESA mounting holes on your TV you may be able to use two holes per side which will eliminate this.)The brackets are plenty strong. They are versatile and will work for most sizes and applications.All in all I’m pretty pleased with the brackets and the results. Good price.
