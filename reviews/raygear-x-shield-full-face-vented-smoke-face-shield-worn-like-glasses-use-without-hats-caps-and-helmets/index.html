Walking into bitter cold wind is why I bought it. At 32° F is fine. At 16° F, shield gets too foggy. See workaround.

Just got it today and walked a few miles in 32° F with winds at 15 mph. Completely took the bite out of what would have been an unpleasant walk.Tried it with glasses and was not a problem, but you wind up looking through a lower part of the glasses lenses.Shield did not fog up at all, though there was some condensation on the lower half at the end of the walk.Wore it with a scarf and knit hat.Wind did not break through the vents.My nose was cold at the end of the walk, but no where as cold as it would have been without the X-Shield. Ordinarily I would have my nose covered with a scarf, but chose not to while wearing the shield.I was able to hear myself inhaling and exhaling throughout the walk more than usual, as the sound is directed towards my ears with the shield.Update: Weeks after my original test I went out in 16° F weather with wind of 17 mph and the results were not as good. Glasses really fogged up under the shield. I was just not getting enough air passing through to take away the moisture. Though it is likely I would have had issues with my glasses at that temperature anyway. Condensation blocked much of the mask, visually. Was bearable walking on sidewalks that I knew, but my vision was too blocked by the condensation on the shield to make it safe to walk in an area I didn’t know. I had to look out the side of one side that was clear, which is not a perfect solution. On the plus side, my face was no colder than the first time, which would have been great except for glasses and shield being covered with condensation from breathing.Update 2: By fashioning a nose apron out of a cut cereal bag (bag in a cereal box) I was able to direct all of my breathing downward and out of the shield. This worked in 11° F with 20 mph. wind, resulting in a fog-free shield.
