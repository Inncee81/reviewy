Meh…I guess it works.

For the price, it’s okay. Got this for my daughter (2.5 years old) so she could start playing with a camera. Only reasons I got it was because it had a viewing screen and it was at a good price point since she will likely break it. So far, she enjoys playing with it. I haven’t tried taking any outdoor pics with it and most of the shots she takes are very blurry (if she can manage to keep her fingers from blocking the lens. I’m an experienced photographer and this camera is quite terrible at taking indoor photos, but what can you expect at this price point and at 14.1 MP. See the attached photo. I and they were both moving but it was literally the best shot we had on her memory card. Picture quality compares to an old flip phone camera. Also, it does do videos but they are pretty poor quality. They have sound as well. For still pictures, you can use flash if you turn off “macro” located on the side of the camera. Took a while to find that -.-The batteries randomly pop out which is really annoying. We have a second child who is under one and loves to eat everything SO yeah.The one that got shipped to me was the red version. I would have rather had the black but it said color may vary so can’t really get angry about that. It is weird to me that the black flips up and the red flips sideways. The way the screen is attached seems flimsy and it will likely break off before anything else fails. Doubt these are actually vivitar…likely just cheap knockoffs with the name on the front.It does not come with a memory card (or batteries) so make sure you buy one if you end up purchasing this camera. I would not recommend it and would not buy again myself. But since we have it, she’ll use it till it breaks or until she’s old enough to appreciate a more expensive one.
