Product Reviews

Best sounding and fit of the bunch

Of all of the other blue tooth headphones I’ve tested in the $100 to $150 price range, these are the best sounding and have the best fit. For reference I only use Bluetooth ear buds for working out and use Beats Studio wireless over the ear headphones for around the house listening.Back to the ear buds. I’ve tried the Bose Soundsport wireless but the ear piece was heavy and it always felt as if it were falling out o my ear. I’ve tried Beats Powerbeats 3 but I don’t like the over the ear hook as it tends to just dangle after a while especially if I’m running or jumping rope. Both above mentioned sound good as long as the bud is in the ear. I’ve also tried the Monster iSport Superslim which sound the worst with tinny sound, but they have an OK fit.However the Jabra Sport Coach has a solid sound, good bass, clear but not tinny, and they stay in the ear very well. They also come with more size attachments than the others.My only complaint is I’m on my third pair in three years. It seems to know exactly when the warranty expires and they die right after a year. However, when shopping for this third pair I again tried all the above and returned all of them. The Jabra Sport is still the best in my book.

Just OK

The piece of junk fabric carrying pouch they ship with the product is shameful and offers no protection for a $120 pair of headphones. It came apart at the seams after two months. I contacted Jabra and they said they could not replace it but sent me a link to BUY a better case. Really, Jabra?Had to update the firmware right out of the box which was a pain. You have to download the update to a computer and plug the headphones in, run the installer, etc. I’m not sure why they can’t update wirelessly from the phone.Otherwise, decent wireless headphones and they’re easy to pair. The bass is less bassy than I’d hoped it would be but good enough for working out. Comes with different sizes of silicone and memory foam fittings, so you can pick the most comfortable size for you. Personally I feel that even the largest ones could be a little more snug.

Average sound, great noise isolation

I have mixed feelings about these. Sound wise with music they are lacking compared to other ear buds or headphones I own. The sound is not terrible. it is just not that great. Noise cancelling is great with these. very similar to my Etymotic 4P’s which also use passive noise cancelling as these sit more in the ear and block out most sound. I used these while listening to music and cutting my lawn on my tractor and I could barely hear the tractor.As for coaching, I have a tried a few of the exercises with the app and they are Ok but I prefer to follow routines like Focus T25 and PX90x. I get more motivation with those than I do with the coach.I did have a hard time finding the right fit ear pieces. I am currently using the middle size foam ones which seem to stay in place ok but they are not easy to put in. Once in, they are comfortable and stay in place for the most part. The cable does get a bit annoying at times as it is a bit short for me and tends to hit the collar of my shirt at times. The buttons are hard to adjust when you are wearing them since you cannot see what you are doing.As for Bluetooth, like every other pair I own, these also cut out from time to time which I find annoying. It is why I still own and want to use wired headphones.Overall, not bad. But not great.** I downgraded my star rating to two stars after using these a bit longer. First, battery life is horrible. I tried using these to listen to music during the workday but the battery won’t last more than 3 or 4 hours max. Second, sound quality is just not there. At louder volumes, these headphones just create a mush of sound and any detail is completely lost. Definitely not in my top 20 for music based applications. Third, pairing these with my Iphone has become a nuisance. While the actual pairing was easy, getting them to register on my phone takes two attempts every single time. I have to turn them on and then off and then on again. I have tried to manually select them from the Bluetooth area on my phone but they will not connect without repeating the process.Overall, a big eh…
