Product Reviews

Great receiver, awesome value

This receiver has met or exceeded all of my expectations. I used it with a 5.1 Polk speaker setup. There are connections for two subwoofers but you only need one.The sound output is great. The higher sounds are clear with no tinniness, and the bass sounds from the subwoofer really help bring the overall sound to life. Use the Auto Calibration to start, then use the Manual setup to adjust to your liking. I ended up increasing the volume of the rear speakers a lot so I can really hear the sound behind me.The inputs offer both HDMI for newer devices and A/V inputs for older devices.The only critique I have is that there was a quite a lot of setup necessary to get everything working properly. The HDTV setup was fine, just make sure that the input is set to ARC, if your TV offers it.The main difficulty I had with the setup was to get my Wii and Blu-ray player hooked up with the older video settings. First, when I plugged it in, nothing worked. Then, I got the video but no sound, then the sound but no video. I think the receiver may not identify the input at first. Try turning the receiver off and then on again to get it to recognize the input. Also, the input from the TV will be different for A/V input.Once I got both the sound and video working, the sound came through the speakers but only the front ones, not the rear. I tried changing all of the settings on the Speaker settings menu with no luck. The speaker pattern only listed options with two or three speakers so I thought I was out of luck. Then, I spotted a clue in the manual under the Audio Settings. It was the Sound Field. The default setting was 2 ch stereo. I changed it to Multi Stereo and TA DA! It worked great. I learned that there is a difference between Linear PCM (what the TV uses) and Dolby Digital (what the Wii and Blu-Ray player use), which is why the TV had been working but not the other inputs. So to sum up, the process took a long time, but in the end everything works exactly as I had hoped.I would definitely recommend this as a first time receiver to anyone interested in having surround sound for their TV, Blu-ray player, or video game system.

Excellent Receiver for medium size room

Very easy setup. I needed a unit to support the following equiptment: Sat (Directv) receiver, Xbox 360, Roku 2. As I have a Sony HD TV, the remote for the receiver powers both units. After connecting all the speaker wires for surround sound, I then connected HDMI cords to each unit, plugged them into the associated HDMI input ports on the receiver, then used one HDMI cord, plugged it into the “HDMI Out” port on the receiver, and to one of the HDMI input ports on the TV. Piece of cake! Using the remote, all I have to do is select “Game” for the Xbox, “Sat/Cable” for TV, or “BD” for the Roku. I have high speed internet, so streaming is terrific, oustanding HD and sound! Great little unit , sounds almost as good as the large Yamaha we have in the great room.Would highly recommend it especially for those with a modest budget like me.
