I had read reviews of TCL’s 2017 lineup of TVs and I decided to buy one when it was time to upgrade our old 1080p 52” TV. I settled on this 55” 4K LED TV with HDR. The price was amazing for the quality of the television. I must say the picture is superb. It will not disappoint in the least. I particularly like the way they have incorporated the Roku, which we already used, into the TV. It even scans for channels on your antenna and puts an icon on the Roku screen to quickly switch to the broadcast side. The picture is very watchable right out of the box but some tweaking makes it even more beautiful. Though I Searched for the smaller model, I ‘settled’ for this bigger one; 55” seems to be the only size available at the moment.What a delight … as good or better than anything I can remember seeing. Price was not too bad, considering the absolutely superb 4k video quality and technology, either. I agree with Review comments, regarding off-axis viewing … `seems to be a function of the LED technology, rather than peculiar to any manufacturer. But, on-axis … W o W ! … an absolute delight.At first startup, it detected available software updates … and installed them with no issues whatever. The ‘smart’ operating system operates nearly instantly, a noticeable improvement from my previous (Panasonic Viera).The remote control is deliberately sparse - a welcome alternative to common mega-controls. There’s a commonality to the approach across input Sources; put on your thinking-cap, get used to it and you’ll find it refreshingly stress-free upon familiarity.Though I have auxiliary speakers and often read disparaging comments about flat-panel TV speakers, I find the built-in speakers surprisingly (to me) Good. Especially when listening to just talking, I find myself omitting turning on the auxiliary speakers and just listening to the built-in speakers. I’m, also, surprised that, even in the LARGE room, I need not turn the built-in speakers volume up to more than 20 - 30%, usually. The speakers are located on the bottom and face the viewer/listener. As flat-screen TV speakers go, these strike me as the BEST I’ve heard!Would I re-buy it? … without a moment’s hesitation. Don’t you hesitate, either; if you’re disappointed, I’d be shocked.
