Product Reviews

Five Stars

Nice!

Nice 2 in 1 Computer

The tablet portion of this 2-1 is really a nice piece of technology. Thanks to the generous SSD drive and the 8 MB of RAM, this dude boots up from Off to usable mode in mere seconds. The touch screen is responsive and accurate. The colors pop on the screen and it is really clear and sharp. But, remember, this resolution on this small of a screen, everything is pretty small. 11 point fonts look more like a 5 or 6 point would appear on a normal laptop with less resolution for example.The keyboard is the part of the package that I find lacking. The mouse pad is decent, the keys are different but you adjust to them after a bit but this thing is flimsy. It was made perhaps a bit too thin in Samsung’s quest to make this unit extremely portable (and it is very light and portable). As I was setting the computer up - you know, changing all the privacy invading settings in WIndows 10 and such - my butt head cat stepped on the keyboard and bent it slightly! What is that thing with cats and keyboards, anyway? It bent back to flat easily enough, but that gives you an idea how thin and weak the keyboard is. I also believe the Samsung pen should be included with this computer but after reading the reviews on the pen, maybe it’s just as well it isn’t part of the package. Also, if you have the tablet attached to the keyboard while typing in your lap and need to move the unit, be sure to support the tablet or it might just flop off the keyboard, It happened to me twice before I got it into my hard head to remember to support it.The specs and speed of this 2-1 makes it a great alternative to a pricey Microsoft Surface. It does perform well and is quite fun to use, I’m just a little leery that the keyboard is going to last as long as the tablet. It would be nice if the keys were backlit, but I knew it wasn’t when I ordered it, so that’s on me. The included cover, that serves to support the tablet while attached to the keyboard is a nice addition that I appreciate because it also protects the tablet and keyboard from scratches while using or transporting.I don’t use graphic intensive or other heavy duty such programs, I’m mainly using MS Office programs and doing web browsing so I can’t speak to how well this M3 processor would handle a kick-butt application, but for every day computing needs it’s plenty powerful and fast enough in my opinion. All-in-all a nice product.

Battery life is awful

The battery life on this is laughable. “10.5 hours” is a complete lie and should be removed from the description. I had this PLUGGED IN into an aukey power bank with the brightness on the lowest setting plus one click, and with only light browser use, it died within 6 hours. 100 to 0, while plugged in. I could barely read the screen from how low I turned the brightness. When I had it at a regular brightness, the drain was on course to die within 3-4 hours (while plugged in).This problem is compounded by the fact that you cannot put this into sleep mode. It wakes up from sleep after about 15 seconds. So you essentially have to power it down any time you want to move it, or it’ll drain a ton of battery (even just sitting at the home screen under the lowest brightness setting)Just to note, I’ve had this laptop for only a couple weeks, and haven’t downloaded any battery hogs; this is not an issue related to software on my machine. Entirely a hardware issue.Returning this to amazon soon.

Combinnig it withTabPro pen was disappointing

Does its job as a PC really well. I give it 3 because i got much less out of the tablet aspect than I was hoping for. I bought the pen to go with it hoping that it would be nice to take notes with but its actually terrible. The pen does not write very smoothly, and sometimes my palm causes it glitch and give create large pen marks or jump to different parts of the page. Furthermore, I was initially disappointed at how the pen compared to Apple’s pen but even more disappointed when Samsung released the S-pen to go with their tablets and Galaxy Book, and not the TabPro S! I tried the S Pen at a basic electronics shop and it was significantly better than what you can do with the TabPros S. If you want a tablet PC and you care about how nice you can write with a pen buy something the S Pen is compatible with, not this.
