Product Reviews

Happy customer

Why in the world Garmin degraded their units to use regular glass instead of non-glare is beyond me. Obviously, in a vehicle, there is going to be a lot of glare. To me, a non-glare screen protector for my Garmin Nuvi 2589LMT was a no-brainer - my questions in ordering this particular one were, would it arrive in time to a trip I was taking, would it be easy to apply, would it stick, and would it make the screen too dark? I have been very pleased. It actually arrived earlier than I expected and I did get it in time for my trip. I was very careful in applying it, and it took a few tries to get it on without any significant bubbles, but pulling it up to try again didn’t seem to hurt it, and I did get it applied successfully. It did what it was supposed to do - make the glare go away, without overly darkening my screen. I’ve been carrying it arround (in a case) for 5 weeks now, and so far, it is sticking perfectly. I’m a happy customer.

Prep is very important, as is taking your time to line up the protector so it is put on evenly. Make sure you watch youtube videos using the tape method to clean any dust off and to remove any obstructions on the backside of the film when you’re putting them on. This particular protector must be PERFECTLY placed since this screen protector is nearly exact, if not a hair larger. I had to line it up several times before I got it right.PRO TIP: only expose 1” of the adhesive when lining it up. As you place it on the garmin, glide your finger on the protector side to side to watch for any bubbles. Bubbles = dust. Use your tape to clean it off. (watch youtube). If you follow this method, you will have a perfect screen protector on any one of your electronics.
