Definitely worth getting!

This case and keyboard are definitely worth getting. With the Galaxy Tablet tucked snugly in its side and the case closed, it is about 1 inch thick. A nice size for hand carry or tucking into an attache case. The keyboard is magnet-mounted to its side of the case and removes easily to work wirelessly (Blutooth) away from the tablet. The case makes a nice adjustable stand for the tablet. Looks good closed up and functions well when opened. I give it two thumbs up. My only comment is that there is no place to stash the charger and some cables.

great Keyboard for the cost.

I’ve had this for a few weeks now. i like it a lot because the section that props up the tablet is the full length of the case. This allows wide stable base when you set the unit on your lap. You can’t adjust the viewing angle and there is not a slot to hold the bottom edge of the tablet. Sometimes the tablet will pop out and press on the keys. So if you notice that your screen is jumping around, thats probably why. The battery life on the keyboard is real good. I’ve only re-charged it once in about 3 weeks. I have forgotten to turn off the keyboard a few time but it went into standby mode. This is my third keyboard. the last one stopped charging and I hope this one will not do the same thing. Connectivity is easy . I wish there was a battery indicator on the unit or that you receive a msg on the screen telling you the status of the battery. there may be an led on the keyboard that changes color but you can’t see it while using the unit because the view is blocked by the tablet. The keys all function well and have a good feel and layout. The leatherish case feels like leather. With it’s raised grain, very nice. My major negative is there is not mouse pad.

The keyboard has great functions that fully work the Note Pro and feels …

The battery life in this keyboard is amazingly long. The keyboard has great functions that fully work the Note Pro and feels just like my laptop keyboard. The case holds the Note Pro securely in place and looks nice. The magnetic flap that holds the case closed does not interfere with using the keyboard for you can simply fold it under. The only thing I would change about this keyboard is adding a groove in front of the keyboard for the Note Pro to rest in so it doesn’t move. Without having a groove the Note Pro moves to much.

Excellent case and keyboard, Micro-USB cable included, no adapter.

This is my revised review based on new information and experience with the keyboard.I really like this leather case and blue-tooth keyboard. The two issues I discuss dropped it from five to four stars. Don’t let that deter you though… it really is a great case, and I have no regrets buying it.Pros: They are sturdy and well built. The leather is really nice…durable but comfortable to hold. The keyboard is fabulous. It easily connects up to the tablet and has a nice feel to it, not cheap. Also, the tablet will turn on or off automatically depending on whether the case is opened or closed.Cons: The charging cable for the blue-tooth keyboard IS included but the charging adapter is NOT included, which is rather strange. But, it’s the same adapter used to charge the Samsung smartphones and tablets, so if you have one of those, you’ll be in great shape. You just won’t be able to charge them both at the same time. I recommend buying an extra here at Amazon, which you can do pretty inexpensively. Aside from that, two other things to note: one, the flap that holds the case closed can get in the way when it’s open. I just tuck it underneath the case to get it out of the way. It does latch nicely though. Also, with the blue-tooth keyboard, there are no slots for the tablet to rest in to hold it in position while standing up. If you remove the keyboard, which is magnetically attached, there are slots in the cover. But they are not accessible when the keyboard is in place. I suppose you could pull the keyboard out and move the tablet a little farther away to use the keyboard if you wanted to. I haven’t tried that.
