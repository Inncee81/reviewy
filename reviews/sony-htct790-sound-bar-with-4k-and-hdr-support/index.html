Great form factor, plays very well with Sony HDTVs but unfortunately it doesn’t sound great. Bought the Sony HTNT5 instead.

Ended up returning this for Sony’s top-tier model, Sony HTNT5 Sound Bar with Hi-Res Audio and Wireless Streaming, it does everything the HTCT790 does and it sounds amazing.I originally bought this to replace an aging Yamaha sound bar. My old sound bar sounded good but after buying a new Sony HDTV I wanted something:1) SlimMy previous soundbar was tall and used to hide the bottom part of the screen. This sound bar is slim and doesn’t suffer the same problem.In fact, this is as slim as it gets.2) Plays nice with the TVMy old soundbar, like most, was connected by toslink (optical cable). I had to juggle between remotes, one to control the TV, one to control the volume / settings.I got tired of that and opted for this Sony soundbar which supports Bravia Sync (HDMI CEC). Now whenever I turn the TV on the sound bar starts automatically. Turning off the entire system is just a matter of pressing the on/off button of the TV remote.This, by the way, works with other bravia sync enabled products, such as the PS4. Turning on the PS4 controller turns on the PS4, which in turns powers on the TV, soundbar and subwoofer.Extremely convenient.The volume can also be controlled with the TV remote.I barely use the soundbar remote - only to tweak settings from time to time. And this is not even required, the soundbar settings are also available through the Bravia Sync menu of the TV.4) ConnectedI wanted a bluetooth sound bar to play music from my phone. The HTCT790 has that and much more. It supports Google Cast and Spotify out of the box which I like a lot. The quality of direct streaming from Spotify rather than bluetooth is noticeable and it doesn’t drain my phone battery.3) Sounded better than my old sound barAnd this is where the HTCT790 fell short. My ~$200, 3 years old sound bar actually sounded better than this one.I really wanted to love this product but the main goal of a sound bar is to, well, deliver sound.I ended up returning this and ordering the higher end version of this one, the HTNT5: Sony HTNT5 Sound Bar with Hi-Res Audio and Wireless StreamingIt’s everything this sound bar is but it sounds great.It’s the same software, almost same form factor but instead of using 2 full range drivers, ht HTNT5 has 2 mid-range drivers, 2 tweeters and 2 super-tweeters and they do make a difference.

I like the voice+ option which enhances dialogue

I am not an audiophile. I just wanted a sound bar that would play 4k audio and works seamlessly with my sony 4k tv. set up was very simple. No issues with the sub connectivity with the actual sound bar itself-that wireless aspect is a huge plus. I haven’t messed with any audio settings, just have it set on default. I had an old sony sound bar that was not wireless and this new set up makes life a tad bit easier. I like the voice+ option which enhances dialogue, that was a problem with my old sound bar, the background audio sometimes masked the voice of the actors. I haven’t tried setting up any other bluetooth peripherals with the sound bar itself but I don’t anticipate it being an issue. Overall to a novice ear, I couldn’t be happier with my set up.
