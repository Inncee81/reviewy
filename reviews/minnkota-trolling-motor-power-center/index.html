    Product Description

Product Description The trolling motor power center is ideal for small boats without a battery compartment. It has easy to access external battery terminals that allow trolling motor leads and charger leads to be connected without opening the box. Built-in battery meter displays current “state of charge”’ It also features 2 manual reset circuit breakers: 15 amp for accessory plugs and 60 amp for trolling motor.

Amazon.com Ideal for small-boat transom applications (i.e., boats that don’t have battery compartments), the Minn Kota Motor Power Center stores your battery securely and out of the way while you boat. The Motor Power Center features easy-access external battery terminals that allow you to connect trolling motor leads and charger leads without opening the box. The built-in battery meter, meanwhile, displays the current “state of charge.” Designed to fit group 24- and 27-size batteries, the Motor Power Center offers two 12-volt accessory plugs and two manual reset circuit breakers: a 15 amp breaker for accessory plugs and a 60 amp breaker for the trolling motor.

With the pole configuration on that battery the lead pole (not the wing nut one) hits the plastic shield under the lid. I was up against the wall time wise as we were leaving the next morning so I drilled a hole in the shield and wrapped the battery pole in electrical tape as otherwise it might hit the wiring in the lid. I also had to put rubber foam alongside the battery to shove it to one side in the case. It seems there are two different configurations of the poles on marine batteries so just make sure yours will work before buying.

I have a older 70 LB (freighter) Navarro canoe. Being 60+ I am getting to old to paddle back sometimes from the “other side” of the lakes up here in Oregon. I mounted a MinnKota trolling motor on the rear of my canoe, and so had to get a Deep Cycle Marine battery to power the trolling motor. I purchased a Interstate Group 27 size battery and it fit perfect! The first time out, using this set up we had to fight swells on Elk Lake, which we had avoided because of afternoon wind swells. We cruised around for almost three hours and I was worried that I was running out of battery. Pushed the battery monitor button and it showed that we still had plenty of power left. This battery box is well built. Very thick case. Great carrying handle on top. The “How much juice is left in the battery meter” alone is worth the extra money compared to your average battery box. I run my fish finder out of one of the accessory outlets. I used velcro strips on the bottom to keep it from sliding around, and placed it behind the center seat which is not used most of the time. I think Minnkota products are excellent and well thought out.
