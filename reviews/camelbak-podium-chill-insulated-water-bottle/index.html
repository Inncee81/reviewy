Product Specifications

  • Like most water bottles, the Camelbak 21oz Podium Chill Insulated Water Bottle keeps you hydrated on runs or bike rides.
  • Unlike most bottles, this one keeps your water cold from the first hour to the last.
  • Its BPA-free TruTaste polypropylene material is safer to drink out of than polycarbonate plastics and it won’t distort your water’s taste, and HydroGuard insulation keeps your beverage chilled twice as long as regular sports bottles.

  • The Jet Valve cap is spill-proof and self-sealing so you can squeeze some deliciousness down your gullet quickly and get back to what you were doing.

    Product Description

CamelBak Podium Chill is a high performance insulated sport bottle that keeps your water cooler longer. A double-walled bottle construction uses closed cell foam technology to keep your water cold twice as long as a standard bottle. The patented spill-proof Jet Valve allows the user to hydrate immediately without having to open or close a drinking interface and the bottle’s streamlined ergonomic design offers excellent squeezability without compromising bottle grip. The large aperture of the drinking nozzle generates an exceptional water flow rate and is easy to remove for thorough cleaning. The Podium Chill also has a lockout dial with easy to read graphics for leak-proof transport.

Product Reviews

Great bottles - very easy to use and keeps things cold

I love these. I bought three (one for cycling and two for running) to replace bottles that required me to pull up the valve top so I could drink (one had busted). I only wish I had replaced them earlier. These make taking a quick drink super easy - especially hike on a bike. Just point and squeeze. I have found that I am drinking way more water while cycling with this bottle and that is a good thing. Now I debate buying the larger size! I have run for over an hour in warm temps and the water is still very cold at the end, even if my ice cubes have melted. I wholeheartedly recommend these.

The best bottle for your full-suspension mountain bike

The insulation is decent. It kept my water from getting hot in the sun and from freezing when temps dropped. It’s no Yeti cooler, but it’s better than a standard bottle.This size just fits my full suspension bike (2013 Kona Satori, large frame). I like to carry as much as possible (not a hydration pack fan) and this is the biggest one that’ll fit.If you’ve never tried CamelBak’s mechanism, it works great, doesn’t leak (even in the “open” position), and you can replace the valve without tossing the bottle. Overall, it’s the best bike bottle out there.

No cute designs, but functional.

I needed a water bottle to take when I go walking with my husband. I used to use a Brita filtering bottle, but we recently moved to a place with well water, and the filter is no longer necessary. I looked at many different brands and types of bottles and settled for this (pink/gray). I say settled, because I would have really liked something with a cuter design, but function was more important to me. I like the spill-proof jet valve, because it allows for good control and no leaking. I just wish they didn’t insist on covering their products with the brand name. Putting it somewhere in plain view is fine, but cuter patterns would be nice.

The locking feature on this bottle is excellent and avoids spills in my kids backpack and at …

We have 3 of these bottles. 1 now for over a year. The locking feature on this bottle is excellent and avoids spills in my kids backpack and at school. Looking at the design I don’t believe the water travels over this now grimy area but it looks disgusting. I wish they would sell replacement caps.

Although I love my Podium Chill bottles for everyday use

Although I love my Podium Chill bottles for everyday use, I find they’re not easy to pull in and out of my bike cage. I prefer the shape of a couple of other insulated bottles over these since I have small hands. Also, even though I fill them to the brim with ice and then add super cold water, on hot days the ice is usually melted before I can even ride 20 miles. One trick I did learn, though, is to fill them 3⁄4 with water and freeze them upright and then add cold water before I ride. I’ve enjoyed 30-40 mile rides with ice still in the bottle by the end of the ride.
