Product Specifications

  • 4K Video / 12 Megapixel Camera
  • Wi-Fi Video Downlink
  • Maximum Transmission Distance of 0.75mi (1.2km)
  • Easy to Fly, Intelligent Flight System

  • 3-Axis Stabilization Gimbal

    Product Description

Built from the ground up for aerial filmmaking and photography, the Phantom 3 4K from DJI features an integrated 12MP camera attached to a 3-axis gimbal. Apart from 12MP JPEG or RAW photos, the camera can record cinema-spec DCI 4K (4096 x 2160) at 24 fps and UDH 4K (3840 x 2160) at 30 fps. With the included flight battery, the quad copter can fly for up to 25 minutes, depending on conditions. A 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi link with range extender allows you to use the venerable DJI Go app for live HD monitoring and camera control. Additionally, you can access intelligent flight control features tailored to needs of the filmmaker, including Follow Me, Course Lock, Home Lock, and waypoint navigation. The Wi-Fi extender as well as the included handheld controller (transmitter) feature a line-of-sight range of up to 3937’.

So easy to fly a child could do it

I did soooo much research before purchasing this drone. I knew I wanted a drone for good photo and video, wasn’t concerned with distance, and didn’t want to break the bank. After tons of research, I was between the Phantom 3 Standard, the Advanced, and the 4K.I had really been convinced I was going to get the Advanced over the Standard. It had the upgraded sensor, had the better controller, and used Light Bridge. After more research, I found that the Phantom 4K was a combination of the Standard and the Professional. It has the 4K camera that I really wanted, but then it comes with the upgraded remote for additional camera features and controls. The way it ends up being cheaper is that it uses WiFi like the Standard, vs Light Bridge like the Advanced and Professional. Since distance wasn’t important to me (1 mile is plenty fine, I don’t need to have it out 3 miles), the 4K was the logical choice for my needs.Given the statement above, I have found the weakness of WiFi already. I easily loose signal in high WiFi areas or if I lose line of sight. The Light Bridge technology would take care of these issues. However, this has only happened a couple of times and in by no means has it been a deal breaker for me.As far as flight goes, I’ve been flying manual, cheaper quads for about two years. They are no doubt challenging and have a major learning curve. I was very nervous about flying the Phantom 3 for the first time. I read the manual, watched multiple training videos, etc. Basically, I didn’t want to crash my new $600 piece of equipment. On my very first flight, I couldn’t believe how ridiculously easy it is to fly. It takes off by itself, will hover when you let go of the controls, and will land itself (but you probably knew all of that from the research you’ve been doing). My 7 year old daughter can fly this, it’s really that easy.After playing around with different settings in the app, I finally got the camera and video modes where I want. The picture quality is amazing. I’ve purchased additional filters for the camera to enhance the image some and am so pleased with the quality.Overall, I absolutely love this quad and highly recommend it to anyone. My dad ended up getting one, having no flying experience and is also in love. I really can’t say enough good things about it.
