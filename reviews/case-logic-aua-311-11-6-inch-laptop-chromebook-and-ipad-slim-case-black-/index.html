Fits my 11” laptop plus extras

I love this case! It has two exterior zipped pockets (one small, one large), and a padded, divided main interior. The large zipped pocket has two pen/pencil loops and a few slip pockets. The interior main dividers are just that–dividers to help keep things organized. It is the usual Case Logic quality, which is to say it is very well made.In the main interior divider sections, I have my Acer 11” laptop, my Japanese textbook, and a spiral-bound notebook. In the larger zipped pocket, I have the power cord for the laptop, a wireless mouse, an Ethernet dongle, a 6’ Ethernet cable, a cellphone-style headphone set, a pen and pencil set, and a few odds and ends. It’s all a snug fit, but the case doesn’t bulge (which would offend my sensibilities), and there’s room for a bit more.

Great Case for my 2011 Mac Mini

I’m about to put two Samsung 840 EVO - 1 TB drives in my 2011 Mac Mini which should make it portable. Now, it’s attached to a Promise Technology Pegasus R4 Raid Drive which makes it NOT portable at all. That sucker is a 9.5” X 7.5” X 7.25” block of metal. Once I decided that I’d pony up the money and speed up my Mac Mini, then I got excited about all the possibilities if it were easily portable. I could take it to nearly anyone’s house and attach it to their TV through HDMI. I could take it to the Apple Store for my 1 to 1 training sessions. I could detach it from my 40” TV and attach it to a smaller 24” monitor across the room on my desk and use it as a more typical computer. All those things will now be possibilities. But I wanted it to be as easy as possible to go portable.I started thinking about a bag to carry it in. I searched and searched the web for ideas of how to tote a Mac Mini around. It looks like it was a topic of discussion some years ago and some bags were even made for the earlier Mac Mini, but there isn’t much discussion about it now. I finally settled on this bag and I really love it. It’s definitely a TIGHT fit, but I can get my Apple Wireless Keyboard, Apple Trackpad and Apple Mouse all into the bag along with the Mac Mini and a power cord. The Mac Mini uses a figure 8 cord. I have generic ones around the house that will power it. I called Apple and they said the generic cord should be fine, but since I still have AppleCare they are sending me an extra Apple power cord for free.I can hardly wait for my new drives to arrive so I can install them and take my Mac Mini to my next 1 to 1 for some additional training at the Apple Store. The bag seems to be well made. Even though the bag is brand new it has very little smell. I’m extremely sensitive to new smells in freshly made products, but this is faint enough I can handle it.

This bag is great if I am just going to a class where I need nothing but my computer

This is the 3rd case logic product I’ve bought. I always thought they were just some cheap brand, but now that I own 3 of their products I can say that they make really solid products. The other two bags I own are the Griffith Park Deluxe backpack, which is my daily backpack, and the DLC-115 Laptop bag for my 15” MacBook Pro Retina.I bought the AUA-311 bag for my 11” air.Be warned: this bag will NOT fit ANY laptop larger than the 11.6”. The air firs perfectly in the bag, but if it was .2 of an inch bigger the computer wouldn’t fit.However: Those saying that you can’t fit anything in to the bag besides the computer are dead wrong. In the second pocket I can fit a charger, my Logitech Anywhere MX mouse, and my earbuds case, plus a pen and a couple other small things. This bag is great if I am just going to a class where I need only my computer and then not doing anything else afterwards. It certainly beats lugging around a large backpack.
