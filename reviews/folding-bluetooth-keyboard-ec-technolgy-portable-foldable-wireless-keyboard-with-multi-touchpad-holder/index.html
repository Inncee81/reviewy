Works amazingly well

 We have a lot of devices around the house, from an iPad and iPhones, to kindles, and android tablets. Buying individual keyboards would be expensive. Saw this and thought I’d give it a shot. So glad I did.This works amazing with android systems. Shortcut to mail works as well as all functions. Using this with a fire, the touchpad brings up a curser and makes it like a netbook.Tried it on a windows based tablet, again worked great!On the iPad, well it works as good as the Apple keyboard I used to have. (Read limited compared to more open operating systems, thanks Apple)Then I paired all three at the same time as this can support three devices. That really works!! Just hit the function button and the Bluetooth number above the touchpad to switch between. I like that it is not a single button and can not be accidentally pressed, but two buttons on opposite side of keyboard.Another thing I like is that unlike some folding keyboards that just separate the keyboard in half with a blank space, when this is opened, the seems are virtually invisible and there is no odd separation between keys.Speaking of keys, the keys have a really nice feel and movement to them. I was thinking that this would be like other keyboards with little movement, but I was wrong. There is a good amount of movement to the keys that leads to a more enjoyable and user friendly experience.Overall I am super impressed with this and it’s ability to work so well with different operating systems, pair with three devices, has a touchpad that works great, keys with a great feel, folds up small, lightweight, metal and durable. What more could be asked for?

And I couldn’t be happier. I wouldn’t really put it in a pants …

Incredibly convenient, lightweight keyboard. One of the indicator lights shows how much battery life is left (based on number of blinks). I switched from using a Galaxy Note 8.0 tablet for taking handwritten notes for meetings to using this keyboard instead, because I realized many times I couldn’t decipher my own handwriting, and after all, typing is faster. And I couldn’t be happier. I wouldn’t really put it in a pants pocket though, because each of the two panels is stiff (as they SHOULD be) and I’m afraid putting it in my back pocket and sitting down may break it. It sorta looks like a large wallet when folded, and its thickness when folded is about the same as a typical wallet, if not thinner. But don’t fall into the temptation to put it in your pocket! The sound made from typing keystrokes is about the same as a typical notebook PC, meaning relatively quiet.Only spent one day with it so far so I don’t know how long one charge would really last, but it is rated for 40 hours of continuous use, meaning on my usual schedule of meetings (which isn’t too many), it should last me at least a couple of months.Comparing this to Microsoft’s Universal Fold able Keyboard (which I admit I haven’t used), this one seems to be better because the keys are split into two sets at an ANGLE, meaning your hands and wrists are at more natural positions when typing. AND it’s at least 50% cheaper than the Microsoft.
