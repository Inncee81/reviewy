Product Reviews

Good news and bad news

Just came back from a white river rating with this camera attached to my helmet. Good news and bad news…The good is that the 1080p 60fps quality is great (considering the price of the camera I’d say even beyond expectations), and the case is really waterproof (at some rapids I, and the camera, were fully underwater and the camera stayed dry). Though small (in size and in price), it is packed with many features that you would only find in higher end (and price) cameras: you can set the view angle from 70 to 170 degrees, use the internal gyroscope for added image stability, and of course the 4K to name a few.What I see as bad may not affect you, depends on your use for the camera. The problem is that when the camera is mounted on a helmet and you don’t see the LCS screen, there’s no way to tell if the video is running or not, and sometimes I thought it was recording and it wasn’t. The camera has a good feature that it announces when it starts recording (I guess it was made exactly for this situation, when you can’t see the back screen), but with the waterproof case you cannot hear it. Other cameras solved that problem with providing a wrist remote to control the camera when it’s out of reach/site.Another problem was that when battery was low, the camera shut itself down after about 10-30 seconds while recording. I turned it on again, started recording (there was still enough juice in the battery to start another recording) but again it shut off after a few seconds. Very annoying. The battery lasted about an hour, as promised, however, considering that when it’s at a low level it starts acting up (as I described above), I’d say that it only runs reliably for about 40 minutes.I sent an e-mail with my concerns to the manufacturer and they were very prompt and responsive. They tried to work with me on the problems and suggested that there might be a problem with the specific unit that I received. I am going to return it and try a new one to see if battery life improves.
