Sony TDG-BT500A glasses for Sony KDL-70W850B 70” 480Hz 1080p

I wonder how much of the quality of the 3D viewing is affected by the specifications of the TV.For the past few years I’ve used the older KDL-EX700 TV Sony active 3D system……with its 240 motionflow rate and the black frames interposed between each lighted frame it produces an effective rate of 60 frames/sec per eye in 3D mode…..this system required the use of the older, heavier, uncomfortable IR 3D glasses whose flicker in a bright lighted room was annoying, my spouse rarely got through a whole movie without taking a break…….viewing was particularly sensitive to left/right tilting of the head which introduced cross talk and changes in light levels between eyes…….. 3D viewing was just OK.Upgraded to a KDL70W850B which uses the Sony active Bluetooth RF system and a 480 Hz motion flow rate with Image blur reduction which doubles effective rate to 120 frames/sec. per eye in 3D. The set came with the older TDG-BT400A 3D glasses but manual integral in the TV software specified BT500A replacements. The difference between the old and new system is dramatic……lightweight glasses with easily changeable battery, …..removable nose piece for Rx glasses users for a great fit……….no critical line of sight requirement with the IR transmitter in the TV…….no critical leveling of the head to prevent crosstalk and lighting changes.Watched all my 3D movies again on the newer system and the experience is no longer just OK; it is incredible…even the spouse agrees and can now sit through a whole movie without taking a break…….finally an impressive 3D viewing experience……viewing is no longer hampered by frame flickering or weight of the glasses……and a more pleasing and realistic depth of view focus of objects on the screen.

We Love Them!

Bought these to work with my Sony XBR75X850C smart TV. I’ve watched several 3D movies using these glasses and I am very satisfied with their performance. They sync up to the TV quickly and the quality of the 3D is great - probably better than what you experience in the theater. The nose pieces come off so you can wear them over your prescription glasses.I also ordered a storage case that can hold four pairs of these glasses. That many pairs of these glasses will set you back a bit, so storing them in a nice soft case is cheap insurance against them getting broken while not in use.If you have a smart TV that requires active 3D glasses, these are a great choice, and they are made by Sony, so they are made to last. I would definitely recommend these to anyone needing active 3D glasses. However, they are getting harder and harder to find as most TVs are using passive 3D technology which I feel is not as good as the active technology.
