The good: covered in rubber and lens caps attached to …

The good: covered in rubber and lens caps attached to binoculars with rubber strap; this helps them from being lost.The bad: horrible optics. binoculars show original image and ghost image. Unless you don’t mind seeing double image, these glasses are practically useless.And yes, I know how to go about adjusting them. Adjust lens spacing to accommodate your eye span, set focus for left eye using middle wheel (with right eye closed). Set focus for right eye using wheel on right lens (with left eye close). Adjust distance between eye and binocular eye pieces for best visibility.All this and still seeing ghost image (bad collimation). Typically, binoculars have screws that you can adjust to at least adjust the prisms to get the images somewhat aligned. As far as I can tell, they are no where to be found on these binoculars.These glasses are junk in my opinion.

Not Night Vision

This purchase does not include night vision as shown on the binoculars and this listing, but is an excellent tool for viewing far away items up close and clearly. I did contact one of the sellers about the night vision and they replied that the listing clearly states that it is not night vision as follows:“As we have clearly mentioned this in our listing.It is not night vision it has night prism feature which absorbs light for easier viewing at night.Hope you will understand.”
