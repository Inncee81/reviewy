Windows drivers are old and not free, Linux works well after some updates.

Camera part is very good. The four microphones won’t work in Windows without purchasing some outdated drivers for Windows 7. Linux can use the camera perfectly but I believe the sound requires a newer Kernel or updated libraries. At first, I could get Linux drivers going. I think before the updates it needed to be unplugged, then plugged back in. Works perfectly on Linux Kernel 4.12 with Xubuntu 17.04.

Bargain webcam!

I never realized I could use a PS Eye on a PC as a webcam until browsing the web one day. I really just wanted a microphone to use with Cortana, but for the price, why not get a camera too? I used third party drivers that are easy to find on Google and installation was a breeze on Windows 10 Pro. I have a working camera / mic now with excellent picture quality and virtually no motion blur for next to nothing. There’s really no reason to spend more money than this on a “real” webcam when you can have this.

Works okay as cheap webcam/mic

Great for OpenTrack and other DIY NIR applications

I can’t speak to it’s usefulness as a normal camera since I purchased this to build a head tracking rig, but if that’s what you intend to do, this is the one you want. I received the version with the easy (well, easy-ish) to remove IR filter and used a square of exposed, developed 35mm film between the lens mount and sensor as a filter with excellent results after turning down the exposure and gain settings under Options in CLTest. Perfectly sized dots at 640x480px, 60 FPS for OpenTrack to see regardless of lighting conditions.
