Less features than the older model

For about 10 years I had one of these handy devices. It was my go-to for 90% of household jobs (plus art installs at shows) since it’s lighter than my DeWalt 18V drill / screwdriver. My old one died not long ago so I bought this one to replace it. Sadly, they made some changes to it that are a bit disappointing. My older version had a mini tape measure in the handle, the new one does not. The old one had a drill head bit that fit in a holder on one side and a double-side flathead / Phillip head bit for the other. This one comes with three separate bits but still only two storage slots on the side. The drill bit on my old one got lost pretty early on because it wouldn’t stay in the little storage slot. The new one has the same problem, the drill bit sits in that slot very loosely so it falls out really easy. The AC adapter is smaller on this one so I guess that’s a minor plus. It still does that job well but it cost me about $10 more that it did 10 years ago, but with less features. The price increase I expected, the changes I did not.

Good power screwdriver, good value for the price

Have bought several of these over the years. I use these daily in an electronics repair shop. Usually they last a year or two and then need replacement. The weakest part seems to be the switch, after awhile either forward or reverse will quit or become intermittent. If you’ve got the time and patience they can be dissassembled and repaired, but sooner or later the switch will fail completely. Also had a couple with bad clutches. Overall happy with this unit for the hours I use it, spending 20$ a year for a power driver is a good deal. I would expect, under normal use at home it would last much longer, as long as the user understands it’s just a power screwdriver, not an impact driver or made for high torque requirements.
