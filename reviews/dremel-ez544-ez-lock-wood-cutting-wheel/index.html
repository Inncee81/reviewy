Product Reviews

Used on Hard Maple

I used this cutting wheel on a Dremel 100 series tool to cut hard maple. Because I move quite often I try not buy many power tools, but I do have my compact/versatile Dremel. So I tried to use my Dremel to cut a 1” x 6” x 4’ piece of hard maple to make a paddle.Overall it worked well. I was able to cut the straight portions of my paddle in a half hour or so. I used a coping saw for the curved portions. I orginally tried to use the coping saw for the whole paddle but it was a lot of work and my cuts weren’t nearly as straight as when I used the cutting wheel. Because the cutting wheel only cuts about 1⁄2” deep I had to cut on one side and then flip the piece over and cut on the other side to make a cut the full depth of the wood. While I wouldn’t recommend to use this method to make many cuts, I would recommend it for small projects on hard woods for 1” thick or less and ideally less than 1⁄2” thick. I haven’t tried it on soft woods like pine but I would guess it would cut through it like butter.
