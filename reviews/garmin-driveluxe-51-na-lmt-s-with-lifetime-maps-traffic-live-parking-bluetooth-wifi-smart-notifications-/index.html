Garmin 51 DriveLuxe

I was very hesitant on purchasing the new 51 DriveLuxe LMT-S since I owned and really liked the Nuvi 3597 version. I recently took a 250 mile trip through Illinois and compared the 51 DriveLuxe to my cars 2016 Lexus Enform navigation system side by side. Also compared the 3597 to the new 51 DriveLuxe prior to the trip.First impressions comparing the two units 3597 & 51 DriveLuxe:• DISPLAY: The 3597 wins hands down in visual display. Why Garmin has chosen to change the graphics look of the display is beyond me. See attached picture showing the 3597 on the left. It has more contrast in colors and also shows green when driving in wooded and grass areas where the 51 DriveLuxe looks like a water color painting with no contrast within the colors. It looks like the surrounding areas are all concrete.• VOICE COMMAND: I give the 51 DriveLuxe the edge on this. I recently started using the “Voice Command” features on both the Garmin’s and my cars navigation system. The 51 seems to understand commands slightly better over the 3597. I’m using the voice command function as much as possible, it just makes sense and it works most of the time.• LINKING to iPhone: I have switched to Garmin Smartphone Link live traffic updates over a year ago because of trips to Nashville and found it to be better than built in HD traffic on the 3597. Since the HD traffic cable does not come with the new Garmin it was not a deal breaker. At first I had trouble linking to the new 51 DriveLuxe but blame the fact I had my 3597 linked at the same time. Once I figured out the linking process it connected without issues also I disabled making and receiving phone calls through the Garmin in the Bluetooth settings on the Garmin.• FOURSQUARE and TRIPADVISIOR: Too early to tell if these have any real benefit but currently call them a plus since they come with the unit. If you have a companion with you searching for restaurants or other POI with a smartphone it’s no big deal. I know down loading Foursquare app on my iPhone prove to be another annoying app so I disable notifications.• GPS: It appears the 51 DriveLuxe boots and connects quicker to GPS signals. What is annoying is why Garmin does not publish process speeds and storage capacity of their units. I’m sure a lot of this has to do with how the software is written.• ON SCREEN NOTIFICATIONS: While both units have these the new DriveLuxe has refined it and added a few more touches. Adding, reduced speed, curves are the ones that come to mind and although not necessary they are there and may come in handy.• WiFi UPDATES: No big deal one way or another, no problem connecting to my Wi-Fi but did notice it showed no updates available but when I connected using a cable to Garmin Express it showed map updates available. My thoughts are you still need to be connected to power for long updates why not just use Garmin Express since you have to be cabled anyway.• NAVIGATION: 3597 and DriveLuxe seem to mimic each other.Comparing the Lexus Enform navigation to the DriveLuxe. Both were basically the same as far as navigation except when getting off the interstates. The Garmin’s route guidance seemed to be not as efficient in saving driving time. Obviously the Lexus Enform system display is much better due to its size but also was better at Birdseye view when it came to interstate changes some of which the Garmin did not pickup. These were not critical lane changes but should have been shown. The voice command feature hands down goes to the Garmin. Even after voice training on the Enform navigation I gave up on using it.Conclusion it’s a judgement call between the 3597 and 51 DriveLuxe, both work well and as usual the new version offers some nice updates but overall nothing that really jumps out and makes you feel money well spent. The display is my biggest disappointment.
