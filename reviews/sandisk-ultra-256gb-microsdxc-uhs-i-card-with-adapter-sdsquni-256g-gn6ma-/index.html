You want the best, so you buy the best.

SanDisk is great at producing quality products. I have a bunch of these cards and this one is no different. I purchased this memory card because I needed one for the Samsung Galaxy Gear 360. The cool thing is I did not have to format the card. I popped it in to the Gear 360 and the formatting was done for me. Keep in mind that buying a 64 gb card does not mean you get 64 gb of storage. You get 59.60 gb. Keep in mind that if you plan on shooting 4k video on any camera to factor in the amount of space per minute. For the Gear 360 the 64 gb card holds about 13 minutes of 4k footage (450 mb per minute). This card keeps up with the writing speed of 4k.I also have the same card in a Raspberry Pi 3 and I have had no issues. For anyone without a Raspberry Pi 3 just know that it is not always easy to tell that your device is powered off because the power light remains on when the device is powered down. So, there have been plenty of times that I have thought the device was turned off, unplugged, and found out that I forgot to do a step or two before the device was fully powered down. Still, I have had no issues with corruption on the memory card.For the current price of this card, you would be hard pressed to find anything better. I haven’t had any issues with the card melting, overheating, or corrupting. The only issue I could think of is that microSD cards are so small that they are easily lost. That’s not a knock on SanDisk.PROS■ Price/performance ratio is stellar;■ Great speed for 4k video; and■ No corruption issues (so far);CONS■ Not a knock on SanDisk but these cards are easily lost.
