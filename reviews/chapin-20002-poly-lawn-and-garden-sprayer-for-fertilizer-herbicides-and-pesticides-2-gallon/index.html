Will do the job if you plan on taking exemplary care of it

I operate a small farm so I’ve bought a few 2 gallon sprayers in my time. I was hoping for an imitation of the rugged model I bought in a big box store. So, the pros: the tank is solidly built and will not leak or rupture. The cons: The wand and hose and pump handle are made from plastics that are close to the cheapest of the cheap. Especially the hose and wand. I can guarantee that if this unit is left out exposed to the elements, especially heat, it will disintegrate to uselessness in a big hurry…it’s obvious that I’ll need to baby this along to get any life out of it. By habit I always thoroughly rinse sprayers and flush them with both water and air after each use, and store them in our workshop. For about $6 more, there are other models with extra features that make them much more serviceable and likely to last, like more rugged wand and hose components, and a pressure vent. For the price I suppose this was adequate, but I will not be purchasing this unit again because others out there are so much better made.

Caveat Emptor – The Chapin 2002 is a Basic Sprayer

Chapin 20002 Poly Lawn and Garden Sprayer For Fertilizer, Herbicides and Pesticides , 2 GallonI just started using this Chapin 20002 sprayer so I can’t honestly comment on its durability, which seems to be a major point of contention from other users. To that end, I do agree that it doesn’t seem to be made with some of the more solid materials I’ve seen in other sprayers. I won’t say it’s flimsy because I just haven’t given it enough time to make that evaluation in conjunction with other reviews I’ve read. To be fair, this sprayer worked very well on its first use. The spray pattern is quite satisfactory however the plastic components of the wand may cause problems in the future. The locking mechanism for the pump is uncertain. It requires about a ¼ turn to lock or unlock the pump and it doesn’t have a positive locked feel which could lead to unintended consequences. Unlike a lot of sprayers, including other Chapin products, the 20002 doesn’t have a constant spray lock in the handle for “fatigue-free spraying.” Also the 20002 does not have a pressure relief valve requiring a potentially more hazardous procedure of turning the pump handle to release the pressure. The tank quantity markings aren’t very well-defined but that can be remedied with a marker or some paint. The wand holder is composed of 2 indentations built into the lower part of the pump handle. They hold the wand parallel to the ground and are a little more trouble to utilize than I feel they need to be. So to sum it up I’ll give this sprayer a fair chance before deciding if it’s built to last. However, it’s important to note that the 20002 is very BASIC inexpensive sprayer whose features are accurately described on Amazon’s website. No more. No less.

Pump cracked, lost pressure, oops – then I found the problem and fixed it myself.

Worked for about 4-5 sprayings, then the threaded pump portion cracked, causing it to lose pressure.Update – I examined the pump while epoxying the small crack (it turned out to be superficial). I found that one of the gaskets had not been seated properly. I carefully reseated the gasket and voila, works again. If yours stops working, pull the pump and give it a quick look over, especially the rubber gaskets at the end of the pump. The top gasket should be flat, centered, and fit over the the whole rim of the plastic tube opening. Mine had folded into the tube on one side. I simply pulled the gasket edges out of the tube (did not take the whole thing off), and gently repositioned / centered it using some jeweler’s pliers (ones without the teeth which could tear rubber).
