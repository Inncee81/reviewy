Nice Showerhead and Different in Design

We decided to try this shower head after a recommendation from a friend. It has more of a “waterfall” effect rather than a direct power spray. We like it and would recommend it with that caveat. The spray is adjustable but not powerful. It also adjusts for height and direction. Easy to install.

Nice and gentle!

I have been pelted so long with a regular shower head and the force would suck my shower curtain in. After having a shower while visiting my brother, I discovered this beautiful Waterpik showerhead! Wow - what a nice easy rain that was engulfing me without the usual morning beating! I had to have one. It was easily installed (my husband however did have to add a washer to one of the connections). It has 8 settings which all work fine, but ours will remain set on the drenching rain - lovely! The shower curtain no longer sucks in and the shower walls are not soaked (since I wipe them down after each shower this makes me happy). I thought that perhaps with such a gentle rain I would not be able to get the soap out of my hair, but this is not the case at all. There is enough pressure and all is well.

I don’t feel drenched nor power sprayed

I purchased this shower head a week ago. The very reason I purchased this was the drenching rain + powerspray™. Waterpik even trademarked the word “powerspray™”.Well, my home has a strong water pressure and I don’t feel drenched nor power sprayed. You can forget the pulsating massage. I’ve done an hour of testing, spanning several days. I turned on the water at full-blast and slowly lowered the pressure. There’s a certain pressure threshold and no matter how much pressure you increase, the water output seems the same. I even removed the water-saver and that didn’t help.3 things that really bug me:(1) About one-fifth of the little rubber nozzles don’t work - they’re clogged. I just purchased this a week ago!(2) Changing the settings by rotating the knob is awkward. You have to use two hands. If the shower head is not tightly connected then the whole shower unit rotates along with the knob.(3) Bottom line, weak water output even with high water pressure.When I have more time, I will disassemble the shower head and clean it according to the instructions by using vinegar (as I said, it’s only been a week and now I’m cleaning?? This can’t be normal).The one feature I like is the long arm. You can position it high enough where the shower head is on top of you, just like rain. If I can’t get the nozzles to unclog then I might remove the shower head all together and replaced it with my old Delta Faucet 75152 Water Amplifying Showerhead but I keep the adjustable long arm. That Delta Faucet is 3 years old and powerful!
