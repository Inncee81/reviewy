6 FT - BRAIDED 28 AWG - HDMI 2.0 CABLE - 18 GBPS - ETHERNET & AUDIO RETURN CHANNEL

Get your hands on this heavy duty high quality HDMI 2.0 cable! One of a few cables that will drive your 4K TV/Monitor at 2160P/60 Hz (if both ports are HDMI 2.0 ports).

Don’t be fooled by thin Copper Clad Aluminium (CCA) cables. This beauty is made of 100% pure oxygen-free bare copper and is triple-shielded for maximum performance and ultra low SNR.

Never mind it’s thicker than most cables widely sold because a good cable is meant to be thick. Most 6 FT cables use cheap & thin 30 AWG wiring (10 GBPS), which is not enough for 4K @ 60 Hz. SecurOMax 6 FT cable uses thicker 28 AWG wiring which enables 18 GBPS speed required for 4K @ 60 Hz.

Connectors are 24K gold-plated and soldering points are covered by thick aluminium foil to achieve better shielding and even lower SNR.

Ethernet & Audio Return channels eliminate extra network and audio cables. Less cables means slimmer cable management box!

Outstanding HDMI cable

Hi. This business of having to get a “premium” cable to get a better picture seemed like a myth to me. After all, the signal being sent down an HDMI cable is digital, not analog like the old hi-fi audio. Well, something happens when you pump digital data very quickly over a cable and it only gets worse as the data rate increases. Data starts to arrive at the end point (ie: TV or monitor) at different times, data lines interfere with nearby lines (crossover) or data reflects back to the sending unit causing retransmission’s and corruption.I’m saying all that to justify why you need this cable over a cheap one. It is absolutely true that a good HDMI cable will yield a better picture and reliability per the points above. This cable in particular is absolutely superb. The thicker gauge wires conduct signal better and the gold-plated connectors improve connectivity. You will immediately notice the difference when you handle this cable. It’s thick and stout, not flimsy like generic cables.I was able to reach full UHD @ 60 Hz signal on my new TV whereas the old HDMI cable had me thinking something was wrong with the TV as it wouldn’t go beyond 2K @ 30 Hz. Even at 1080p, video quality wasn’t all that great. The different is huge with this cable. I highly recommend the 6’ or (for a little more) the 15’ version.
