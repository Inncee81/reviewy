Works great!

This mask showed up as I was in the middle of scrapping and sanding window frames. I was able to directly compare it to a similar 3M respirator which I’ve been using for 10 years. The MSA mask fits better and is more comfortable. I used it for over 2 hours and didn’t need to adjust the fit during the job as with the 3M unit. It’s light weight and has has a superior field of vision. I later tried it while using some harsh solvent while preparing surfaces to be glued. It did both jobs with flying colors. No hint at all of sanding dust or chemical odor inside the mask.UPDATE: I’ve been using it for 10+ hours now. I’m happy to report that it remains comfortable and highly effective. No aches or jaw fatigue after wearing it even with the straps pulled up a couple notches tighter than I could tolerate on the 3M mask. It keeps out everything: dust, chemicals, and faint odors wafting in on the breezes.No mention of size in the description, it’s a medium. It comes with a nice storage case and a bracket to hang the case on a wall.Further opinions: Half mask respirators are for the birds when you’re dealing with dust. For me any job that rates a respirator rates a full face respirator. I bought the previously mentioned 3M full mask for a nasty overhead scrapping and sanding job. I began this project using an industrial quality half face respirator and safety glasses. I couldn’t make good time on this job because I had to stop frequently to clean or defog the safety glasses, not to mention my eyes getting irritated by fine dust that got around the glasses. After buying at first full face mask I was amazed to find that my productivity doubled. Besides eliminating many stops to clean the safety glasses I was also willing to get directly underneath of my work as opposed to previously working from off to the side. The half mask hasn’t been out of its box since.

Excellent fit, coverage, and comfort.

I’ve been wearing full face respirators for 13 years, mostly 3M, but also North. As nice as the 3M respirators are, they tend to only last me about one year before a strap breaks. Depending on what part breaks, this can be rectified with a replacement part, but it’s still inconvenient and annoying. When my last 3M mask strap assembly broke, only after about 5 months wear, I thought maybe I’d try something new.The MSA Safety Works respirator came in just a few days ago, but I’m pretty excited about this mask. Upon unpacking, my first reaction was that it didn’t quite seem as nicely made as the 3M masks. It doesn’t feel cheap, mind you, but it doesn’t quite have the same feeling/appearance of quality that the nicer 3M models have. I was very impressed with the included carrying case and wall mount (although it would be much better if you didn’t have to remove the cartridges from the mask before putting it in the case).I was very pleasantly surprised, however, with the fit and comfort of this mask. I have sort of a small face I guess, because I sometimes have a hard time getting a good seal on other respirators (I normally wear a small or medium mask). This one size fits all mask had me a little concerned about fitment, but the seal is great. So, too, is the comfort. Definitely the most comfortable full face respirator I’ve owned.Time will tell how well this one holds up. Hopefully I can make it a couple years without a strap breaking. So far I’m very happy with this purchase.
