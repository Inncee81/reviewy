Case, heatsink and price come up considerably short.

I’ve ordered CanaKit’s Raspberry Pi 2 Ultimate Starter Kit in the past for myself and my students, and been quite happy with them. But CanaKit’s new Raspberry Pi 3 Ultimate Starter Kit leaves a few things to be desired which I hope they’ll be able to resolve, as this is otherwise a great way to jump in.1) The position of the processor cutout is in the wrong place in the new clear case. You would need to use a file or jig saw to enlarge the cutout so that it is in the proper position.2) The heatsink which was delivered is considerably smaller than what shipped with CanaKit’s older RPi2 Ultimate Kits, and is also smaller than the heatsink pictured in product shots. Given the higher power output of the Broadcom BCM2837 SoC, I notice that my processor runs considerably hotter during compute-intensive tasks, which is a bit worrisome.3) CanaKit’s kit price is perhaps $15 higher than where it should be, adding up all the component prices. Perhaps this will change as the supply chain grows and demand goes down. We’ll see. I bought my kit despite the obvious price hike.

Excellent product and a good value. Buy with confidence.

Very experienced IT professional here but totally new to the Raspberry Pi. This is a great, completely turn-key computer (but you’re going to have to add a keyboard, mouse, and monitor for a complete, usable system). The (included) 32 GB micro SD card will boot up automatically once power is applied and will by default install the Raspbian operating system for you. I am very impressed (shocked, even) by the performance level of the Pi’s CPU and the video image quality provided by the HDMI connection. Bluetooth and Wi-Fi support is built-in and both are enabled once the Raspbian OS install is complete. Raspbian includes a very simple, clean GUI environment, a special version of Google’s Chrome browser, a PDF viewer, multiple programming languages including Python, Java, and Scratch. It comes with dozens of Python-created games (including the source code), a special version of Minecraft which can be code-modified, and the full open-source LibreOffice office suite. From opening the box to a functional, Internet-connected computer took me about 20 minutes. A Raspberry Pi is still a step into the unknown for many - you will have to be willing to do some research on your own at times after you’ve completed the base installation. But there’s plenty of community support and raspberrypi dot org is a treasure-trove of information and advice for you. I have yet to use the breadboard and electronic components but my 10 year-old son is already searching for available projects.
