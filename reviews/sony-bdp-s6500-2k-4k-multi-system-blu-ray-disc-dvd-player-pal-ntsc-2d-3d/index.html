A terrific multi-system Blu-ray player at a very affordable price

Hailing from the UK (I moved to the United States in 1999) I always missed some of my favorite British TV shows, and so I ended up accumulating quite a collection of DVDs from across the pond. When my old multi-region DVD player finally bit the dust, those discs gathered dust on the shelves for a number of years. Finally needing a new Blu Ray player, I decided to go the multi-region route, and after a bit of research, settled on this as my new player of choice.I was not disappointed! The player switches between zones at the push of a button when you power it on. It plays British and US DVDs and Blu-Rays without a hitch, and does so with excellent picture and sound quality (the only limitation is the 480 resolution of some of those old DVDs). The player connected to my home wireless network without a problem and streams Netflix and Amazon TV shows very nicely too. The unit is slim and compact. The only gripe I have (and it’s a very minor one) is the size and layout of the remote control, which is on the tiny side and a little clunky to use. I have no hesitation in recommending either this player or the seller to you.

A Small but Mighty Blu-Ray

This Sony Blu-Ray player was a replacement for a much more expensive high-end brand player that developed a tendency to “stutter” during Blu-Ray playback. I have been a movie and classical music critic during my working years, and having a reliable and versatile player is a must. This player has been entirely satisfactory during the month I have had it. I have used it for CDs, Super Audio CDs, standard DVDs, DVD-Audio only, Blu-Ray, and Blu-Ray Audio discs, and all work immediately and transparently. The only function I haven’t tested is the manufactyurer-state ability to play DVDs from outside the stated region. (I don’t expect it will be able to play discs in the non-NTSC video standards that are used in much of the Eastern Hemisphere, such as PAL.)The sound is clean, accurate, and full-ranged, worthy of the mid-to-high-end 7-chanel system we have. The picture is equally good. The up-converting of standard definition video discs’ output to a picture approaching HD is excellent, superior to the high-end device the Sony replaced. My wife, who rarely takes notice of technical issues, immediately praised the improved picture and sound.The compact design of the player is noteworthy. Actually, being next to some bigger components it looks likes like a bit of a wimp. But this is the wrong impression, in addition to being trivial. In everything that counts, this player delivers. I imagine most customers would appreciate that this device is no space hog.I think the layout of the rather small remote control is just a little less ergonomic than the previous one. But, again, consumers who feel they are drowning in big, clunky remotes will likely appreciate this feature as well.It came well-packaged. The instruction booklet is clear and useful. Delivery was on time, and Bombay has followed up in a considerate manner, by which I man that hey are not hitting me over the head with post-marketing ads and such.
