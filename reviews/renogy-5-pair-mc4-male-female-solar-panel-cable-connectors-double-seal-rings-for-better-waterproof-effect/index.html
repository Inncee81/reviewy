Specifically there is a round rubber-like collar and round plastic strain relief that fits over the rubber collar, all of which goes into the connector body and under the screw-on cap. When the rubber collar is pushed over the wire’s insulation, it expands to the point where the plastic strain relief won’t fit over it, and then the whole assembly won’t fit into the connector body without using great force. I felt like I was doing something wrong – perhaps I was, but I’ll never know b/c (a) absolutely no instructions whatsoever come with these connectors – just a bunch of loose parts in a bag and (b) no help at all from the Renogy YouTube on this topic – the video doesn’t show the steps involving the rubber collar and strain relief items. Including some instructions on a slip of paper enclosed in the bag would have cost you practically nothing and would have made my experience a lot better.

High quality.

These appear to be the real deal of high quality. Mine have an o-ring where the connectors mate together and an elastomeric bushing around the wire entry that squeezes down when you tighten the “backshell”. It looks like they would make a weatherproof connection as long as your wire is snug through the bushing. (I had to wrap a couple turns of self-vulcanizing rubber tape around 16 ga. wires to fill the radial gap.) The contacts seem suitable for 30 amps and both the pin and socket are protected from accidental contact even when un-mated. Study these a bit before you insert a contact into a barrel –it is possible to insert the pin where the socket should be or vice-versa. Once they are inserted, I don’t think they can be extracted if you made a mistake. Also, I don’t understand why this seller sells this kit with an odd number (5) of mating pairs, since you would almost always use two mated pairs together (one pair for the positive and one for the return).
