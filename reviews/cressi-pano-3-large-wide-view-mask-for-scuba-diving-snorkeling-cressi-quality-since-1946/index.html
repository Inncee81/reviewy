Product Reviews

Decent mask with one unavoidable flaw

I ordered this mask with the hopes of finding a better, 3-panel scuba mask, but ended up returning it.The Good: The lenses and face seal were a nice fit and comfortable. The strap was typical, but I prefer to swap in a 3rd party nylon strap and avoid the straps breaking on me before a dive.The Bad: Unfortunately I had a larger problem with this mask: the buckle straps connect to the hard lens-frame via rubber connectors that are the same material as the strap. Meaning that sooner or later, one of the buckles will break and render the mask useless. Perhaps there’s a reason for this, but I couldn’t help but feel like it was a feature, not an oversight - in order to shorten the usable life of the mask.

Made in Taiwan?

Giving this a 3 rating for now, since I haven’t actually used it yet. Will revise once I get back from hawaii.However, it has a big sticker right on the lenses with a standard safety warning, which shows the mask is made in Taiwan, so all these reviews touting great European silicone and build quality of all Cressi products are bogus. Having said that, the mask does seem much better made than the ones I’ve rented in the past, so I’m hopeful that the manufacturing location doesn’t really matter much. I used a lighter on the inside of the lenses, and yes, you really could see the thin film burn away - then lightly cleaned with softscrub - doesn’t fog up like when I first test-fit it.
