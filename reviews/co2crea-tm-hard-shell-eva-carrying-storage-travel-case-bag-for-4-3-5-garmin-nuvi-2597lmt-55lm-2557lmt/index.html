Good As Long As You Are Storing Cords

I debated the stars on this. First it’s exactly as advertised, a nice hard shell case. It’s lined with a soft gray material - the top part holds my Garmin 2598 snugly (without the little detachable ball socket). In one netted pocket on that divider I put the socket, the other the strap. Inside the larger bottom I’ve put the power cord and USB cord. Now, here’s my issue. That bottom net has a zipper across the middle. It actually makes it difficult to slide bulkier items in and out. I can squeeze in a mount (ARKON GN079WD) as well, but I feel like a few times of doing that I’m going to rip something as it’s awkward trying to get something like that in and out through the small separation allowed by the zipper. I’m thinking it might just make sense to cut that netting out, but then it won’t look as nice…well you see the dilemma. I didn’t want give it four stars when it’s not an all in one solution. However, once I add a second cord (traffic receiver/backup camera) then a mount was never going to fit anyway, and in that case would deserve four stars. So, because it’s so nicely made and sturdy, going to give it four, but keep in mind that center zip design is going to be frustrating for anything other than cords (and still is even with those).

Bigger on the inside than on the outside??

This case is reasonably well made. There are two zippers to close it, which is a nice feature. The case is a hard-enough shell that it will protect the contents if the case is dropped or put under pressure in luggage for example. It feels as if the stiffening is molded plastic, however, and I doubt it would withstand serious crushing forces like metal would.My main reason for writing this review is to report on the case’s capacity for the accessories. Some answers to questions posed by customers indicated that the suction-cup holder for the GPS unit would fit in the case but not the weighted mount. I followed the suggestion of several reviewers and carefully cut away the zippered net over the large compartment. With the GPS itself in the smaller padded compartment, I could fit the weighted holder, power plug, and cord all in the larger compartment. It zipped shut easily, with no strain on the zippers. This may be very dependent on the style of the weighted holder, so I have attached photos to show the Garmin nuvi weighted base, and everything packed into the large compartment of the case.I may still pick up a suction-cup holder before my trip just because the weighted base is so darn heavy, but I have the option–everything does fit in this hard-shell case!
