I even had a guy walking down the street stop and tell me how nice it looks. It is more expensive than many but I believe it is worth it.I installed it myself. The most difficult step was digging the hole. That took several hours. It is a larger than average hole to dig because it is a larger than average size and weight basketball setup. It was delivered on a pallet and weighed about 500 pounds. I used 26 of the 50 lb bags of Quikrete mix. I mixed each bag individually in a wheelbarrow. It was time consuming but not that difficult once I got into a rhythm after the first few. It took about 3 hours to mix it all and fill up the hole. I let it cure for a full week since we had a few days of rain during the week.Setting up the pole and cross/linkage bars wasn’t too difficult. I set it up with my 14 and 16 year old sons. However, I had a friend from work help my sons and I install the backboard. That is the heaviest and most awkward step. Having the extra person made it much easier.The video gave a realistic look at the setup. The directions in the manual were mostly accurate and understandable. The only confusion I had with the directions was about leveling the pole when attaching it to the base. I had to jump ahead to the end of the directions when it talked about how to level the entire setup to understand how to level the pole.We’ve been shooting around several times since installing it and it looks and responds like a pro!
