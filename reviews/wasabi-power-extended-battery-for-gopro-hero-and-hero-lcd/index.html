Product Specifications

  • Seamlessly attaches to the back of your GoPro HERO+ or HERO+ LCD camera to extend battery life
  • Not Compatible with GoPro HERO
  • Provides 3.8V and 2500mAh for extended shooting; Premium Japanese cells for longer battery life with no memory effect; Displays battery level and charging status on GoPro camera
  • Fully compatible with the GoPro HERO+ and HERO+ LCD; Can be charged using any standard USB charger

  • Includes HERO+ extended battery with waterproof backdoor and USB charging cable; All items include 3-year manufacturer warranty

    Product Description

This extended battery attaches to the back of the GoPro HERO+ or HERO+ LCD to increase battery life. It’s perfect for long-duration Time Lapse photo sessions, endurance motorsports racing or any application where you need extended battery life. You can charge with any USB charge from a wall, computer, car, or other USB source.

Compatible with: GoPro HERO+ GoPro HERO+ LCD

Note: Not compatible with GoPro HERO

Product Reviews

Great Battery, Great Price, Much Needed Accessory

The battery arrived quickly as usual for an Amazon transaction. It was a perfect fit for my GoPro. It has multiple indicator lights to show the level of charge, I really like that feature. It charged quickly off my laptop which was important for me because I use my GoPro on a race car and there is rarely an available outlet to plug in a charger. I like the fact that with the extended recording time, I did not need to recharge at all at the race track, the battery lasted all day. We used it 30 minutes on the track, then 30 minutes off while the car was being refueled and prepped, then back out on the track. We repeated that scenario 3 times in the morning then 3 times after lunch. Other guys at the track had other brands of extended batteries and half of them had dead batteries each day by mid afternoon. My Wasabi lasted all day each day. There is a separate power button on the battery, so you have the option of using the GoPro battery first, then turning on the Wasabi battery. I don’t know it that gives longer battery performance or not, I just turned on the GoPro and the Wasabi at the same time and recorded. My only gripe is not with Wasabi, but with GoPro. Their documentation is pathetic. They presume that the consumer knows too much about their product and they leave out important basic information. I noticed on GoPro’s website that many people had the same questions about basic information that should have been in the user’s guide but was left out. I had a glitch where the camera would not turn OFF when the Wasabi was attached, so I simply removed the battery then powered down the GoPro. That is a GoPro issue, not the battery. I also found some of the menu’s on the GoPro were difficult to navigate. 4 other guys with GoPro cameras tried helping me and they also had trouble navigating the menus on my camera as well, so it was not my lack of comprehension; it was poor documentation from GoPro. One piece of advice, when recharging the battery, slow charging via a USB on a laptop is generally better than a quick charger plugged into an outlet or car. Quick chargers can overheat a battery and damage it quickly.

Learn to use it

The Wasabi Extended Battery works great when you learn how to use it. I first thought I received a defective battery because it would stay on when attached to my fully charged Gopro causing it to get very hot, after troubleshooting with Amazon I was sent out a replacement which also did the same thing. The lack of instructions didn’t help, so after messing with them I figured out that one must hold the power button on the Wasabi Battery to turn it off. If not it stays on causing you to use both batteries at once.
