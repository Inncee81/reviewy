Great replacement, “drilled a hole to secure”

I have had this type of lock for several years on all of my windows in addition to the original windows locks. I needed to replace the ones in the bathroom since the screw on the old ones rusted. I shouldn’t have a problem with these since they are aluminum. I’d like to mention, to make these locks secure and not slide around when the window is pushed to open or close and move them, I drilled a hole in the metal frame of the window a little bigger than the screw threads, and put the screw through the hole when I connected the lock to the window frame. The window will not open unless I take the locks off.
