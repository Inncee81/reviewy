Great Little Drive

I recently purchased the drive for backing up multimedia files at work. I got the 2 TB A60 drive and appreciate the military specs as I will be taking it with me everywhere in my work bag and having it protected against shocks will be useful. The drive is solidly built with rubber around all sides and a small rubber flap covering the usb 3.0 port. It comes with a USB 3.0 male to male cable with a full size connector on each side. I don’t plan on losing the cable but it can be difficult to locate this type of cable as they are not the ones routinely (found mini connector on one side and full size on the other).The drive came formatted with FAT 32 and since I work with both PCs and Macs I formatted it for exFAT which provides good compatibility with both PC and Macs, you will have an issue with Linux with this format unfortunately. The plus side is that you can use files larger than 4 GB (limitation of FAT32) with native support on both Mac and Windows. Note that if you are want to use the drive as a TimeMachine backup you must format it to HFS+ (there are work arounds for that but I wouldn’t trust my data to a work around). If you format as HFS+ you will not be able to read or write on Windows without installing other drivers.After formatting it I tested it with the BlackMagic Disk Speed Test on my Macbook Pro Retina (mid 2014) 13.3”, 8GB, 2.6 GHz and it averaged between 100 and 110 MB for both read and writes.
