Great for the price, but outdated model doesn’t compare…

I bought two different pairs of Bolle Goggles, the Mojo and the Carve. Don’t waste your money on these. The Carves are often times only $5-10 more than the Mojo and there is a WORLD of difference between the two. I’ll start with the positives of the Mojo then move on to the differences.The Mojo frame is grippy and I like that! It’s not a hard slippery plastic, which when you have thick gloves on is impossible to grab. Bolle seems to have figured this out quickly as it appears to be the standard frame on all their models. The lens is huge and clear! You can’t really see any part of the frame in your view which is also perfect. I bought the citrus color lens and while it does look red inside the box, once you put them on it is CLEARLY orange. While I did this on accident and I don’t recommend it, I was able to look almost directly at the sun without a problem, so the darkness of the lens is almost perfect for a sunny/partly cloudy day as others have said. They are rated S.2 which fits the description for the weather and view perfectly. On to the differences…First off, the padding on the Mojo is just cheap scratchy foam. It’s breathable like all of their foam but the carve has a nice, soft felt lining on the part that touches your face. With the Mojo your face will be itchy from the moment you put these on.Secondly, the Carve has a double band system which makes it extremely easy to tighten and loosen. What I mean by this is there are two plastic connectors on the back instead of one. With the Mojo you have to pull on one side of the strap, slide the connecter the opposite direction, then adjust the strap on the frame. With the Carves you just grab both plastic pieces, pull them to opposite sides, and done.Overall, the price you probably pay for the Mojo and the quality of the lenses you get are adequate, but they simply don’t compare when you put them up against the newer model Carves. Description Incomplete – These are for Partly Cloudy Days

The goggles seem fine and the price is right, however, be aware that these goggles are for “Partly Cloudy” exposure. The product description at Amazon fails to give any of this information. According to the packaging, goggles are rated by the percentage of visible light transmitted through the lenses. These goggles are “S-2” meaning they permit 37% of visible light through – which is rated sufficient for partly cloudy days. For full sun, you need goggles that allow <20% of visible light. Would be helpful if the description said these were “S-2” and “party cloudy” goggles.
