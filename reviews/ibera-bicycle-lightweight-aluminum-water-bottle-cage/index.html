Excellent for Holding 28 ounce Steel Insulated Bottles

Bough these to replace a traditional set of bottle holders and allow us to bring along insulated water bottles.My chief concern was whether these would be able to support the weight of those bottles. They’re 28 ounce monsters from Costco that are double-wall vacuum insulated. When full of water and ice, they’re actually a few pounds and could easily double as blunt weapons to kill a man. The traditional holders we had on our bikes were no where near big enough, and part of the problem is the design automatically limits the type of bottle.Not the case with the Ibera holders. Because they open to the sides, it was much easier to get the bottles in. I was worried the small tab at the bottom wouldn’t be sufficient, but so far we’ve biked a dozen or so miles with the 28 ounce bottles and they’ve stayed firmly in place. I even tested them by dropping the bike from a foot or so off the ground repeatedly and the bottles didn’t budge.As some other reviewers have noted, these holders will likely scrape up your bottles. But (and this is in response to a rather prominent complaint), I would point out that a little paint or some scrapes and dings on a water bottle does not actually ruin said water bottle. Believe it or not (talking at this other reviewer), a metal cylinder still holds water regardless of what it looks like. That said, if it’s a real concern, I would imagine that a little electrical tape wrapped around the lips of the holder would clear this problem right up.
