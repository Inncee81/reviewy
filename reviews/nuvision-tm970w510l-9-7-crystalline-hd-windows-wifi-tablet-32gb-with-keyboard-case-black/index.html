After trying to troubleshoot it, I was ready to give up and send it back. So, I reset it so none of my information would be on it when it went back. Within Win8.1, I was able to reset the user settings and basically start off as if it was a new PC. Since that was successful, I decided to try setting it up again. Glad I did! That worked! Logged in with no problems and it wanted to sync my OneDrive. Gotta stop that since this will not sync the hundreds of GB. I also turned off Automatic Updates. I did not install any applications or updates and just configured a bluetooth mouse and keyboard. Then I upgraded to Windows 10. There is enough room to upgrade without needing a extra SD card or USB drive. The upgrade went smoothly also. I had to reconnect the bluetooth devices, but that was no big deal.The screen resolution is great!. Actually it’s too good for the few apps I intend to run, so I lowered the resolution. I really like the 4:3 format compared to 16:9 of many tablets this size. I tested some streaming video (with the best resolution of course) and the picture is incredible, but sometimes it buffered. Not a big deal for me, but this is not a high-powered machine. Don’t expect it to play video flawlessly and well as any CPU/graphics intensive apps at lightning speed.It does come with a bluetooth keyboard and case, which is worth possibly 1⁄3 of the whole purchase price. That is a nice bonus though I am not going to use either with this device. There are two layers of screen protection install on the device for shipping: the top layer with printed marketing text. The lower one is clear and can be used as an interim screen protector. There is a little nub that sticks out so you can lift and remove the screen protector completely, but I cut it off. A few people mention in forums that the screen is eerily the same size and dimensions (and resolution) of an iPad air 2 screen, so I ordered a screen protector that size. We’ll see if it fits.As I mentioned earlier, do your homework about this. Google tabletpcreview and this model number and you will find an extensive forum with someone who has also put together all the drivers if you decide to start from scratch. If you are willing to put up with any issues that pop up, this is definitely a great buy. If you want a turnkey product that will work with no problems, you may want to look elsewhere. As you can tell, I am glad I had the patience to stick with this tablet!Updated (4/26/17)Still enjoying the tablet. It’s under-powered, but it’s not my main device. It’s great for media content and other minor programs that I use. One thing that definitely helped after installing Win10 is optimizing the OS. Search on youtube and there are multiple resources.
