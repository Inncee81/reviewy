Not for fine work, but rough work only.

The price is good, the quality no so much. If you need these grinding burr bits for fine work or for art/jewelry then you are taking your chances. Most bits are not straight - they don’t turn “true” and wobble (not my device - I checked). Many of the fine-tipped bits are bent over at the tips - shaped more like hooks than cones. I tested those that I thought were the “better” ones from the batch on a few small soft stones in a water bath. They seem to have kept their grit, but again, once they began spinning I could see nearly all wobbled. This problem could end up costing quite a lot of money to a beginning gem or metal jewelry worker artist.BUT… if you just need something around the workshop to trim off a “little extra something” and aren’t too very worried about the end result then these might be a good purchase for you.
