Product Reviews

This projector won’t disappoint !

This projector was purchased to replace an Epson EX50, 2200 Lumen, projector we acquired in 2008. The rebuilt EX5240 was less expensive than a new model, is a 1000 lumens brighter (plus the decreased output from our aging bulbs, and the EX5240 also has an hdmi port, supporting newer laptops. We used this as a primary projector at a conference and were very please. It’s only drawback is it’s lower resolution than newed projectors. all in all I highly recommend this unit

Love this projector

Love this projector. I plugged it in, connected it to my computer and it worked! A first for me with electronics of any kind. We used it at an event in a hotel meeting room and the image was exceptional. No AV help from the hotel needed which is another first for us.

It’s easy to use and an effective business tool

I’ve been using it weekly for business meetings plus the occasional home movie with the kids. It’s easy to use and an effective business tool. The unit provides the ability to shut off the lamp instantly and then turn it on right away (with the use of a slide panel) without having to wait for the lamp to power up or cool down. That is a really nice feature that has not been available on other projectors I have used. It has great tools also that allow you to easilly adjust your image when the projection is off-center. Overall… great projection unit

The picture clarity is awesome. It works well with any HDMI input including …

The picture clarity is awesome. It works well with any HDMI input including Chromcast. The only reason I gave a 4 star and not 5 star is because this projector does not have audio output. It has only an audio RCA input. If you need to connect to external speakers you will have to connect the source of video to external speakers. If you use an HDMI input like chromcast, you probably need to buy HDMI video audio splitter (around 25$ on amazon) to stream only video through projector and audio to your speakers/sound bar. Hope this this helps.
