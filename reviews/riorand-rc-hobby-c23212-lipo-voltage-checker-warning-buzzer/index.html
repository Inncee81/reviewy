A Great Tool for Protecting Your Batteries

I didn’t know I needed anything like this, but Amazon kept suggesting it so I looked into it. I’m so glad I did. This is a great tool for knowing when your LiPo is running out of power and its time to charge it. I didn’t know that if you let a LiPo get too low it can damage it beyond repair or even cause it to become unstable. This device keeps me well within the safe zone.I do have one con for this item:It is VERY loud. As soon as you plug it in, it beeps at you to let you know its connected, but its extremely startling to myself and anyone else around me because its so loud. Anyone using LiPo’s has seen the LiPo exploding videos, so plugging something directly into the battery and having an immediate loud alarm go off is always going to be startling. When it goes off to alert you of a low battery, pretty much anyone within a 1⁄2 mile line of sight will be able to hear this.Now, this is of course good because you want to be able to hear the alarm if its attached to a quad copter or something, but I would hope that you are not flying your quad copter that far away.

Received a defective one that quit after a short time.

I connected this up to a 3S Li-Po battery I am using for a FPV system on a tripod powering a 5.8 receiver and a 7” monitor to see how long the battery would last if I left it on. Well, I forgot about it and ran the battery all the way down (never heard the low voltage alarm) but it did something to this voltmeter and now it only shows “Err4” or “Err8” on the LED. Don’t know why running the battery for an extended time would damage this device but it did. It isn’t worth the trouble of sending it back so I tossed it and ordered another one from a different vendor (and cheaper too) as it is a handy device for checking voltage before a flight. The low voltage buzzer is very loud and very annoying and buzzes when you first plug it in so unless you are using it inside an RC device and need to hear it, a small piece of electrical tape over the buzzer openings will quiet it down.Edit 10/15//16: I have about 5 of these and don’t trust any of them. No wonder they are so cheap. Some of them work some of the time and others don’t work at any time. If you want to save your expensive LiPos from discharging them too low don’t depend on these things.

It’s a great way to quickly check the status of my Lipo batteries.Per the labeling on the unit, it can be used with 1S-8S Lipo, Li-ion, LiMn and LiFe batteries.Shows overall voltage for entire battery pack, as well as voltage for each cell in the pack. If you do want it for an alarm, it is preconfigured to an threshold of 3.3v on a cell. It can be configured with different thresholds, using a single button on the unit. Instructions are contained nicely on bottom of unit. Granted, you need some good eyesight or glasses to read it due to the small size of the unit. But you would most likely be configuring it once.Measures about 1 inch by 1.5 inch by 0.5 inch. So it’s very easy to have in a pocket as needed, and should be easy to fit in an RC if desired.Overall pleased with it. A simple solution to a common need with Lithium based batteries.

Best investment I have made yet for my hexacopter

Best investment I have made yet for my hexacopter. You mount it on your ‘copter near the front end of the battery, plug your battery’s data cable onto the pins and VIOLA! - It sequentially gives you (1) the overall voltage charge your battery has, then (2) the voltage charge of each cell that makes up your battery. This is GREAT for a last-minute check of your battery’s charge just prior to lift-off. It will sound a (very loud) alarm if your battery’s overall voltage drops below a certain (user-set) point - alerting you to the fact that you need to get the ‘copter on the ground. I won’t fly without one of these aboard.
