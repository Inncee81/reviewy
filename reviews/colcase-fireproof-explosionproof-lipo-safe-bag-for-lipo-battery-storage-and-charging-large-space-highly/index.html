Very good bag at a very good price

The Colcase Lipo Safe Bag is a very good lipo bag that can hold a good quantity of batteries. I fit four 3200mah 3s batteries attached to a balance board and put them (including the balance board) in the bag. Less than an hour later they were fully cooked and ready to come out! And I don’t mean they caught fire!The bag has a carry handle which is convenient when the dual zipper is closed and it’s large enough to fit almost any size battery. It also collapses down flat so it’s very convenient to place in a field or tool box to take to the field.The one issue I see is when connecting batteries directly to the charger (not through a balance board), there may be a limited length of wires coming from the battery (charge and balance wires) not allowing the batteries to lie flat in the bag. With the way I charge and not using balance connector extensions, the battery has to be close to the charger. Therefore, you have to have the battery wires protruding from the bag through a gap in the zipper and then connect to the charger so the bag will have to be close to the charger. I would like to see a port or hole in the lower portion of the battery from which the battery wires can be routed to the outside to connect to the charger. Even better, have the option to purchase a balance board in various types of connectors to connect batteries to INSIDE the bag and then have the charger connections on the outside of the bag.Another enhancement can be a battery divider system so one can transport and store batteries in the bag. Sure, a divider system can be fashioned or the battery connectors can be covered to prevent shorts, but a divider system sold with the bag may be a great option that I would certainly purchase.Overall, for the price, this is a good lipo safe bag that will come in handy for a long time. I am more at ease when charging lipos in this bag when I have to step away for a few minutes. It’s much safer than charging the batteries out in the open. The true test of this bag I hope to never experience, is if a battery decides to go up in flames. More to come on that if it ever happens!
