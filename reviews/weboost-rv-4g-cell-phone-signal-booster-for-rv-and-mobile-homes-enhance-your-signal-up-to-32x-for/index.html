It works!

Initially I had mixed feelings about the Weboost and was ready to return it. We decided to call customer support and they are really helpful in guiding us in the proper way to set up and remove interference. Once we had the right set-up we did see an appreciable boost in our signals. We have T-mobile that is notorious for having a bad signal but I love the all inclusive pricing package of Tmobile vs Verizon or ATT. The Weboost has allowed us to keep using Tmoblie as our only hotspot as we fulltime in our RV throughout the country. We have stayed in BLM land, National Parks and sometimes we go from no signals to LTE. The signals have been good enough to watch Netflix. However, if you are too out of reach of a signal it cannot boost anything that does not exist.

Not perfect, but it has significantly improved signal in a marginal signal area

We replaced a five and a half year old 3G system with this one. Our camp is located in the middle of a national forest with 80- to 100-year-old loblolly pines and various species of oak trees in a county with a small population, so cell signal where we are is lousy for any of the popular carriers. With this signal booster, we usually have decent 3G or sometimes 4G signal. In the past, with the old booster, we obviously didn’t get 4G, but often got 1X or no signal at all. This booster hasn’t solved the problem completely but it is a good improvement over the older booster.We are using a yagi (directional) antenna on a 40-foot antenna tower to reach a tower seven or eight miles away so our situation is probably very different from that of most people using this booster. We did have to use different cabling/connectors from the antenna to the booster than the older booster used.

You’ll need an RV longer than 30 feet to use it

I tried it in a 30 foot class A Motorhome and there was just no way to get enough separation between the inside and outside antennas to prevent oscillation. I was able to get it to work a little by wrapping the inside antenna In aluminum foil with my phone. When I contacted customer support they said that installing it as directed by the instructions was not recommended. In addition, the outside antenna is directional which sounds great but in reality only gives you three directions to aim it. If your rig is facing the tower there is no way to point the antenna at it unless you put the outside antenna in front and the inside antenna (and your phone) in the back.I sent it back and purchased another model from the same manufacturer and it works great.

Was hopeful, but too inconsistent

I really wanted this product to work for us but it was far too annoying to get it working. While we knew that we would have to identify the right direction and mount it each trip, we didn’t anticipate how difficult it would be to get it working. If we got the right direction, then we would have issues with it not liking how close the inside and outside units were to each other. If we attempted to spread them apart, we would lose the increased reception. Just too much of a pain to worry about. We will consider a permenantly mounted option instead.
