Munchkin Tiny Diner 9 Piece Apple Plate and Spoon Set

by people ...

Product Specifications

  • Set includes (3) Lil’ Apple plates and (6) Lift Infant Spoons
  • Lil’ Apple plates have three separate sections with sloping sides for easy scooping
  • Plates stack and store neatly
  • Lift Infant Spoon base keeps tip elevated, off of counters and away from germs

  • BPA-free and top rack dishwasher safe

    Product Description

This Munchkin dining set has everything you need to encourage your Tiny Diner to try solid foods and (eventually) start self-feeding. Pack includes a set of 3 Lil’ Apple plates and 6 Lift Infant Spoons. Lil’ Apple plates have deep sides and dividers to keep foods separate to help little ones scoop to their hearts’ content. And the fresh apple design and bright colors keep mealtime fun for even the pickiest eaters. The Lift Infant Spoons have tips that do not touch countertops when set down, and are made of soft plastic that is gentle on baby’s gums. The shallow spoon bowl even makes it easy for your little novice to eat. Ready, set, feed with ease.

Product Reviews

The apple shaped plates are a hit

We like it. The plates are 3 cheerful colors and the apple shape just adds to the little ones curiosity to eat. The divisions are useful to separate food. My 2 yr old is specific about how her food should not touch the gravy or the fruit an this helps a lot (instead of having individual bowls for veggies and rice for example) They are also dishwasher and microwave safe which is easy for us mommy’s. The spoons are geared more towards infants so we don’t use the spoon much to scoop food off the plate.i would have liked it if they gave 3 spoons and 3 forks instead of 6 spoons but that’s just my opinion and hence the 4 stars. We use her regular spoon/fork instead. The price i think is right for the 9 piece set. We are a happy customer.

Can’t beat the price…unless you don’t need the spoons

My 2-year-old loves these apple-shaped plates! He gets excited when I take them out. I like that the plate is divided into portions just like our ezpz Mini Mat - One-piece silicone placemat + plate (Blue), One Size. For example, we will serve him cheese and crackers int the smaller slots and fruits in the larger area. Unlike the ez pz mat, the Munchkin plates also don’t suction but I’ve found many similar plates saying they offer this feature really don’t as it’s all dependent on your high chair, table, etc.The spoons are definitely perfectly sized an infant, as stated, but for a toddler you’re going to have to upgrade to some proper toddler spoons. If you don’t need the spoons, check out the spoon-less version: Munchkin Lil’ Apple Divided Toddler Plates, 3 Count, which is very reasonably priced and affordable.Somehow on the packaging I received, which may just be for Amazon Vine, it wasn’t clear to me that the plates were microwave safe. They are really easy to store compared to my ez pz mats, which take up a LOT of room, sitting on a shelf all by themselves. I haven’t used the dishwasher to wash these and usually wash these by hands.Also, we keep kosher so having separate colors for meat, dairy and pareve is very useful. I can also see these being repurposed as an artist’s palette once the portions are too small for a bigger kid.

Spoons are fine, dislike the plates.

This set contains 3 plates and six spoons.I like the spoons. I am serving my 3rd child solids for the first time. Previously, I’d used Munchkin soft tip spoons, and those spoons were fine. These I like more, because they are a bit bigger and flatter. This child is a bigger eater, and likes to hold the spoon herself, and these work well for that.I don’t like the plates much. They feel flimsy. Unlike other brands I’ve used, there is no rubber or suction on the bottom that keeps these plates from sliding around. This is annoying, not just because baby can toss the plate off the table easily, but also for me, as I like to be able to stir food on baby’s plate, sometimes to mix it up, or to cool it off. When I try to stir on these plates, they slide around the table. And the partitions are not deep enough for baby to practice her utensil skills on.

Cute but strong
