This fan is almost junk.

For such a big, loud fan, you’d like it would move some air. Unfortunately, that air movement seems to be limited to about 5 feet from the front of the fan. Beyond that, and you hardly feel anything. Yet, when the fan is running even on the lowest speed, it literally sounds like an airplane. This might be good for someone who has this massive fan right in front of their face and doesn’t mind the noise. I just wanted a low noise fan that would circulate air. Too bad this isn’t it. I think it’s because the fan blades are so poorly designed, they almost look flat, No air is going to move with a flat fan. Even my tabletop fan from years ago moves air further and more quietly. This is just utter junk and I should have returned it when I had the chance.

Fire Hazard; Stopped Working After 2 Months

The motor on this unit ran fine at first, but eventually it started to run hot; I’d have to turn it off every few hours and couldn’t sleep with it on (which is the exact reason why I bought this fan). Today, after about 2 months of use, the motor burnt out - it was on for only a few hours - but that was enough to burn the motor out.Honestly I consider this unit a major fire hazard. If it were to fall on it’s back against a rug or carpet then that would probably be enough to start a fire - especially if the unit was kept on in that position for multiple hours.I really hate giving products a 1 star rating, especially in this case because it was a really good fan at first. But since I consider this product the be a legitimate fire hazard I feel the 1 star review is justified, if anything just to shed some light on this issue or perhaps bring this issue to the attention of the manufacturer.Maybe I got a defective unit? I don’t know. All I know is it’s not worth the $75 I spent. I know some shop fans that run 24⁄7 for YEARS and never get hot, heck I’ve had a 20” Lasko for years and it’s never presented any problem with overheating.

Great fan for pushing tons of air. Not a quiet house fan.

I bought this to cool off my garage at the peak of summer. I opened the side door of the garage in the early morning or late evening and turned on this fan and it would bring down the garage temp by 5 degrees in a few minutes. It worked perfectly.Some rain recently leaked through a gap under the side door of the garage and I had a mini-flood in the garage. No problem. I swept out most of the water with a push broom, and then turned this fan on. It dried the floor off in 30 minutes. I then pointed it to the other areas got wet and it dried those out too.If you want to move tons of air and get the job done with gusto, this is a great fan. If you want a quiet fan to gently circulate the air in your house, this is NOT the fan to buy. Speed 1 isn’t too bad if you are near the fan–but it would probably drown out a typical TV or radio volume. Speeds 2 and 3 produce some serious wind noise (and crazy wind), so I don’t use those too much.The fan is not perfectly balanced, so with mine there is a slight vibration, but it is not enough to make it move. The rubber feet keep the fan firmly planted. There are no rattles or other noises–just the motor and the whine of the blades along with a healthy sound of wind.I was curious about the power consumption. Mine uses about 100W on speed 1, 110W on speed 2, and about 125W on speed 3. Perfectly acceptable for me.
