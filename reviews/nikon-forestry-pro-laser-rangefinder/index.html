Nikon Forestry Pro Laser Rangefinder

by people ...

Product Specifications

  • Powerful, multi-use rangefinder by Nikon
  • Measures existing Actual distance, horizontal distance, height, angle, and vertical separation
  • Also features three-point measurement
  • Internal and external LCD display panels; external displays all results simultaneously, while internal displays only selected results

  • Two measurement modes with target priority switch System - first target priority mode and distant target priority mode

    Product Description

In addition to actual distance, horizontal distance, height, angle and vertical separation (difference in height between two targets) measurement functions, three-point measurement (height between two points) is available The results are displayed on both internal and external LCD panels. The external panel displays all results simultaneously. Two measurement modes (Target Priority Switch System) are available: First Target Priority Mode displays the range to the nearest target taken from multiple results obtained with a single measurement. Distant Target Priority Mode displays the range to the farthest target taken from multiple results obtained with a single measurement - especially useful for forestry and hunting. High-quality 6x monocular with multilayer coating produces bright images. Waterproof (up to 1 meter for 10 minutes) but not for underwater usage. The battery chamber is water resistant. High-eyepoint design allows easy viewing even with eyeglasses.

Nikon Forestry Pro Range Finder

Actually what I needed for work! This unit is accurate, sturdy, easy to use and understand, compact, easy to read in bright sunlight. I work in the Telecommunication field and I needed a range finder to measure distance between utility poles and this unit fit the bill. You could purchase the TruPulse device, but expect to pay around $800 + for their range finders - I purchased the Nikon Forestry Pro for $300 and saving $500 is a big plus. The only negative to the device is the lazer does not display ranges less than 30 feet, but in my field that is not a big deal, because I can use my measure wheel for distances less than 30 feet. I would recommend this device to everyone looking for a range finder.

Crane operator’s best friend

I am a mobile crane operator, and I use this tool at least weekly if not daily. It helps me figure radius and boom length required for different situations. The best feature is the ability to show distances in feet. All of my crane charts are in feet, so there is no excess converting required. It is also very accurate. If I am going to work I don’t leave home without it. I would say it’s the most useful tool I have purchased as a crane operator.
