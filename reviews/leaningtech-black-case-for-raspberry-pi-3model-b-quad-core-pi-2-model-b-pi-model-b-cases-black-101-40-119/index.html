These are great cases for projects like a Retropie where you only need the ports open. The rest of the case is enclosed aside from a mesh at the back for air venting. A lot of other cases have large cut outs to access things like the GPIO pins, but that’s not needed for a single-use project. The case fits together well and there is not movement or play once screwed together.

Perfect for simple applications

I really enjoy this case a lot! The aesthetics of the case are a highlight, but also its simplicity. It’s a low profile case with no corners and as few edges as possible, creating a sleek look. The micro SD card access on the rear panel of the case is a huge plus, as I am always swapping cards out.The only problem that I have with this case is that it doesn’t vent heat very well. Other cases will feature a vent on the top that is in one shape or another- a raspberry, for instance. There’s no good way for this to dissipate heat, even if you have heat sinks.If you’re going to be using your PI for very simple tasks, this case is great. If you’re going to be putting a bigger load on your CPU, find a case that ventilates heat a lot better.
