Great bass, very powerful and sturdy - my favorite bluetooth speaker!

When I received this set of bluetooth speakers, I put it through my usual testing protocol.First, I checked the bluetooth connection. The speakers paired effortlessly with my Ipad, iPhone and Mac. It also had no issue maintaining the connection when I walked from the living room to the opposite end of the apartment in the bathroom.One thing I noticed is that the Vomach speakers don’t have a voice saying “power on”, “connected”, etc. Instead they just make a chime. Personally I don’t mind, in fact I actually prefer the chime vs voice, since the voices are often weird and super Chinese.In terms of construction quality, the Vomach looks and feels very good. I know this ultimately doesn’t mean much, since I’ve had products that felt cheap and were instead excellent, and others that felt good but were of very poor quality. So, even if it’s not too relevant, these speakers definitely get a thumbs up for how they feel.I put the Vomach against what were my favorite speakers (the Geega) and went on to test it with a variety of music, from high pitched vocals and piano, to dance and heavy metal.The Vomach speakers performed flawlessly in every situation, not only that, but they obviously outperformed the Geega thanks to deeper bass, and an overall richer and more room-filling sound.The Vomach speakers also happen to be splash resistant. Which means, you can’t submerge them in water, but if you want to use them in the shower or outdoor (where rain or swimming pool splashes can occur) you don’t have to worry about them getting destroyed. The buttons are sealed and the mini USB charging outlet is protected by a rubber tab that provides a good seal. Personally, I would rather use a cheaper speaker set if I’m in the shower or outdoor, simply because in these situation you don’t really need the best sound quality you can get, and I would rather not have to find the limits of what my $50 set can withstand. But that’s just me. It’s good to know that if something involving water were to happen, the Vomach should be fine.The Vomach also comes with a rummer loop and carabiner, so it gives you more options in term of where to put it.Now, the price. I noticed some other reviewers subtracted 1 star because they believed the price was too high. As I mentioned before in my other reviews, the star rating should reflect how good the product is, not how good of a bargain it is. So, for me this is a 5 star product. You are paying for the quality you are getting. The Vomach is more powerful than the Geega, in spite of both being rated at 10W, which means that the Geega is probably overrated.UPDATE 08/14/16: Since the writing of this review, I tested the Vomach against two more speakers, and while they were very good, the Vomach is still my favorite! Additionally, I noticed that they lowered the price from around $50 to $33! So, if at $50 I believed that you were getting a good value for your money, at $33 this speaker is an absolute bargain, as it outperforms every other speaker I tested in the same price range.If you have any questions, let me know, I’m happy to help!
