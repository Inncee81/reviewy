AT&T Cisco 3G MicroCell GPS Antenna for Wireless Network GPS signal Extender

by people ...

  • All AT&T Microcell home cell units are required to have GPS signal to operate properly
  • The GPS antenna with extended cable allows you to place GPS antenna near a window without having to have whole unit be nest to a window
  • High gain GPS antenna has a built in 20 Db singnal amp to give you best receprion
  • Unit will detect the lowest GPS signals through the window and channel it directly to your Microcell unit for best performace

The AT&T 3G Home Microcell Wireless Network Extender requires GPS signal to operate properly. Inside the house where your microcell is located you could have little or no GPS signal at all….To get proper signal you will need to locate a GPS antenna next to a window. This unit will detect and amplify the GPS signal and channel it into your Microcell for best possible reception and operation.

Does not lock on to GPS any quicker than before. HOWEVER, I no longer have to set the AT&T micro-cell in the windowsill! AT&T really should have included this like Sprint does with their Airave.This product does what it says it does. Now I can move the AT&T micro-cell out of the window and on to the shelf, next to the Airave. (Footnote… An AT&T micro-cell can share the same DSL line with a Sprint Airave).This is also supposed to be a Cisco product, per the Amazon listing which reads “by Cisco”. I purchased this one, even though it was a couple bucks more than the off-brand one. This is NOT labeled “Cisco”, either on the device or on the packaging (a simple plastic baggie). That doesn’t mean it isn’t a Cisco product as there is no brand label whatsoever. The sticker on the back only reads “GPS Antenna” and lists the frequency & voltage - no company name at all. It does strike me odd that a company like Cisco would pass up the chance to stick their brand name on everything. My gut check says this is a knock-off and not an official Cisco product. I mention it here because if you are looking for an official Cisco branded product, you should probably just buy the cheaper off-brand one from Amazon.
