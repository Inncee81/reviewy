Harman Kardon Aura Wireless Stereo Speaker lacks a Digital Optical Input shown in online manuals

My 2-star rating for the HK Aura Wireless Stereo Speaker may change to a higher rating if two issues are resolved with this recent purchase. First, the HK Aura Wireless Speaker System that I received is not as described in the HK online Owner’s Manual and in the Quick Start Guide. This is NOT the product in the pictures or the title, but it’s still AWESOME.

This is NOT the “HARMAN/KARDON AURA” this is a lesser known model, the “HARMAN/KARDON AURA STUDIO” (which explains the enormous discount). This is advertised as being the “Aura”, but it is not. This “Aura STUDIO” version has the same great design and sound, but connectivity is different. This model does not have Wi-Fi connectivity, input source control, USB 2.0 port, optical audio out, or even a remote app (and therefore no bass level control).Another small design difference is in the volume control. The “Aura” has widely spaced plus and minus touch sensitive buttons which you can slide your finger between to adjust volume. This “Aura Studio” model has closely spaced touch sensitive plus and minus volume control buttons with no swipe volume functionality. The circular light in the middle also does not change to reflect the volume level.Now, what the “Aura Studio” DOES have is bluetooth connectivity, an aux port, and a micro USB port for service. Also, INCREDIBLE sound. Its incredible how the DSP tech in this thing widens the stereo sound so you really do get left and right channel stereo sound from a single device. The bass is rich, warm and very very powerful. The 4.5” downward firing sub will literally shake a room and seems to never distort even at the highest volume. The H/K Aura Studio really does fill a room with sound. Standing 15 feet away, it sounds just as loud as it does 2 feet away.So no, you’re not getting a $400 speaker for just over $200, you’re getting a lesser version. It is very deceptive for this to be sold as an “Aura”, advertising all of the features and showing images of the “Aura” when in fact what you get is a version that has significantly less connectivity options. I hope everyone who buys this reads this or another review noting the difference.
