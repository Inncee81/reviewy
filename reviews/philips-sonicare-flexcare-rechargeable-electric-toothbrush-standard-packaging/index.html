The Oral B didn’t perform as well, performance of the Oral B decreased fairly quickly (brush head quit rotating with the slightest bit of pressure) after a year or so. Based upon past experience, the Phillips Sonicare is a much better device, and vs. the Oral B maintains its performance for an extended period of time. A very good toothbrush, but most of the fancy features are unnecessary for the majority of people. Go with the Easy Clean model.

My dentist had recommended Sonicare to me, and told me that any model that comes with their Quadpacer timer would be fine. You basically divide your mouth into four sections, and the brush will notify you every 30 seconds so that you can switch to brushing a different section. It seems kind of silly and trivial, but it really has gotten me to brush more consistently. I always brush for the full two minutes now, and each corner of my mouth gets a full 30 seconds.I’ve been using the less expensive Sonicare Easy Clean model for about 6 months now, and I jumped at the chance to upgrade to the Flexcare Plus. Unfortunately, I’ve been pretty disappointed with the extra features that comes with the Flexcare.The travel case is NOT the same as the USB charging case, in case [sic] anyone is wondering. It’s just a plain, plastic, clam shell case that will hold the toothbrush and the two brush heads. The Flexcare Plus and my old Easy Clean actually have the same charging base, but now it’s called the travel charger. The Flexcare Plus also comes with a wider, plastic, snap-on base that you clip around the travel charger to give it a wider footprint along with two holders for extra brush heads.The Flexcare not only has the regular brushing mode with Quadpacer, but it also offers modes for gum care, refresh, sensitive, and massage. They basically offer different vibration settings and cleaning patterns. If you have incredibly sensitive teeth and gums, then you might find some value in buying a Sonicare model that offers different intensity settings, but I feel like the regular cleaning mode on these brushes is gentle enough for most people. I tend to get sensitive spots on my teeth and gums, and I’ve never had any issues with my Easy Clean model being too strong.Overall, the Flexcare Plus is still a great toothbrush, but I feel like the extra features are going to go unused for most people. If you travel a lot, you really should splurge for the high-end model that comes with the USB charger. Otherwise, I think the basic Easy Clean model is definitely the best bang for your buck.
