Product Reviews

Very Sturdy Mount

I purchased this mount as a alternative to sticking the suction mount onto the windshield or dashboard. This mount is a far better solution. The blades that fit into the CD slot are rubberized so once it’s in place and tightened down, it does not move. So far, I’ve had no problems with the mount loosening over time. The GPS device itself doesn’t vibrate or move as you’re driving, and once set at the proper viewing angle, stays in place quite well. See the photos attached of the mount installed in my 2014 Ford Focus and with my nuvi 255W attached. I had to install the mount “upside down” as the dash in my car slopes away towards the CD slot. There’s plenty of clearance all around so that the GPS can be viewed at any angle. I’d highly recommend this mount.

Perfect mount

I was skeptical about this but it is the best mount I have ever had. Fits securely in my Tundra, does not bounce, and places the GPS in a great location. No suction cup marks to worry about or things sliding across the dash.

Works Well

I have tried this gadget in two cars and find that it works pretty well. The connection ball holds my Garmin nuvi 2460 very securely. In my 2008 Volvo 70V, it fits best with the tightening screw downwards, while in my 2013 Hyundai Azera, the screw needs to be on the top to hold it securely - and that is even a little shaky because the CD slot opening is cut in further on the top edge than the bottom. I have driven over railroad tracks, and it stays in place. In the Azera, it gets a little in the way of some control buttons, but it will work. I have built in navigation on the Azera but it is crap, and the data is outdated already.

Nice bracket, but a couple notes of caution.

Very nice mount. Holds very securely in CD player slot. Pay attention to caution about over-tightening, as this could easily cause damage to CD player if over-tightened. Depending on where your CD slot is located, part of your sound system display may be blocked by a GPS unit mounted on this. Suggestion for improvement would be for the arm that goes to the ball-mount to be adjustable, either via a goose-neck or another ball-joint at the base of the arm. . My CD slot is at the very top of my system, and GPS still blocks the top third of my system display. I plan on buying & cannibalizing another cheap mounting bracket to make a short extension & add another point of articulation so my GPS can be adjusted out of the way of the sound system display.

Liked the idea. not so much the result

This is a great idea, but unfortunately, it didn’t work for my application. Easy to set-up and remove, puts GPS in a great location. It does tend to bounce and move when driving, a little more than I liked or wanted. If you are going to use this for a rental car then this would be fine if you are planning on using this in a more permanent situation, them I would look for something else.

like I was

The placement in the middle of the dash takes a little getting used to compared to what you may like about windshield height, and it may obscure some of the radio display, but the mount is solid once you tighten the adjusting screw.
