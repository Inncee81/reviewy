Decent monitor, a bit flimsy/unstable, terrible controls

It works, and looks nice, though I haven’t compared the image quality side by side to other monitors. Surprisingly lightweight, though the base seems a bit flimsy, which may be an issue if subject to frequent motion or vibration (e.g. in an RV or boat). Since the base is light and relatively small for such a large screen, the monitor could be easily knocked over if you live with cats or klutzes, so you may want to place it defensively against a wall.At its brightest setting, the screen is extremely bright (painfully so for dungeon dwellers), and can be adjusted in 1% increments to make it very dim.The controls are downright convoluted. The five buttons on the frame’s lower right underside let you navigate a menu of settings, which can be saved in five preset groups of settings. This allows you to switch between a group of settings you prefer for word processing vs. gaming. But the settings user interface is so non-intuitive that it’s quite confusing to figure out. Simply changing the screen brightness to compensate for your room’s current brightness is needlessly time consuming, and even frustrating until you figure it out. Since brightness is such a basic setting, it would have been better accomplished with a brightness knob. Or to save a few cents on manufacturing, with separate “brighter” and “dimmer” buttons.

White Trails of Shame

I ordered the Asus VS238H-P to match an identical model from several years ago. When I set it up with the same HDMI feed, the colors didn’t match at all. I’m a color TV broadcast engineer by trade, and I used to repair the old CRT TV monitors. I know what they’re supposed to look like. Undeterred, I got them close enough and went about my work. It didn’t take but a minute to notice the white trails behind every movement of black text or graphics, as if the refresh rate was not the advertised 2mS, but about 200mS. It looked like some of the lousy monitor/graphics card combos of the early 1990s. I thought I might ignore the defect, but I just couldn’t. Yes, this monitor had a black handle on the box. I didn’t want to risk getting another by asking for a direct replacement. Fortunately, Amazon took it back for a full refund. I went out and bought the slightly newer VX238H-P with a white handle on its box and got perfectly matching color and no trails of shame.
