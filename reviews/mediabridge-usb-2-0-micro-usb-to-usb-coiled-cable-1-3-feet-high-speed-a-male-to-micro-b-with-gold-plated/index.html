    Product Description

Mediabridge™ 2.0 Micro-USB to USB Coiled Cable

Sync or charge the latest Smartphones, tablets, MP3 players, mobile phones, PDA’s, digital cameras/camcorders & more, from your laptop, computer (Mac/PC) or other USB-enabled device. The coiled function stretches up to 3FT when necessary, & neatly retracts back to 1FT for portability. Corrosion-resistant gold-plated connectors & foil/braid shielding, gives this cable long-term use & defense against RF/EM interference, as well as bandwidth loss. A tough, yet flexible PVC jacket, well-defined fins, & durable connector mold ensures secure fits & easy installs. Full USB 2.0 capability; including 480 Mbps data transfer speed & backwards compatibility with older versions.

Product Features - Sync/charge Smartphones, tablets, MP3 players, mobile phones, PDA’s, digital or cameras/camcorders, from your laptop, computer & more - Coiled design can stretch from 1FT-3FT - Corrosion-resistant gold-plated connectors & foil/braided shielding - Full USB 2.0 capability, 480 Mbps data transfer speed & backwards compatibility - Tough, yet flexible PVC jacket, well-defined fins & durable connector mold - Transfer files, update software or charge devices

Compatibility - Mobile devices with a Micro-USB port, like Android, Samsung, Kindle, HTC, Motorola, Sprint, Nokia, LG, HP, Sony & Blackberry - Charges Samsung Galaxy Note 3 at full power - Doesn’t work with the Samsung Galaxy Tab series

Installation Plug-&-play: Plug the standard Type-A Male connector into your computer, and the Micro Type-B connector into your other device to begin using

Notes - Verify your device has a Micro-USB port & not a standard USB, or Mini-USB port - Do not stretch over 3FT

Solid and firm cable - Will NOT do 5 feet like it says

I picked up this cable specifically to keep it in my car. This cable is coiled with two straight ends. Between the front seats of my car (an Equus) is a covered compartment and inside is a USB plug. I am able to plug this in, and the straight part fits perfectly to the top of the covered compartment and out a small notch, allowing me to close the top and have the cable sticking out. The coiled part is very firm, so if you plan on stretching it, it is going to coil back up unless you keep some pressure on it (the weight of your phone will not do it if that is what you have in mind).It’s actually quite short, a realistic length is just under 2 feet. The specs say it stretches to 5 feet, but in reality, you can forget about that. If you need to do that with a cord, get a 6’ cable, not this thing. This works great for small spaces (like a car) where you might want to have it extend as needed a little bit (few feet max) and that’s it. It is a very sturdy cable, fits firmly into my phone (Samsung Note) and the USB plug. I only gave it 4 stars because it was wrong of them to say it is good for up to 5 feet. While it might actually be stretchable to that length, it’s not at all practical.
