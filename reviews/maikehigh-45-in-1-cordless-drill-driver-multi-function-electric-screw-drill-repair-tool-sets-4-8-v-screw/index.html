A great drill driver for light duty tasks and the case / bits are SUPER helpful

Quick Tip: For light duty jobs, the MAIKEHIGH Cordless Drill Driver does the trick. It is light and comes with a super handy carrying case with a ton of bits. The Ryobi Cordless Screwdriver sells for about the same price and is a little more powerful, but by the time you purchase all the bits, you’ll be into it for a lot more than this (selling for around $30 at the time of this review).I purchased this to help out with light duty assembly of furniture and such. I have a cordless and a corded drill for heavier duty assembly projects - but they are heavier and really cumbersome when it comes to light duty tasks. This is small, lightweight, it gets in tight spots easily - and after testing it for about a month, I think it does a great job. The battery holds a long charge, it has plenty of power for light tasks.What I like:* The trigger controls both clockwise and counter-clockwise spinning by just pushing the top or the bottom* The battery really seems to last* The carrying case with all the bits - I can’t tell you what a plus this is… when you grab it, you want to do your job quickly and not have to search around for the bits… and this thing has tons of bits - I haven’t hit a job yet where these bits didn’t do it* light weight - very easy to hold and use for a long time while putting in a ton of screws or boltsWhat I didn’t like as much:* The bits don’t fit tightly in the tip of the driver - so they tend to fall out between screws / bolts… not a bit deal, but can be a little bit of a nuisance* The bits themselves are a lower grade casting… while I haven’t stripped one yet, it is only because I have been super careful… I make sure I am using the right Phillips head bit for the job and make sure the hex heads are well seated before drillingThese things aren’t huge concerns, but enough to keep me from going full glow on a five-star review. Overall, I recommend this because of its ease of use and super convenient and helpful case / tips.
