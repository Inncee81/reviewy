Quality Set Of Bits, Just A Note To The Extremely Fine Tipped Ones…

I purchased this set of bits to fit my Dremel 8220 to cut ornamental shapes out of wood as well as do some edging on the dog house we’re building. I’m really happy with this set. I looked around, and these were definitely the best deal for the quality and materials used to make these bits. They come in a container with an individual space for each bit that feels sturdy and has helped keep them organized and sharp. Honestly, I haven’t had any problems with these bits. That being said, I’ve taken the typical precautions of not sinking the thin tipped bits too far into the board which, could break if they are driven in too far and moved to either side-but that’s just the nature of bits in general (in my own experience at least!). I’m not even sure if that would be the case with the tungsten steel carbide, but because some of these bits are so fine at the ends it would still be prudent to maintain the tip. The tungsten steel carbide bits will last you a lot longer than whatever it is they sell at Harbor Freight or the typical steel bits you’ll find in similar stores so keep in mind that the extra cost you might be paying for these will more than pay off after a while. Nice for fine detail work

Nice carbide bits for fine detail wood carving. They fit my older Dremel tool collet perfectly without slipping or jamming. They seem, so far, to be well balanced. I haven’t tried every single one yet. These are not for aggressive work or removing large amounts of wood. They are small points of various shapes with accordingly fine teeth. 20 carbide cutters for under 20 bucks is a really good deal in my opinion. Nice little plastic case too.

Ok for the money….

Also shafts are not hardened, so high rpm with to much pressure and you will most certainly bend the shaft. But that said these burrs aren’t several hundred dollars a set either. So probably depends on application, these will work for most general cutting where carbide is required.
