    Product Description

Size:Standard The Movo GM100 Lavalier Clip-on Omnidirectional Condenser Microphone is designed for video use and provides clear and accurate voice reproduction. Newscaster-style design provides effective omni-directional pickup pattern. This is the best performance+cost value available for your complete video needs.

Unit is extremely lightweight, designed for optimum portability and comfort, and delivers some of the clearest recorded sound you can find. The Movo GM100 Lavalier Clip-on Omnidirectional Condenser Microphone is compatible with the HERO3 & HERO3+. Microphone performance is much better than most built-in camera microphones: outstanding for interviews, podcasts, dictation, and much more. This microphone is a workhorse, never get caught with mushy, muffled sound again.

Windscreen & Tie-clip are included. The components are extremely compact and fit inside many standard camera cases. The GM100 includes 1-year manufacturer warranty.

Poor low frequency performance, relatively crisp otherwise.

It’s pretty good for most applications, but I am finding that it does very poorly with very low frequency sounds. I use this primarily inside a car and the mic will cut out when I go over a hard enough bump that results in a low frequency thump, it also will cut out when I have the windows down and there is any degree of buffeting. I do not mean wind noise itself, which you can hear on the mic, but the lower frequency buffets. These also cause the mic to cut out for a brief moment. I’m not complaining about wind noise (Ive generally use a dead cat wind muffle), just the poor low frequency performance.Outside of that the mic is quite sensitive and crisp, unfortunately my sole application requires it perform better with low frequency.

Movo GM300 Lavalier Lapel Microphone Great For Go Pro

I just purchased a Go Pro 4 Silver Surf Edition and after the 1st 2 minute trial video, I had little to no sound! With the waterproof case that comes with this, the small mic on the camera is completely covered so you will have no sound. After reading up on a remedy, I saw that an external mic is recommended by other users. I reviewed several and kept coming back to this one. Just an FYI, if you purchase an external microphone make sure you have a skeleton case in order to have access to the side ports of the camera. With that said, I tried it our for the 1st time yesterday, both with and without the microphone. Without, even with the skeleton case covering the mic on the camera, there is little to no sound unless you are literally one or two inches away. I hooked up this microphone and the quality is great! Included is a battery for power (don’t remember the exact size but a general watch battery size) and On/Off switch. With the included lapel clip, I am able to attach it to the screw on the side of the housing without it getting in the way. The cord is about 9 feet long, plenty of room to maneuver around. There is also another clip, a belt clip, to keep it attached to your person or on a stick. I haven’t tried out the sensitivity but it did pick up sound from another room in my house, about 20+ feet away.This may not be the most elaborate microphone on the market but for shooting routine videos, live streaming, even catching yourself out in a boat hauling in big fish or about to drop that monster buck, I can see it doing the job. For the price of this vs. the $100 plus “pro” microphones, I am quite happy.
