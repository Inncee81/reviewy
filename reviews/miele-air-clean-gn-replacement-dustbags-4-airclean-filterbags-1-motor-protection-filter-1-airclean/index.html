Expensive, but long lasting. A good value in the long run.

They’re a lot more expensive than the paper bags used by most vacuum cleaners, but they seem to pack a lot more dust and dirt before the airflow slows down. I’ve been using my Miele canister vacuum for a solid two weeks and haven’t seen any loss of suction, and other reviewers here say that they get as much as six months from a bag. The bags are constructed of a non-woven fabric that’s thicker than paper bags and seems to have more possible paths for the air to follow, so even when it’s nearly full, plenty of air can flow and carry dust in. However it works, it does hold a lot more dirt than other bags. When you consider that a box of four bags comes with a pair of disposable filters as well, it starts to look like an even better deal. At any rate, I’m sold on the Miele and these bags.

What a Dustbag!

How do you review a vacuum cleaner dustbag, of all things? I give the Miele bags 5 stars. Does that mean, as Amazon describes the star system, that I “love” them!!!??? Well, no. It’s a dustbag. But like virtually all Miele products, these are excellent … dustbags. Seriously, they are made of high-quality materials that leave no residue from micro-fine dust leakage inside the vac.
