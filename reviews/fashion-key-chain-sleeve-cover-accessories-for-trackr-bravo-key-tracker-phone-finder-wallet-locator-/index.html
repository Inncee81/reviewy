I purchased a four pack of the TrackR Bravo, gold color. They are the size of a Kennedy half dollar (fifty cent piece) and came with a convenient key ring.The app setup of the Bluetooth locator device was simple but I had previously setup an account. Initially I downloaded the app so I could locate my cell phone at home utilizing my Amazon Alexa and the free TrackR skill.The TrackR Bravo works, it also pins a last known location on a Google map type application within the TrackR app. Upon initial setup of the Bluetooth locator between the device and a cell phone, the app states that a 82 decibel audible sound. I can hear the sound two rooms away in my house and my hearing isn’t the greatest. You can extend the duration of the sound of the device from 1 to 10 seconds if you enable either phone separation or device separation alert.Overall the construction of the device is sturdy, has a thin build frame and is the size of a fifty cent piece. I’m looking forward to losing my keys so I can actually use the TrackR Bravo for its intended purpose but then again I hate losing my keys. I may place another TrackR Bravo in my car so I can easily locate my car in a shopping center parking lot.Hopefully the TrackR team will re-engineering the Bravo Bluetooth so it’ll have more features such as a 50 lumens LED light or a MicroSD usage. Don’t hesitate purchasing a four pack, you’ll end up wanting more.
