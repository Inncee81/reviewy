Product Specifications

  • At the heart of the product is the Intelligent EV3 Brick with powerful ARM9 processor, USB port for WiFi and Internet connectivity, Micro SD card reader, back-lit buttons, and 4 motor ports
  • Equipped with touch, color, and infrared sensors
  • Weapons include: tri-blade blender, heavy hammer, blasting bazooka, and gripping claws/arms
  • Intuitive software (PC and Mac) with icon-based drag-and-drop programming interface

  • Control and command your robot with the enclosed remote control or download the free app for your smart device

    Product Description

Unleash the creative powers of the new LEGO Mindstorms EV3 set with the most advanced technology LEGO ever developed to create and command robots that walk, talk, think and do anything you can imagine. Follow the step-by-step 3D building instructions to create five different models: TRACK3R, R3PTAR, SPIK3R, EV3RSTORM and GRIPP3R and bring them to life with an easy, intuitive and icon-based programming interface. Grab the enclosed remote control and take on challenging ready-made missions or download the free app and command your robot using your smart device. The LEGO Mindstorms EV3 robot revolution has begun!

Product Reviews

Great Product, but misleading advertising

Great Product, but misleading advertising. Exact same thing is available from Amazon for $120 less. Just does not say “Customizable Robot w/ Sensors” in the title. The product is LEGO part # 31313 Mindstorms EV3 Robot. Software is a free download from LEGO, no documentation on software is included.

You Can’t Put a Price on an Education

I bought this for my nephew, whose 10, to start learning code and, not just code, but to see in real life how it can effect the world around us. This is definitely a pricey item (far pricier than Lego’s typical items), but not only do you get what you pay for, you really can’t put a price tag on a real-world education. I only hope that it is challenging enough for him so that he won’t get bored and will always want to do bigger and better things. My next purchase for him will be the Raspberry PI and maybe even a drone.
