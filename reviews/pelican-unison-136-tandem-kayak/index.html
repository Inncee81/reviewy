    Product Description

The Pelican UNISON 136T blends polished design with outstanding overall performance. Built on a multi-chine flat bottom hull platform, this sit-in tandem kayak provides a high level of stability, great manueverability and tracking, and superior capacity. Pelican used extensive computer modeling in its design process to reduce drag, improve tracking and cruising speed. The UNISON 136T features adjustable footrests and the ERGOFIT seating system for increased comfort, a quick lock hatch with a 60 L storage bag, a cockpit table with a 4” day hatch and a bungee cord to store your personal items, paddle tie-downs and a keel extension. Product Reviews

Perfect tandem kayak!

Love this kayak!!! Plenty of leg room for my 6’3” hubby, and The Little enjoys riding in front. We got this so we can all kayak together, and are now thinking about selling my single and getting another tandem so our kiddo can bring a friend, or the German Shepherd. What I really like is the stability. Hubby and I went out on the lake together the other day, and we were both able to fish and paddle comfortably. Love the dry compartments, and the divider between front and back, and the cup holders! Have looked at other tandems and I’m so glad we went with this one, we initially bought based on price, but in comparing others after the fact, nothing really measures up. So very happy with this kayak, seems very durable, comfortable even with fishing poles and gear, and amazingly stable. Highly recommend.

Great family kayak!

Quite impressed when it arrived. Much better quality than I was expecting. Seats are comfortable and have backs that adjust to your incline preference. Pods are very spacious, I have no problem with my 3 year old between my legs, he’s not in the way of paddling at all and still has plenty of room to move and turn around as he desires. The kayak is very well balance, “nearly” impossible to flip over (on calm water). My 3 year old was just about hanging out trying to see the fish and it didn’t even come close to the feeling we might tip. We just glided through the water with little effort. The dry storage compartment in the back is actually quite large, and there are straps on the front to put a bag or whatever under.My main concern was the weight of the kayak, as I’m not the strongest woman ever and have my 10 year old won’t be able to carry an end for too long. But the kayak is actually quite light. I’m able to easily maneuver it onto and off the dolly, into and out of the water, and pull it around on my own (even with my 3 year old in it).I bought it at Dick’s Sporting Goods for $100 less during their sale, but otherwise this is the same price I found it everywhere ($699).
