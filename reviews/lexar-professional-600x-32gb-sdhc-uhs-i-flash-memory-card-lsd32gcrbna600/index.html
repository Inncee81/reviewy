Product Reviews

Pathetic Write Speed. Get the SanDisk Extreme Pro Instead.

I purchased this card for use in my Nikon D810 and was very disappointed by it’s performance. It’s advertised as a high speed Class 10 card with READ transfer speed up to 600x (90MB/s). That may be true for reads, but write speeds for this “600X” Lexar card are pathetic. After filling the camera’s buffer, it took close to two minutes (yes, minutes) to write the 18 raw images. Testing in the SD card slot on a late 2013 MacBook Pro Retina confirmed that the write speed is pathetic there as well, with write speed measuring less than 1/10th of the read speed (well under 10MB/s).This card might work for those looking for a high capacity card that is written to infrequently and read often, such as storing music/photo/video libraries, but I wouldn’t recommend using it in your camera. I returned my Lexar cards and purchased the SanDisk Extreme Pro 64GB instead.The SanDisk Extreme Pro performs flawlessly in the D810, making it possible to capture several more frames before the buffer fills and emptying the buffer in seconds (~20s), rather than minutes, which the Lexar card required.

I sent it for service and got a replacement back within 10 days.I’m going to try this one again, but also backing up any additional files to an external drive. I wouldn’t put anything critical on this disk based upon my experience and other reviews. Pass, heading back to PNY

Won’t Try this Brand again. I have a Canon T3i as my backup, portable camera. I took it with my 24mm EF-S lens as my point and shoot on a family vacation. I put this card, brand new in my camera before our trip. I came home and 2 or 3 of the pictures wouldn’t read. Then, I used my camera the following week and two of the pictures had different colored bars across the bottom. I’ve never had any issues with my camera or my old card (I just wanted a larger card), so I started by taking the memory card out and putting my old one back in. It worked just fine. It was a plain old PNY card, which I’m going back to. I feel bad that I spent the extra money on this card. I’m definitely also going to keep several smaller cards in the future, rather than ever going with a large card. The risk is too great if a card fails and all of your pictures are on one card.
