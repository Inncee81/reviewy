Comply Ts-100 Comfort tips worked for me.

I carefully selected the right style and size of the tips based on other reviewers. I am using Klipsch R6i earphones. The provided silicon ear pieces from Klipsch fit reasonably well but do not secure the earphones in my ears. I have convex ear lobes, meaning no support for the ear pieces and they don’t fit well enough to stay in my ear canal. Therefore, I usually wear them upside down over the top of my ear. For the R6i, this meant that that the audio/phone control on the left wire hangs agains my jaw - not very comfortable.I purchased the Ts-100 Comfort Foam earphone tips to see if I could get a better fit which would allow me to stop wearing the Klipsch earphones upside down. They do. The instructions are to roll the tips into a longer more tapered form before inserting, and holding them in place a few seconds so they expand back to their original shape (memory foam) making for a better fit. These are softer than the silicon tips from Klipsch and eventually more comfortable for long periods. My left ear canal is appreciably larger than my right, and these tips give a better fit to the left than right, but altogether comfortable, never falling out of either ear like the silicon tips did. Sound isolation is perfectly fine for all but the noisiest backgrounds and I haven’t found sound quality diminished in any way over the Klipsch silicon tips.I was somewhat concerned about one reviewer who said these didn’t last very long. I have been using them now for over six weeks with no apparent degradation. They inevitably get a little wax on them but have been using hydrogen peroxide on a swab to clean them. This does a good job without affecting the foam, except you have to give them a little time to completely dry. They continue to hold their shape. I do not use the earphones during sweaty exercise and my average use is only a couple of hours per day. But these are doing the job for me. Yes, they are a bit pricy but if they can last a couple of months before swapping out for new, they are worth it.
