The plastic is holding up great. The drive is fast

UPDATE: The plastic is not holding up great. Over the last year, the part of the drive that plugs into the USB port has gotten chewed up in my pocket by my keys and is becoming increasingly difficult to plug into a USB port. Meanwhile my older metal thumb drive is going strong. Carrying this on a keyring? Look for a metal thumb drive.I used to carry the metal version of this thumb drive in a smaller capacity. Well, actually, I still do. But I needed something a little bigger, and I decided to try the plastic version this time (if for no other reason than to have a different color thumb drive in my pocket to differentiate them).The plastic is holding up great. The drive is fast. It “nests” well on my keyring and doesn’t make a mess in my pants pocket with the keys. This is pretty much perfect.I really, really wish I could get this in 256GB and 512GB.

my biggest disappointment was that the blue version was made of plastic …

Important: I ordered the Blue Version and that is what my review is based on. The blue version is plastic, not the “high quality metal” that exists in the silver edition.Needless to say, my biggest disappointment was that the blue version was made of plastic which concerns me from a durability perspective. I did want to have this on my keychain in my pocket most of the time. I’m guessing this will last several months, but will be far less durable than it’s metal brother.The “keyring” is a big design-before-function. I ended up clipping it to a tiny keyring with a tiny caribeaner. If placed directly on a larger keyring, it never seems to sit right in my pocket.Strictly speaking from a technical perspective, it’s a solid drive. It’s fast, it’s USB 3.0. It does everything it’s advertised to do. I just wish it weren’t plastic.

I like the chrome version of this usb fob

Personally, I like the chrome version of this usb fob. I ran over my plastic one, and although it survived, it took a while to straighten it back out, and it hasn’t been the same, since.The performance is excellent. But what I really like is the size and form factor. It is not too small to handle, and isn’t too big to get in the way. The butt end of the usb drive has a rise to it, so you never have to look to see which way to plug it in. You always know just from holding the drive. That removes one of the main annoyances that I have with USB drives. Which side is up?

Low cost USB storage.

Very fast and is able to sustain high write speeds (80-90 mb/s) without slowing down. I copied a 9 Gigabyte file to this and it got only slightly warm, usually my low profile drives get hot and slow down after 1 GB. This drive was smaller than expected and feels solid in the hand. If you want to store anything larger than 32 GB I recommend just buying an external hard drive because the cost per GB is much lower.
