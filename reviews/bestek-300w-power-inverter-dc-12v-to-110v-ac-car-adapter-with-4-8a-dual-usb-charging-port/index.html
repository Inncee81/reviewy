Product Reviews

Very quiet inverter with useful USB charging ports!

Got this to use in the RV while doing lots of photography. Needed to power my 17 inch Samsung i7 laptop, with a 220 watt power supply. This unit did very well while either running the laptop (batteries fully charged) or charging the batteries in the laptop (computer off). If I had run the laptop batteries down and was doing serious work editing or moving photos between drives, the inverter would cut off, and I would have to toggle the power switch on the back of the inverter to start it up again. I eventually got used to charging my laptop up fully before using it to with the inverter, and the inverter was able to keep the laptop running without a hitch. It may have been an issue with the 12 volt supply circuit. I didn’t run the inverter on a known high amperage 12 volt circuit in the RV, so I am not deducting a star for that.There is a quiet fan that is always on when the switch in the back is turned on. It is quiet enough that I was easily able to sleep with the fan running in very quiet campsites. The volume and pitch of the fan are unobtrusive.As others have noted, the USB ports are always powered if the inverter is plugged into the 12 volt supply, independent of the position of the power switch on the back. I like this very much, since I can charge my iPhone or Kindle with the fan turned off if I am not using the 110 volt section.A really excellent value!

Meets my needs, seems to meet all advertised specifications.

I like it! I bought this to power a TV and IPTV box in my RV without running the generator, about a 180w load. The fan is audible, but barely noticeable in anything other than a library-like setting. Build quality seems perfectly reasonable for the price. I’d recommend it for any application where it’ll put out enough power. In the instructions, Bestek recommends not using a cigar lighter outlet for loads over 150w. At my current draw, I did not blow the outlet fuse, nor did the 12v wire seem warmer than room temp. Other users who choose to ignore the manual should have spare fuses available, and check the wire temperature during use to prevent unwanted drama.
