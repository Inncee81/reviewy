Great value but be realistic.

It’s good…really good for the money, but you should know a few things before you buy this item. I’ve owned Bose docking stations and I’ve tested the Bose Soundlink as well, and the Vtin isn’t even close in sound. But, the price difference is enormous so it’s not a fair comparison. Here’s what you need to know about the Vtin:POSITIVES:- It gets very loud for it’s size. It will easily fill a large room with sound or a garage/shop.- It is extremely portable.- It’s pretty tough, but the grills probably will dent easily if dropped.- The Bluetooth connects very easily and has great range.- The sound is good for the money.- The battery life is exceptional.- Has a convenient carabiner hanger so you can hang it on a hook in the garage or in the backyard.- It’s splash proof so it’s great for by the pool or while washing cars.- Has a headphone jack and works very well as a computer speaker as well.- Love the size (7” x 2.75” x 2”). Just big enough to be loud yet very easy to grab with one hand and carry.NEGATIVES:- Not great low end tones like the Bose SoundLink and distorts a bit at high volumes. Overall it’s still really good, but be realistic about the quality of the sound at this price point.- The lowest volume setting with an iPhone connected is still pretty loud, so if you like super quiet background music at the desk, this may not work.In summary, for the price I’m not sure you can do much better than this. I’m really pleased with what I got for the money, but if you’re super particular about sound quality then I’d recommend you pay the additional 100-150 bucks to get something a bit more high end.Hope that helps.
