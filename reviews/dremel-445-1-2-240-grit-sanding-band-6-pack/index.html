Great sanding bands for trimming my dogs nails. (18 pound dog & 85 pound dog)

I use this for my dogs nails. Just let your dog sniff & touch it before turning it on. Be very patient. Turn it on and let them hear the Dremel tool. Then turn it off and let them sniff it or lay it near them. I sit comfortably near them and try not to stress as I do one nail and if they balk at the 2nd nail, I turn it off. Anytime they want to remove their paw from my hand or lap, I let them. I act as if I have all the time in the world. They relax and allow me to hold their paw for the 2nd nail.These Dremel sanding bands are just wonderful for giving my dogs their pawdecures. There are no sharp edges to snag on anything. I also noticed my small dog no longer digs holes in my back yard now that I keep his nails trimmed. No idea if those two are related - just thrilled no holes in my yard.

Vastly superior longevity and quality than the cheap knock-off sanding bands

This is a review of Dremel 445 1⁄2” 240 grit sanding band, 6 Pack.I bought my Dremel tool, Dremel 8050-N/18 Micro Rotary Tool Kit with 18 Accessories, primarily for grinding my dog’s (Standard Poodle) nails. I’ve had much better luck using Dremel brand sanding bands for that purpose. The knock-off sanding bands (which are far less expensive) are simply not as effective or as long lasting as the Dremel brand. Yes the Dremel brand bands cost more but the quality is worth the cost.I can grind the nails of my dog many times before I need to replace the sanding band. Totally worth it as I will only use 3-240 grit bands a year to do my dog’s nails. The sanding band in my photo is a 120 grit and has been used at least 10-14 times to grind my dog’s nails. It is still getting the job done. I first use the 120 grit bands to quickly get most of the nail ground down then use the 240 grit bands to finish the nails. I find that the 240 grit bands can’t be used as many times as the more coarse 120 grit bands but I still get many uses out of them before I need to replace them
