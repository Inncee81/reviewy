Perfect for high bitrate video (100mbit+ @ 4k resolution)

Let me give some background first. I owned a transcend 300x speed 32gb micro sd card (rated for writes of 20MBps). My primary use case for the microsd card is Samsung Galaxy Note 3. I store movies (1080p at good bitrates), podcasts, music, camera videos in ultra hd (4k resolution), etc.The vast majority of microsd cards can easily handle podcasts and music. The true test for good/performance/high quality micro sd cards is their read/write speed with files over 4-5gb, transferring large files without corruption, and surviving a lot of data write and erase.My Transcend 32gb 300x card was quite good for everything on my Note 3 until I rooted the phone and used a mod to increase the 4k video capture default bitrate from 48,000 kbps to 100,000 kbps (this leads to better video quality). 100,000 kbps (100mbit) requires a microsd write speed of atleast 12.5MBps (100mbit divided by 8 = 12.5MBps). The card could manage this however it was right on the edge. Also, keep in mind that this card’s real write speed is well over 12.5MBps but cell phones as of 2014 don’t use the full UHS-1 interface speed. The phone would take 10-15 seconds of “processing time” when I hit “Stop recording” on the phone after a clip that was 3-5mins long. This was due to the large file size (3-4gb) from the increased bit rate. I could still live with it but it caused me to miss out on many family moments. This scenario was going to increase as future cell phones and camcorders would have higher and higher bitrates.I purchased this Samsung pro 64 GB microsd card due to its high write speed. Mobile phones cannot get the full 80MBps+ speeds at the moment but I am getting at least 20 MBps+ which is enough for future cell phones with 150Mbit modded camera bitrates.Companies always advertise read speeds but they don’t like to mention write speeds. This card is expensive for a reason. The flash memory is high grade and exceeds the UHS-1 spec by a long margin for write speeds. I can now record 4k videos on my Note 3 at 100mbit bit rate with 1-2 seconds “Processing time” after clicking “Stop”. This is a major help since my cell phone is the primary video camera. I expect this microsd card to also help with my DSLR burst shots.Transfer speeds over micro usb 3.0 are very close to the 90⁄80 MBps speeds listed on the packaging.
