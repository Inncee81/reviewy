Good for range and Halloween, okay for hunting. Not seriously tactical, surely. Probably why the price was so attractive.

The phrase “Army Fans” in the title is actually a good clue as to the suitability of this vest, I think. I open by admitting freely that I stopped at 4 stars because I am not qualified to rate the item as a serious user. As a moderately skilled shooter, and simply unable to resist the low price, I bought the vest fully knowing that I will have little, if any, compelling reason to use it. I will wear it at the range, just to become comfortable and familiar with it, and learn how to load it and access things. My only other very likely use it perhaps as part of a costume around Halloween. Having said all that - the build quality seems reasonable to me. If you adjust it too tight for your trunk, you can, pretty easily, pull the zipper apart, so that’s a rather critical adjustment. It is a plastic zipper. I suspect that metal ones resist pulling open better. Still, why adjust the vest tight enough to make the proboem, so, make of that what you will. I did get AR magazines and my Taurus 24⁄7 G2 .45 magazines in the intended holders. As others have stated, I don’t see being able to get two AR magazines in the pockets, but given my uses it’s not a problem. I suppose that if civilized society falls apart and I am actually defending out streets, I might have a different take on that. I’ll go on record as saying I don’t expect any such thing, so I’m not going to worry about that. The pocket for radio is too small for my Baofeng UV82 UHF/VHF radio, but that’s a sort of general purpose radio. Smaller, “tacical” radios might fit fine. I just moved it to the right top pocket, which is more than big enough.I very much like the belt positioning and sizing arrangement. The belt hangs from the vest by a number of velcro or snap straps. You have to undo all of those and remove the belt to size it easily. You could probably leave a few closed and leave it attached, but it hardly seems worth juggling. The belt sizing is with considerable overlap of velcro strips. You get the size right, then put it back in the mounting straps, and it seems very well held. A note: Notice that the belt is suspended from the vest, so if you’re not hanging things on it, the size can be just adequate to hold everything still. If you want to hang, for instance, a holster, you will want to adjust the belt tight enough to transfer that weight to your hips. A noted that several folks do that, because the velcro mounting for the holster on the left side lower front of the vest does not seem adequate to hold a firearm of significant size or weight, especially if you’re actually moving around quickly at all.The material itself is certainly thick and seems well made. The adjustment to trunk size is simple and allows for following your lines, so whether your spare tire is showing these days or you’re lean and mean, you can shape it to fit you nicely.I guess I think overall that this is a fine hobbyist vest, for someone like me, more serious than just costume gear, but not really for serious tactical uses. With a price in the high 30s, I found that a reasonable and fun acquisition. A few hunting friends even decided that it would do for most of their hunting rounds, if they were of a mind to wear such a thing, which most of them don’t seem to be. It’s not a hunting jacket. If you’re looking for serious tactical, I’d say look elsewhere. For everything else, this is probably fine.
