Lightweight and well-made

The Pellem 2014SC case is a slim, lightweight case for a Galaxy Tab Pro 8.4 tablet. This will not fit the Galaxy Tab S or the Galaxy Tab, as the cut-outs aren’t in quite the right place. The tablet snaps tightly and securely into the case, which I like (I’m not a big fan of Amazon’s approach where the case is held onto the tablet magnetically).The top cover is held closed with a magnet and it is held well. It has the auto-wake and auto-shutoff that you would expect from a good case. Unlike some cases I’ve used, when you fold the top of the case back, you don’t run the risk of a too-strong magnet acting to shut off the tablet again.The inside of the front cover is a soft micro-fiber material that doesn’t leave marks on the screen. My experience with other tablet cases is that this material shows dirt very easily and that it cannot be easily cleaned. I have a similar tablet case for another tablet and the inside of the front cover is pretty badly stained because of my nasty habit of using this in the kitchen while cooking and eating. This is purely an aesthetic issue; it doesn’t affect the tablet.The stand is fairly basic and it only works in landscape mode, so no good way to prop it up in portrait mode to read a book or a recipe. The stand works fine on a flat hard surface, less well on a bed or, say, on your stomach while you’re laying in bed watching a video.This is not an OtterBox case. If you need a strong, protective case that will stand up to repeated bashing and drops, this likely isn’t it. On the other hand, you’ll pay significantly more for that level of protection and you’ll find that a case like that also adds considerable bulk and weight. If you just want a lightweight case for basic protection, then this case should be fine. At $12.99 at the time I’m writing this, this is a good value and I can recommend it.
