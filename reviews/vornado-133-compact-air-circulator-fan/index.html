Product Reviews

The Vornado 133 Small Size, you are paying for the “quiet”

I ordered the Vornado 133 fan, but when the Amazon box showed up I thought for sure it was way too small to have a fan inside. Surprisingly small, this fan is effective for a bedroom. I have two 24” box fans that move a lot of air, but they are so noisy I worry about what I may not be hearing when they are running (door bells, kids calling for me, etc). This Vornado truly is quiet, with two settings… quiet, and almost silent. It has 4 rubber feet that dampen sound further. It fits on my window sill no problem… my window sill measures 4 7⁄8” deep. The fan base itself measures 5 1⁄8” deep, but the rubber feet are only 4 1⁄2 deep, so the front lip of the base hangs past that with no problem. It’s main job is to move air, and it does that fine. I do not agree with the Vornado marketing illustration that it sends up a magic spiral tube of air to return at the base… it’s just a fan, and how it moves air depends on what windows and doors you happen to have open… but it is well made, attractive, and quiet, which is why I bought it… I just wasn’t sure if my definition of quiet was the same as other reviewers… but I doubt you will find a quieter fan. It is quiet for 3 reasons… it does not vibrate, the shape of these particular blades are quiet, and the rubber feet don’t transmit noise. There is no such thing as a silent fan, as rapidly moving air makes it’s own noise. In the end, you are paying more for quiet than as an air mover.

I don’t find it overly loud like some of the other reviewers but I’m used to …

Whew, this fan puts out quite the power for such a tiny fan. I don’t find it overly loud like some of the other reviewers but I’m used to having a fan going pretty much all day both at work and at home. I’m planning on buying a second one of these to take into the office due to the powerful air it can produce. I know Vornado fans are a bit more expensive, but so far it has been very worth it. I’ve also got the large room scale version of a Vornado fan in another room which prompted me to buy this small desk one for my home office. Highly recommended.

Too small, weak, and loud

Accidentally purchased a smaller model than intended due to the stock photo being of a different model, showing the dial being on the bottom of the back side (as is on the 530), but the 133’s is located on the center of the back. As it was intended to be used as a deck fan, I figured it should work just as well anyways. But upon testing it, I found the smaller size gives a very narrow airflow, with almost no power at all. Even at max you can barely feel it, and it is far more audible than a low setting with more airflow on a bigger unit. It’s not worth getting this small version.

Quiet, solid performance fan that could use more power

As someone who has owned four different models of Vornado fans, I have generally been pleased with their quality. This is a solid, quiet compact fan. My only real complaint is that could use a more powerful motor. Compact high-powered blowers can be ten times the price of this fan, which wouldn’t be justified for most consumers. It would be nice to have an eight to ten inch fan with three times the power and twice the price of this fan.Depending on the application, I have found high performance, small-diameter DC radiator fans a better solution. They do produce quite a bit of noise and require a sufficiently powerful DC power supply that the average consumer might not own.
