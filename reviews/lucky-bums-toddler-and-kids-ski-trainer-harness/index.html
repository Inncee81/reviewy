Product Reviews

A must have for children learning to ski

I purchased two of these (for children 3 & 5), and we love them. It is so easy to pick the kids up with the handle in the back when they fall. the handle is also great for sitting on the lift, because you have a hand on the kids at all time. The leashes are awesome for letting the little ones think they are going down the hill at their own pace. The little backpack compartment makes it easy to tuck away the leashes quickly before getting back on the lift (or if you are on an easier trail that your kids dont need the leashes for)

This is a cool little product.

You will only use this for half a season for skiing and the rest of the time is to tow your slow kid to the lift. It is most useful for lifting your child onto chair lift. When your kid gets braver you need to extend the leashes, because you will carry too much speed to stop with the short leashes. I am embarrassed to say I used my two 6’ dog leashes to extend it. But it worked. This product will last through all of your children.

Handle is better than the straps

This is a handy little thing to have when teaching incompetent little skiers. I used this with my 3 and 6 year old and the best feature is really that there is handle on the back that allows you to lift your child onto the chair lift and then to subtly but surely hold them there safely as if you’ve got your arm around their back as they fidget 50 feet up in the air (you can tell I don’t trust my kids up there). It is also very helpful to pick up the fallen, whining little skiers up off the slope.As for the straps, I agree with one of the other reviews that they end up being as much a hindrance as a help. Skiers are meant to lean forward to place pressure on their skis but when you pull on the straps to slow your child down you end hauling them backwards and throwing off that balance a little. I also found that the handles are too short and that if you get even a little speed going then if your kids whipes out you run the risk of running right over them (that happened once which was more than enough). I found that linking the two straps together (thus doubling the distance from my child as we skied) and attaching it to the back handle worked better at letting them ski freely but be able to go down most basic trails and not build up too much speed.Overall worthwhile for piece of mind on the slopes but definitely not a ski “trainer” as they get no technique from this and you really can’t help guide their turns.

Good for Beginners

Neither my husband nor I don’t agree with parents who leash their children on the street. Having said that, it did take a few talks for him to consider us hooking our kids up to these straps, but the idea gave me piece of mind that they wouldn’t be able to fly uncontrollably down the hill, into a tree and be hurt. Parents beware, if you use these, it takes arm and back strength and stamina to do a full day with a 40 lbs child with these on. To me, it was a safety issue. The straps are not too long, as to get tangled up and hurt anyone, if used properly and putting them on was easy. You’ll be sore at the end of the day, but your child will be safe and sound.I would recommend these for beginners, but take it slow.
