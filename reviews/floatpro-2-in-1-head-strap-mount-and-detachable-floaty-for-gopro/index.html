Great item, but could be even better with minor improvements

We used the floating head strap this week in some small to moderate surf and it worked fine, but it was necessary to hold onto the camera/strap when duck-diving through waves, and when getting tumbled in the surf. When the wave size increased we chose not to use it since we were concerned that it may be lost in the surf. The best suggestion we can offer is to provide an adjustable chin strap system that will prevent it from being pulled from the head in waves, or even whitewater. A simple adjustable strap similar to the top strap would do fine - some Velcro type loops on each end would allow the user to quickly add or remove it. Another option is to provide a tether that could be attached to an arm-band. Otherwise, this is a great mounting system that will fit over helmets & wet-suit hoods and has many applications for a variety of action sports. All good designs can stand some improvement so we hope our suggestions were helpful!

Good product, saved my GoPro twice already, and I’ve only owned it for 2 weeks

Saved my Hero4 Silver in two surf sessions over the past 10 days. First one on NS Oahu, wave ripped it off, looked for about a minute in the whitewater, found it. Then a couple of days ago off of Magic Island a friend I was surfing with and filming zigged when I thought he was going to zag, gouged my board and in the ensuing froth, assessments and apologies … camera and headgear were gone. No amount of looking in the ocean or on the shore rockline turned it up, even went back at low tide with no luck. 3 or 4 hours later I got a call from a Honolulu fisherman who said she found it floating about a mile offshore; I had it back before the end of the day. Suggestions: (1) Make the whole top of the FloatPro bright orange or yellow, not just the edge trim; (2) leave a white space somewhere on the inside where someone can use a black Sharpie to write their name (I had mine written inside the GoPro casing); (3) consider an alternate/additional model that has some sort of chin and/or back strap to keep it from being ripped off in wipeouts (duck diving is not bad, that actually forces it back onto your head if done right); and (4) maybe offer a strap that is a little bit thicker/floatier, so it sticks out of the water more (w/ the weight of a camera dangling down from the headgear, the FloatPro floats a little low sometimes, sometimes being sucked a little under, which makes it more difficult to find). That said, mine worked and, since I don’t want to mount the camera on the board and don’t want to use a mouthpiece, it’s the best solution I have found so far. For now, I’m going to try to develop a habit to, where possible, grab the headgear with one hand when things get gnarly/foamy.
