MY FAVORITE THING IN THE WORLD

Where do I start with this? All I can say is, THIS. IS. MY. BABY. I have loved this thing since the moment I laid eyes on it. Having been my first drone I was very nervous to start out with a $50 drone because you see all those fancy shmancy drones that your friends use and that are on YouTube videos and you hope you can just get one for $50 to start out. Well let me tell you, this it the PERFECT beginner-intermediate drone. I mean, this thing is AWESOME.The drone can easily be used indoors and outdoors but I HIGHLY recommend hours and hours of indoor use before going outside. My worst nightmare was my first outdoor flight when I was visiting in Louisiana. I didn’t realize how important headless mode was and flew this baby to the tippity top of a 60 foot tree. And I mean this thing was STUCK and the tree was literally un-climbable. It took three hours, a spool of 30lb fishing line, 6 fishing weights, a sling shot and about 200 shots to get it down and there was barely a scratch on it. If that doesn’t scream durable. I don’t know what does.Having learned my lesson from the crash I spent a lot more time on my skills indoors before attempting again in a big, clear backyard and that’s when I was REALLY impressed. This thing goes up at least 100 feet and is s controllable. The camera function is not High Def by ANY means but it is still fun and is impressive to friends and family. Especially the live viewing. It’s not perfect and lags by .5-2 seconds but it’s still really neat. The flips are fun and really make people have a good time watching you fly a piece of plastic around the yard. I have crashed on concrete, grass, trees and from high elevation and this thing is still just as good as new.I recommend immediately purchasing a few batteries that are 1000mah and higher as it will extend your flight time from about 5 minutes to 12-15 minutes per battery. You’re gonna want that. I also purchased an extra set of propellers, propeller guards and screws as you will lose/damage yours eventually. I have a black X5SW that came with white propeller guards so I made sure the new ones were all black and they look even better and more legit.ONE THING TO REMEMBER: The propellers have to go back on in the exact direction as the picture of the unit. I removed the propellers to take it on a plane and then put them back on and thought I ruined my drone. I was devastated them read online about the directional requirement of the propellers and switched them to the correct positions.I like the controller a lot and have found that you do not need to adjust trim levels or anything very much. Just resetting after crashes is advised.Don’t get discouraged if you find the flight to be too difficult in the beginning. It takes a lot of getting used to because it’s an aerial RC device, not and RC car. I promise you’ll get it soon.All-in-all this is a wonderful addition to my life and I hope to add a few nano and higher end drones in the future. But for now. I am more than content with this Syma X5SW.

Way More Fun than I Expected!

I got this for my son’s 9th birthday. Knowing that it would require a little finesse to fly, I tested it out before giving it to him. So. Much. Fun. Of course, I had it stuck in a tree within five minutes of unboxing it. I’m happy to report that a few swift tosses of a soccer ball dislodged it. My 9-yr-old is able to do it, though he still needs a little practice to land it smoothly on hard surfaces. It handles trees and 9-year-old zeal with grace. For the price (about $75 with extra batteries and props), it’s cheaper than taking the family to the movies after popcorn and drinks, and worth many more entertainment hours.
