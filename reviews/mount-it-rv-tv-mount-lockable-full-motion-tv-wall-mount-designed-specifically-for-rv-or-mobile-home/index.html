    Product Description

Mount-It! Full Motion RV / Mobile Home TV Mount

TV wall mount specifically designed to provide secure and stable TV installation in RVs, mobile homes, boats and other dynamic use applications. 

This Mount-It! Articulating LCD/LED TV Wall mount supports most 22” to 42 inch TVs or monitors depending on the VESA bolt hole pattern.  

The strong stable arm is equipped with locking mechanisms to hold the TV in its locked in position. 

The tilt function can move 55 degrees up and 35 degrees down for a full 90 degree range to help create ergonomic environments. These combined functions can help your screen adapt for sitting, standing, or laying down positions depending on your needs at the time. 

This wall mount stand can also extend over 15.3 inches and a half from the wall and retract down to a mere 3 inches. It is compatible for most scenarios and settings. The ergonomic positions will reduce eye, back, or neck strain and can even reduce sun glare. Aluminum alloy and steel construction is powder coated with a sleek black and gray for a contemporary appearance. The arms are strong enough to endure 33 lbs of weight and is built to last over frequent on long usage. 

Hardware and easy to follow installation guides are included.

A versatile and well-designed TV mount.

I used this to mount a 39-inch TV, weighing 15 pounds, in an RV. I wanted to b able to lock the TV at whatever orientation we needed to be able to view it easily from the couch. This item filled the bill completely. It extends some 14 inches or so from the mounting point. In addition, there are three joints that allow swiveling in the horizontal plane. A fourth joint allows tilting the TV in the vertical plane. Each joint has teeth that can then be locked down by turning a knob at each joint. I had to mount this TV so that it sits in front of and up against the front of the original TV’s cabinet. So, that the mount is set about six inches within the cabinet. The only problem I have is that when I have to stow it, I just loosen the pivots and then push the TV flat against the cabinet. The result is that once the TV is in its stowed position, I cannot access the knobs to tighten and secure the TV in its stowed position, when it’s time to go on the road again. I will probably have to devise some sort of strap that can be secured from outside of, and in front of the TV. Otherwise, it’s a very good TV mount.
