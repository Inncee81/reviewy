This review seems to want to be written.. so writing it. :)

I am rusty on this item, but as this one item always shows up as an “open review” on the page of “review your purchases”, am writing a review to hopefully clear it out.From what I can recall on this, it was a simple hand blender that came with a little cup. Unlike other more complicated models, it lacked an Annoying Safety Switch which you had to mess with whenever you let your finger off the button. The metal Spinning-part can detach for washing, and a few people had issues with it coming off during use. One person commented that there were “marks” on the two pieces to show where they were to be attached, and if you attached it any other way, you’d have an issue… So looks for the two “marks” on the blender when attaching the two pieces. I think it also had a knob on top to set between two speeds. There was also a second (more expensive) version of this which had more accessories. I think that they were two completely different devices, as the accessories from the more expensive one could not be used with this (the less expensive) one. So if one wanted more options, I guess try the more expensive one. But if one just wanted a blender (and a cup) then this would be a good choice.Other item of note is that one does NOT use hand blenders to “crush ice”. Even if one had special attachment, even if one had an attachment that actually SAID it would “crush ice”, what they actually mean is “will crush PRE-CRUSHED ice” not actual cubes. If you want to “crush ice” (like me) then get a something like a “snow-cone machine”. However even those usually say to let the ice “melt a bit” before crushing-away. If one tried to use a hand blender (attachment or not) to “crush ice”, odds are you’ll end up burning out the blender’s motor. Unfortunately I can speak with experience on this, so if you want to “crush ice” get something designed for that particular task rather than a hand blender. Amazon offers many different varieties from simple use to ones that can mash up pound-after-pound of frozen water.
