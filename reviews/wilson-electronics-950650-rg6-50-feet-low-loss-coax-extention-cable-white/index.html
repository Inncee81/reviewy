A CABLE SO WHITE IT JUST ABOUT SCREAMS OUT “HERE I AM MOE! AND ITS EASY TO WORK WITH TOO!

This white cable was perfect for my needs. I had the high speed internet people come in and they did what they always do. They ran cable around every door sill and wall board till they got to their destination and in so doing made my otherwise nice looking house look tacky and cheap. I’ve had it on my mind to remove the tacky cable company installed Coax with new coax that takes a direct unseen route. So I needed 50 feet of replacement white coax cable and I brought this.I ran this cable through a few walls on a direct route to my computer room and after a few connections I had high speed internet service restored. Then I had the greatest joy ever tearing out all that old cable company installed nasty nice cabling that snaked its way around every door in the house. This cable has been a god send and since there is less cable and fewer connections I think my internet service is even faster and it was not bad before.This cable is easy to work with and does not kink easily. Best of all its bright WHITE which makes this coax much easier to see as you snake it through dark wall cavities. Black coax in a dark wall cavity is almost impossible to see making its installation in this application much harder than it has to be. White Coax almost yells out, “HERE I AM MOE!” even when within the darkest wall spaces. Sorry I had to interject from old 3 Stooges humor in this review. If you remember or love the 3 Stooges you are my kind of people. Anyways this is awesome white coax cable suitable for high speed applications and easy to work with in challenging environments.

Not a Standard Size - Poor Choice if YouInstall Your Own Terminals

I bought a roll of this cable to pass through to an antenna mounted in my attic. I was passing the cable through a plastic bulkhead and I knew I would be cutting the cable and then terminating it to a custom length. The cable seems to be of good quality, but good quality cable is easy to find. The first thing I noticed was that my Data Shark cable stripper was cutting the braid no matter how I set the blades. So I ended up stripping the cable with a simple single edge razor. I have a good pair of GB crimping pliers with compound jaws that have always done a great job for me. With this cable the F-connector pulled right off when I gave my crimp the tug test. This happened with 3 different brands of good quality F terminals. I finally got the job finished by using the RG59 die instead of the RG6 die and backing off a lot on the force I applied to test the crimp.I think this cable will work fine if you plan on just using the factory terminals, but if you need to shorten your cable to a custom length I would buy different cable.

Excellent, for the price, but not as pictured.

The cable works fine and is a great bargain at this price. The only annoying thing is that the product picture clearly shows the connectors covered by molded strain reliefs, which the product does not have. The product actually has a metal sleeve and plastic bushing.
