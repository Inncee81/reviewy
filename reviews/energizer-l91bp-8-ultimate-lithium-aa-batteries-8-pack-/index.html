Five advantages over alkaline cells, at a considerably higher price.

These batteries – actually cells – are drop-in replacements for ‘AA’ cells in most devices.Nonrechargeable (called ‘primary’) lithium iron disulfide (Li-FeS2) cells like this have three advantages over ‘regular’ 1.5-volt alkaline or heavy-duty zinc-carbon dry cells:1. Much higher energy capacity. How much depends mostly on the amount of power draw from the device.2. Much longer shelf life – cells are now marked ‘best before’ 15 years from date of manufacture!3. Much lighter weight – useful when backpacking and many other applications.4. More power output in cold conditions.5. Much less likely to leak and corrode devices from the inside.The main disadvantage is cost. Additionally, a few devices don’t understand the discharge curve of the lithium cells and prematurely indicate full discharge of these batteries. If you want to measure the capacity of these or any other rechargeable or nonrechargeable batteries or cells, I highly recommend the expensive but very well built The ZTS Multi-Battery Tester With Case - MBT-1.A somewhat cheaper option, if you don’t need the highest possible capacity/runtime but do want the other advantages, are Energizer Advanced Lithium Batteries, AA Size, 8-Count.
