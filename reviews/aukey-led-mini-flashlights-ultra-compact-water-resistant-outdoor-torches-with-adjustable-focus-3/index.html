Works fine, but it a little weird.

As a fan of cheap flashlights, these Aukeys do some things really well, but don’t totally follow through with the execution.The flashlights themselves look realy cool and well machined. The focus feature is smooth and the lens is clear. I’m also surprised that no matter what mode you turned it off in, if you leave it off for a few seconds, it restarts again in high beam. That’s a feature common in much more expensive lights.The downside is that my black flashlight had a stray metal shaving in the switch base that kept the dust cover from seating until I removed it. Also, the switch is so deep you have to work to turn it on and off. There’s no hole for a lanyard either, which is kind’ve essential in these tiny flashlights. Usually there’s a groove on the back of the flashlight to allow reversing the pocket clip. The Aukey has plenty of grooves in back, but none fit the clip. The weirdest aspect of this light is that the battery compartment has an O-ring, but the lens parts are completely unsealed. Other than keeping battery cover from falling off, why bother sealing any part of it?Aukey is about 80% of the way to making a great flashlight. It’s much nicer looking and more compact than alot of the AA flashlights on Amazon. They just need to refine their product.
