Half way towards a great mount.

I’m really torn on this one. The wall mounting is great. Good system, easy to adjust, very secure. The arms are a little stiff, but clearly very strong. Then we get to the TV mounting hardware, and it’s flimsy and cheap. I don’t get it. It’s like two very different departments were in charge of building this thing. The wall-side department wanted to make the best product possible, the TV-mount-side wanted to make something cheap and profitable. The mounts to the TV are flimsy and the screws are just barely long enough to secure. But for me the worst part is the interface between the wall side and the TV side. There are two tiny little screws connecting both halves, both only secured with thumb screws which are barely 4-5 threads deep. This leaves the TV vertically secure, but it takes no effort to rotate the TV and there is no way to make it more secure. Dust gathering tilts my TV off-kilter. I left the little level they provided on top of my TV so I can make the daily adjustments necessary to keep it horizontal.Clearly someone cared about this product. The wall mounting half is easy, intuitive, and they include all the tools you need to do the job right. Then I guess they ran out of time, rushed the TV-mount and called it a finished product.Not bad enough to return, but I would not buy another one.

No, no, no

It works, but… my 40” (16lbs) TV is barely mounted to it. Two tiny thumbscrews are all that secure the TV to the wall mount, and there are no washers or anything keeping it from falling off. I leveled everything during every step, and still the TV is slightly tilted to the left and will not tilt level. I’ve got it mounted now and I’m trying to avoid touching it. The back plate was also a little funny… I had to use red loctite to secure the screws and they’ll never come out now. I added some super glue and baking soda to the back plate just as an extra security measure.
