Nice, innovative, future-proofed but not perfect TV

This is a nice TV, especially if you can spend a little more than average and want a bit of future-proofing but still need to stay under $2k. Even though I could technically afford it I will always have some qualms with paying over $2k for a TV.The picture looks good in HD and non, and the sound is decent without a soundbar (although I’d recommend getting one anyway). For the UHD content you can find, it does look amazing. I do wish there was a THX movie display option like on my old TV; I had to mess around with the movie settings and Google for advice before I got it to look the way I wanted. And I also wish there was a Custom profile I could set up separate from the existing display profiles.I am not ready to give this TV five stars because I haven’t watched enough of what I bought it for: pro football and 4k/HDR movies. There’s still a lack of the kinds of streaming movies I’d like to watch in the format: new and older action, sci-fi, and summer blockbuster type flicks.All that having been said, the curved choice is still an interesting debate. If you are going to have more than a couple of people watching it on the regular from broad angles, you might still go with the flat screen model. It really just depends on how wide your sofa is and how far away from the TV you are. If I glance from the kitchen while I’m cooking, as an example, I’m probably looking at it from a 45-60 degree angle. The picture doesn’t look bad; maybe no worse than on a flat TV. But the curve really visually amplifies the fact that you’re not looking at it from an optimal angle.A few things I don’t like: these minimal button remotes suck. I prefer the old style where I had 30 buttons for one-click nav. Scrolling through menus is such a waste of time. I also don’t like that fact that there’s no central pedestal-type stand like on my old TV. I ended up buying a stand to attach because it was cheaper than buying and building a new, wider TV stand. Setting it up with all of this Samsung account garbage (my cell phone is also a Samsung) was a pain and another example of how everyone is being forced into being analyzed and tracked. To that end, the fact that the remote has a mic also bothers me. I do not need or want a MICROPHONE IN MY TV REMOTE. I can’t wait until they find a reason to start putting cameras and mics into smart headboards and toilets.Overall, I would recommend this TV. If they sports and eventual broader 4k/HDR content looks good, I might eventually bump it to 5 stars. For now 4 is fair.
