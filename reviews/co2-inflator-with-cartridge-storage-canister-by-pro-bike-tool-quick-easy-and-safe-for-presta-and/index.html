So… you’re looking for a quality CO2 inflation tool, eh?

I’m a bit disappointed… that I didn’t buy MORE of these to give away as quality gifts when they were priced at rock bottom prices! Not too long ago on a solo ride, I was forced to use my compact, spring-switch activated CO2 inflator since I neglected to bring along a mini pump. With little to no real control over the inflation with a CO2 canister, it was a dismal affair on the side of the road. So, that night, I checked Amazon and came across the Pro Bike Tool CO2 inflator. What I liked about it was the fact the Presta/Schrader (it has a twin valve head) connection is a screw-on attachment to the valve, AND it has a controlled release lever, from “Closed” to “Open” position, and is variable dependent on how much you turn the lever. It’s that simple! I gave it a test run on a recycled/patched tube (700x18-25), and I have to say, it was a total waste of time as well as a perfectly good CO2 cartridge. Of course it worked! Why wouldn’t it? I don’t know what I was thinkin’… it was, however, nice to see how easy it was to control the CO2 airflow rate.If there’s one minor thing I could complain about (believe me, it’s trivial and you could skip the next couple sentences if you’d like), it’s when storing the CO2 cartridge. I use a 16g cartridge (it’s designed to be safely stored upside down in the canister), it rattles a bit since I keep it in a tool storage container in my water bottle holder. I did a little hack and cut a small 2” strip of leftover butyl inner tube and placed it loose, lengthwise within the container. That did the trick, but ultimately, it’s a minor thing and if you’re used to carrying such road tools in your jersey back pocket, you’ll hardly notice the rattling. I added a few pics and I think they’re fairly self-explanatory, but I just wanted to add that I’m NOT a weight weenie. Yes, I own a digital scale, but that’s for my reloading hobby… the weight was included in this review for those that just want to know. So, a total of 59 grams, that’s just under 2.1 oz, and without the CO2 cartridge. Awesome, in my opinion. I think Pro Bike Tool has an absolute winner here.
