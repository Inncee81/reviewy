Samson LTS50 Laptop Stand

by people ...

Product Specifications

  • Heavy duty laptop stand with tripod base
  • Silicon surface holds laptop securely in place
  • Tilt control on laptop surface for optimal viewing
  • Roadworthy steel construction

  • Adjustable up to 4.5’ in height

    Product Description

Samson’s LTS50 Laptop Stand offers convenient, comfortable placement of your laptop computer in live and studio environments. The lightweight LTS50 offers a roadworthy, steel-constructed design with a sleek black finish, as well as a removable top and fold-up design for increased portability. The top part of the stand features a silicon surface to hold your laptop securely in place with tilt control for optimal positioning in any application. Adjustable up to 4.5’ in height with locking latches and a pin lock for increased support, you can count on the LTS50 to keep your laptop right at your fingertips and ready to perform.

Great Laptop Stand!

 I’ve tried several laptop stands including K&M , Quik-Lok, Steller Labs and Pyle-Pro and this ones blows them away!First off in terms of construction and build quality this stand is top notch.It’s steel construction with a smooth black powder coated finish and has quick release heavy duty locking latches to allow quick adjustment and easy fold-up or down.A safety locking pin, tethered by a short nylon strap provides increased support and safety for heavy laptops or projectors.The laptop table measures 15.5 inches with by 11 inches deep and is large enough to hold most laptops and some protectors.The front lip of the table is 3⁄4 inches high while the rear lip is 1⁄4” high, there are no lips on the side so as long as your laptop is 11 inches deep or less it will fit securely onto the table.Samson includes a silicone sticky mat which you can put down on the table to cushion your laptop if you choose but I prefer to use the table without the silicone mat.My 13” and 15” Macbook Pros have rubber feet on them and fit perfectly on the table platter.The tilt control on the table will allow you to adjust your computer to the optimum viewing angle and the locking knob tightens the platter to hold the angle adjustment securely in place.The base of this stand is a sturdy tripod with rubber feet and the stand will adjust up to 4.5 feet.I purchased this stand primarily for use in my video studio to hold my laptop teleprompter but I’ve found it useful as a portable tool stand and mobile stand for my epson projector.If your looking for a sturdy, secure laptop stand that’s well built and easy to use I highly recommend the Samson LTS50 …. it’s Fantastic!Tip: The quick lock latches on the Samson LTS50 I received didn’t hold as tightly as I liked so I had to adjust before I began using the stand.Adjusting the tightness is simple and easy, you just need a #2 Phillips screwdriver.Open the latch and inside the latching mechanism you will see a black phillips screw head, turn the screw a little bit and then test the tightness of the latch by closing it.Repeat as necessary, turning the screw a little each time till you achieve the desired tightness. Be careful not to over tighten the screw.

Not much to say really. It’s a well made, sturdy, easy to put together stand for laptop use, music tablature, or whatever. My only concern now is trying to find ways to keep my cats from thinking this is a landing pad.While I would recommend this stand, I do have 3 complaints. The first is the strong odor that leeches off of the silicone surface. It took about a week for this to dissipate. Any strong odor like that means that chemicals are leaching into the air.The second complaint is something I was aware of when purchasing, but it’s that the stand doesn’t go tall enough for people like myself at around 6’3”. This causes poor posture. I’m not sure why they couldn’t make it a couple inches taller in height.The third criticism is that the stand should have a dedicated keyboard tray option for better ergonomics. It’s best to have elbows at around 90 degrees, yet doing so, means the laptop screen is too low.So in summary… it needs to be taller, and it needs a keyboard tray attachment option (which would be very easy to add).
