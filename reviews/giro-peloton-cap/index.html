Product Specifications

    Product Description

You need a cycling cap, so it may as well look good. The Giro Peloton Cap keeps it classy with a time-tested fit and modern patterns. It’ll keep your hair in check and keep the sweat out of your eyes, so you can concentrate on dropping the hammer. Best of all, when you stop for coffee, everyone will know you actually ride. Consider it a modern twist on a time-tested essential. The Giro Peloton Cap is available in one size, and in the colors Camo/black/orange, Black/white/gray, and Red/white/gray.

Product Reviews

fits comfortably on a size 7.5 head (unlike most other brands)

This is a nice cycling hat. It is thin, stretchy, and contoured so it fits easily under a cycling helmet. I have a larger head (size 7.5) and it fits me fine (most cycling caps are too small for me), but it has elastic in the back so it should fit a smaller head as well. Sizing is a little larger than the Castelli hats, which are snugger and less comfortable on my head. The brim flips up or down for protection against sun or rain in various conditions. Poly fabric wicks and breathes much better than the old-style cotton caps. It doesn’t get soggy with sweat like cotton hats can on hot humid days. Good quality cotton caps can be bought for half this price, so you do have to decide if this one is worth it for your riding conditions.

It’s good, but the bill holds a lot of sweat

It’s good, but the bill holds a lot of sweat. If you flip it down in sunny conditions, the sweat drips off pretty bad.

I am also quite disappointed in the quality of brim\peak, its far way too flimsy. For the price of this item, you can find far better quality elsewhere on Amazon.

All around great cap.

I’ve been wearing this cap for awhile. I was concerned it would be too hot in warmer weather, but it has been fine thus far (currently June in the northern hemisphere). I’ve been caught in the rain and the bill kept the worst of the water from dripping down the back of my glasses. The bill provides decent sun protection in the morning/evening when the sun is low on the horizon. The cap keeps my hair from poking through the helmet vents (don’t you hate that?), and best of all I don’t look like a pirate. What’s not to like?
