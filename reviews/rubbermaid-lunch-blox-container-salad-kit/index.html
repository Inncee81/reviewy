Product Specifications

  • Modular containers snap together to save space and stay organized, and can be stacked to fit various lunch bag shapes and sizes
  • Blue Ice freezer packs snap directly to food containers, keeping salads or lunch chilled when you’re on the go

  • Kit Includes: 1 Salad Container (5.2 cup capacity), 1 Toppings Tray, 1 Salad Dressing Container (1.8 oz), 1 Medium Blue Ice freezer pack (Size: 4.81” L x 5.625” W x 5.25” H when stacked

    Product Description

Style Name:Salad Kit The Rubbermaid LunchBlox Salad Kit is designed to make taking lunch to school or work more convenient and organized. With a large 5.2 cup container, one dressing container and a third container for additional salad toppings, taking a healthy, vibrant salad for lunch to school or work has never been easier. All containers snap together around the included medium Blue Ice pack so your salad stays crisp and fresh without refrigeration. Designed to work together to save space, the kit will stay organized in any lunch bag. All BPA-free LunchBlox containers are leak-proof, microwave-, dishwasher-, and freezer-safe. Fits perfectly inside LunchBlox Small and Medium lunch bags. Kit includes 1 Salad Container (5.2 cup, 4.88” L x 5.55” W x 4.37” H), 1 Toppings Tray, 1 Salad Dressing Container (1.8 oz), 1 Medium Blue Ice (5.55” L x 4.71” W x 0.78” H)

Bento Box with a built-in cooler

This nifty New Zealand version of the bento box has enabled me to enjoy healthier lunches at work. I’m able to to stow fresh veggies, fruit, a power bar, cheese cubes and cold-cuts in its two-story tight-sealing container. The rig’s nicest gimmick is a clip-on blue-ice pack that keeps the contents cool; I can leave it in my rucksack without having to resort to storing it in my office’s overcrowded refrigerator. The Lunch Blox is sturdy and washes out easily. Don’t be put off by the blue-ice brick’s overly snug fit to the bottom of the container: be patient and persistent, it will clip on easily once you’ve figured out how it works.

Used everyday in kids lunchboxes and as almond snack containers in the car

I don’t particularly care for the bottom of these containers, the extended plastic border that allows them to lock together when stacked. I only comment about this because it allows the container to collect more water when it is cleaned in the dishwasher (upside down). I really like the square (ish) profile because I can fit more containers in the square (ish) lunch boxes. Round containers waste valuable real estate in a lunchbox! I have used this size for nuts, candies, dried fruit, and blueberries. They are also nicely sized for baby/toddler portions.
