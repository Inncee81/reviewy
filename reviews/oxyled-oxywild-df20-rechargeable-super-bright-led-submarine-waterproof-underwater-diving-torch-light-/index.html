Great diving light for the price

I have used this flashlight for 4 night dives so far and it works good. I have noticed a tiny bit of moisture in the battery compartment but it hasn’t hindered the operation of the flashlight. You should open it up after a dive anyway to air it out. The only drawback I see is that you need two hands to turn this on - one to hold the light and the other to twist the end. If you have this strapped to your hand, you cannot turn it on/off because you only have one hand to use and it’s hard to twist the end with one hand. So I have it hanging from my Gear Keeper retractor so I can use both hands to turn it on/off. Otherwise I love this light for the price.

Exceeds my expectations.

This review will cover my initial impressions of this light. I will not have a chance to actually dive with it for a few weeks, at which time I will update this. This flashlight arrived quickly via Amazon Prime shipping. It has, so far, exceeded my expectations. First, it comes in a handy hard plastic case. What I did not expect was the quality of the case, with egg-crate foam padding and four latches. The flashlight itself is a nice size and heft. It is nice and bright, with a well focused beam. It seems to be pretty tight, with almost no halo effect. There is a very solid loop molded into the body of the light, for the wrist-strap, which is also included. There is also a lanyard strap, a rechargeable battery, and a 3-battery clip for using standard AAA batteries. That is a lot of comprehensive equipment for this price point. The switch is integrated into the battery cap. Turn it once for bright. Turn it off and on for dim. Do it again for strobe. Pretty simple. There are three O-rings sealing the cap, and they are nicely greased with jelly. I am excited to try this out on my next dive!
