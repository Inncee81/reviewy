Product Reviews

Works without the FastTrack rails!

I could not find any other way to hang my string trimmer on the wall effectively. I have a 14 foot 2”x6” lag bolted to the garage studs which I hang various yard tools from. The Rubbermaud FasTrack hangers are made to hang from their track system. The top and bottom screw holes do not line up flat against the 2”x6” as Rubbermaid makes them offset to only properly fit their track. I did not want to use their track for a number of reasons including being stuck with buying only their system hangers. I used construction screws and some washers to make this hanger lie flat against the 2”x6” and it worked fine!

Great hook on a great system.

The power tool hook is great! It fits my Stihl string trimmer perfectly. It won’t hold my Stihl blower though, the longer ladder or multi purpose hooks work great for that. One thing to note, I had it on the rail in my garage, and it works perfectly. That was temporary as I was in the process of getting a new shed. I hung a 84in rail in the shed, on open studs. The weight of the trimmer (15lbs) and the lack of solid wall behind the rail pushed the plastic clip and the spring behind it out of the little hinges that hold it onto the metal. This resulted in the hook only being held on by the top and not the bottom, so it could easily be pulled off the wall with the tool. I was able to get the plastic back on and put a screw behind it to offer more support for the plastic piece. This hook should be designed for the extra weight that a trimmer with a full tank of gas presents. But it’s only a problem on open stud walls. Other than that, I totally recommend this hook.

What is it?

This is described as a mesh basket but depicted as a wire basket. So, what do you get? I got a wire basket which is fine with me since I really did not care what type it was but I just needed a basket. I love the FastTrack system and have many of the attachments including a Rubbermaid mesh basket which I purchased at Lowes. I have a wire basket from Lowes as well but needed another one and did not feel like making a trip to Lowes when Amazon delivers right to my door.This is a sturdy basket which I use to store various garden hose parts and attachments. It is two feet long, a foot wide and about eight and a half inches deep. It holds a lot of stuff.FYI, the basket comes with an instruction sheet which refers to it as a wire basket. On the basket itself is a label which also calls it a wire basket. If you are in need of a mesh basket you will need to look elsewhere.

basket has 2 hanging options!

OK… if you are BUYING THE BASKETS, look at the photos, so you know how to attach them to the brackets.I installed 2 baskets in my garage and thought “Wow… these look really sloppy. The online photos are fakes! The baskets tip down at a 30 degree angle. OK for my use, but I feel mis-lead.” Then I came back to this page to write exactly this message, but decided to poke about a bit to see if other people had this issue and how they might have fixed it.It turns out that there are 2 cross bars on each of the brackets (the part that clamps on to the rail). If you hang the basket off of the lower bar (the one with the cut-out), you get the 30 degree droop like I did. If you hang the basket off of the upper cross bar, you get a perfectly even-hanging basket. I just noticed that the product photos show both options being used.See my attached photo to see both options at the same time.
