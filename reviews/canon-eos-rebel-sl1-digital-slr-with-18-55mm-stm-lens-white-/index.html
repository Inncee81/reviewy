A Very Capable Starter DSLR

I should open this review by saying I don’t even consider myself an ‘amateur photographer’, as that now seems to be read as implying some kind of expertise. I do enjoy taking photos, and since I print a good amount of them I like them to be as high quality as I can make them without breaking the bank. I had a lower end, compact point and shoot along with a better point and shoot for special events or trips. Over time, it became pretty clear that my cell phone would easily match the cheaper point and shoot and so I decided to get an actual DSLR as my sole non-cell camera. I’ve used Canon for most of my life, and I wanted to stick with that so I started looking into cameras that were good but under $1000. After a few weeks of reading the pros and cons of a variety of models, I went with the SL1. I had seen some very nice images from it, the size was right, and Amazon had a good deal on a bundle with it.So far, it’s been a great experience with this camera. It pretty much nailed the sweet spot I was looking for as far as giving me really good image quality in a fairly compact DSLR. I found the response time on taking images to very speedy, the clarity in both day and night shots is very good, and overall I feel like there’s really not much I can’t handle with the unit. For my first DSLR, I think I made the right decision is picking this one up. I also tested it using one of the EyeFi memory cards, and it works well with it although the delay in between taking shots is much more noticeable. Even so, it’s great to have the option for times where I want to quickly transfer photos to my phone and post them.

Good, solid purchase

This is an awesome travel camera. For most people it can even serve as their go to everyday camera. For travel, if you pop in a 40mm pancake lens, it’s extremely compact and good for shooting street photography. The lens wont get in your way and you actually think about composing shots due to lack of zoom if using a prime lens. Picture quality is decent and pretty much on par with slightly more upscale camera (t5i etc). The battery life is ok, but not great. I averaged around 3 to 400 shots before I needed to change battery. I def recommending getting an extra battery along with your purchase. Overall, a solid purchase if you want to use a travel camera (due to it’s weight and size) or if you want a starting dslr (pretty good value). However, keep in mind that lack of a swivel screen can affect you if you take a lot of abnormal angle shots.

I love it!It’s a bit of an adjustment from full-frame, and a smaller body, but that hasn’t been too hard. And it’s great that all my EF lenses work on this camera too.
