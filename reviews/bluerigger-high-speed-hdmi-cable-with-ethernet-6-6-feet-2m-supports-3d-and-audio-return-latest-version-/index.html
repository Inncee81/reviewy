Great Cables, and I’ve literally seen most

So to begin, I’m a Home Theater Installer. I spent five years with Best Buy both selling and installing TVs. I never got too much into the Monster Cable stuff, as at the end of the day, it’s copper wire with covering; I used to sell people the less expensive cables, citing this as the reason. That said, you can judge an HDMI Cable on two (2) things: Pictures Quality, which, for the vast majority, will be absolutely the same, whether the cable costs you $1 or $1,000. This is why people say don’t spend more money, and they’d be mostly right. However, the 2nd category that’s used to judge HDMI Cables is build quality, and THAT can be a big difference. All build quality really refers to is the head (The gold part you plug into the devices you have), and how it will do considering the rest of the cable is hanging off of it, thus potentially causing it to bend and snap. THIS is why some cables are worth spending more money on. I mean if you don’t put it on the wall and hide the wires, it really doesn’t matter, as you have easy access. But this is the reason I’ve stuck with Bluerigger. In my mind, despite the fact that I don’t buy Monster, they are the Smart Shoppers Monster Equivalent. I would use them in my own system, and I use them with all of my customers. They make a good product, and they offer it at an amazing price. Buy this cable. The only reason cables cost more at big box stores, for those of you unfamiliar with retail, is that is where they make up the money they lose when they sell you a TV. That’s right folks, a $1,000 TV costs the store/company about $850 to $900 to buy, maybe a little less. They make their money on cables and other accessories.

Surprising quality, great price!

Well made, works perfectly, fits snug and stays in place. Highly recommended. I have read many different articles on the quality of HDMI cable brands. Some manufactures try to impress you that you must use this kind or that kind for different resolution visual quality. Some science magazines and Internet articles say basically this type of marketing is a lot of hype just to grab your cash at lightning speed. Bottom line, digital signal is a digital signal. Like over-the-air HD TV signals, you either have a great picture or none at all. In other words, to the human eye and ear, you really can’t tell the difference when the digital signal is properly received then transferred to your TV. However, there are different quality materials that will make your HDMI cable more usable, more likely it will last longer, and you must order the right cable length – meaning no more than you have to. Do remember the signal does start disintegrating with useless extra cable length curled up behind your entertainment center. How much will it lower picture/sound quality over a certain length? From my research, you won’t be able to tell the difference again if you use a 6’ cable vs a 12’ cable, but you may if you stretch the HDMI signal 50’ across your attic and down into another part of your house. In that case, it’s time to call the cable guy. Back to the quality of BlueRigger High Speed HDMI – I purchased the 2m (6.6’) cable which was all I needed and it left a little slack for easy installing. The cable is thick, appears very well made, and the actual connector looks very similar to my older $65 HDMI cable that I purchased about 5 years ago when I believed all the marketing hype. I’m a techie, I love electronics, I build my own computers, and I spend hours “tuning” brand new TVs. I love this stuff. My education is in industrial engineering – not electrical engineering. But I believe I have enough knowledge to recognize a well designed, well crafted HDMI cable. I’m very surprised you can buy this type of quality for the Amazon.com’s asking price. Great purchase.
