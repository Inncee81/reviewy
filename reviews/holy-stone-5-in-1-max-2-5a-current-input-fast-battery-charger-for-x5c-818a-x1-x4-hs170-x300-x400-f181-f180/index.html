Nice little charger that does the job

I’ve used this charger a lot since receiving it a week ago and have no complaints. The connectors snap solidly into the unit so they won’t dislodge during charging and the LEDs will turn off one by one as each battery becomes fully charged. As one reviewer mentioned they are very difficult to unplug once charging is complete due to their small size. I use a pair of small needle-nose pliers to gently grip the plastic connector and this makes removal very easy thus avoiding having to pull on the connector wires and possibly damaging your batteries.It’s very convenient to be able to charge 5 batteries at one time since quadrotors discharge them so fast. I’d recommend also picking up a dedicated USB wall charger to go along with this unit so that you won’t have to use your computer’s slow charging ports. USB2 ports on a PC will only spit out 500mA and even standard USB3 ports will only do 900mA, so if you are trying to charge 5 dead 380mAh batteries it will take well over 2 hours using a PC USB3 port and almost 4 hours on a USB2. I purchased the Anker 20-watt 2-port wall charger which can produce 2.4A per port and can fully recharge 6 dead batteries in only about 45 minutes.http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00LFL2JB6/ref=oh_aui_detailpage_o03_s00?ie=UTF8&psc=1Combining these two little gizmos allows me to spend more time flying quads and less time charging batteries.
