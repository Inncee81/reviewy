Plenty of fun for not much money

These are pretty good fun. I’m a professional photographer, and I wanted some flexibility with my phone photography. These little lenses do a good job of offering that.The macro is, in my opinion, the most fun. Just make sure you’re shooting something on a fairly level plane, or else be prepared to see why a set aperture of f/2.0 isn’t always that great. Something turned even a little to one side is suddenly lost in the bokeh, which is sometimes cool, sometimes maddening (examples of both attached). But if you know what to expect, you can make things turn out.The fisheye and wide angle I haven’t used as much, but they’re fine. Maybe a little blurry sometimes? But that probably has something to do with not positioning them just right (I’ll give the lens the benefit of the doubt.) The wide angle is great for group photos where you don’t want the people on the edges to look like the Nazis who opened the Ark of the Covenant.If you’re looking for a bit more flexibility and fun possibilities out of your phone camera, I’d say these are worth the relatively minimal investment.

Do not think that you necessarily need to buy a very expensive product, this price has the same effect, which is definitely a product worth buying.
