Great for cloth diapers!

This thing is awesome and such a great space saver. I use it to hang up my daughters cloth diapers and it have saved me so much room. When we do a load of wash it can be around 20 or so diapers and then in the drying process we would lay them all over the house to dry. This slowly became a pain and I was desperate for an alternative. It was always an interesting conversation if someone happened to do a pop in on laundry day. After doing some research I came across this option. Since its made of plastic I wasn’t too sure on its durability but decided to give it a shot.The clip was awesome and I was able to hang it on my door frame right next to the laundry room and load it with diapers straight from the washer, this not only saved me time it was so convenient to be able to do it right out of the washer and not drip water around if the diapers were still a bit on the wet side. I am able to use every hook for a diaper and I would hang it from the middle of the diaper like the picture below shows that way I would not put any strain on the elastics.Being in the Arizona heat I could transport these to my shaded patio so that they could dry even quicker without taking the risk of line drying in direct sunlight and heat, and bring them right back in, I would unclip them very quickly due to the quick release and we were all set.This is a must if you cloth diaper and don’t have the option to line dry due to excessive sunlight or heat or maybe you live in an area that its cold often. I bought two, one for the inserts and one for the pockets. Don’t be wary of the price it’s very strong and durable and works great!READ MORE

Wonderful product for tight spaces

I have owned two of these for years and absolutely love them. I originally bought them to help dry delicates, but after a while realized that they can easily hold pairs of jeans if you use more than two hooks. When I lived in a cramped apartment I was able to fit two of these from the shower curtain rod and dry almost all of my laundry at the same time. I do realize that I do smaller loads than a lot of people, so I would say that for me all the laundry was about 1 medium load spread across 2 of these, with some creative Tetris work involved. The things that would not fit were mostly things that I needed to flat dry anyway, so I never thought that I needed to get a third.I originally purchased these in 2005, it is now 2012 and both of them are going strong. For apartments where the only thing you have to dry on is the shower curtain rod, these cannot be beat. For everyone else, these are great for outdoor hanging, hanging in the closet, hanging from a curtain rod… basically just hanging clothing in spaces and areas that you might never have considered hanging laundry from before.

Very handy device for those who prefer to air dry clothes.

If you don’t have a clothes dryer or don’t like using it, a clip drying rack is indispensable. I’m such a person; I prefer to air dry clothes. I’ve tried several kinds of racks of the years, and this is the best. The capacity is usually sufficient for all the socks and underwear, etc in a load of wash, however I bought a second one for those overflow loads.The clips have a good, springy action and are easily gripped–easier than clothespins. The spring-loaded hook at the top allows you to hang the rack on most anything. (I move mine around to take advantage of the breeze.) It’s also handy for projects such a repairing objects with glue. I make the repair, then clamp it into the rack.I’ve had mine for over a year. I keep it outside most of the time, but not exposed to direct sunlight. It seems very durable thus far. I live in a humid climate and some plastics simply degrade after a year of so. This Whitmor laundry hanger is made of plastic (and metal springs) that is holding up very well.
