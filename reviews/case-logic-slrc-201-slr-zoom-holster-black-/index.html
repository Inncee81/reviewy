Solid case, great padding

If there was a 4.5 stars I’d use that, but I have to round down slightly..First, this is a great, solid case. The “hammock suspension” is basically a neoprene type sling at the top with a hole for the lens. It’s well designed and holds my trusty Canon T3i very securely, whether with the 18-55 kit lens or with the 55-250 zoom. Yes, it most definitely fits the zoom lens.Zippers seem solid enough, will be putting it to the test on a trip next month, the strap attaches securely, and the high-visibility interior color means it’s hard to lose things in any of the pockets. Speaking of pockets, they’re all pretty tiny. Plenty big enough for a filter kit, SD cards, spare batteries, etc… but don’t plan to pack chargers and such in this bag. That’s just not what it’s for.The two drawbacks for me that led to less than 5 stars are room for an attached camera strap and overall weight. First, I have a strap attached to me T3i. Stuff happens, I’d rather not drop it. Trying to get the Camera in with the strap attached and not having it pressed against the exposed LCD requires some beginner origami; it’s do-able, I just wish the foam that does such a great job of holding the camera in and protecting the LCD had a recess on each side to give some extra room to tuck the strap into.Second, this case is beefy. That’s both a good and a bad thing - I really like the excellent level of protection it provides, but part of me is wondering if I need that much due to the tradeoff of some weight. and bulk when trying to fit it into a daypack with this case. But I really didn’t want yet another backpack so this is a good tradeoff.I’d definitely recommend this case to others - good price, good quality, great protection. If you don’t have a strap on your DSLR you’ll really appreciate how snugly it holds everything in there… if you do have a strap, well, you’ll manage.
