One of the few 12VDC players out there

My rating is somewhat reflective of the fact that this is a 12VDC player and that is uncommon; most small electronics that aren’t USB powered run at 19 volts. I wanted this for our off-grid cabin where we only run 12 volt solar/battery DC and did not want to have to step up voltage, but it could also be used on an RV or boat - very low power consumption. I built an automotive-style power cable by cannibalizing a Hosa ACD-477 Universal Power Adaptor I already had for a matching tip, and a Roadpro 12V Fused Replacement Cigarette Lighter Plug with Leads for the plug with a spot of wire between. I could have sacrificed the power adapter that came with it, but wanted to preserve that in case we brought it home in the future. Using a long HDMI cable, we have this connected to a AAXA P300 Pico/Micro LED Projector with 60 Minute Battery Life, WXGA 1280x800 Resolution, 400 Lumens, HDMI, Mini-VGA, 20,000 Hour LED Life, Media Player, which is a projector that also runs at 12 volts. So far so good. Because the projector is mounted overhead, we wanted to use the USB port on this to load media and movies. We found that movies ripped to 1280x800 with 2 channel stereo at 44kHz sample rate play fine. We also tested out Netflix - we use a phone based hotspot in an cradle amplifier for internet - and in our 10 minute test it worked perfectly, even given our limited bandwidth. Overall we are quite happy. If I was buying this for home I might choose a player that had component video out, optical audio out, or analog audio out as secondary - this is HDMI only - but for our purposes this is fine.
