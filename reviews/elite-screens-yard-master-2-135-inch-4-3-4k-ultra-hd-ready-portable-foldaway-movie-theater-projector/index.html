120 inch front version.Already have a “permanent” high-end wall mount - but needed something portable (primarily for indoor use in various locations where I don’t want to have a permanent installation).Was a bit concerned about the folded screen comments and the expectation of creases. Once this arrived it was easy enough to set up - and I found that while there are creases in the screen, they are very light and uniform (as the screen was folded extremely well) and are only vaguely noticeable when whitespace is projected… and after a few days even less so. As someone who is picky when it comes to creases in a screen, I’m happy to say it’s a non-issue for me w/this product.The one piece frame concept is really cool, though did notice there is a VERY MINOR curvature or “sag” of the top bar once assembled - but again not enough to be an issue for me (and yes this type of stuff typically bothers me), so that’s how minor it is - and doesn’t impact the screen tension in any way from what I can see. Screen itself snaps on fairly easily, was able to set it up myself though a second person would have been nice.The screen material is not quite as good as my permanent wall mount (which was over $1k) - but I would say it’s very decent for the price and better than many of the screens I’ve had in the past.For indoor use, can be placed against a wall and only fold the front legs out if that’s how you want to elevate it - though in my case I created hidden wall mounts in the locations I plan to use it, so I can just carry it over and use the mounts, no need for the legs. Did try it with the legs attached though, and it fit nicely in the basement @ 88 inch ceiling height.Nice packaging, and overall the product has a quality feel to it - moreso than I expected. I would give it 8⁄10 if it cost $300+, but at this price point I really think it’s a 9+. Can’t really go wrong with this.

Great movie screen

This screen is awesome. It is huge and takes up the whole room and it is not fully assembled, as I have low ceilings in the den. Feels like you are in the theater when viewing on this. Not sure why some are complaining about wrinkles/fold lines. When you snap this onto the frame they stretch and smooth out. It does not affect the quality of the movie and you would have to be standing right in front and on top of the screen to even detect where they are. I have only used this indoors so far due to the weather, but cannot wait to have a real outdoor movie night. I am also looking forward to taking it on camping trips. It is great that I do not need another power supply to blow up a screen, this comes in a carrying case which may be a bit bulky and a tad heavy (but I’ll manage) . My husband has set it up and taken it down by himself with no problem, I expect the same. You get what you pay for and this is a real quality product. The screen framework is sturdy and the screen is not cheap vinyl.

This thing is so easy to put up and tear down it’s not even …

This thing is so easy to put up and tear down it’s not even funny. If you can fold and unfold an umbrella, you can assemble and disassemble this. In fact, snapping buttons together from the screen to the frame is probably what takes the longest. But even that is only about 3 minutes. The screen itself is very high quality. When folded, there are creases that appear, but on a warm day they disappear after a few minutes in the sun after you set it up. The frame itself is very light and easy to manipulate. It’s sturdy enough to stand on it’s own if there’s no wind. When there is wind, we just place something heavy on the legs, like pavers blocks or plastic weights. It also comes with a convenient carry/storage bag. Which when all bundled up, is light enough to carry with one hand, and small enough to store in a closet. It is a little pricey, there are cheaper options which I explored. But I’m happy I went with this both for quality of viewing and convenience of setting up and tearing down.
