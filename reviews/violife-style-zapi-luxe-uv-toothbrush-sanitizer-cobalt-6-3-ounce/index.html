    Product Description

Color:Cobalt Odds are good that you clean your silverware, cups, plates, and bowls- almost anything that goes into your mouth-after every use. Why not your toothbrush? Unlike most of the other things you put in your mouth every day, your toothbrush has tightly packed bristles which form nooks and crannies that hold on to moisture, forming a perfect sanctuary for germs that cause illnesses like flu, cold, and diarrhea. Violife sanitizers use the same ultraviolet technology found in hospitals and high-end water purification systems to hit the germs where they live-in your toothbrush. The UV rays penetrate areas that you simply can’t reach when running your brush under the tap, then safely and effectively obliterate up to 99.9% of disease-causing germs.

Needs redesign, bottom is curved, needs to have a flat area to stabilize

Works very well. Not stable. Bottom is curved, with less than a dime size area that is flat. I have an electric toothbrush and when I place it in the egg, it falls over. This would have been very easy to design with a flatter, more stable bottom. The manual shows the bottom as having about a silver dollar size flat area. My suggestion unless manufacturer redesigns: Be careful when you remove the egg from its original box. Leave the clear plastic that is “hot glued” to the bottom of the egg attached. Replacing my old Zapi

I had a Zapi for about three years prior to purchasing this unit to replace it. The one I replaced was still working at the time I received the new one. Although the units separate to allow for internal cleaning, it’s nearly impossible to get it truly clean. Areas in and around the UV bulb get some ugly stuff for which you just can’t clean around. After a while, I started to get a bit uncomfortable about my toothbrush sitting in there with that funk even if the UV light is supposed to keep it sanitized. I can’t help wondering if the UV light is actually working to sanitize the toothbrush, particularly when the nasty stuff, which is mold looking, is right in and around the light. Yet, I do like that it keeps the brush head out of the exposed surrounding environment and supposedly is receiving some benefits from the UV light. I am also pleased that my old unit worked without any operational issues until I decided to replace it. At the price of these units, even if I decide to replace it every year or two, I think the it’s worth the value of the product.
