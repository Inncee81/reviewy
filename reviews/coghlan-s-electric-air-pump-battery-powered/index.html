Coghlan's Electric Air Pump, Battery Powered

Product Specifications

  • Economical battery powered pump powered by four D-size cell batteries (not included)
  • Portable pump is ideal for air mattresses and beds, pools, towable boats, tubes, sleds, and inflatable furniture
  • Three adapter nozzles fit most recreational inflatables; quick inflation and deflation of double lock valves, Boston valves and pinch valves
  • Maximum PSI pressure rate: 0.42

  • Affordable, essential gear for car camping, RV trailers, motor homes, cabins, and more

    Product Description

Easily Inflate or deflate an airbed, air mattress, towable tubes, boats, kayaks, SUPs, pools and floats, and inflatable furniture with this easy-to-use portable Coghlan’s battery operated air pump. Included are three interconnecting nozzle adapters that provide quick inflation/deflation of recreational and home inflatables with double lock, Boston, and pinch valves. This economically priced air pump is powered by four D-size cell batteries (not included). Not for use with stem or needle valves that are required for basket balls, footballs, tires, and more. Coghlan’s makes the outdoors more comfortable and enjoyable with over 450 accessories for camping, hiking, fishing, and RVing – from cooking gear to first aid kits, insect netting, firestarters, tent repair, and more.

Product Reviews

No electricity? No problem!

I was a little hesitant to get this air pump simply because I didn’t know if it could handle pumping up our extra large queen mattress as well as two twins running on battery. Our mattresses are the kind that have the built-in air pump, but you need to have electricity to plug them in (obviously), and we were camping at a site that not only didn’t have electricity, but we had to carry our gear to the site after parking our car (too) far away, unable to utilize the A/C receptacle our cars are fortunate to have.The only gripe I have is the attachments that come with this - there are three different size nozzles to choose from - none of which fit the mattress plug to keep the air going into the mattress vs. leaking out all around the nozzle. So it took a little patience (and holding the nozzle and air pump firm, blocking the leakage with my hands) to get the air in, but it did ultimately work. The size configuration of the nozzle adapters is the only thing that knocks one star off this air pump.I was worried about battery power lasting, and it certainly does - in fact, the batteries still had plenty of power to fill up the kids’ mattresses to do a camp out in the backyard a week or so later - and that was after we borrowed the batteries for a battery powered lantern we used throughout the weekend, since we forgot to buy enough batteries for both.Word to the wise, though - if you have long hair, make sure you hold this thing away from you, or it will try to eat your hair where the intake fan is. Ahem.
