It’s the Winner !

I recently ordered four of the highest-rated, low cost portable Bluetooth speakers for comparison. My intent was to buy the one I liked best for my personal use when travelling and to return the others. Following are my non-professional impressions.The products I purchased from Amazon, and the prices I actually paid, were:Sony SRSX11 $48DKnight MagicBox II $35Cambridge SoundWorks OontZ Angle 3 $30Anker Classic $37I played a variety of music on each of them, including Van Morrison, Miles Davis, U2 and Pink Floyd, constantly rotating between the speakers mid-song as I compared their sound.1. The Anker was by far the least impressive. It sounded tinny and flat with little or no bass. Very disappointing and was an easy reject, the first to go back into its box.2. The OontZ Angle sounded pretty good at first, and showed excellent midrange and treble notes. It also played at a nice loud volume. I liked it a lot but ultimately I noticed the bass response was lacking and it sounded a little thin compared to the remaining others, so it was next to go. Too bad, because I liked the styling of it, and the fact that, unlike the others, it had no annoying blue light to bother me in a dark room.The last two standing were a tough choice, but …3. The MagicBox was really good and almost won out. It had a full sound and great style, but in the end I found it didn’t have the best range from highs to lows that I heard in the Sony. Just not quite enough bass to make me happy. If it had been the only one I’d heard I would have been quite happy with it, and felt sad to send it back.4. The Sony was totally surprising given its very small size. I thought it would be easy to rule out before I heard it, but it kept impressing me with its excellent bass response and midrange tones. I’d say it has a cleaner sound overall. Maybe in this case, a little higher cost is worth the price. I’m keeping this one, knowing it’ll fill up a hotel room with great sound.Hope this helps, and happy listening!
