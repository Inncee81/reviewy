Great glasses but pricey

I tried some of the cheaper pairs of glasses with my Acer H6510BD 3D Projector. While they worked all right the nose guard seemed to be made for a child or someone with a really thin nose. No such problems with these wonderful glasses. 3D looked great , they didn’t pinch my nose, stayed put throughout the movie, and while I can see how some may judge them heavy to wear…I had no such problems. The battery seems to last a long time. I haven’t run it down, usually after two movies I charge it…though it doesn’t need it.The only negatives I have is it comes with a special power cable (USB to a very small barrel connector) and it’s hard to tell if you have it turned on.In the next update, I would like to see a USB to microUSB power adapter and a slide switch to power it on. Perhaps a slightly more comfortable nose piece or an option to use nose pieces from Oakley Sunglasses.

Looks great

Fits comfortably, looks dorky (as do most of us who are actually buying 3d glasses) and takes hardly any time to set up. All that I had to do was turn the glasses on and set my Optoma H180X to DLP link 3d and pop the disk into my PS4. Everything worked wonderfully. My wife thought the glasses were a bit big but I think she was being Whiny ; ) (she has a small head) The smaller nose piece that came with them helped her out a bit.Great product with Excellent 3d image. My only issue is the image was kind of dark and there was a glare on the back side of the lenses (very shiny lenses) but that was easily fixed by turning off all the ambient lights in the room and turning projector brightness up.The product comes with a nice little bag to keep them in and a proprietary usb cable to charge them (I am sad they aren’t Micro USB)
