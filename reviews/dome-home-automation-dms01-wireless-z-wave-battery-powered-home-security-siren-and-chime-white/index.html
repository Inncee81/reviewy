Product Reviews

Works well, not super user friendly, and is a little overpriced

Bought this for use with a Vera controller in a family member’s house. The advantage is the ten chime settings that allow you to use as a door chime. Part of what was desired was a chime like traditional wired alarms so that there would be an audible alert when a door in the house is opened. For some reason this is the only product that i could find that does this. The tones are pretty weak in terms of appeal to me. There are two out of the ten that work for a chime, although one of those is really just a doorbell sound. I configured it for the single beep when a door is opened. It is pretty reliable as far as i can tell. The delay is very slight… about the time the door is opened 30 - 45 degrees the chime beeps. That’s perfectly acceptable for me. There are three volume settings but i have yet to see how to adjust those without LUA code. For Vera there is a plug-in that makes selecting the tones easy via the GUI rather than having to write that stupid LUA code to call the function. Dome’s website has how-tos on triggering the chimes via LUA code, but i didn’t have any luck getting one of those to run. The plug-in is pretty stupid in my opinion as it doesn’t seem to provide any useful feedback to let you know the chime is actually configured. It makes a second dome siren in your devices, so look for that. The second one created will have buttons to select tone chimes by clicking a button instead of writing a LUA function. It would be really nice if Dome made these a) without the useless LED, b) with better options for chime tones that don’t require an expert to program them into the device c) for less than $50, and d) make an outdoor version. The price seems ludicrous for just being a “beeper”. The build quality seems good so i would definitely say it’s not a cheap-o z-wave device.READ MORE

Awesome with the Custom SmartThings Device Code

Has many many more options than similar products on the market for zwave and smart things. The alternate device is available for free as a github download and you simply have to get on your computer access your home in smart things, find this device and copy paste the code into the device and you’re done. The altered code lets you change volume and ring tone of the alarm – which is great. I live in an apartment, I do not need high heavens to hear the alarm. Just enough ruckus to stop the intruder which in most cases is the apt complex trying to enter to fix things and lately they have been bad about letting me know.
