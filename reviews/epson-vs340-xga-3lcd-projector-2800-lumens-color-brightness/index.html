but it was worth it because this is a great projector. The two key elements for me were …

I researched a long time before buying this projector, but it was worth it because this is a great projector. The two key elements for me were the lumens and the resolution. The 2800 lumens is bright enough to project clearly in a lit room. I don’t think there is much difference between 2800 and 3000 lumens, but there is a big difference between 2800 and the ones in the lower range that require a dark room for projection.The other key element was the resolution. I read many reviews that talked about how the 800x600 resolution was not sharp enough, so I paid a little bit more and got this projector with the XGA (1024x768) resolution. It projects a sharp picture which is good enough to read Powerpoint briefings with regular size print. I am impressed with it so far.Another factor I looked at is replacement bulbs. This has economy bulbs for less than $40 or premium for around $90. .

This is a bright and easy to use projector at a reasonable price.

This unit was purchased to use in outreach programs at widely varying venues and room conditions. It is also used by people unfamiliar with setting up and using projectors. It was worked out beautifully for us. We plan to buy another one now.Using HDMI connections we are able to move seamlessly between MAC and Windows computers eliminating the need for adapters. That simple connection also means it is extremely simple to set up for our inexperienced people. The built-in speakers are adequate for all but the largest rooms so far. Sound quality is not theater quality, but does have enough clarity and volume.There is a significant price difference between the XGA and WXGA. In my testing I couldn’t detect that much difference. The XGA has worked well for our Powerpoint presentations featuring photos.2800 lumens translates into a bright image in most situations. The lens system produces a sharp image. The keystone correction option is pretty amazing providing side to side as well as up and down adjustments to fix keystoning. Controls are easy to learn and use. I like the fact that the screen can be darkened from the remote control.One limitation for me is that filling a wall with an image requires the projector to be located well back in the room. The zoom and lens combination doesn’t allow that short throw. That is a minor issue for most of us.Time will tell if we get 10,000 hours out of the lamp. It shouldn’t be a problem since they are relatively inexpensive to replace.In summary, this is a lightweight, bright and easy to use projector at a reasonable price.
