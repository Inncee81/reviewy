Product Reviews

The batteries and charger seems to work just fine fine and I haven’t had the problem with batteries …

The batteries and charger work fine, but the battery capacity isn’t any different from stock. I haven’t had the problem with batteries falling out of the charger yet that some have. However the listing claims that the battery is higher capacity than the stock battery. My testing indicated that this is not true. To test this I first weighed the battery and its weight was the same as my stock battery to the best my scale would read (one tenth oz). Lithium is an alkali metal so larger capacity batteries should weigh more. This made me suspicious enough to go the next step and test it in the camera. I set up the camera on a tripod to take time laps flash photos of the same seen until the battery capacity was exhausted. All batteries were fully charged at the start with the same “BM Premium” charger, the camera was set to the same mode and was set to shut down between flash photos. The stock battery (1250 mAh) shot 212 pictures before it quit, the “BM Premium” shot 209 photos before it quit which is close enough to indicate that it is probably just a stock battery that has been relabeled. A third battery I tested “Kastar” which claimed to be 1800 mAh also shot 209 photos before it quit. So while these batteries seem to work fine, they are most likely nothing more than relabeled stock batteries.

Worked well on my travels abroad…

I bought this charger and battery set to supplement the one battery that came with the camera and the requirement by Panasonic to charge the battery in the camera. The MAH were a little higher with these new batteries and they lasted a little longer than the Panasonic original. The charger came with a European adapter which worked well while travelling in Italy. The charger did not have any issues charging the original Panasonic battery, but I would not expect any issues as the battery specifications should be the same. I really did not pay attention to time it took to complete a recharge cycle as I would hook it up before bed and it was done before morning. A feature I would have liked on this charger is a USB charging port. It seems outlets in Italian hotel rooms are usually limited to one here or there and it would be nice to have a USB port to charge a phone or another device built into the charger.

Battery holder comes loose, and one battery has poor capacity

You get what you pay for. The battery charger “works”. It is composed of two pieces. One part plugs into the wall, the other part clips onto it, and holds the battery. I assume the plug in part is universal, and they pair it with the appropriate battery adapter. The problem I have is the battery holder doesn’t stay attached well. A slight bump and the battery holder detaches from the plug in half. I may try tape or super glue to remedy that problem. Unfortunately, one of the two batteries does not hold as much charge as the other. One battery is actually quite good, or at least as good as the stock Panasonic battery. The other battery only provides about 1⁄4 of the use before I need to recharge it. I’ve gone through about 4 charge cycles on each, hoping that might improve the capacity but no improvement. I did not get a chance to try out the 12 volt car charging adapter.
