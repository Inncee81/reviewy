More Features than Expected!

For the money, this is a great product! For around $20, I was expecting a basic, low quality product to get me by. All it had to do was hold my camera (Nikon D5000) in place and I would have been satisfied. To my surprise, there are features included with this tripod that are on $40+ tripods! Two different bubble levelers, one for the base and another for the camera, an independent crank riser for additional height, multiple tightening locations to keep your camera in place, and the ability to hold your camera in both landscape and portrait.Since receiving this tripod, I did a “test” where I installed my camera and left it alone for almost five days with the legs fully extended and positioning locations locked / secured. This tripod held its position without any trouble, easing my concerns about it falling over and damaging my camera.One issue I found is related to my specific camera (See Image). When attached to the tripod, I am unable to flip the screen around due to the locking arm being in the way. This is not a major issue since you only need to release the camera, flip the screen as desired, and re-secure.Overall, I’m completely satisfied with my purchase and would strongly recommend it to anyone. Take a look at my added images where I give a closer look of its features and capabilities.

Not a bad first tripod for the price

It’s really not bad, considering the price. But it has some fatal flaws:- there is too much friction in the plastic-on-plastic joints, which makes the up/down and left/right swivel actions very difficult to fine-tune- It’s so lightweight that it’ll be top-heavy if you have a heavy camera/rigging- The light weight also makes it impossible to not move/nudge it while adjusting it- the large mounting shoe covers the battery/SD card hatch on the bottom of my G7X. So I have to totally remove it in order to change the battery or put in a fresh storage card, negating the “quick” part of the “quick mount” system.The good parts:- it’s light weight makes it nearly effortless to carry around- lots of flexibility in height adjustment

Great tripod for the price!

I needed something to use for a family photo session on vacation and bought this because I’d lost the camera shoes for my old and much more expensive tripod. The pan/tilt is smooth, elevation crank is fairly smooth as well. The weight hook at the bottom of the tripod is a great addition and is needed since the tripod is light–if only I’d used it for the family photo since it was a windy day and the tripod was blown over. There is a leveling bubble (works well) and the handle is a welcome addition. While I’ve only used it for a few hours over the course of a couple of days in a sandy environment, the leg locks held well and all moving parts are still working without grit. I’d buy it again if the need arose.

Very servicable, light, a bit bulky

For context I am a hobbyist who hadn’t bought a new tripod in something like 15 years. I was very impressed with this tripod. It has a solid and stable feel to it. It adjusts, easily, smoothly, and intuitively. It is reasonably light, though not light enough for hiking. It is also quite bulky, even when collapsed. The reason for the bulk is that this is the larger version of this tripod–they make a smaller version that might be of more practical use to many. For those who care about such things, the legs are constrained in how far they can swing out and cannot swing out independently of each other. It has a large handle for ease of moving around that can be a minor obstacle when setting up.
