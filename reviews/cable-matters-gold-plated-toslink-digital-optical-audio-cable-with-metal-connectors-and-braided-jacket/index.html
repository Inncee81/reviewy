It’s stiffer than most, thus does not readily kink. The plastic covers are tied to the cable with sleeves. Neat!

Good Quality Cable

Probably not much need for digital optical cables unless one is in need of workaround connections between new and previous generation equipment which is what I was trying to accomplish. I used it to supply a audio connection between my new TV and a 3 year old surround sound system without ARC capability. This appears to be a very high quality cable and works perfectly for my application. Maybe less expensive cables would have worked just as well but, in my experience, paying just a little more for a higher quality cable is worth the few extra dollars.

Impressive construction

I haven’t used other optical cables before so this would be my first, but I knew going in that I wanted something that would be sturdy and not bend easily where the cable is terminated (usually a problem for thinner cables such as USB or 3.5mm TRS). This cable is built to be very strong, in fact it would actually pull the DAC I’m using it with onto its side before I positioned the cable in a certain orientation. This is difficult to explain, but it will be obvious if you are plugging one end into something that doesn’t weigh very much. I think this product should last quite a long time and certainly won’t pose the same issues that thinner cables would when they eventually start to drop signals because of wear and tear.

Value-Priced Cables that Perform As Well or Better than Expensive Brands

I bought a 2-pack of these Toslink cables to use with an outboard DAC and tube amp (both made by Little Dot). I must say, I was both surprised and impressed by the build quality, design, and style of these cables as I had never used any made by Cable Matters. The cloth jacket, terminals, thickness, and protective caps are all areas that I think these cables deserve credit for given their price point. More importantly, their performance rivals that of more expensive brands. Based on my experience with Cable Matters, I would happily buy its cables again in the future – especially if the company makes HDMI 2.0 and USB 3.0 cables.
