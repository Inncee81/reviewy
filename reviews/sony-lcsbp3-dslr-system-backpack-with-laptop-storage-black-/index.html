First look at Sony LCSBP3 backpack

Just got it today. Went through it thoroughly. Claims are correct, it holds my SLT-A99V with SAL70400 4-5.6 G SSM II lens and Vertical Grip attached. Bag is very well made. Like the rubber feet on bottom so back sets up upright off the ground. The rain cover is accessed at bottom back of backpack. Like the left side zippered pocket, mesh pocket and flapped pocket. However, that’s side tripod fits on so those pockets aren’t accessible with tripod attached. There is also a deep mesh pocket on right side which is same side used to access camera without taking off the backpack (although with a large lens like the SAL70400 it’s a bit awkward not to mention if you also have a computer in computer pouch and items in the top compartment due to weight but it can be done). That brings me to the computer compartment. Appears it will hold 15” laptop because there is plenty room and it’s well padded. In fact the laptop compartment will hold my 12” laptop and my 7” tablet even with the case on the tablet. That’s cool. I won’t bore you with more features because you can get that in the product specs here on Amazon or on Sony’s website. But I will tell you I like this bag. I own a much cheaper bag like this that is ok, too but what I like most about the Sony bag is that the Velcro on the separators comes off really well with out making a lot of noise. I know it sounds petty but after tugging at the other bag this is a welcome feature. Now, there is one gripe, I don’t understand why there’s nowhere dedicated to stow media cards or put writing utensils that you can get at quickly. I don’t understand this because the front bottom flap appears to have lots of room to put an external or even internal pocket to hold writing utensils even a small notebook. You might say use the left side pockets which is certainly possible if you’re not carrying a tripod, too. This is why I’m rating 4 stars instead of 5.

This backpack is SO much better than other camera packs that I have used before.
