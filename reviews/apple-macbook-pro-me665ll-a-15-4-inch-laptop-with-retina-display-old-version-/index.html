From an ex-PC junkie

I’ve used PC’s since the day I was born. As an avid gamer, I’ve always been a fan of Asus or Alienware branded laptops. That being said, my three year old Alienware m11x R1 saw it’s last days after a fatal screen hinge problem. The LCD screen doesn’t turn on anymore and Dell refuses to answer my calls or emails.As a college student, it was imperative that I found a replacement laptop within the two weeks of mine dying. I wanted to get something with a bigger screen but also portability. It also had to be something that would keep me from gaming (this is what happens when you’re a science major). Looked around at a lot of other PCs. Then I decided to go to the “dark side” and give Apple a try. Testing out a MBP for the first time was probably one of the best technological experiences I’ve had. Everything seems so intuitive and easier to learn. You can get more done quickly using gestures or commands on a Mac. This was one of its greatest selling points to me along with the build quality. When you pick a Mac up, it feels solid. Nothing rattles or makes cracking noises, and the design is so clean and minimal. I was sold after my first test run… then I found out there was a retina option. This display is incomparable to anything else. The images look sharper than real life! After being sold to the computer, I got sold to the amazing screen resolution. Not to mention how much THINNER this Mac is than its predecessors. It weighs the same amount as a regular MBP 13”!I’ve had so many friends keep their Macs for more than 5 years at a time. And it’s usually because they want to upgrade to a better model. For a PC user, that’s insane. Three years of use is enough to make your computer a veteran. You usually have to replace it because of all the viruses or issues with hardware. So, in essence, I feel that the money invested in a Mac is well spent. I’d rather have a quality machine that will survive the test of time and get me through the rest of my undergraduate and graduate school career than get a cheaper, new PC that will need to be replaced in another three years. Granted, PCs have a much more open platform and more freedom, but the quality of PC products just doesn’t compare to Apple. If PC companies can take that into consideration, I’d be sold to their newest product.On the actual usage side, I have to warn you that some programs have still yet to be “retina optimized”. Text and graphics will look pixelated on some windows. Microsoft office was like this until they released a patch. The text is still pixelated, but it’s not enough to gripe over anymore. The 8GB RAM and solid state drive make running programs a breeze. I’ve had 6 PDF articles open along with Photoshop CS6, Safari, Word, and iTunes open without any hitches.
