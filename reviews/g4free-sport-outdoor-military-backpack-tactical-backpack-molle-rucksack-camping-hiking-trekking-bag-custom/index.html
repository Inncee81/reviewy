Sturdy and affordable

I will start my saying that I purchased this backpack on my own for work and did not receive any benefits for this review. Just trying to help others who have questions regarding this product.Size: This back pack is the perfect size for a 48-72 hour pack. I am a Paramedic who works 24 hour shifts and I am able to pack workout clothes (basketball shorts and a t-shirt), a spare uniform (polo, EMS duty pants, t-shirt, extra socks, and boxers), my eye glasses case, a beanie, a winter hood, and winter gloves in the main compartment (the one closest to your back). In the large middle compartment I carry magazines and a 24 oz coffee mug. In the front compartments I carry an extra pair of sunglasses, toiletries, and other assorted items. All of this is packed with room for another set of clothes.Quality: Even though I have only had this pack for a month the stitching appears to be very sturdy. Straps are thick and most of the, with the exception of the upper part of the shoulder strap, are doubled over at the ends and stitched so they don’t come through the tightener. The shoulder straps that are not doubled over are tight enough in the tightener that they have not came loose in the least. The shoulder straps are padded and the padding was stiff for the first few weeks so it seems to be good for the long term. They have black D rings on the straps that are fairly thin but are perfect for hanging glasses cases, flashlights, keys, or any other items. The zippers are also sturdy metal and the zippers themselves are smooth on opening. As others have mentioned, the paracord around the zippers needs re-tied in a better knot but otherwise it’s just find out of the box. The only main downside is that the inside liner seems to be slightly loose and appears that it could rip out or tear easier than the rest of the pack but it appears to be stitched separately so if it does happen it will not hurt the integrity of he bag itself.Overall I would suggest this pack to anyone who is looking for an affordable 48-72 hour hiking, camping, work, or utility backpack. It is well worth the extra few dollars than paying for a cheaper, less sturdy backpack and more cost efficient than the 5.11 or other brand name ruck or duty blocks.
