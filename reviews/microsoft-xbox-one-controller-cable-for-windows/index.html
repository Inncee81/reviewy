Product Specifications

Endless Problems on PC

Trigger vibration (aka impulse triggers) doesn’t work at all in windows outside of the UWP windows store walled garden. Trigger vibration in steam games will NOT work. This is, believe it or not, an intentional design feature to encourage you to buy games on xbox and UWP. You can still get the basic left and right vibration motors working, but they are much weaker than the ones in the 360. Also in the 360 you can feel it instantly, whereas on the xbox one controller there is about a half second delay in the exact same game. This is because the impulse triggers were supposed to provide the immediate feedback while the 2 lower motors provide ambient vibration. So all you get is ambient vibration with none of the important vibration.I looked it up, apparently they didnt even add trigger vibration to the Xinput API, so developers cant even put it in their games even if they want to. Great job M$, you really outdid yourself this time.It was also all but impossible to get this controller working properly on windows 7. I spent about an hour before I got wired working, and about four more hours before I got wireless to work. That is totally crazy to me, holy smokes M$ get it together. On windows 10 it worked much better, the wired version worked right out of the box and the wireless only took me about 15 minutes that time (but I knew what I was doing that second time, so your mileage may vary.) Even now the wireless adapter will randomly de-sync from the controller on windows 10. I have no idea why. I start up my computer like normal and it just forgot about its previous pairing for some reason. A quick re-sync and it works again.On the bright side, the D-pad is much better this time around. So thats something.
