This is a great quadcopter. But it does take some knowledge and …

This is a great quadcopter. But it does take some knowledge and preparation. It needs to have the compass calibrated before flight. Just takes a few minutes.. You must allow time for it to get a GPS lock. Check the return home to see that both the Bebop and the Flight Controller have GPS, and good GPS. Don’t fly with only 2 bars. TURN OFF SMART DEVICE (PHONE) WIFI when using the Flight Controller. Read everything you can about flying the Bebop. Study the tutorials before flying (Parrot has some very good ones). Research YouTube.Camera has pros and cons. Center line camera with stabilization is great, but number of pixels are limited so it does not have great low light resolution. Stabilization is faster than gimbal. The gimbal adds weight and is highly susceptible to damage in even a minor crash. This is a hobby grade drone, it is not a toy or and entry level drone. It takes a little experience to get the most out of it. It is not an out of the box flyer. This is a flying camera. And with a little prep it will make great aerial videos and photos.Other quadcopter experience: Blade 350, Chroma, Bebop 1.

Great for the price (on discount)

I have had two flight sessions with the drone so far and am enjoying the experience. The camera quality is good but definitely not as good as hardware stabilization.The Good: The drone is well build for the price and comes with some nice accessories. I like that the design separates the motors from most of the other components with elastomers rather than just isolating the camera. There are a lot of settings that can be confusing at first but will be appreciated once you get the hang of what everything does. The FPV goggles (phone holder) worked well and FPV mode has a nice interface to help keep track of where the drone is compared to where you are.The Bad: The nose cone on the drone is made out of a material that looks similar to what is used on cycling helmets. I don’t think there’s anything wrong with using this material, but but would be nice if they had covered all of it up with the thin layer of plastic that covers the top.My phone does seem to get a little warm when using the FPV goggles. I don’t think this will be a problem if the goggles aren’t used flight after flight or on warmer days. It’s unfortunate though that the goggles replace the option that the old remote had of connecting a set of more expensive FPV glasses. I would have preferred that both options be possible. If you decided you really liked FVP mode you’d have an upgrade path to get dedicated FPV glasses.The new remote does have any way to attach a neck harness. It looks like you might be able to find an eyelet that could screw into the phone mount, but then you wouldn’t be able to use the phone mount while using a harness.The Bebop 2 no longer comes with prop guards and the ones that are available (not made by Parrot) look a bit flimsy.Most of the things that I don’t like about the drone are vey minor issues and none of them alone would add up to a lower than perfect rating, but together they add up to one less star.When I purchased this, it was offered at a significant discount. I am very happy with it for the money that I paid, but at the normal price, I probably would have looked closer at some of the other available drones.
