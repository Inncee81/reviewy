  • Auto-locking accordion expanders adjust to fit most windows from 22 1⁄2” to 33”
  • Manually reversible by rotating the fan for intake (brings fresh air in) or exhaust (takes stale air out)

  • Measures 22 1⁄2” - 33” L x 4” W x 10 3⁄4” H

Stay comfortable during the summer months with this 9-inch dual motor twin window fan. 2-speed dial control this window fan provides a quiet operation that is great for during the day or in a bedroom at night. Set on exhaust during the day, the fan creates a refreshing indoor breeze that expels accumulating heat. Set on intake at night, it draws in the cool evening air. The side panels are extendable to fit in double-hung and slider windows of different sizes and the fan housing is made of plastic for durability. Window fan comes with removable feet for tabletop use or standard in-the-window use. This window fan also includes a removable fabric cover to keep out hot air, humidity and bugs when not in use. For your safety this product is ETL/US Listed.

and sturdy enough not to break when it fell out of …

The fan is quiet, lightweight, and sturdy enough not to break when it fell out of the window, twice. Therein lies the problem. Since it’s reversible, we bought it mostly to use as an exhaust fan in the kitchen, where we have a small double vinyl double-hung window, to push out the hot air and draw in cold air from the other side of the house. In the few seconds it stayed in the window, it seemed to be doing just that. But there was nothing keeping it in the window other than the open window pressing down on it. (There’s no lip as there invariably is on an air conditioner.) So, obeying Newton’s third law of motion (think: jet engine), the force of the air blowing out propelled the fan in the other direction, crashing onto the kitchen floor. After this happened twice, I tried moving it into a larger living room window where I tried to get the expandable baffles to fit behind the window frame. But they wouldn’t stay open; they kept flopping back. So again nothing was going to keep the fan in this window either, and there also wouldn’t be a seal necessary for the exhaust to bring in cold air from elsewhere. You’d think the manufacturer would have tested the fan in an actual window. So it’s going back.
