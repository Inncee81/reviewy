it doesn’t even look like there’s something extra on there and the only difference …

Some of the keys on my Macbook had started to wear off, leaving ugly white splotches where letters used to be (for some reason, it appears the black part is what wears off rather than the letters themselves rubbing off the black keys). I wanted to find a skin that could cover and protect the keys, but also maintain the original look of the keyboard. This one fit the bill perfectly.Depending on the lighting, it doesn’t even look like there’s something extra on there and the only difference felt when typing is the softer texture of the keys. I also haven’t noticed any movement in the skin while typing on it, but the edges do lift up if you happen to brush against the corners or edges of the skin. There is nothing that holds it in place other than how it shapes itself to the keys and fits into the depression that holds the keyboard. The backlight shows perfectly through the skin as well.The main negative I have noticed is how easily the rubbery texture attracts dust, lint, hair, and any other small particle that might come into contact with it. There doesn’t appear to be any decent way to clean them off and keep the keys looking sleek and black, but I suppose I should just be happy this stuff is no longer getting stuck between or under the actual keys.
