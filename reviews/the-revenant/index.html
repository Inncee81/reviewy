Beautiful in all ways

This has become one of my very favorite movies and I have watched it four times. “The Revenant” disserved every Oscar it won and probably more. The story is based on the true story of Hugh Glass, who is 1823, went on an expedition into uncharted territory with a group of fur trappers and was actually mauled by a bear, but apparently not to the extent that DiCaprio was in the movie. The difference was that the real Glass was not left behind to die alone in the snow and cold of a northern winter.The movie is a wonder of excellent acting, cinematography, sound, directing, and DiCaprio tells his story without saying a whole lot throughout the movie. It is a story of revenge and survival that is told so well by the wrting, co-production and direction of Alejandro G. Inarritu. The first inkling for making the movie first surfaced with Inarritu in 2001, so it took a long time to finally get it made. This film is a work of art that plays out in snow-filled, icy and uninhabited beautiful but sometimes deadly land, with Indian raids happening and the poignant story of a lost Indian daughter.Tom Hardy did an excellent job of acting, also. His accent in the movie made me laugh every time I heard it at first. This is a movie that shouldn’t be missed.

GREAT MOVIE - A WORK OF ART MEANT TO BE SEEN SEVERAL TIMES - EACH TIME SEEING MORE DETAILS ! ! !

What a movie !!! It is an absolute work of art. The people who say it is dark, moody and depressing should not be watching it at all. It does an excellent job of portraying life in the wilderness in the 1800’s of men who trapped furs for a living and lived a very raw life just to survive. I honestly do not know how Leonardo DiCaprio ever managed to live through this movie while it was being made - I was frozen the whole time and felt like I was right there with him & the other trappers and Indians. Life back then was horrible and for the men that chose trapping in the wild during the time that Indians stalked everywhere; they really had a rough life. Eating raw fish & meat and even rotten food was just the way they had to take it sometimes, to survive. The Cinematographer Lubezki and the Director Alejandro certainly deserved awards for their skills and talents. I am going to look up the movie “Man in the Wilderness” with Richard Harris just to compare how the story was portrayed differently. This is an incredible movie, which certainly entertained me, even if it was hard to believe that the main character could survive so many unbelievable threats to his life!
