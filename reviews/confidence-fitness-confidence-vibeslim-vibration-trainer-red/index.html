This Vibration Platform is FANTASTIC! Here is What You Need to Know, How to Use It, and How to Find a User Manual!

I originally bought this vibration platform because I broke my wrist, and being unable to work out (I usually work out or dance at least five evenings a week, I have workout equipment at home (an elliptical machine, a stair machine, and a Pilates chair)) I wanted to do something for my body while I was unable to work out. Ironically, I broke my wrist while dancing! Both being mindful that weeks without being able to work out would not do my body well, and having read that a vibration platform can help strengthen bones (hello, broken wrist here), I decided to get a vibration platform. And after doing some research and reading lots of reviews about many of the different platforms available on Amazon, this is the one I decided to get.And I am so glad that I did!Here is what I have learned, and what you need to know, about this vibration platform.First of all, this thing does not come with anything remotely like a user’s manual. At least mine didn’t. And if you try searching online for the user’s manual you won’t find it. But what I did find was the user’s manual for an identical vibration platform, only that has handrails attached. So all of the information and instructions and exercises are the same, minus the handrails! I found that manual by searching for ‘confidence vibration plate owner’s manual’.And speaking of remotely, the remote definitely comes in handy when you want to switch speeds while on the platform (see what I did there? :-) )Also, a lot of people want to know whether this machine oscillates. That completely depends on what you mean by oscillate. If what you mean is going up-and-down alternating between feet, like a seesaw (teeter totter) rather than just vibrate, yes it does. In fact what I have learned is that the vibrating is basically that oscillation at very high speed (you can set it from speeds 1 to 50â€¦ 50 is the fastest vibration, 1 is the slowest oscillation).The next thing I learned was that, somewhat counter-intuitively, the slower the oscillation, the greater the workout. This is because at low speeds it makes you more wobbly, for lack of a better description, and so your core is firing all the time. At the higher speeds, as noted above, you are getting more of a vibration.Relatedly, the closer together your feet are the less oscillation you are going to get (and so the less core firing work out). If you think, again, of a teeter totter, this makes perfect sense: the closer to the center of the teeter totter, the smaller the up-and-down motion (oscillation). Way out at the ends of the teeter totter, the ends are going aaaaall the way up and aaaaall the way down (greater oscillation).Finally, does it work? In a word, yes! Of course that also depends on what you mean by it working. If you use this thing at lower oscillations, you will definitely feel your abs and core muscles working. That said, everything I’ve read says that just standing there while it does its thing is not going to really make a huge difference, you need to be doing exercises while it’s going. The user’s manual (and also videos you can find online) will give you a good idea of how best to use this thing to its best advantage.Happy vibrating!
