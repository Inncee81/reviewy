Download Retropie from their website, extract the program from the Zip Folder, and write it onto a Micro SD Card using win32diskimager (a free, easy to find program). Then just insert that Micro SD Card in the Raspberry Pi and turn it on. After it boots up, plug any empty flash drive into the Raspberry Pi, and it’ll create all the necessary folders for roms. Then remove the flash drive when its light stops flashing and use your PC to put the right roms into the right folders, then plug that flash drive back into the Raspberry Pi. It’ll copy over all the roms, so you won’t need the flash drive anymore. And that’s it! Just configure your controller and your done! Its so easy even a dummy like me could do it! Excellent kit wtih a nice case and quality power supply

This is a really nice kit. The power supply and its cable feel thick and robust, the clear case is nice, fits the Raspberry Pi perfectly, and clicks together nicely. I didn’t bother with the heat sinks though as there is debate on weather they’re necessary with some reports of them actually raising chip temperatures if not used with better thermal conducting adhesive.The Raspberry Pi 3 is noticeably higher performance than my 2 was, I especially see this when trying to play Youtube videos in Raspbian or playing retro video games. It has integrated wifi and bluetooth too so no need for USB wifi. My only complaint is that my 2 had a spring loaded micro-SD slot but the 3’s microSD slot isn’t spring loaded so it’s a smidge harder when swapping cards in and out.

Case doesn’t fit well and causes Pi to fail

Each time the Pi had been working fine until I closed it in the case. Once it caused a great inconvenience as I had to rebuild the entire OS and app software stack while on the road. Now I use an alternative case. Two stars as it is a nice kit, and cheap enough, but this failure point makes it a pain if you’re using it for an application beyond playing around.
