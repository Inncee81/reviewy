The camera and lenses are ok, the rest not so much, but ok for starters

This kit is a good entry-level package, but you need to bear in mind that most of the accesories are cheap and wont last that much. The camera itself and the two lenses are Canon, which means top quality at its level, but that’s basically the only stuff that you won’t throw away in the short run.The Memory card is ok in storage but you’ll find the need to buy bigger/faster cards in no time.The wide/tele lens adapter are ok only if you don’t mind poor focusing trouble. They are near useless in low light conditions, so you better spend in the proper adapters if you really want to gain that functionality.The 3-piece filter are good for starters, but sooner rather than later you’ll be replacing them.The battery replacement is ok, it says it’s OEM and so far performs well.The cleaning kit is the usual you’ll get.Card reader, those come even in cereal boxes these days.Wrist grip looks and feels quite cheap. It’s a good alternative to the strap, but if you’re going to use a wrist grip, youll get a leather one.Silicone band? Not even need to be mentioned.The tripod looks durable and sturdy but I wouldn’t trust too much on it while using the 75-300mm lens.The HDMI cable I guess is ok. Haven’t use it yet.Bottom line: My kit was priced at 499 (price has varied since). I’d say it was a good deal for the money, and I do plan to improve the accesories over time.
