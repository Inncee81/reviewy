A Wonderful Souvenir for a Party.

I bought this film for use with the Fujifilm Instax camera. It worked perfectly and was very easy to install, even with English-as-a-second-language directions.The pictures themselves are small but charming, about the length of my middle finger and less than half as wide as the length. The film shoots out of the camera just like a classic Polaroid and takes about as long to develop as the classic brand did, so be patient if you don’t seem your image come through for a couple of minutes. It develops with that wonderful, yellowy vintage sheen that’s so appealing to my nostalgia, and because the picture sizes are so small, they make wonderful gifts and be tucked anywhere–wallets, bulletin boards, desk frames.For my part, I bought these for my son’s third birthday carnival. You can see in the attached photo that they were part of the giveaways to my guests after they took pictures at our photo wall. My guests went gaga over them. Lots of happy friends that day!

Extremely handy, more cost-efficient than buying two packs at a time.

I strongly prefer this size to the wider sheets. I rarely have a poor exposure with this film, and if I do, it’s always due to some silly user error. I don’t know if it’s explicitly stated anywhere on here, but these photos all print out the exact dimensions of a credit card, so pro tip: invest in a handy credit card holder and you’ll have a cool way to keep your prints clean and wrinkle-free at parties.One thing I did find weird and kind of unsettling is (and I did see it in several reviews before I placed my order), while this is advertised as one big box of 10 sheets x 5 packs… it came in two standard two packs in retail packaging, and one lopse pack in its foil wrapper (with no expiration date). Don’t expect a nice, tidy box from Fuji to show up at your door.
