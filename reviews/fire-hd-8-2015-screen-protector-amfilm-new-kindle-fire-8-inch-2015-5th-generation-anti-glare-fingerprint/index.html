    Product Description

Introducing amFilm Premium Anti-Glare/Anti-Fingerprint (Matte) Screen Protectors for your Fire HD8 2015.

amFilm Premium Anti-Glare/Anti-Fingerprint Protectors are made from superior quality 3-layer PET film, and specifically designed to fit your Fire HD8 2015 device. The Anti-Glare top coating not only reduce the glare but also reduce the fingerprints from daily use. In addition it effectively protects the screen from daily scratches, dust and scrapes. With a self-adhering surface, the screen protectors are designed for easy application and zero-to-minimal bubbles during application. A squeeze card is also included to remove the occasional bubble easily.

Works with: Fire HD8 2015

Contains: 2xScreen Protector , Micro-Fiber Cleaning Cloth, Plastic Squeeze Card, Hinge/Dust Removal Stickers  Color: Anti-Glare/Anti-Fingerprint Application: Each of the screen protectors contains 3 layers of PET film. The first and last films are mask films, where the stickers are affixed. The actual screen protector for your device is the film in the middle. Product Reviews

Really nice look and feel, and was one of the easier installs I’ve done.

I’ve put on my fair share of screen protectors over the years, so I’ve had plenty of practice. I found installing this one pretty easy, and the instructions clear. I especially like the little rubber bands that help you place the film perfectly before you attach the tape hinges. That was a good idea and helped make it easier to do. I was able to apply the first one flawlessly. I don’t like the reflective screens (which is why I purchase the anti-glare, but I use that feature to rotate the device under a good light to see from all angles if there’s any dust there. And then clean, clean, clean and tape, tape, tape before installing. If you’re in a dusty room, that may take a lot of work, but worth it.The film itself is matt finish, and is nice and clear. It keeps the sharp screen looking sharp, but without all that reflection. I’ve had it on just a few hours, but it really seems to help with finger prints. It will have to be cleaned once in a while, but not as often as the unprotected Kindle Fire does.It also seems to respond to touch really well under the film. There’s no problem there.I’ll be testing it out for quite a while, but it’s started out really well and I really like it.

Install in a steam shower room?

The problem with every screen protector I have bought to date, for my iPhones and previous Kindle Fire, have been painful to put on. This one was easier, because of their approach, in being able to slide the protector on. They do recommend that it be put on in the steam of a hot shower room. While I did not do this, a version of the small “bubbles” I have always gotten still did appear, but I was able to squeeze most of them out. I consider this a reflection of my not following their installation tip and will redo it, when I find I need to. I have had this for quite a while and nothing about my initial install bothers me, in any lighting conditions. I would buy this product again, for myself or as a gift.

Hit and miss.

Although they’re decent, sturdy screen protectors, why the complicated instructions? It’s recommended that you apply the screen protector in the bathroom after running hot water. The instructions state the steam will lessen the amount of bubbles accumulated while installing a protector. Is that really a practical suggestion? You’d think steam and electronics are like oil and water. Who has the time or wants so much bother. I applied the first protector and had to remove it because there were way too many bubbles, even after cleaning the screen with a cloth and using the device included to eliminate bubbling. I tried and failed. There’s got to be an easier way to install a screen protector. Protector #2 is still inside the package. It’s way too aggravating to install them or maybe I’m a simpleton.
