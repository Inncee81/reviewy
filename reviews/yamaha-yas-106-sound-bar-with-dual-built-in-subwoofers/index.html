Rich sound and a “Clear Voice” button that makes dialogue clear…never turn it off. +1 for including an optical cable

I just love this soundbar.I replaced a piece of crap Vizio. My wife and I could not make out any dialogue with the Vizio—anytime we watched an action movie, I had to have my hand on the volume control so I could turn it up during dialogue and then turn it down during action.Enter the Yamaha YAS-106 with a special button called “Clear Voice”. Exceeded expectations for price

Initial impressions of sound bar after having it a week:So far, extremely pleased with it, exceeded my expectations for the price.Problem we were trying to solve is improve the sound for a TV in a basement room where a treadmill and sewing machine are also used. We needed something wall-mountable, but did not want a separate subwoofer for space reasons. Our previous attempt to improve the sound was to use the speakers in the treadmill (through a stereo audio cable) which helped, but was still far below the quality of the sound from our Yamaha 5.1 system in another room. We had begun to notice the dramatic differences between the two on the details we could here.So some notes:- Sound quality is a dramatic and noticeable improvement than what we had. We do get the sense of direction and surround sound which didn’t get before. Much closer to, but not quite at the level of our true Yamaha 5.1 system.- Clear Voice. This setting was the biggest pleasant surprise. When using the treadmill and the noise from that, use of this setting made the dialog appear much clearer, and much easier to listen to.- Wall mounting installation was easy. Note: The manual tells the consumer not to do this themselves, but we ignored that and did it ourselves. Comes with two adhesive spacers so the unit doesn’t rest flush against the wall, which is something to remember for length of screw needed. Does include a template for where two drill the two holes for mounting, but does not come with the actual hardware screws for mounting.- Cables. Came with a digital optical audio cable (be sure to take off the plastic caps before trying to plug it in), but does not come with an HDMI cable. We’re using the optical audio. I tried to set up the HDMI with ARC (so that the TV remote could control the volume), but were unsuccessful in getting it to work. I suspect that the problem is that I didn’t get the settings on the TV right.- LED indicators and buttons. There are LED indicators and buttons on the unit so you don’t have to use the remote control. The labels are unreadable from any distance, I imagine that I’ll just use the positions of the lights.- Remote. Remote uses a “coin”-type battery not something like a AAA which I would have preferred, but isn’t a big deal.- Bluetooth pairing with an iPhone was easy, it immediately asked if I wanted to also download the app. Note: The app is not the same app as what I have for our other Yamaha sound system.
