Product Reviews

Great alternative to the expensive leather ones

I bought three of these for infield drills for our team. We’re on season 4 and all 3 have survived. I have to be mindful where I put them as they are foam and if left in a position (IE under something with shape) it will form to that and will take awhile to flatten itself back out. Other than that they are great for teaching kids to use 2 hands when receiving a ball for quicker delivery. If you’re not sold on these cheap ones (though they work perfectly for their application), check out the leather versions of the trainer.

Great concept, poorly executed

I bought several of these for my 10U travel softball team and they started falling apart as soon as the players put them on. There are two foam sheets glued together but the glue does not hold! The two sheets delaminate and make the glove useless. We taped them together with trainer’s tape out of our medical kit to get through practice.I’ve bought quite a few SKLZ-brand training aides and I’ve always been happy with the products but these flat gloves didn’t live up to my expectations. Pure junk!

Good, Not Great

Good training tool. The foam is a little thinner than expected so you need to use wiffle balls. Needs to be a lot thicker to use with baseballs. I recommend getting one of the leather versions instead if you are looking to do infield and/or catcher drills with a real ball. This is perfect for younger kids to learn to catch (receive ball).

This is great for teaching a player to use two hands when …

This is great for teaching a player to use two hands when catching a ball. Highly recommend for coaches and players. I use it for fastpitch and it works well.

A must have for coaches

Great training and from t-ball up though adults. Gets them working proper mechanics while fielding a ground ball.

Good tool for keeping throwing hand involved when fielding ground balls.

My son plays a lot of middle infield. I had trouble getting my son to use two hand fielding ground balls. This makes him focus on keeping the throwing hand near his glove. It also makes him keep his head and hands down until the ball is secured between this trainer and his throwing hand. While I do not hit game speed ground balls to him with this yet, using it for drills has been effective for improving his form and fielding mechanics.This also is a good tool for practicing shovel passes on double plays.
